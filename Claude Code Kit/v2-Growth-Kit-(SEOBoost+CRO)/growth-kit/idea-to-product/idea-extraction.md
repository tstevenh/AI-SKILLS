# Idea Extraction

Turn a vague product idea into a validated business concept. This skill extracts comprehensive information through structured questions to build a Master Strategy Document with viability scoring.

**Duration:** 30-60 minutes
**Output:** Master Strategy Document + Viability Score

---

## When to Use

- Starting with a new product idea that needs validation
- Have a rough concept but haven't documented the business model
- Need to score viability before committing development time
- Want structured thinking about market, revenue, and technical requirements

---

## Interview Framework

### Core Questions (25 Total)

**Core Concept (5 Questions)**

1. **What problem does your product solve?**

   - Be specific about the pain point
   - Who experiences this pain?
   - How are they solving it today?

2. **What is your product's core value proposition?**

   - "My product helps [target] do [action] so they can [benefit]"
   - What makes this valuable enough to pay for?

3. **What is your key innovation or differentiator?**

   - What do you do better/different than alternatives?
   - Why can't competitors easily copy this?

4. **What type of product is this?**

   - Pure SaaS (subscription)
   - Productized service (human + software)
   - Marketplace / Tool / Information product

5. **What is your product name and positioning statement?**
   - "[Product] is a [category] that [key benefit]"

---

**Target Market (4 Questions)**

6. **Who is your ideal customer?**

   - Demographics or firmographics
   - Job title / role
   - Company size / stage

7. **What is their current behavior?**

   - How do they solve this problem today?
   - What tools do they currently use?
   - How much do they spend on alternatives?

8. **What triggers them to seek a solution?**

   - Pain event or growth stage
   - Seasonal patterns
   - External pressures

9. **Where do they hang out online?**
   - Communities (Reddit, Discord, Slack)
   - Social platforms
   - Content they consume

---

**Revenue Model (4 Questions)**

10. **What is your pricing model?**

    - Subscription tiers (monthly/annual)
    - One-time purchase / Usage-based / Freemium

11. **What are your target price points?**

    - Entry tier / Most popular tier / Enterprise tier
    - Reasoning for these prices

12. **What are the tier differentiators?**

    - Usage limits / Feature gates / Support levels

13. **What is your revenue target?**
    - 6-month MRR goal / 12-month MRR goal
    - Customer count targets
    - Average Revenue Per User (ARPU) estimate

---

**Technical Requirements (5 Questions)**

14. **What core features are required for MVP?**

    - List 3-5 must-have features
    - What can be deferred to v2?

15. **What external APIs or services will you integrate?**

    - Data sources / Payment processing / Email / AI/ML / Analytics

16. **What are your data requirements?**

    - User data to store / Content handling / Privacy considerations

17. **Do you need background processing?**

    - Scheduled jobs / Async processing / Real-time features

18. **What are your scaling expectations?**
    - Users at launch / 6 months / 12 months
    - Traffic patterns (steady vs spiky)

---

**Go-to-Market (4 Questions)**

19. **What is your launch strategy?**

    - Beta/waitlist / ProductHunt / Community seeding / Content marketing

20. **What are your primary acquisition channels?**

    - Organic (SEO, content) / Paid (ads, sponsorships)
    - Community (Reddit, Twitter) / Partnerships

21. **What is your content strategy?**

    - Blog topics / Social presence / Email marketing

22. **Do you have existing audience or distribution?**
    - Email list / Social following / Community presence

---

**Risks & Constraints (3 Questions)**

23. **What are your biggest technical risks?**

    - API dependencies / Scaling / Data accuracy / Third-party reliability

24. **What are your business constraints?**

    - Budget / Time to launch / Geographic/legal restrictions

25. **What keeps you up at night about this idea?**
    - Market timing / Competition / Execution / Personal commitment

## Viability Scorecard

**Purpose:** Score your idea's viability using 7 weighted factors. Aim for 70+ out of 100.

### Scoring Matrix

| Factor                | Weight | Description                                                         |
| --------------------- | ------ | ------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Market Potential      | 20%    | Large, growing market with real pain and willingness to pay         |
| Technical Feasibility | 20%    | Can be built with available technology within budget                |
| Competitive Landscape | 15%    | Can differentiate meaningfully and defend position                  |
| Revenue Potential     | 15%    | Can reach meaningful recurring revenue ($10K+ MRR) within 12 months |
| Time to MVP           | 10%    | Can launch valuable MVP within 4 weeks                              |
| Founder Fit           | 10%    | Skills, motivation, and context to build this                       |
| Growth Potential      | 10%    | Can grow through virality, SEO, or network effects                  |

### Score Interpretation

| Total Score  | Interpretation          | Recommendation          |
| ------------ | ----------------------- | ----------------------- |
| **85-100**   | Exceptional opportunity | Proceed immediately     |
| **70-84**    | Strong opportunity      | Proceed with confidence |
| **55-69**    | Moderate opportunity    | Proceed with caution    |
| **40-54**    | Weak opportunity        | Reconsider or pivot     |
| **Below 40** | Poor opportunity        | Find different idea     |

## Interview Completion Checklist

Before generating the Strategy Document:

- [ ] Clear problem statement articulated
- [ ] Target customer specifically defined
- [ ] Pricing model with actual numbers
- [ ] MVP features prioritized (not everything)
- [ ] Technical requirements scoped
- [ ] Launch strategy outlined
- [ ] Key risks identified
- [ ] Revenue targets set
