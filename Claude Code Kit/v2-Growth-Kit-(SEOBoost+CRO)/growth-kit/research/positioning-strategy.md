# Market Positioning Strategy

Positioning is the strategic foundation that informs all marketing execution. This skill helps you define where you sit in the market and why customers should choose you over alternatives.

---

## The Core Job

Define a **clear market position** that answers:

1. **Category:** What type of product are you?
2. **Audience:** Who is this for specifically?
3. **Differentiation:** Why choose you over alternatives?
4. **Value:** What transformation do you deliver?
5. **Proof:** Why should they believe you?

## The Positioning Framework

### Category Definition

**What are you?**

| Approach              | Example                           | When to Use                                            |
| --------------------- | --------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------ |
| **Existing category** | "A project management tool"       | Category is established, you have clear differentiator |
| **Modified category** | "AI-first project management"     | Need to signal innovation within known space           |
| **New category**      | "A productivity operating system" | Breaking new ground, requires more education           |

**Considerations:**

- Existing categories = easier explanation, more competition
- New categories = differentiation built-in, harder to explain
- Most products should use existing or modified categories

## Positioning Statement Template

Combine the elements above:

```
[Your product] is a [category] for [specific audience].

Unlike [primary alternative], we [key differentiator].

This means you can [primary benefit], without [pain you eliminate].

[Proof point] proves this works.
```

**Example:**

> "Linear is a project management tool for product teams building software.
>
> Unlike Jira, we prioritize speed and simplicity over configuration.
>
> This means you can spend more time building and less time managing your tool.
>
> 5,000+ teams including Vercel and Ramp use Linear daily."

## Positioning Validation

### Internal Alignment Test

Can everyone on your team answer these identically?

- [ ] What category are we in?
- [ ] Who is our ideal customer?
- [ ] What makes us different?
- [ ] Why should they choose us?
- [ ] What's our primary proof point?

If answers vary, positioning isn't clear enough.

### External Clarity Test

Show your positioning to 5 people in your target audience.

- [ ] Do they understand what you do in 5 seconds?
- [ ] Do they understand who it's for?
- [ ] Do they understand why you're different?
- [ ] Does it resonate with their situation?
- [ ] Can they repeat it back accurately?

If not, positioning needs work.

### Competitive Differentiation Test

- [ ] Could a competitor claim the same thing? (If yes, not differentiated enough)
- [ ] Is the opposite a valid position? (If no, too generic)
- [ ] Does it exclude some customers? (If no, too broad)

## Positioning Levels

### Company-Level Positioning

Your overall market position. This is stable and rarely changes.

**Example:** "The AI-native productivity platform for developers"

### Product-Level Positioning

Specific products may have variations.

**Example:**

- Core product: "AI productivity for developers"
- Enterprise version: "AI productivity with enterprise security"
- Individual plan: "Personal AI productivity toolkit"

### Campaign-Level Positioning

Temporary emphasis for specific initiatives.

**Example:** During a launch campaign, emphasize speed: "Ship twice as fast with AI"

---

## Positioning vs Messaging

| Positioning               | Messaging                             |
| ------------------------- | ------------------------------------- |
| Strategic (what we claim) | Tactical (how we say it)              |
| Stable (rarely changes)   | Variable (adapts to channel/audience) |
| Internal alignment tool   | External communication                |
| Answers "why us"          | Implements "why us"                   |

**Positioning:** "We're the fastest PM tool for shipping teams"
**Messaging:** "Ship in days, not weeks" (headline) / "Built for teams that move fast" (tagline) / "Join 5,000 teams who ship faster" (CTA)

---

## Positioning Evolution

### When to Revisit Positioning

- Market category has shifted
- New competitors changed the landscape
- Customer needs have evolved
- Your product capabilities have changed
- Current positioning isn't resonating (low conversion)

### When NOT to Change Positioning

- Single campaign underperformed (might be execution)
- One customer gave negative feedback
- Competitor copied your positioning (often validates it)
- Sales team wants "more features" in messaging

## The Test

Strong positioning:

1. **Is it specific?** (Not "for everyone")
2. **Is it different?** (Not what competitors claim)
3. **Is it true?** (Defensible and provable)
4. **Is it relevant?** (Matters to target audience)
5. **Is it memorable?** (Can be repeated back)
6. **Is it consistent?** (Same across all touchpoints)

If any answer is no, positioning needs refinement.
