# Target Segments Analysis

Understanding who you're selling to is the foundation of effective marketing. Generic messaging to "everyone" converts no one. This skill helps identify, prioritize, and understand specific market segments.

---

## The Core Job

Identify and profile **3-5 distinct customer segments** that represent your best opportunities for growth.

For each segment, understand:

- Who they are (demographics, role, situation)
- What they need (pain points, desires, goals)
- How they buy (decision process, objections, triggers)
- Where to find them (channels, communities, watering holes)
- What messaging resonates (language, proof, positioning)

---

## Segment Discovery Process

### Step 1: List All Possible Segments

Start broad. Who could potentially use your product?

**By Role:**

- Job titles, functions, responsibilities
- Decision-makers vs users vs influencers

**By Company:**

- Size (solo, SMB, mid-market, enterprise)
- Industry or vertical
- Stage (startup, growth, established)

**By Situation:**

- Problem severity (acute vs chronic)
- Current solutions (none, competitor, DIY)
- Buying stage (aware, considering, ready)

**By Behavior:**

- How they solve this problem now
- What they've tried before
- How they discover new solutions

## Example: SaaS Startup Founder Segment

### Solo/Early-Stage SaaS Founders

**One-line definition:** Technical founders building their first or second SaaS product with limited budget and time.

#### Demographics

| Attribute     | Value                                   |
| ------------- | --------------------------------------- |
| Job title(s)  | Founder, CEO, CTO, "Building in public" |
| Company size  | Solo to 5 employees                     |
| Industry      | SaaS, developer tools, B2B              |
| Geographic    | Global, English-speaking                |
| Age range     | 25-45                                   |
| Income/budget | $0-5k/month on tools                    |

#### Psychographics

**Goals:**

- Launch and validate product quickly
- Reach first $1k, then $10k MRR
- Build sustainable business, not VC-scale

**Fears:**

- Building wrong thing
- Running out of runway
- Being outcompeted by funded startups
- Wasting time on wrong activities

**Frustrations:**

- Too many tasks, not enough time
- Tools designed for bigger teams
- Marketing feels like a distraction from building
- Can't afford to hire specialists

**Motivations:**

- Desire for autonomy and flexibility
- Want to build something meaningful
- Financial freedom
- Creative expression through building

#### Current State

**How they solve this problem now:**

- Cobbling together free tools
- Spending nights and weekends on marketing
- Reading Twitter threads and blogs
- Trial and error

**What they've tried:**

- Various free tools
- Courses that were too theoretical
- Advice that didn't apply to their stage

**Awareness level:**

- [x] Solution aware (know solutions exist, looking for the right one)

#### Buying Behavior

**Decision process:**

- Solo decision-maker
- Heavy research phase (reads reviews, asks community)
- Prefers one-time purchase over subscription
- Values lifetime access

**Timeline:**

- 1-2 weeks from discovery to purchase
- Often buys during launch promotions

**Objections:**

- "Is this worth the money when I'm bootstrapping?"
- "Will this work for my specific situation?"
- "Is there a cheaper alternative?"

**Purchase triggers:**

- Frustration with current approach peaks
- See peer success with the product
- Launch discount or special offer
- Recommendation from trusted source

#### Where to Find Them

**Online communities:**

- r/SaaS, r/startups, r/Entrepreneur
- Indie Hackers community
- Twitter/X #buildinpublic

**Content they consume:**

- Indie Hackers podcast
- Build in public newsletters
- Founder Twitter threads

**Social platforms:**

- Twitter/X (primary)
- LinkedIn (secondary)

**Search behavior:**

- "How to [specific task] for SaaS"
- "[Tool] alternative"
- "Best [tool category] for startups"

#### Messaging That Resonates

**Core message:**

> "Stop wasting your limited time on marketing trial and error. Get a proven system that works for bootstrapped founders."

**Pain point messaging:**

- "You can't hire a marketing team. But you can use their playbooks."
- "Stop guessing what to write. Know exactly what converts."

**Benefit messaging:**

- "Launch in weeks, not months"
- "Marketing that works while you build"

**Social proof they trust:**

- Other indie founders they recognize
- Specific MRR numbers and timelines
- Before/after comparisons

**Language to use:**

- Ship, launch, build, bootstrap
- MRR, ARR, churn
- Casual, peer-to-peer tone

**Language to avoid:**

- Enterprise jargon
- "Scale" (too VC-focused)
- Overly salesy language

## Integration with Other Skills

**Target segments feed into:**

- **hook-finder** - Different segments may need different angles
- **lead-magnet-builder** - Segment-specific opt-in offers
- **direct-response-copy** - Tailored landing pages per segment
- **email-sequences** - Segment-specific nurture flows
- **content-repurposer** - Platform selection based on where segments live

**The flow:**

1. Identify and profile target segments
2. Create segment-specific positioning
3. Build segment-specific funnels
4. Measure and refine based on segment performance

---

## The Test

Good segment analysis answers:

1. **Who exactly are we targeting?** (Specific, not generic)
2. **Why this segment?** (Clear prioritization logic)
3. **What do they need?** (Deep understanding of problems)
4. **Where do we find them?** (Concrete channels and communities)
5. **What messaging works?** (Segment-specific language)

If any answer is vague, dig deeper into research.
