# Demo & Sales Conversion

## Introduction

For many B2B SaaS products, especially those with higher ACVs, a demo or sales conversation is a critical conversion point. The demo is often where deals are won or lost. This guide covers optimizing demo booking, demo structure, follow-up sequences, and proposal best practices.

---

## 1. Demo Booking Optimization

### Getting More Demo Requests

**Website CTA Optimization:**

Instead of: "Contact Us"
Use: "Book a Demo" or "Get a Demo"

Instead of: "Request Information"
Use: "See [Product] in Action"

**Demo Page Best Practices:**

```
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│             Book Your Personalized Demo                 │
│                                                         │
│  See how [Product] can help you [main benefit].        │
│                                                         │
│  In your 30-minute demo, you'll:                       │
│  • See [Product] in action                             │
│  • Learn how to [solve problem]                        │
│  • Get answers to your questions                       │
│  • Receive custom recommendations                      │
│                                                         │
│  ┌─────────────────────────────────────┐               │
│  │ First Name [____________]           │               │
│  │ Work Email [____________]           │               │
│  │ Company [____________]              │               │
│  │ Role [____________]                 │               │
│  │ [Book My Demo]                      │               │
│  └─────────────────────────────────────┘               │
│                                                         │
│  Prefer to explore yourself? [Start Free Trial]        │
│                                                         │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

**Form Fields to Ask:**
- Name (required)
- Work email (required)
- Company (required)
- Job title/role (helpful for prep)
- Company size (helpful for qualification)
- Use case (optional, helps personalization)

**Avoid:**
- Too many required fields
- Vague CTA
- No alternative option
- Long wait for confirmation

### Scheduling Tools

**Self-Scheduling:**
- Calendly
- HubSpot meetings
- Chili Piper
- SavvyCal

**Benefits:**
- Reduces back-and-forth
- Instant confirmation
- Better show rates
- Professional impression

**Best Practices:**
- Offer multiple time slots
- Show timezone clearly
- Send calendar invite immediately
- Include video link

### Reducing No-Shows

**Confirmation Email:**
```
Subject: Your [Product] demo is confirmed for [Date/Time]

Hi [Name],

Your demo is booked! Here are the details:

📅 Date: [Date]
🕐 Time: [Time] [Timezone]
🔗 Video link: [Link]

Before we meet, you might find these helpful:
• [Resource 1]
• [Resource 2]

See you soon!
[Your Name]
```

**Reminder Sequence:**
- 24 hours before: Email reminder
- 1 hour before: Email reminder
- 15 minutes before: Optional SMS

**No-Show Follow-Up:**
```
Subject: Missed our call — let's reschedule

Hi [Name],

I was looking forward to our demo today. No worries if 
something came up!

Here's my calendar to reschedule: [Link]

Or if you'd prefer, I recorded a quick personalized 
video overview: [Loom Link]

[Your Name]
```

### Demo Booking Benchmarks

| Metric | Target |
|--------|--------|
| Demo page conversion | 15-30% |
| Booking to show rate | 70-85% |
| Re-schedule rate | 10-20% |
| No-show rate | <15% |

## 2. Demo Structure

### Pre-Demo Preparation

**Research the prospect:**
- Company background
- Recent news
- Linkedin profiles
- Technology stack
- Likely pain points

**Prepare demo environment:**
- Relevant data/examples
- Features to highlight
- Scenarios to walk through
- Questions to ask

### Demo Framework (60 minutes)

**1. Rapport & Agenda (5 minutes)**
```
"Hi [Name], great to meet you! Before we dive in, 
I'd love to learn a bit about you and what brought 
you to [Product]. Then I'll show you how we can help, 
and we'll have plenty of time for questions. Sound good?"
```

**2. Discovery (15 minutes)**

Key questions:
- "What prompted you to look at [category] tools?"
- "How are you handling [problem] today?"
- "What would success look like for you?"
- "Who else is involved in this decision?"
- "What's your timeline for making a decision?"

**3. Demo (25 minutes)**

Structure:
- Address their main pain point first
- Show relevant features only
- Tell stories, not features
- Pause for questions throughout
- Relate everything back to their situation

**Demo Principles:**
- Show, don't tell
- 70% product, 30% talk
- Customize to their use case
- Anticipate objections
- Build to wow moment

**4. Q&A (10 minutes)**
```
"What questions do you have so far?"
"Is there anything specific you'd like to see?"
"Any concerns about what we've covered?"
```

**5. Next Steps (5 minutes)**
```
"Based on what we've discussed, I think [recommendation].

As a next step, I'd suggest [specific action]:
- Starting a trial to test with your data
- Meeting with your team to discuss
- Sharing a proposal for your review

What would be most helpful?"
```

### Demo Best Practices

**Do:**
- Ask questions before showing
- Customize to their situation
- Tell customer stories
- Involve multiple stakeholders
- Get commitment to next step

**Don't:**
- Feature dump
- Talk at them
- Skip discovery
- Ignore their questions
- End without next step

## 3. Follow-Up Sequences

### Post-Demo Follow-Up

**Same Day Email:**
```
Subject: Great meeting you, [Name] — recap & next steps

Hi [Name],

Thanks for your time today! Here's a recap of what we covered:

What we discussed:
• [Their challenge] and how [Product] addresses it
• [Feature 1] for [their use case]
• [Feature 2] to solve [their problem]

Resources:
• [Relevant case study]
• [Documentation link]
• [Video overview]

Next steps:
• [Action 1]
• [Action 2]

I'll follow up [Day] to [specific action]. Let me know 
if you have questions before then.

[Your Name]
```

**Day 3: Value Add**
```
Subject: Thought you might find this useful

Hi [Name],

Was thinking about our conversation and wanted to share 
this [resource/article/case study] about [relevant topic].

[Brief summary of why it's relevant]

Let me know if you have questions!
[Your Name]
```

**Day 7: Check-In**
```
Subject: Quick follow-up on [Product]

Hi [Name],

Wanted to check in on [Product]. Have you had a chance 
to [review proposal/discuss with team/try trial]?

Happy to answer any questions or hop on a quick call 
if helpful.

[Your Name]
```

**Day 14: Re-Engage**
```
Subject: Still thinking about [Product]?

Hi [Name],

I wanted to follow up one more time. Is [Product] still 
on your radar?

If timing isn't right, no worries—I don't want to be 
pushy. But if I can help move things forward, I'm here.

[Your Name]
```

### Follow-Up Cadence

| Day | Action | Goal |
|-----|--------|------|
| 0 | Recap email | Document & align |
| 3 | Value add | Stay relevant |
| 7 | Check-in | Move forward |
| 14 | Re-engage | Get response |
| 21 | Break-up | Final attempt |
| 30+ | Nurture | Long-term |

## 4. Proposal Best Practices

### Proposal Structure

**1. Executive Summary**
- Their challenge
- Your solution
- Expected outcomes

**2. Understanding Your Needs**
- What you learned in discovery
- Specific pain points
- Goals and success criteria

**3. Proposed Solution**
- Recommended configuration
- Why this recommendation
- How it addresses their needs

**4. Implementation Plan**
- Timeline
- Milestones
- Responsibilities

**5. Investment**
- Pricing breakdown
- What's included
- Payment terms

**6. Next Steps**
- How to move forward
- Contact information
- Expiration date

### Proposal Delivery

**Options:**
- Email attachment (PDF)
- Document link (Google Docs)
- Proposal software (PandaDoc, Proposify)

**Best Practice:**
Walk through proposal on a call rather than sending cold.

```
"I've put together a proposal based on our conversation. 
Rather than just email it, I'd like to walk you through 
it so I can answer questions in real time. Can we do a 
quick 15-minute call?"
```

### Proposal Benchmarks

| Metric | Target |
|--------|--------|
| Demo to proposal | 40-60% |
| Proposal to close | 30-50% |
| Time to close | <30 days (SMB) |
| Proposal views | Track in software |

## 5. Handling Objections

### Common Objections & Responses

**"It's too expensive"**
```
"I understand budget is a consideration. Can you help 
me understand what you're comparing us to?

When customers evaluate us against [alternative], they 
typically find that [value justification].

What if we looked at [alternative structure/timeline]?"
```

**"We're already using [competitor]"**
```
"[Competitor] is a solid choice. What made you take 
this call—is there something not working?

Usually when teams evaluate us alongside [competitor], 
it's because of [common gap]. Is that something you're 
experiencing?"
```

**"I need to talk to my team/boss"**
```
"Absolutely. Who else should be involved in this decision?

Would it help if I joined a call with your team? I could 
answer their questions directly and save you from having 
to relay everything."
```

**"We're not ready right now"**
```
"Totally understand. When you say 'not ready,' is it a 
timing thing, or are there other factors?

What would need to change for this to become a priority?

Can we reconnect in [timeframe] to see how things 
have evolved?"
```

**"I need more time to think"**
```
"Of course. Is there specific information that would 
help you decide?

What concerns do you still have that I could address?

When would be a good time to reconnect?"
```

### Objection Handling Framework

**LAER Method:**
- **L**isten: Fully hear the objection
- **A**cknowledge: Validate their concern
- **E**xplore: Understand underlying issue
- **R**espond: Address with solution
