# Slack Growth Case Study

## Company Overview

**Founded:** 2013 (pivoted from gaming company Tiny Speck)
**Category:** Team communication
**Pricing Model:** Freemium + per-seat
**Exit:** Acquired by Salesforce for $27.7B (2021)

## What Made Slack Successful

### 1. Product-Led Growth Foundation

**The Freemium Model:**
- Free tier with unlimited users
- 90-day message history limit (key gate)
- 10 integration limit
- No compliance features

**Why It Worked:**
- Teams could adopt without budget approval
- Viral within organizations
- Natural upgrade trigger (history limit)

### 2. Viral Team Adoption

**The Slack Viral Loop:**
```
Team starts using Slack
→ Invite colleagues (required for value)
→ Colleagues experience Slack
→ Colleagues want Slack for their team
→ New teams adopt
→ Company-wide rollout
```

**Key Insight:** Every team member invited was product marketing.

### 3. Bottom-Up Enterprise Sales

**The Motion:**
1. Single team adopts (free or paid)
2. Spreads to other teams
3. IT notices shadow adoption
4. Sales engages for enterprise deal

**Result:** Reduced CAC, accelerated enterprise deals

### 4. Integrations Ecosystem

**The Strategy:**
- 2,400+ integrations
- App Directory as distribution
- Integrations increased stickiness
- Partner ecosystem drove adoption

### 5. Brand and Experience

**Brand Investment:**
- Distinctive personality
- Playful copy and design
- Memorable logo and colors
- Strong word-of-mouth

---

## Key Lessons from Slack

1. **Free tier drives virality**: Generous free tier enabled organic spread
2. **Product IS marketing**: Every invite, every channel = exposure
3. **Limits create upgrades**: 90-day history was brilliant gate
4. **Integrations = stickiness**: Ecosystem locks in customers
5. **Bottom-up works for enterprise**: PLG can land enterprise deals
