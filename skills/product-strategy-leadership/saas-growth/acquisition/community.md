# Community & Word of Mouth

## Introduction

Word of mouth is the most trusted form of marketing—people trust recommendations from friends and peers far more than ads. Building community and encouraging advocacy turns your customers into your marketing team. For SaaS companies, community can become a sustainable competitive advantage.

---

## 1. Building Community

### Types of SaaS Communities

**User Community:**
- Customers helping customers
- Product discussions
- Feature requests
- Use case sharing

**Industry Community:**
- Broader than your product
- Thought leadership
- Networking
- Education

**Developer Community:**
- Technical users
- API and integration focus
- Open source contributions
- Documentation collaboration

### Community Platforms

| Platform | Best For | Pros | Cons |
|----------|----------|------|------|
| Slack | Active discussion | Real-time | Can be noisy |
| Discord | Tech/gaming audience | Feature-rich | Learning curve |
| Circle | Structured community | Clean UX | Cost |
| Facebook Groups | Broad reach | Familiar | Platform risk |
| Forum (Discourse) | Searchable knowledge | Persistent | Less engagement |

### Building Community

**Phase 1: Founding Members**
- Invite best customers
- Create content yourself
- Set culture and tone
- High-touch engagement

**Phase 2: Growth**
- Encourage member content
- Add moderators
- Create rituals (weekly threads, AMAs)
- Cross-promote

**Phase 3: Scale**
- Community-led content
- Ambassador programs
- Self-sustaining engagement
- Reduced founder involvement

### Community Engagement Tactics

**Regular Rituals:**
- Weekly Q&A thread
- Monthly AMAs
- Feature feedback sessions
- User showcases

**Content:**
- Educational resources
- Templates and examples
- Case study highlights
- Product tips

**Recognition:**
- Top contributor badges
- Community spotlights
- Early access for active members
- Swag for top participants

## 2. Customer Advocacy

### Turning Customers into Advocates

**Identify Advocates:**
- High NPS scores (9-10)
- Active users
- Reference-willing customers
- Social sharers

**Enable Advocacy:**
- Make sharing easy
- Provide shareable content
- Create referral incentives
- Recognize publicly

### Advocacy Programs

**Reference Program:**
- Customers willing to be references
- Different levels (call, case study, video)
- Incentivized participation
- Tracked and managed

**Advisory Board:**
- Strategic customers
- Regular input on product
- Exclusive access and recognition
- Deep relationship

**Champion Program:**
- Internal advocates at customer companies
- Training and certification
- Exclusive benefits
- Community among champions

## 3. Review Generation

### Why Reviews Matter

- 92% read reviews before buying B2B software
- Reviews improve SEO
- Third-party validation
- Competitive positioning

### Review Platforms

| Platform | Importance | Focus |
|----------|------------|-------|
| G2 | Very High | Enterprise SaaS |
| Capterra | High | SMB SaaS |
| TrustRadius | High | Enterprise |
| Trustpilot | Medium | Broad |
| Google Business | Medium | Local |

### Review Generation Strategy

**When to Ask:**
- After successful milestone
- After positive support interaction
- After renewal
- During NPS survey (high scorers)

**How to Ask:**
```
Subject: Would you share your experience?

Hi [Name],

Thrilled that [Company] has achieved [milestone] with [Product].

Would you be willing to share your experience on G2? 
It takes about 3 minutes and really helps other companies 
like yours find us.

[Direct link to review form]

As a thank you, I'd love to send you [small gift/swag].

Thanks for being an amazing customer!
[Your name]
```

### Review Best Practices

**Do:**
- Ask happy customers
- Make it easy (direct links)
- Thank reviewers
- Respond to all reviews

**Don't:**
- Incentivize for positive reviews
- Fake reviews
- Ignore negative reviews
- Spam customers

## 4. Referral Programs

### Referral Program Design

**Incentive Models:**

| Model | Description | Best For |
|-------|-------------|----------|
| One-sided | Referrer gets reward | Simple, low cost |
| Two-sided | Both get reward | Higher conversion |
| Tiered | More referrals = better rewards | Power referrers |
| Credit | Account credits | Low cash cost |

**Reward Types:**
- Account credit
- Cash/gift card
- Extended features
- Swag
- Charity donation

### Two-Sided Referral Example

```
Invite a friend, you both get $50!

Share your unique link: [link]

When your friend signs up for a paid plan:
• They get $50 off their first month
• You get $50 credit

No limit on referrals!
```

### Referral Program Best Practices

**1. Make Sharing Easy**
- Unique referral link
- One-click sharing
- Pre-written messages
- Email, social, copy options

**2. Track Everything**
- Attribution clear
- Status visible
- Rewards automatic
- Fraud prevention

**3. Promote Consistently**
- In-app prompts
- Email reminders
- Account page presence
- Post-purchase promotion

**4. Reward Quickly**
- Immediate credit
- Clear terms
- No hoops to jump through

### Referral Benchmarks

| Metric | Low | Good | Great |
|--------|-----|------|-------|
| Invite rate | 5% | 15% | 25%+ |
| Conversion rate | 5% | 15% | 25%+ |
| Referrals per referrer | 1 | 2-3 | 5+ |
