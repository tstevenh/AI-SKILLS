# Pricing Changes

## Introduction

Pricing isn't set once and forgotten—it evolves as your product, market, and company mature. But changing prices is tricky: do it poorly and you'll face customer backlash, churn, and trust erosion. Do it well and you'll capture more value while maintaining strong relationships.

This guide covers when to raise prices, how to communicate changes, migration strategies, and grandfather policies.

---

## 1. When to Raise Prices

### Signs You Should Raise Prices

**1. No Price Resistance in Sales**

If prospects never push back on price:
- You're probably underpriced
- Sales cycles too easy
- Test higher price points

**2. High Conversion Rates**

Unusually high trial-to-paid conversion:
- Current price may be too low
- Customers see clear value surplus
- Room to capture more value

**3. Product Value Increased**

Major improvements since pricing set:
- New features added
- Performance improved
- Integrations expanded
- Value delivered increased

**4. Costs Increased**

External cost pressures:
- Infrastructure costs up
- Talent costs up
- Compliance requirements
- Maintain margins

**5. Competitive Positioning**

Market has evolved:
- Competitors raised prices
- You're cheapest (unintentionally)
- Premium positioning desired

**6. Segmentation Clarity**

Better understanding of segments:
- Different segments have different WTP
- Current pricing doesn't capture
- Tiering opportunities

### When NOT to Raise Prices

**Avoid Raising When:**
- No value improvement to justify
- Customer satisfaction declining
- High churn already
- Major competitor just lowered prices
- Economic downturn (carefully)
- Right before key renewal period

### How Often to Review Pricing

**Recommended Cadence:**
- **Annual review**: Full pricing audit
- **Semi-annual check**: Market and competitive scan
- **Continuous**: Monitor pricing feedback signals

**Pricing Review Triggers:**
- Major product release
- Entering new segment
- Competitive change
- 12 months since last change
- Unit economics pressure

## 2. How to Communicate Price Changes

### Communication Principles

**1. Lead with Value**

Don't just announce increase—explain value delivered:
```
BAD: "We're raising prices by 20%"
GOOD: "We've added [features], improved [metrics], and are investing in [roadmap]. Pricing is updating to reflect this value."
```

**2. Give Advance Notice**

Minimum 30 days, ideally 60-90:
- Time to budget
- Time to decide
- Feels respectful
- Required by some contracts

**3. Be Specific**

Clear information about change:
- Current price → New price
- Effective date
- Who's affected
- What's changing

**4. Offer Options**

Where possible, give choices:
- Lock current rate with annual
- Downgrade to keep price
- Grandfather for loyal customers

**5. Personalize Communication**

Segment your messaging:
- Long-term customers: Different tone than new
- High-value accounts: Personal outreach
- At-risk accounts: Extra care

### Communication Channels

**Email (Primary):**
- Direct to account owner
- Written record
- Time to process

**In-App (Secondary):**
- Banner or modal
- Links to details
- Reaches active users

**Account Manager (High-Value):**
- Personal call or meeting
- Discuss impact
- Handle objections
- Preserve relationship

**Blog/Public (Optional):**
- For transparency
- Addresses community
- Can backfire if poorly received

### Communication Templates

**New Customer Pricing Announcement:**
```
Subject: Updates to our pricing (effective [date])

Hi [Name],

Starting [date], we're updating our pricing for new customers.

What's changing:
[Current plan] will be [new price]/month (currently [old price])

Why:
Over the past year, we've:
• Launched [feature 1]
• Improved [metric]
• Added [integration]

This investment in the product is reflected in updated pricing.

Important: As a current customer, [grandfather policy].

Questions? Reply to this email or schedule time with me: [link]

[Name]
```

**Existing Customer Price Increase:**
```
Subject: Changes to your subscription (action may be required)

Hi [Name],

I'm reaching out about a change to your [Product] subscription.

Starting [date], your plan will be [new price]/month 
(currently [old price]/month).

What's staying the same:
• All your features
• All your data
• Our commitment to you

What's changing:
• Monthly price: [old] → [new]
• [Any other changes]

Your options:
1. Continue at new pricing (no action needed)
2. Switch to annual and lock in [discount]
3. Adjust your plan to [alternative]

If you have questions or concerns, please reach out. 
I'm happy to discuss.

[Name]
```

**Grandfathered Customer Message:**
```
Subject: Pricing update — you're protected

Hi [Name],

We're updating our pricing on [date]. I wanted to let you know 
what this means for you: nothing changes.

As a loyal customer since [date], you'll keep your current 
pricing of [price]. No action needed.

New customers will pay [new price] starting [date].

Thank you for being part of [Product]. We appreciate you.

[Name]
```

## 3. Migration Strategies

### Migration Approach Options

**1. Immediate Migration**

All customers move to new pricing at once:
```
Date X: Everyone on new pricing
```

**Pros:** Clean, simple
**Cons:** Risky, may cause churn wave

**When to Use:**
- Small customer base
- Minor increase
- Clear value justification

**2. Renewal Migration**

Customers move at their renewal date:
```
Annual customers: New price at annual renewal
Monthly customers: New price in 30 days
```

**Pros:** Natural timing, less jarring
**Cons:** Long rollout, complex tracking

**When to Use:**
- Mix of annual/monthly
- Significant change
- Want gradual impact

**3. Cohort Migration**

Move customers in waves:
```
Wave 1 (Month 1): Newest customers
Wave 2 (Month 2): Mid-tenure customers
Wave 3 (Month 3): Longest customers
```

**Pros:** Controlled, learnable
**Cons:** Complex, extended timeline

**When to Use:**
- Large customer base
- Want to test messaging
- High risk change

**4. Selective Migration**

Only certain segments migrate:
```
SMB: New pricing
Enterprise: Grandfathered
```

**Pros:** Targeted, preserves key accounts
**Cons:** May feel unfair if discovered

**When to Use:**
- Clear segment differences
- Enterprise relationships critical
- Testing pricing changes

### Migration Communication Timeline

**90 Days Before:**
- Internal preparation
- Document changes
- Prepare FAQs
- Train support/success

**60 Days Before:**
- Notify affected customers
- Personal outreach to high-value
- Blog post (if public)

**30 Days Before:**
- Reminder communication
- In-app notification
- FAQ published

**Day of Change:**
- Price updates live
- Support ready for questions
- Monitor churn/feedback

**Post-Change:**
- Monitor churn
- Collect feedback
- Adjust if needed

## 4. Grandfather Policies

### Designing Your Policy

**Key Questions:**

1. **Who gets grandfathered?**
   - All existing customers?
   - Customers above X tenure?
   - Customers above X value?
   - Active accounts only?

2. **What is grandfathered?**
   - Price only?
   - Price + features?
   - Specific plan?

3. **For how long?**
   - Forever?
   - 12 months?
   - Until next change?

4. **Under what conditions?**
   - Current plan only?
   - Any plan changes = new pricing?
   - Upgrades allowed?

### Common Grandfather Policies

**Policy 1: Full Perpetual**
```
"Keep your current price forever on your current plan.
Upgrades, downgrades, or plan changes will be at new pricing."
```

Most generous, least complex.

**Policy 2: Time-Limited**
```
"Keep your current price for 12 months from [date].
After that, you'll move to current pricing."
```

Grace period with eventual migration.

**Policy 3: Plan Freeze**
```
"Your plan is grandfathered. Features will not change.
New features are only available on current plans."
```

Incentivizes upgrade to get new value.

**Policy 4: Loyalty Discount**
```
"As a long-term customer, you'll receive a 25% discount
on the new pricing, forever."
```

Acknowledge loyalty, still increase somewhat.

### Grandfather Policy Documentation

Create clear policy documentation:

```
PRICING CHANGE GRANDFATHER POLICY
Effective: [Date]

Eligibility:
- Active subscription as of [date]
- Account in good standing

What's Protected:
- Current subscription price
- Current plan features

Conditions:
- Applies to current plan only
- Downgrades maintain grandfather pricing
- Upgrades use new pricing
- Cancellation and resubscription uses new pricing

Duration:
- Perpetual (no expiration)

Exceptions:
- Enterprise custom contracts: Per contract terms
```

### Handling Grandfather Requests

**Customer Asks to Be Grandfathered:**
```
"I understand the concern. Let me look at your account...

You've been with us for [time] and we really value your business.

Here's what I can offer:
[Option 1: Grandfather at current price]
[Option 2: X months at current, then new]
[Option 3: Discount on new pricing]

Which works best for you?"
```

**Empowerment Framework:**

Give support/success authority to offer:
- Level 1: Standard policy (everyone gets)
- Level 2: Extended policy (tenure >12 months)
- Level 3: Custom (approval required)

## 5. Handling Pricing Backlash

### Common Objections

**"This is unfair!"**
```
Response: "I understand this is frustrating. The change reflects
[value added]. For your account, [what we can offer]."
```

**"I can't afford this."**
```
Response: "Let's look at options. We have [lower tier], 
or [annual discount]. What would work for your budget?"
```

**"I'll cancel."**
```
Response: "I'd hate to lose you. Before you decide, let me 
[explore options / connect you with manager / offer alternative]."
```

**"Your competitors are cheaper."**
```
Response: "I appreciate you sharing that. What we offer that 
they don't is [differentiation]. Is that valuable to you?"
```

### Damage Control

**If Backlash is Significant:**

1. **Acknowledge quickly**
   - Don't hide
   - Respond to complaints
   - Show you're listening

2. **Assess the situation**
   - Is it loud minority or widespread?
   - Are key customers at risk?
   - Is the policy the problem or communication?

3. **Adjust if necessary**
   - Extend grandfather period
   - Offer transition discount
   - Roll back (last resort)

4. **Learn for next time**
   - What went wrong?
   - Better communication?
   - Better policy?

### Rollback Decision

**Consider Rollback If:**
- Churn is significantly above normal
- Key customers leaving
- Public backlash damaging brand
- Error in judgment was made

**Rollback Carefully:**
- Acknowledge the miss
- Explain new approach
- Make it right for affected
- Don't just reverse—improve

## Summary: Pricing Changes Done Right

### Change Management Framework

```
1. DECIDE
   - Analyze signals
   - Determine change needed
   - Define policy

2. PREPARE
   - Document changes
   - Prepare communications
   - Train team
   - Define grandfather policy

3. COMMUNICATE
   - 60+ days advance notice
   - Personal outreach to key accounts
   - Clear, value-led messaging

4. EXECUTE
   - Implement changes
   - Monitor response
   - Handle objections

5. LEARN
   - Measure impact
   - Gather feedback
   - Document lessons
```

### Pricing Change Checklist

**Preparation:**
- [ ] Clear rationale for change
- [ ] New pricing documented
- [ ] Grandfather policy defined
- [ ] FAQ prepared
- [ ] Support team trained
- [ ] Success team prepared

**Communication:**
- [ ] Timeline established (60+ days)
- [ ] Email templates ready
- [ ] High-value accounts identified for personal outreach
- [ ] In-app messaging prepared
- [ ] Public announcement (if applicable)

**Execution:**
- [ ] Technical changes implemented
- [ ] Billing updated
- [ ] Monitoring in place
- [ ] Escalation path for issues

**Post-Change:**
- [ ] Churn monitoring
- [ ] Feedback collection
- [ ] Lessons documented
- [ ] Team debrief
