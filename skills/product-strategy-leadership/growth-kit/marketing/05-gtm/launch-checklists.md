## The ORB Framework

Structure your launch marketing across three channel types. Everything should ultimately lead back to owned channels.

### Owned Channels

You own the channel (though not the audience). Direct access without algorithms or platform rules.

**Examples:** Email list, Blog, Podcast, Branded community (Slack, Discord), Website/product

**Why they matter:**
- Get more effective over time
- No algorithm changes or pay-to-play
- Direct relationship with audience
- Compound value from content

**Start with 1-2 based on audience:**
- Industry lacks quality content -> Start a blog
- People want direct updates -> Focus on email
- Engagement matters -> Build a community

### Rented Channels

Platforms that provide visibility but you don't control. Algorithms shift, rules change.

**Examples:** Social media (Twitter/X, LinkedIn, Instagram), App stores, YouTube, Reddit

**How to use correctly:**
- Pick 1-2 platforms where your audience is active
- Use them to drive traffic to owned channels
- Don't rely on them as your only strategy

**Platform tactics:**
- Twitter/X: Threads that spark conversation -> link to newsletter
- LinkedIn: High-value posts -> lead to gated content or email signup
- Marketplaces: Optimize listing -> drive to site for more

Rented channels give speed, not stability. Capture momentum by bringing users into owned ecosystem.

### Borrowed Channels

Tap into someone else's audience to shortcut getting noticed.

**Examples:** Guest content (blog posts, podcast interviews, newsletter features), Collaborations (webinars, co-marketing), Speaking engagements, Influencer partnerships

**Be proactive:**
1. List industry leaders your audience follows
2. Pitch win-win collaborations
3. Use SparkToro or Listen Notes to find audience overlap
4. Set up affiliate/referral incentives

Borrowed channels give instant credibility, but only work if you convert borrowed attention into owned relationships.

## Five-Phase Launch Approach

Launching isn't a one-day event. It's a phased process that builds momentum.

### Phase 1: Internal Launch

Gather initial feedback and iron out issues before going public.

**Actions:**
- [ ] Recruit early users one-on-one to test for free
- [ ] Collect feedback on usability gaps and missing features
- [ ] Ensure prototype is functional enough to demo

**Goal:** Validate core functionality with friendly users.

### Phase 2: Alpha Launch

Put the product in front of external users in a controlled way.

**Actions:**
- [ ] Create landing page with early access signup form
- [ ] Announce the product exists
- [ ] Invite users individually to start testing
- [ ] MVP should be working in production

**Goal:** First external validation and initial waitlist building.

### Phase 3: Beta Launch

Scale up early access while generating external buzz.

**Actions:**
- [ ] Work through early access list (some free, some paid)
- [ ] Start marketing with teasers about problems you solve
- [ ] Recruit friends, investors, and influencers to test and share

**Consider adding:**
- [ ] Coming soon landing page or waitlist
- [ ] "Beta" sticker in dashboard navigation
- [ ] Email invites to early access list
- [ ] Early access toggle for experimental features

**Goal:** Build buzz and refine product with broader feedback.

### Phase 4: Early Access Launch

Shift from small-scale testing to controlled expansion.

**Actions:**
- [ ] Leak product details: screenshots, feature GIFs, demos
- [ ] Gather quantitative usage data and qualitative feedback
- [ ] Run user research with engaged users (incentivize with credits)
- [ ] Optionally run product/market fit survey

**Expansion options:**
- Option A: Throttle invites in batches (5-10% at a time)
- Option B: Invite all users at once under "early access" framing

**Goal:** Validate at scale and prepare for full launch.

### Phase 5: Full Launch

Open the floodgates.

**Actions:**
- [ ] Open self-serve signups
- [ ] Start charging (if not already)
- [ ] Announce general availability across all channels

**Launch touchpoints:**
- [ ] Customer emails
- [ ] In-app popups and product tours
- [ ] Website banner linking to launch assets
- [ ] "New" sticker in dashboard navigation
- [ ] Blog post announcement
- [ ] Social posts across platforms
- [ ] Product Hunt, BetaList, Hacker News

**Goal:** Maximum visibility and conversion to paying users.

## Ongoing Launch Strategy

Don't rely on a single launch event. Regular updates sustain engagement.

### Prioritization Matrix

**Major updates** (new features, product overhauls):
- Full campaign across multiple channels
- Blog post, email campaign, in-app messages, social media

**Medium updates** (new integrations, UI enhancements):
- Targeted announcement
- Email to relevant segments, in-app banner

**Minor updates** (bug fixes, small tweaks):
- Changelog and release notes
- Signal product is improving

### Announcement Tactics

- **Space out releases:** Stagger announcements to maintain momentum
- **Reuse high-performing tactics:** Apply insights from previous wins
- **Signal active development:** Even small changelog updates build confidence

## Major Product Launch Checklist

### 2 Weeks Before Launch

**Product Readiness:**

- [ ] Feature complete and documented
- [ ] All bugs from beta testing fixed
- [ ] User onboarding flow tested
- [ ] Demo/tutorial content created
- [ ] FAQ section updated
- [ ] Support documentation complete

**Marketing Preparation:**

- [ ] Landing page updated with new features
- [ ] Comparison content created (what's new)
- [ ] Social media content calendar drafted
- [ ] Email announcement sequence written
- [ ] Press kit updated (if applicable)
- [ ] Screenshots/videos captured

**Technical Setup:**

- [ ] Staging environment tested
- [ ] Rollback plan documented
- [ ] Analytics tracking verified
- [ ] Payment/checkout flow tested
- [ ] Email delivery tested

### 1 Week Before Launch

**Beta & Validation:**

- [ ] Beta testers provided final feedback
- [ ] Critical issues addressed
- [ ] Testimonials collected from beta users
- [ ] Case study drafted (if applicable)

**Marketing Finalization:**

- [ ] All social posts scheduled
- [ ] Email sequences loaded and scheduled
- [ ] Landing page final review complete
- [ ] Pricing finalized and updated
- [ ] Community announcement drafted

**Team Coordination:**

- [ ] Support team briefed on new features
- [ ] Community moderators notified
- [ ] Launch day schedule confirmed
- [ ] Emergency contacts listed

### Launch Day

**Morning (Before Announcement):**

- [ ] Final production deployment
- [ ] Smoke test all critical paths
- [ ] Verify payment processing works
- [ ] Confirm email delivery working
- [ ] Landing page live and correct

**Launch Sequence:**

- [ ] Push product update live
- [ ] Update landing page
- [ ] Publish changelog/release notes
- [ ] Send email to existing customers
- [ ] Post main announcement on Twitter/X
- [ ] Share in community channels
- [ ] Notify beta testers it's live

**Post-Launch (Same Day):**

- [ ] Monitor for critical issues
- [ ] Respond to all social comments
- [ ] Answer community questions
- [ ] Track initial sales/signups
- [ ] Thank early adopters publicly

### Week After Launch

**Day 1-2:**

- [ ] Review overnight feedback
- [ ] Fix any reported issues
- [ ] Continue social engagement
- [ ] Monitor support tickets

**Day 3-4:**

- [ ] Send follow-up email with tips
- [ ] Publish tutorial content
- [ ] Collect new testimonials
- [ ] Review initial metrics

**Day 5-7:**

- [ ] Complete launch metrics review
- [ ] Document learnings
- [ ] Plan iteration based on feedback
- [ ] Schedule post-launch content

## Email Launch Sequence Checklist

### Pre-Launch Emails

**Teaser (3-5 days before):**

- [ ] Subject line creates curiosity
- [ ] Body hints without revealing
- [ ] Date/time of launch mentioned
- [ ] No CTA needed yet

**Reminder (1 day before, optional):**

- [ ] Subject line builds anticipation
- [ ] Brief preview of value
- [ ] Exact launch time mentioned

### Launch Day Emails

**Main Announcement:**

- [ ] Clear, benefit-focused subject
- [ ] What's new (headlines)
- [ ] How to access/upgrade
- [ ] Social proof (if available)
- [ ] Clear CTA button
- [ ] P.S. with urgency or bonus

**Existing Customer Email (if different):**

- [ ] Acknowledge their status
- [ ] Explain what they get
- [ ] Show how to access
- [ ] Reinforce value of being early

### Post-Launch Emails

**Day 2-3 Tips Email:**

- [ ] Subject: Getting started tip
- [ ] Quick win they can achieve
- [ ] Link to full documentation
- [ ] Support option mentioned

**Day 5-7 Follow-up:**

- [ ] Subject: Results/testimonials
- [ ] Early wins from customers
- [ ] Feature highlights
- [ ] For non-buyers: gentle nudge

- [ ] Post in community

**Throughout Day:**

- [ ] Respond to all PH comments
- [ ] Update social with progress

- [ ] Thank community regardless of result
- [ ] Screenshot final position

- [ ] Send thank you email to supporters
- [ ] Write retrospective

### Product Hunt Success Factors

**Before launch day:**
1. Build relationships with influential supporters and content hubs
2. Optimize listing: compelling tagline, polished visuals, short demo video
3. Study successful launches to identify what worked
4. Engage in relevant communities (provide value before pitching)
5. Prepare team for all-day engagement

**On launch day:**
1. Treat it as an all-day event
2. Respond to every comment in real-time
3. Answer questions and spark discussions
4. Encourage existing audience to engage
5. Direct traffic back to site to capture signups

**After launch day:**
1. Follow up with everyone who engaged
2. Convert PH traffic into owned relationships (email signups)
3. Continue momentum with post-launch content

## Post-Launch Product Marketing Checklist

Your launch isn't over when the announcement goes live. Now comes adoption work.

### Immediate Post-Launch Actions

- [ ] **Educate new users:** Set up automated onboarding email sequence introducing key features
- [ ] **Reinforce the launch:** Include announcement in weekly/monthly roundup email
- [ ] **Differentiate:** Publish comparison pages highlighting why you're the choice
- [ ] **Update web pages:** Add dedicated sections about new features across site
- [ ] **Offer preview:** Create interactive demo so visitors can explore before signing up

### Keeping Momentum

It's easier to build on existing momentum than start from scratch. Every touchpoint reinforces the launch.

**Weekly actions:**
- [ ] Share customer wins and use cases on social
- [ ] Answer questions publicly to build visibility
- [ ] Create tutorial content addressing common questions

**Monthly actions:**
- [ ] Review launch metrics and adjust messaging
- [ ] Publish case studies from early adopters
- [ ] Plan next feature/update announcement
