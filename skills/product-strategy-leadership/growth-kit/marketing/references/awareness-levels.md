# Awareness Levels

The five levels of buyer awareness determine how you should write your copy. Match your message to where your prospect actually is, not where you wish they were.

---

## The Five Levels

### Level 1: Unaware

**State:** They don't know they have a problem

**They're thinking:** "Everything's fine" (even when it's not)

**Your job:** Create awareness of the problem

**Copy approach:**

- Lead with story or education
- No product mention initially
- Agitate a pain they didn't realize they had
- Content marketing, not direct selling

**Example opening:**

> "Most founders spend 60% of their week on tasks that could be automated. They don't realize it because they've normalized the chaos."

**Best content types:**

- Blog posts about industry problems
- Educational content
- Stories that reveal hidden problems
- Social content that sparks "wait, that's me" moments

---

### Level 2: Problem-Aware

**State:** They know they have a problem but don't know solutions exist

**They're thinking:** "This [problem] is killing me, but I don't know what to do about it"

**Your job:** Educate them that solutions exist

**Copy approach:**

- Lead with the problem (they'll nod along)
- Agitate the consequences of not solving it
- Introduce the category of solutions
- Position your solution as one option

**Example opening:**

> "If you're tired of spending hours on [task] only to see mediocre results, you're not alone. Most [audience] face this exact problem. The good news: there's a better way."

**Best content types:**

- Problem-focused blog posts
- "How to solve [problem]" guides
- Comparison of solution types
- Case studies showing transformation

---

### Level 3: Solution-Aware

**State:** They know solutions exist but don't know about YOUR product

**They're thinking:** "I need to solve this. What are my options?"

**Your job:** Introduce your product as the best solution

**Copy approach:**

- Acknowledge they're looking for solutions
- Position your product clearly
- Differentiate from alternatives
- Focus on unique benefits

**Example opening:**

> "Looking for a way to [solve problem]? You've probably seen [common alternatives]. Here's why [your approach] is different..."

**Best content types:**

- Comparison posts
- "Why we built [product]" content
- Feature-focused landing pages
- Alternative to [competitor] posts

---

### Level 4: Product-Aware

**State:** They know your product exists but haven't bought yet

**They're thinking:** "I've seen [your product]. Is it right for me? Is now the right time?"

**Your job:** Convert with proof and urgency

**Copy approach:**

- Lead with your product name/benefit
- Heavy social proof
- Address specific objections
- Create urgency (real, not fake)
- Risk reversal (guarantees)

**Example opening:**

> "[Product] has helped [X,XXX] [audience] achieve [outcome]. Here's why now is the time to join them..."

**Best content types:**

- Testimonial-heavy pages
- Case studies with results
- Objection-handling content
- Limited-time offers
- Retargeting ads

---

### Level 5: Most Aware

**State:** They know your product and are ready to buy

**They're thinking:** "I want this. Where's the buy button?"

**Your job:** Make purchasing frictionless

**Copy approach:**

- Lead with the offer
- Minimal copy, clear CTA
- Price and what's included
- Urgency if real
- Remove all friction

**Example opening:**

> "Get [Product] for $[X]. Includes [key items]. 30-day guarantee."

**Best content types:**

- Simple checkout pages
- Deal/offer emails
- Reminder emails
- "Last chance" messages

---

## Matching Copy to Awareness

| Level          | Lead With            | Focus On           | CTA Type         |
| -------------- | -------------------- | ------------------ | ---------------- |
| Unaware        | Story/Education      | Problem revelation | Learn more       |
| Problem-Aware  | The problem          | Solution category  | Discover how     |
| Solution-Aware | Your differentiation | Unique benefits    | See how it works |
| Product-Aware  | Social proof         | Trust + urgency    | Get started      |
| Most Aware     | The offer            | Price + simplicity | Buy now          |

## Headlines by Awareness Level

### Unaware Headlines

- "Why [industry] is about to change forever"
- "The hidden cost of [common practice]"
- "[Surprising stat] that explains why you're stuck"

### Problem-Aware Headlines

- "Tired of [problem]? There's a better way"
- "Stop [painful activity]. Start [better activity]."
- "The [problem] fix [audience] swear by"

### Solution-Aware Headlines

- "How [Product] compares to [alternatives]"
- "Why [X] [audience] chose [Product] over [competitor]"
- "The [unique approach] to [solving problem]"

### Product-Aware Headlines

- "[X] reasons to try [Product] today"
- "What [number] customers say about [Product]"
- "[Outcome] is [X] away"

### Most Aware Headlines

- "[Product] - $[Price] - [Key Benefit]"
- "Get [Product] now"
- "Your [Product] is waiting"

## Content Strategy by Awareness

### Top of Funnel (Unaware + Problem-Aware)

**Goal:** Build audience, create awareness

**Content types:**

- Educational blog posts
- Social media content
- Podcasts/interviews
- Industry reports

**Metrics:** Traffic, shares, email signups

### Middle of Funnel (Solution-Aware + Problem-Aware)

**Goal:** Nurture interest, build trust

**Content types:**

- Comparison guides
- Case studies
- Email sequences
- Webinars

**Metrics:** Email engagement, time on page, return visits

### Bottom of Funnel (Product-Aware + Most Aware)

**Goal:** Convert to customers

**Content types:**

- Sales pages
- Demo videos
- Testimonial pages
- Offer emails

**Metrics:** Conversion rate, sales, trial signups

## Email Sequence Mapping

A typical 7-email welcome sequence maps to awareness levels:

| Email | Purpose             | Awareness Target |
| ----- | ------------------- | ---------------- |
| 1     | Deliver lead magnet | Problem-Aware    |
| 2     | Share your story    | Problem-Aware    |
| 3     | Educate (value)     | Solution-Aware   |
| 4     | More education      | Solution-Aware   |
| 5     | Bridge to offer     | Product-Aware    |
| 6     | Soft pitch          | Product-Aware    |
| 7     | Direct pitch        | Most Aware       |

## Common Mistakes

### Mistake 1: Selling to Unaware Audiences

**Problem:** Jumping straight to product pitch when they don't know they have a problem

**Fix:** Educate first, sell later

### Mistake 2: Over-Educating Aware Audiences

**Problem:** Treating Product-Aware prospects like they're Unaware (they already know, just tell them the offer)

**Fix:** Match copy length/depth to awareness level

### Mistake 3: One Message for All

**Problem:** Using the same landing page/email for all traffic sources

**Fix:** Create awareness-specific variations

### Mistake 4: Assuming High Awareness

**Problem:** Most audiences are Problem-Aware at best, but copy assumes they're Product-Aware

**Fix:** Default to lower awareness in broad targeting
