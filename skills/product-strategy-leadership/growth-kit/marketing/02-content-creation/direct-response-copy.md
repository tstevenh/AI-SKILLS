# Direct Response Copy

Copy that gets people to take action. Not brand awareness. Not thought leadership. Direct response: read this, do this.

This skill covers landing pages, sales pages, ad copy, and any copy where the goal is immediate action.

---

## The Core Job

Create copy that **converts readers into action-takers** by:

1. **Grabbing attention** - They stop scrolling
2. **Building interest** - They keep reading
3. **Creating desire** - They want what you offer
4. **Driving action** - They click, buy, or sign up

**Output:** Ready-to-publish copy with headlines, body, and CTAs.

---

## Before Writing: The Conversion Equation

**Value = (Dream Outcome x Perceived Likelihood) / (Time Delay x Effort)**

Your copy must:

- **Amplify** the dream outcome (what they get)
- **Amplify** perceived likelihood (why it will work for them)
- **Minimize** perceived time delay (how fast they'll see results)
- **Minimize** perceived effort (how easy it is)

Every section of copy should move at least one of these levers.

## The Grand Slam Offer

An offer that:

- Cannot be compared to any other product or service
- Combines attractive promotion with unmatchable value
- Commands premium pricing
- Includes an unbeatable guarantee

The goal: Make the offer so asymmetrically valuable that saying "no" feels irrational.

### Premium Pricing Psychology

**Why higher prices work:**

1. **Price signals quality** - People infer that expensive = better
2. **Higher investment = higher commitment** - $5,000 customers implement more than $500 customers
3. **Forces you to over-deliver** - You can't charge premium and under-deliver
4. **Attracts better customers** - Premium buyers complain less, implement more, get better results

**The pricing test:** Charge "as much as you can say out loud without cracking a smile." If the price feels comfortable, it's probably too low.

**Price-to-value discrepancy:** People buy when perceived value dramatically exceeds price. If they perceive $50,000 of value and you charge $5,000, the decision is easy. Increase perceived value rather than decreasing price.

## The MAGIC Naming Formula

For naming offers, products, and programs:

**M** - Magnet (reason to act now: "Free," "Limited," "New")
**A** - Avatar (who it's for: "Busy Moms," "SaaS Founders")
**G** - Goal (the outcome: "Six-Figure," "Pain-Free")
**I** - Interval (timeframe: "30-Day," "6-Week")
**C** - Container (format: "Blueprint," "Bootcamp," "System")

**Examples:**

- "The 6-Week Fit Mom Transformation Blueprint"
- "Free 21-Day Lead Generation Bootcamp for Coaches"
- "The 90-Day Six-Figure Agency System"

Use alliteration and rhyme for memorability.

## Guarantees

Guarantees reverse risk from buyer to seller.

### Types:

**Unconditional:** Full refund, no questions asked, any reason.

**Conditional:** Refund if specific conditions met (completed program, implemented steps, etc.).

**Outcome-based:** Payment tied to results. You only earn if they succeed.

### Stacking guarantees:

Combine multiple guarantees for overwhelming risk reversal:

- 30-day unconditional refund
- 90-day conditional results guarantee
- Continued work until outcome achieved

**The psychology:** Strong guarantees demonstrate confidence. If you won't guarantee results, why should they believe you can deliver?

---

## Bonus Stacking

Bonuses increase perceived value without discounting.

### Rules:

1. **Each bonus solves a specific objection** - "What if I don't have time?" → Bonus: Quick-Start Templates
2. **Name bonuses specifically** - Not "Bonus: Templates" but "The $2,000 Copy Swipe File"
3. **Assign value to each** - State what it would cost separately
4. **Stack until bonus value exceeds offer price** - If offer is $2,000, bonuses should total $5,000+

### Bonuses vs. Discounts:

Never discount. Add bonuses instead.

- Discounting says "my price is negotiable"
- Bonuses say "the price is fixed, but I'll add more value"

## The Landing Page Framework

### Section 1: Hero

**Purpose:** Stop the scroll. State the promise. Create urgency.

**Elements:**

- **Headline:** Primary promise or biggest benefit
- **Subheadline:** Supports headline, adds specificity
- **Hero image/visual:** Shows outcome or product
- **Primary CTA:** Clear action button
- **Trust indicator:** Quick social proof

**Headline formulas:**

| Formula                                      | Example                                   |
| -------------------------------------------- | ----------------------------------------- |
| [Outcome] without [Pain]                     | "Build your audience without the hustle"  |
| The [Adjective] way to [Outcome]             | "The simple way to convert more visitors" |
| [Outcome] in [Timeframe]                     | "Double your email list in 30 days"       |
| Stop [Pain]. Start [Benefit].                | "Stop guessing. Start converting."        |
| [Number] [People] use [Product] to [Outcome] | "5,000+ founders use X to ship faster"    |

### Section 2: Problem (Agitation)

**Purpose:** Make them feel the pain. Show you understand.

**Structure:**

1. Name the problem specifically
2. Describe how it feels (emotional)
3. Quantify the cost (time, money, opportunity)
4. Show you've been there (optional)

**Example:**

> You spend hours writing content that gets no traction.
>
> Every post feels like shouting into the void. You're doing what the "experts" say, but nothing moves.
>
> Meanwhile, competitors with worse products are somehow everywhere.
>
> I've been there. It's exhausting.

### Section 3: Solution Introduction

**Purpose:** Present your product as the answer. Bridge from problem to solution.

**Structure:**

1. Introduce the product
2. Explain what it is (one sentence)
3. Explain what it does (the transformation)
4. Hint at how (method, not features)

**Example:**

> That's why I built [Product].
>
> It's a system that shows you exactly what content to create, how to write it, and where to post it.
>
> No more guessing. No more wasted effort. Just a repeatable process that actually works.

### Section 4: Who This Is For / Who This Is NOT For

**Purpose:** Qualify and disqualify. Build trust through honesty. Help them self-select.

**Who This Is For (Inclusion Criteria):**

This section should paint a clear picture of the ideal customer:

```
## Who This Is For

This is for you if:

- You're a [role] who [situation]
- You've tried [common solutions] but [they didn't work because]
- You want [outcome] but don't have [constraint they face]
- You're ready to [action/commitment required]
- You believe [worldview that aligns with your product]

Specifically, you'll get the most value if:
- [Specific situation 1]
- [Specific situation 2]
- [Specific situation 3]
```

**Who This Is NOT For (Exclusion Criteria):**

Honest exclusion builds trust and prevents bad-fit customers:

```
## Who This Is NOT For

This is NOT for you if:

- You're looking for [thing you don't provide]
- You want [unrealistic outcome]
- You're not willing to [effort required]
- You expect [thing that won't happen]
- You need [thing this doesn't cover]
```

**Example:**

```
## Who This Is For

This is for founders and marketers who:

- Are building something real (not just "thinking about it")
- Have a product or service they believe in
- Want to grow through content but don't have time for guesswork
- Are willing to put in the work if they know it will pay off
- Value systems over hacks

You'll get the most value if:
- You're actively trying to grow your audience
- You've tried content marketing but struggled with consistency
- You want a repeatable process, not random tactics

## Who This Is NOT For

This is NOT for you if:

- You're looking for "get rich quick" schemes
- You want someone else to do the work for you
- You expect results without effort
- You're not willing to invest in your growth
- You haven't started building anything yet (start there first)
```

### Section 5: Features and Benefits

**Purpose:** Show what they get. Connect features to outcomes.

**Structure:**

- Each feature is a benefit in disguise
- Lead with the benefit, support with the feature
- Use specific examples

**Format:**

```
## What's Inside

### [Benefit Statement]
[Feature explanation] that [specific outcome].

**Example:** [Concrete use case]

### [Benefit Statement]
[Feature explanation] that [specific outcome].

**Example:** [Concrete use case]
```

### Section 6: Social Proof

**Purpose:** Show others have succeeded. Reduce perceived risk.

**Types of proof:**

- **Testimonials:** Named customers with specific outcomes
- **Case studies:** Detailed success stories
- **Numbers:** Customer count, results achieved
- **Logos:** Companies or publications
- **Screenshots:** Real results, real feedback

**Testimonial formula:**

> "[Specific result or transformation]"
>
> "[What life was like before]"
>
> "[What changed after]"
>
> - [Name], [Role/Company]

### Section 7: Pricing

**Purpose:** Present the offer. Justify the value.

**Structure:**

1. Anchor high (what this would cost otherwise)
2. Present the price
3. Show what's included
4. Emphasize value (lifetime access, updates, etc.)
5. Risk reversal (guarantee)

**Example:**

> **[Product Name]: $[Price]**
>
> What you'd pay for this elsewhere:
>
> - Consultant: $3,000+
> - Course: $997+
> - Trial and error: Months of wasted time
>
> Or get everything in [Product] for $[Price]. Once.
>
> **Includes:**
>
> - [Item 1]
> - [Item 2]
> - [Item 3]
> - Plus all future updates
>
> **30-day money-back guarantee.** Try it. If it doesn't work for you, get a full refund. No questions.

### Section 8: FAQ / Objection Handling

**Purpose:** Address remaining doubts. Remove friction.

**Common objections to address:**

- "Will this work for my specific situation?"
- "What if I don't have time?"
- "Is this worth the money?"
- "How is this different from [alternative]?"
- "What if it doesn't work for me?"

**FAQ format:**

```
### [Question as customer would ask it]
[Direct answer that addresses the underlying concern]
```

### Section 9: Final CTA

**Purpose:** Clear action. Last chance to convert.

**Structure:**

1. Restate the core promise
2. Urgency (if authentic)
3. Clear button with action text
4. Final reassurance

**Example:**

> Stop guessing. Start growing.
>
> Get [Product] today for $[Price]. Price increases with each update.
>
> [GET INSTANT ACCESS]
>
> Comes with 30-day money-back guarantee. Zero risk.

## Copywriting Principles

### 1. Clarity Over Cleverness

Being understood beats being impressive.

**Bad:** "Synergize your productivity ecosystem"
**Good:** "Get more done in less time"

### 2. Specific Over Vague

Numbers and details create credibility.

**Bad:** "Lots of customers love it"
**Good:** "2,847 customers in 6 months"

### 3. Benefits Over Features

Features are what it does. Benefits are what they get.

**Feature:** "10 email templates included"
**Benefit:** "Never stare at a blank screen wondering what to write"

### 4. You Over We

Make them the hero, not you.

**Bad:** "We built an amazing tool"
**Good:** "You get an amazing tool"

### 5. Short Paragraphs

One idea per paragraph. White space is your friend.

### 6. Active Voice

Subject does the action.

**Passive:** "Results can be seen in days"
**Active:** "You'll see results in days"

### 7. Remove Weasel Words

Delete: maybe, might, could, possibly, somewhat, relatively.

Say what you mean. Stand behind it.

## Headline Formulas

### Pain-Based Headlines

- "Tired of [pain]? Here's what actually works."
- "Stop [painful activity]. Start [desirable activity]."
- "[Number] reasons your [thing] isn't working"

### Benefit-Based Headlines

- "[Outcome] without [pain]"
- "The [adjective] way to [outcome]"
- "How to [outcome] in [timeframe]"

### Curiosity Headlines

- "The [thing] [audience] use to [outcome]"
- "Why [counterintuitive claim]"
- "What [successful people] know about [topic]"

### Proof Headlines

- "How [specific person] achieved [specific result]"
- "[Number] [people] are using this to [outcome]"
- "The [thing] that [result] for [number] customers"

## CTA Best Practices

### Button Text

**Weak:** Submit, Click Here, Learn More
**Strong:** Get Instant Access, Start Growing Today, Download Now

### CTA Formula

"[Action] + [Benefit]"

Examples:

- "Get the Templates"
- "Start Writing Better"
- "Download Your Copy"

### Surrounding Copy

Before the button:

- Restate the benefit
- Remove risk
- Create micro-urgency

## The Test

Good direct response copy:

1. **Is it clear?** (Understood in one read)
2. **Is it specific?** (Numbers, details, examples)
3. **Is it compelling?** (Creates desire)
4. **Is it credible?** (Proof supports claims)
5. **Is there one clear action?** (Know exactly what to do)

If any answer is no, revise until it is.
