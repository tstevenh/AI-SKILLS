## Case Study 1: SaaS Startup Breaking Into Enterprise

### The Challenge

A B2B SaaS company selling marketing automation software wanted to break into the enterprise market. Their product was strong, but they had no enterprise customers and limited brand recognition.

**Starting Point:**
- No enterprise references
- 5-person sales team
- Limited budget
- 90-day timeline to prove the channel

### The Strategy

**Target Definition:**
- Companies: 1,000-10,000 employees
- Industries: SaaS, Fintech, E-commerce
- Titles: VP Marketing, Director of Demand Gen
- Trigger: Recently raised Series B or later

**Infrastructure:**
- 5 domains (one per rep)
- 15 mailboxes total
- 8-week warm-up before launch
- Smartlead for sending
- Clay for enrichment

**Sequence Design:**
- 6 emails over 21 days
- Multi-channel (email + LinkedIn)
- Personalized first lines via AI
- Case study focus (SMB success stories)

### The Execution

**Email 1 (Day 1): The Hook**
```
Subject: Scaling demand gen at [Company]

[Name],

Companies that raise Series B often double down on demand gen—but the manual processes that worked at Series A break.

[Similar Company—Series B like you] faced exactly this. After implementing [Product], they:
- 3x'd MQL volume
- Cut cost per lead by 40%
- Reduced manual work by 20 hours/week

Given [Company]'s recent funding, might be worth exploring.

15 minutes to see if it applies?
```

**Email 3 (Day 8): New Angle**
```
Subject: What [Competitor] doesn't do

[Name],

Most Series B companies I talk to are on [Competitor]. It works—to a point.

Where it breaks down:
- Complex attribution
- Multi-touch journeys
- International expansion

[Similar Company] switched mid-growth phase. The transition took 3 weeks. Results: [specific outcomes].

Worth a comparison call?
```

**Email 5 (Day 15): Social Proof Push**
```
Subject: [3 Peer Companies] are using this

[Name],

Quick list of companies similar to [Company] using [Product]:
- [Company 1]: [Result]
- [Company 2]: [Result]
- [Company 3]: [Result]

There's a pattern here—post-Series B is exactly when this becomes critical.

Last chance to connect before I close the loop. Worth 15 minutes?
```

### The Results

**After 90 Days:**
- Emails sent: 12,000
- Reply rate: 9.2%
- Positive reply rate: 4.8%
- Meetings booked: 127
- Opportunities created: 43
- Pipeline generated: $2.1M
- Deals closed: 7
- Revenue: $380K ARR

**Key Learnings:**
1. The funding trigger was highly effective—these companies were actively investing in growth
2. Naming similar companies in the subject line drove opens
3. The competitor angle (Email 3) had the highest reply rate
4. LinkedIn follow-ups increased total reply rate by 22%

## Case Study 2: Agency Generating $50K/Month in Retainers

### The Challenge

A growth marketing agency wanted to systematically generate new client leads instead of relying on referrals. They had capacity for 5-8 new clients per month.

**Starting Point:**
- Strong results for existing clients
- No outbound experience
- Founder doing sales solo
- $3K/month budget

### The Strategy

**Target Definition:**
- Companies: 20-200 employees
- Industries: B2B SaaS
- Titles: CEO, CMO, VP Marketing
- Signals: Active hiring for marketing roles, recent funding

**Infrastructure:**
- 3 domains
- 9 mailboxes
- 6-week warm-up
- Instantly for sending
- Apollo for data

**Approach:**
- Audit-based offer (free value upfront)
- Specific to their current marketing
- Portfolio of case studies

### The Execution

**Email 1: The Audit Offer**
```
Subject: Quick audit of [Company]'s paid strategy

[Name],

Took a look at [Company]'s paid ads (can see some of your Meta/Google creatives via [tool]).

Few quick observations:
- [Specific observation 1]
- [Specific observation 2]
- [Potential opportunity]

Happy to put together a 10-minute audit video walking through specific improvements—no strings attached.

Want me to send it over?
```

**Follow-Up After Audit Sent:**
```
Subject: Audit video for [Company]

[Name],

Just sent over the audit video. Key takeaways:

1. [Biggest opportunity]
2. [Quick win]
3. [Longer-term idea]

These are the kinds of optimizations we implemented for [Similar Company]—they saw [specific result] within 60 days.

Worth a call to discuss implementing these for [Company]?
```

### The Results

**After 6 Months:**
- Audit requests: 340
- Audits completed: 280 (82% follow-through)
- Meetings from audits: 168
- Proposals sent: 89
- Deals closed: 38
- Total new MRR: $190K
- Average retainer: $5K/month

**Key Learnings:**
1. The free audit created massive goodwill and response rates
2. Personalized observations proved expertise immediately
3. Video audits outperformed written audits 3:1
4. Following up quickly after audit delivery was critical
5. The audit → call → proposal pipeline was highly predictable

## Case Study 3: Recruiting Firm Filling Executive Roles

### The Challenge

An executive recruiting firm wanted to generate both client-side (companies hiring) and candidate-side (executives open to roles) leads through cold email.

**Starting Point:**
- Strong network in finance sector
- Manual outreach taking 30+ hours/week
- Wanted to scale without adding headcount

### The Strategy

**Client-Side Campaign:**
- Target: CFOs and CHROs at PE-backed companies
- Trigger: Recent PE investment or portfolio company acquisition
- Offer: Intro call to discuss hiring plans

**Candidate-Side Campaign:**
- Target: VP Finance and above at Series B+ companies
- Trigger: Company stagnation, restructuring news, or 3+ years in role
- Offer: Confidential conversation about opportunities

### The Execution

**Client Email:**
```
Subject: [Company]'s finance leadership

[Name],

Noticed [PE Firm] invested in [Company] [timeframe]. Congrats.

PE-backed companies at this stage typically assess their finance leadership within 90 days. Common gaps: controller-level depth, FP&A sophistication, or audit readiness.

We specialize in placing CFOs and VP Finance at PE-backed companies. Recent placements include:
- [Company 1]: CFO (placed in 45 days)
- [Company 2]: VP Finance (placed in 30 days)

If you're evaluating your finance team, worth a quick conversation.

[Name]
```

**Candidate Email:**
```
Subject: Confidential - [Title] opportunities

[Name],

Reaching out confidentially regarding CFO/VP Finance opportunities at PE-backed companies in [region/industry].

Given your background at [Company] (particularly [specific achievement]), you'd be a strong fit for several active searches.

These are [salary range] roles with meaningful equity. Confidential conversation to share details?

[Name]
```

### The Results

**Client-Side (After 6 Months):**
- Emails sent: 6,000
- Reply rate: 11.4%
- Meetings: 189
- Active searches won: 23
- Average fee: $85K
- Revenue: $1.96M

**Candidate-Side (After 6 Months):**
- Emails sent: 4,500
- Reply rate: 18.2%
- Conversations: 341
- Candidates placed: 19
- Candidates in pipeline: 200+

**Key Learnings:**
1. PE investment trigger had extremely high relevance
2. Candidate emails had much higher reply rates than client emails
3. "Confidential" subject line drove opens on candidate side
4. Specific salary ranges in candidate emails increased engagement
5. Building candidate inventory made client-side easier over time

## Case Study 4: Software Company ABM Campaign

### The Challenge

An enterprise software company wanted to penetrate 50 named accounts with deal sizes of $100K+. Traditional broad outreach wasn't working for complex enterprise sales.

**Starting Point:**
- 50 named target accounts
- 6-month timeline
- 2 dedicated SDRs
- Existing (unused) intent data subscription

### The Strategy

**Account Prioritization:**
- Tier 1: 10 accounts (strongest intent signals)
- Tier 2: 20 accounts (moderate signals)
- Tier 3: 20 accounts (ICP fit, no signals)

**Multi-Threading:**
For each account, identify 5-8 contacts:
- 2-3 executives (economic buyers)
- 2-3 managers (technical evaluators)
- 1-2 influencers (champions)

**Sequence Design:**
- 8-touch sequence per contact
- Account-level coordination
- Intent-triggered escalation
- Executive air cover emails

### The Execution

**First Contact to Champion:**
```
Subject: [Account]'s [specific tech initiative]

[Name],

Noticed [Account] is investing in [initiative from job posting/news]. That usually means [specific challenge] becomes critical.

[Similar Company in same industry] faced the same situation. After implementing [Product]:
- [Specific result 1]
- [Specific result 2]
- [Specific result 3]

Worth a conversation about how this could apply to [Account]'s initiative?

[Your name]
```

**Executive Air Cover (When Champion Engaged):**
```
Subject: Supporting [Champion name]'s evaluation

[Executive name],

[Champion name] and I have been discussing how [Product] could support [Account]'s [initiative].

Given the strategic importance of [initiative], wanted to make sure you had visibility. [Similar Company] saw [executive-level result].

Happy to provide any additional context that would be helpful at your level.

[Your name]
```

**Intent-Triggered Email:**
```
Subject: Noticed [Account]'s research on [topic]

[Name],

Our intent data shows [Account] researching [topic] actively.

Companies at this research stage typically have [specific questions]. Happy to share how [Similar Company] approached this.

Worth a quick call to discuss?
```

### The Results

**After 6 Months:**
- Target accounts contacted: 50/50
- Accounts with meetings: 34 (68%)
- Meetings held: 127
- Opportunities created: 18
- Pipeline generated: $4.2M
- Deals closed: 6
- Revenue: $780K

**Key Learnings:**
1. Multi-threading dramatically increased account penetration
2. Intent triggers accelerated 40% of opportunities
3. Executive air cover emails shortened sales cycles
4. Tier 1 accounts had 85% meeting rate vs. 45% for Tier 3
5. Coordinated outreach prevented conflicting messages

## Case Study 5: E-commerce Tool at Scale

### The Challenge

A Shopify app wanted to scale outbound to drive app installs. Target market was massive (1M+ Shopify stores) but identifying quality prospects was difficult.

**Starting Point:**
- Limited to Shopify ecosystem
- Low price point ($29-99/month)
- Needed volume, not high-touch sales
- $10K/month budget

### The Strategy

**Targeting by Technology:**
- Stores using complementary apps
- Stores with specific Shopify themes
- Stores in certain revenue brackets (estimated)

**Sequence Design:**
- Short, punchy emails (under 75 words)
- Focus on specific use case
- Self-serve CTA (free trial, no call)
- 4 emails over 14 days

### The Execution

**Email 1:**
```
Subject: Your abandoned carts

[Store name] is leaving money on the table.

Based on typical Shopify stores your size, you're probably losing $X/month to abandoned carts.

[Product] recovers 15-20% of those. Takes 5 minutes to install.

Try it free → [Link]

[Name]
```

**Email 2:**
```
Subject: [Similar store] recovered $X last month

Quick example:

[Similar Store] (similar size, similar products) started using [Product] 60 days ago.

Results: $X recovered revenue. Zero extra work.

Worth 5 minutes to see if it works for [Store name]?

[Link]
```

### The Results

**After 6 Months:**
- Emails sent: 180,000
- Open rate: 42%
- Click rate: 8.2%
- Free trials started: 2,340
- Paid conversions: 514
- Monthly revenue added: $28K MRR
- CAC: $19 (including all costs)
- LTV/CAC: 12:1

**Key Learnings:**
1. Technology-based targeting was highly effective
2. Specific dollar amounts in subject lines drove opens
3. Self-serve CTA worked better than booking calls at this price point
4. Shorter emails (under 50 words) outperformed longer ones
5. Volume was key—casting wide net with targeted criteria

## Case Study 6: Professional Services Lead Generation

### The Challenge

A management consulting firm wanted to generate qualified leads for strategy engagements ($50K-200K). Partners were too busy to prospect, and junior staff lacked credibility.

**Starting Point:**
- Strong reputation in manufacturing sector
- No dedicated sales function
- Partners spending 2-3 hours/week on BD (ineffective)
- Needed 4-6 new engagements per quarter

### The Strategy

**Target Definition:**
- Industries: Manufacturing, industrial
- Company size: $50M-500M revenue
- Titles: CEO, COO, CFO, VP Operations
- Triggers: M&A activity, expansion announcements, operational challenges in news

**Credibility-First Approach:**
- Partner name in from line
- Thought leadership content
- Named client references (with permission)
- No hard sell

### The Execution

**Email 1: Thought Leadership Hook**
```
Subject: [Challenge] in [their industry subsector]

[Name],

I recently published research on how [industry] companies are navigating [challenge]. Given [Company]'s position in [subsector], thought you might find it relevant.

Key finding: Companies that [specific action] are seeing [result], while those that [common approach] are struggling with [problem].

Happy to share the full research. Also happy to discuss how this applies to [Company] specifically—we've helped [3-4 similar companies] with exactly this.

Worth a conversation?

[Partner name]
[Firm]
```

**Follow-Up with Value:**
```
Subject: Frameworks from our [industry] work

[Name],

Following up with something concrete—attached is a framework we used with [Similar Company] to [achieve outcome].

It's sanitized for confidentiality, but the approach is directly applicable to companies like [Company].

Would a 30-minute call to walk through how this could work for your situation be useful?

[Partner name]
```

### The Results

**After 12 Months:**
- Emails sent: 4,800
- Reply rate: 14.2%
- Meetings: 167
- Proposals sent: 52
- Engagements won: 19
- Revenue: $2.3M
- Average engagement: $121K

**Key Learnings:**
1. Partner name recognition dramatically improved open and reply rates
2. Thought leadership content justified the outreach
3. Longer sales cycle (avg. 4 months) required patient follow-up
4. Referencing specific named clients (with permission) was powerful
5. Framework/tool attachments increased conversion to meetings

## Common Patterns Across Case Studies

### What Worked Consistently

1. **Trigger-based timing**: Reaching out when something changed (funding, hiring, news) dramatically improved response rates.

2. **Relevant social proof**: Case studies from similar companies, industries, or situations were consistently effective.

3. **Specific over generic**: Specific numbers, specific names, specific observations always outperformed generic claims.

4. **Value upfront**: Offering something valuable (audit, research, framework) before asking for time worked better than direct asks.

5. **Multi-channel coordination**: Email + LinkedIn + phone, used thoughtfully, improved overall results.

6. **Proper infrastructure**: Adequate warm-up time and healthy sending practices were non-negotiable foundations.

### What Didn't Work

1. **Generic templates**: Copy-paste templates without personalization consistently underperformed.

2. **Aggressive follow-up**: Too many emails too quickly led to complaints and damaged reputation.

3. **Premature automation**: Scaling before validating messaging wasted resources.

4. **Wrong ICP**: Targeting the wrong companies or titles meant optimizing the wrong thing.

5. **Ignoring signals**: Not adapting to replies, objections, and patterns left value on the table.
