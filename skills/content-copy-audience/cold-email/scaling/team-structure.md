# Team Structure

Building a cold email team requires the right roles, the right people, and the right management approach.

## SDR/BDR Roles

### Sales Development Representative (SDR)
**Focus**: Outbound prospecting, booking meetings

**Responsibilities**:
- Research and identify prospects
- Write and send cold emails
- Handle replies and objections
- Book meetings for AEs
- Track activity and results

**Skills Required**:
- Strong written communication
- Research capabilities
- Persistence without annoyance
- CRM proficiency
- Rejection resilience

### Business Development Representative (BDR)
**Focus**: Often broader than SDR, may include strategic partnerships

**Overlap**: In many companies, SDR and BDR are used interchangeably

---

## Team Sizing

### Emails per SDR
**Conservative**: 50-100 emails/day (quality focus)
**Moderate**: 100-200 emails/day (balanced)
**High Volume**: 200-500 emails/day (automation-heavy)

### Team Size by Volume

| Monthly Volume | Team Size |
|----------------|-----------|
| 1,000-5,000 | 1 SDR |
| 5,000-15,000 | 2-3 SDRs |
| 15,000-30,000 | 4-6 SDRs |
| 30,000-60,000 | 7-12 SDRs |
| 60,000+ | 12+ SDRs + management |

## Hiring SDRs

### What to Look For

**Must-Haves**:
- Clear, professional writing
- Attention to detail
- Coachability
- Work ethic
- Basic tech comfort

**Nice-to-Haves**:
- Previous SDR experience
- Industry knowledge
- Tool experience
- Analytics comfort

### Interview Process

1. **Resume Screen**: Writing quality, relevant experience
2. **Phone Screen**: Communication, interest, basics
3. **Writing Test**: Cold email writing exercise
4. **Role Play**: Handle objection scenario
5. **Final Interview**: Culture fit, references

### Writing Test Example
"Write a cold email to the VP of Sales at a 100-person SaaS company. We sell sales training. Include subject line, body, and explain your approach."

## Training SDRs

### Week 1: Foundations
- Company and product knowledge
- ICP and buyer personas
- Cold email fundamentals
- Tool training (CRM, sending tool)
- Shadow experienced reps

### Week 2: Practice
- Write emails with feedback
- Practice objection handling
- Mock reply scenarios
- Start sending (supervised)
- Daily reviews

### Week 3-4: Ramp
- Independent sending
- Gradual volume increase
- Regular coaching sessions
- Metric tracking begins
- Identify weaknesses

### Ongoing Training
- Weekly team reviews
- A/B test learnings sharing
- New template/angle rollouts
- Skill development sessions
- Peer coaching

## Compensation Models

### Base + Commission
**Structure**: Fixed salary + variable based on meetings/pipeline

**Example**:
- Base: $40,000-$60,000
- Variable: $15,000-$30,000
- OTE: $55,000-$90,000

**Commission Triggers**:
- Meeting booked
- Meeting held
- Qualified opportunity
- Pipeline generated

### Typical OTEs by Market

| Market | Entry SDR OTE | Experienced SDR OTE |
|--------|---------------|---------------------|
| Small Market | $45-55K | $55-70K |
| Mid Market | $55-70K | $70-90K |
| Major Market | $65-85K | $85-120K |

### Commission Structure Examples

**Meeting-Based**:
- $50 per meeting held
- Target: 20 meetings/month
- Accelerators above target

**Pipeline-Based**:
- 2-5% of qualified pipeline
- Higher quality focus
- Aligned with downstream

## Performance Management

### Key Metrics to Track

**Activity Metrics**:
- Emails sent
- Calls made
- LinkedIn touchpoints

**Output Metrics**:
- Meetings booked
- Meetings held
- Pipeline generated

**Quality Metrics**:
- Reply rate
- Positive reply rate
- Meeting show rate
- Qualification rate

### Performance Reviews

**Weekly**: Activity review, metric check
**Monthly**: Full performance review, coaching plan
**Quarterly**: Career development, compensation review

### Performance Tiers

| Tier | Performance | Action |
|------|-------------|--------|
| Top Performer | 120%+ quota | Recognize, promote path |
| Solid Performer | 90-120% quota | Continue coaching |
| Developing | 70-90% quota | Intensive coaching |
| Underperforming | <70% quota | PIP or transition |

## Management Best Practices

### Daily Practices
- Morning standup (5-10 min)
- Review key metrics
- Address blockers
- Celebrate wins

### Weekly Practices
- 1:1 with each SDR
- Team meeting with learnings
- Pipeline/forecast review
- Metric deep dive

### Monthly Practices
- Performance reviews
- Process improvements
- Training and development
- Team building

### Common Management Mistakes
- Micromanaging activity vs. coaching quality
- Ignoring underperformance
- Not celebrating success
- Inconsistent expectations
- Poor onboarding

---

## Summary

**Hire for Writing and Coachability**: Skills can be taught.

**Train Thoroughly**: Week 1 investment pays off.

**Compensate Fairly**: Competitive pay attracts talent.

**Manage Actively**: Regular coaching drives improvement.

**Track Everything**: Data enables optimization.
