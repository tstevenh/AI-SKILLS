# Advanced Cold Email Topics

This chapter covers advanced strategies for cold email practitioners who have mastered the basics.

## Multi-Threading Strategy

### What Is Multi-Threading?

Multi-threading means reaching out to multiple people at the same company simultaneously or sequentially. Instead of relying on one contact, you build relationships across the organization.

### Why Multi-Thread?

**Risk Mitigation**: One person leaving, changing roles, or ignoring email doesn't kill the deal.

**Faster Consensus**: Enterprise deals require multiple stakeholders. Reach them all.

**Better Intelligence**: Different perspectives help you understand their needs.

**Higher Success Rate**: Companies contacted at multiple levels convert at 2-3x higher rates.

### Multi-Threading Approaches

**Simultaneous**:
Contact 2-3 people at the same company in the same week.

**Sequential**:
Start with one, then add others if no response or as deal progresses.

**Referral-Based**:
Ask each contact who else should be involved.

### Who to Contact

**The Trifecta**:
1. **End User**: Person who'll use your product daily
2. **Manager**: Person who manages the team/budget
3. **Executive**: Person with strategic authority

**Example for Sales Tool**:
- SDR (user)
- Sales Director (manager)
- VP Sales or CRO (executive)

### Multi-Thread Email Examples

**Email to Executive**:
```
Subject: Quick question about [Company]'s sales strategy

[First name],

[Observation about company or executive's priorities]

Most [CRO/VP Sales] I talk to are focused on [strategic challenge].

We help companies like [Similar Company] achieve [strategic outcome].

Worth a brief conversation to see if this is relevant to [Company]'s priorities?

[Your name]
```

**Email to Manager**:
```
Subject: Your team's [challenge]

[First name],

[Observation about their team or challenges]

Sales managers at companies like [Company] often struggle with [tactical challenge].

We've helped teams like [Similar Team] improve [specific metric] by [percentage].

Is this something your team is working on?

[Your name]
```

**Email to End User**:
```
Subject: Thought about your daily workflow

[First name],

[Observation about their role]

SDRs at fast-growing companies often spend [X hours] on [tedious task] instead of selling.

We built [Product] to fix that. [Similar SDR] told us we save them [X hours/week].

Would you find that useful?

[Your name]
```

### Coordination Rules

1. **Don't send identical emails**: Customize for each role
2. **Don't reference other contacts** (unless they've responded)
3. **Space out initial touches**: Don't all hit same day
4. **Coordinate follow-ups**: If one replies, pause others or adjust approach
5. **Track at account level**: Manage as one opportunity, not separate leads

## Account-Based Email Strategy

### ABE vs. Mass Email

**Mass Email**: Same message to many companies
**Account-Based Email (ABE)**: Customized approach per target account

### When to Use ABE

- Enterprise targets
- High deal values ($50K+)
- Complex buying committees
- Named account lists
- Strategic markets

### ABE Process

**1. Account Selection**
- Define criteria for target accounts
- Build prioritized list (50-200 accounts)
- Research each account thoroughly

**2. Contact Mapping**
- Identify all relevant stakeholders
- Map reporting relationships
- Understand decision process

**3. Account Research**
- Company news and initiatives
- Technology stack
- Competitive situation
- Recent changes

**4. Customized Messaging**
- Account-specific value proposition
- Role-specific emails
- References to their specific situation

**5. Orchestrated Outreach**
- Multi-channel coordination
- Multi-person outreach
- Timing coordination

### ABE Email Example

```
Subject: Thought about [Company]'s [specific initiative]

[First name],

Noticed [Company] announced [specific initiative] last month. [Observation about what it means for them].

Companies making similar moves typically face [specific challenge that relates to your product].

We helped [Similar Company going through similar change] navigate this. They achieved [specific relevant result].

Given [Company]'s direction, this might be worth discussing.

Would a 20-minute conversation be valuable?

[Your name]
```

## Intent-Based Outreach

### What Is Intent Data?

Intent data reveals when companies are actively researching topics related to your solution, signaling potential buying interest.

### Types of Intent

**First-Party Intent**: Activity on your properties
- Website visits
- Content downloads
- Demo requests
- Email engagement

**Third-Party Intent**: Activity across the web
- Researching relevant topics
- Visiting competitor sites
- Consuming related content

### Intent Data Sources

**Bombora**: Topic-based intent from B2B content consumption
**6sense**: Account-based intent platform
**G2**: Review site activity and comparison shopping
**ZoomInfo Intent**: Built into ZoomInfo platform

### Using Intent in Cold Email

**Prioritization**: Contact high-intent accounts first

**Personalization**: Reference what they're researching

**Timing**: Strike while intent is hot (recency matters)

**Messaging**: Address the topic they're researching

### Intent-Based Email Example

```
Subject: Saw you're researching [topic]

[First name],

I noticed [Company] has been exploring [topic/solution area] lately.

Most companies researching this are trying to [common goal]. The challenge is usually [common obstacle].

We help companies like [Similar Company] navigate this. They [specific result].

Since you're already looking into this, would a quick comparison be useful?

[Your name]
```

## Trigger-Based Automation

### What Are Trigger Events?

Trigger events are changes that create opportunities for your solution:
- Funding raised
- New executive hired
- Product launched
- Company acquired
- Competitor issue
- Expansion announcement

### Building Trigger Workflows

**Step 1: Identify Triggers**
List events that signal opportunity for your solution

**Step 2: Set Up Monitoring**
Use tools to track triggers:
- Google Alerts
- Crunchbase (funding)
- LinkedIn (job changes)
- Press release monitoring
- Social listening

**Step 3: Create Trigger Sequences**
Build sequences for each trigger type

**Step 4: Automate Where Possible**
Use tools like Clay or Zapier to automate detection and outreach

### Trigger-Based Email Examples

**Funding Trigger**:
```
Subject: Congrats on the raise

Saw the [round] news—congrats! Companies at this stage typically [common post-funding priority].

We help with exactly that. [Similar funded company] used us to [result].

Worth a quick chat as you plan the next phase?
```

**New Hire Trigger**:
```
Subject: Welcome to [Company]

Congrats on the new role! The first 90 days as [title] are critical for [common priority].

We help [titles] like you [achieve outcome]. [Similar person] credits us with [result].

Would a quick call be useful as you get started?
```

**Expansion Trigger**:
```
Subject: Your expansion to [market]

Saw [Company] is entering [market]. Exciting move—but [common challenge with expansion].

We've helped companies like [Similar Company] navigate this. They [result].

Worth discussing how we could help?
```

## Cold Email for Different Deal Sizes

### SMB ($0-$20K deals)

**Characteristics**:
- Short sales cycle (1-4 weeks)
- Single decision maker
- Price-sensitive
- Speed matters

**Email Approach**:
- Direct value proposition
- Clear pricing indication
- Easy trial/demo path
- Fast follow-up

**Volume**: Higher volume, templated approach

### Mid-Market ($20K-$100K deals)

**Characteristics**:
- Medium sales cycle (1-3 months)
- 2-4 stakeholders
- ROI-focused
- Process emerging

**Email Approach**:
- Balanced personalization
- Multiple contacts
- ROI emphasis
- Case studies important

**Volume**: Moderate volume, semi-personalized

### Enterprise ($100K+ deals)

**Characteristics**:
- Long sales cycle (3-12+ months)
- Many stakeholders
- Complex process
- Strategic focus

**Email Approach**:
- Deep personalization
- Account-based strategy
- Multi-threading essential
- Executive engagement

**Volume**: Low volume, highly personalized

## Cold Email Integration with Other Channels

### Email + LinkedIn

**Sequence Example**:
1. Day 1: Email
2. Day 3: LinkedIn connection request
3. Day 5: Email follow-up (mention LinkedIn)
4. Day 7: LinkedIn message (if connected)
5. Day 10: Email with new angle
6. Day 14: LinkedIn engagement (comment on their post)
7. Day 17: Final email

### Email + Phone

**Sequence Example**:
1. Day 1: Email
2. Day 2: Phone call (reference email)
3. Day 3: Voicemail + email
4. Day 5: Email follow-up
5. Day 8: Phone call
6. Day 10: Email with new angle
7. Day 14: Final attempt (both)

### Email + Direct Mail

**Strategy**:
- Send physical mail to high-value accounts
- Reference in email follow-up
- Creates memorable touchpoint
- Expensive but effective for enterprise

### Email + Video

**Strategy**:
- Include personalized video (Loom, Vidyard)
- Reference video in email copy
- Higher engagement but lower scale
- Good for high-value accounts

## Seasonal and Timing Strategies

### Best Times to Send

**Day of Week**:
- Best: Tuesday, Wednesday, Thursday
- Moderate: Monday afternoon, Friday morning
- Avoid: Saturday, Sunday

**Time of Day**:
- Best: 8-10 AM, 2-4 PM (recipient's timezone)
- Moderate: 10-11 AM, 4-5 PM
- Avoid: Before 7 AM, after 6 PM

### Seasonal Considerations

**Q1 (Jan-Mar)**:
- New budgets, fresh priorities
- Good time to reach out
- Watch for fiscal year end (some companies)

**Q2 (Apr-Jun)**:
- Projects underway
- Evaluations happening
- Good for mid-year initiatives

**Q3 (Jul-Sep)**:
- Summer slowdown (July-Aug)
- September picks up
- Back-to-school mentality

**Q4 (Oct-Dec)**:
- Budget decisions
- Use-it-or-lose-it spending
- Holiday slowdown (mid-Dec+)
- Plan for Q1

### Industry-Specific Timing

**Retail**: Avoid Nov-Dec (holiday crunch)
**Accounting**: Avoid Jan-Apr (tax season)
**Education**: Align with academic calendar
**Healthcare**: Year-round, but busier end of fiscal year

## International Cold Email Considerations

### Region-Specific Regulations

**Europe (GDPR)**:
- Consent or legitimate interest required
- Document your basis
- Easy opt-out essential
- Data subject rights compliance

**UK (GDPR + PECR)**:
- B2B more permissive than B2C
- Corporate addresses without consent OK
- Clear identification required

**Canada (CASL)**:
- Consent generally required
- "Conspicuously published" exception limited
- Implied consent from relationships

**Australia**:
- Consent or inferred consent required
- Business relationship exception exists

### Cultural Considerations

**United States**: Direct approach acceptable
**United Kingdom**: Slightly more formal
**Germany**: Very formal, direct
**France**: Relationship-oriented
**Asia-Pacific**: Relationship-building important, formality varies

### Language Considerations

- Email in recipient's language when possible
- If English, use simple, clear language
- Avoid idioms and cultural references
- Consider local holidays and business hours

## Metrics for Advanced Users

### Beyond Basic Metrics

**Account Penetration**: % of target accounts with engagement
**Multi-Thread Rate**: Accounts with 2+ contacts engaged
**Sequence Completion Rate**: % reaching final email
**Reply Quality Score**: Ratio of positive to negative replies
**Time to First Reply**: Speed of engagement
**Pipeline Velocity**: Speed from first touch to opportunity

### Attribution Modeling

**First Touch**: Credit to first email
**Last Touch**: Credit to final touch before conversion
**Multi-Touch**: Weighted credit across sequence
**Full Path**: Equal credit to all touches

### Predictive Metrics

**Reply Probability Score**: Likelihood of response based on signals
**Conversion Probability**: Likelihood of meeting/opportunity
**Deal Size Prediction**: Expected value based on attributes

## Summary: Advanced Principles

**Multi-Thread for Enterprise**: One contact isn't enough for complex deals.

**Integrate Channels**: Email works best as part of multi-channel approach.
