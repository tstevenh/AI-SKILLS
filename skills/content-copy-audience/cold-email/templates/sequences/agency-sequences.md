# Agency Services Sequences (5 Complete Sequences)

Ready-to-use sequences for agencies selling SEO, marketing, development, design, and other professional services.

---

## Sequence 1: Marketing Agency Prospecting

**Use Case**: Cold outreach to companies that could use marketing services
**Length**: 5 emails over 21 days

### Email 1 - Day 1
```
Subject: Quick idea for [Company]'s marketing

[First name],

[Personalized observation—their content, their ads, their social presence]

Most [industry] companies at [Company]'s stage are [trying to achieve X]. The challenge is usually [relevant marketing problem].

We help with exactly that—helped [Similar Company] go from [state A] to [state B] in [timeframe].

Worth a quick chat to see if there's something here?

[Your name]
[Agency]
```

### Email 2 - Day 4
```
Subject: Re: Quick idea for [Company]'s marketing

Bumping this up—wanted to share what we did for [Similar Company].

They were [briefly describe similar situation]. We [approach], and they saw [result].

Worth 15 minutes to see if relevant to [Company]?
```

### Email 3 - Day 10
```
Subject: What [Competitor] is doing differently

[First name],

Noticed [Competitor or similar company] has been [specific marketing activity you observed].

There's a strategy behind it: [brief insight].

Could be relevant for [Company]. Want me to break it down?
```

### Email 4 - Day 15
```
Subject: Free audit for [Company]

[First name],

Put together a quick audit of [Company]'s [marketing channel/area].

Found [X] opportunities that could [outcome].

Happy to share it—no strings. Takes 5 minutes to review.

Want me to send it over?
```

### Email 5 - Day 21
```
Subject: Closing the loop

[First name],

Last note on this. If marketing isn't a focus right now, totally understand.

If it is, we might be able to help like we did for [Customer 1], [Customer 2], and [Customer 3].

Either way, let me know and I'll update my notes.
```

## Sequence 2: SEO Agency Sequence

**Use Case**: Outreach for SEO services
**Length**: 5 emails over 21 days

### Email 1 - Day 1
```
Subject: [Company]'s search visibility

[First name],

Ran [Company]'s domain through a few tools—found something interesting.

You're ranking for [X] keywords, but there are [Y] high-intent keywords you're missing that competitors are capturing.

That's likely costing [estimate of traffic/leads].

We've helped [similar company] capture [X%] more organic traffic. Worth a quick chat about your SEO priorities?

[Your name]
```

### Email 2 - Day 4
```
Subject: Re: [Company]'s search visibility

Quick follow-up—didn't want this to get buried.

The gap I mentioned ([Y keywords]) represents [estimated opportunity].

Happy to walk through the specifics in 15 minutes.

[Your name]
```

### Email 3 - Day 10
```
Subject: How [Competitor] outranks [Company]

[First name],

Did a quick competitive analysis.

[Competitor] ranks above [Company] for [X] keywords you probably want.

Here's why: [Brief insight—content depth, backlinks, technical factor]

We helped [similar company] flip this dynamic. Want to see the approach?
```

### Email 4 - Day 15
```
Subject: Free SEO report for [Company]

[First name],

Put together a quick SEO analysis for [Company]:

- Current rankings snapshot
- Keyword gap analysis
- Top 3 quick wins

No obligation—just useful data.

Want me to send it?
```

### Email 5 - Day 21
```
Subject: SEO priority check

[First name],

Last check on this.

Is organic search a growth priority for [Company] this year?

If yes, we should talk. If not, I'll check back when it makes sense.

Let me know either way.
```

## Sequence 5: Staffing/Outsourcing Agency

**Use Case**: Selling development, design, or marketing staffing/outsourcing
**Length**: 5 emails over 21 days

### Email 1 - Day 1
```
Subject: Capacity question for [Company]

[First name],

Noticed [Company] has [X open roles / is growing / just raised].

Most companies in that situation face the same dilemma: hiring takes time, but work needs to happen now.

We provide [senior developers/designers/marketers] who can plug in immediately—no recruitment, no ramp-up.

Is bandwidth something you're thinking about?

[Your name]
```

### Email 2 - Day 4
```
Subject: Re: Capacity question

[First name],

Quick follow-up on capacity.

We've helped companies like [Similar Company] bridge the gap while they build their team. They got [X deliverables] done in [Y timeframe].

Worth a quick chat to see if there's a fit?
```

### Email 3 - Day 10
```
Subject: What [Similar Company] did

[First name],

[Similar Company] was in a similar spot—[brief situation].

Instead of waiting [X months] to hire, they brought us in. Result: [specific outcome].

Now they use us for [ongoing relationship].

Could be an option for [Company]. Worth exploring?
```

### Email 4 - Day 15
```
Subject: Try before you commit

[First name],

What if you could test our [developers/designers/marketers] on a small project first?

No long-term commitment—just see if the fit is there.

[X] companies have started this way. Most stick around.

Interested in trying?
```

### Email 5 - Day 21
```
Subject: Bandwidth update

[First name],

Last check on this.

Are you still looking to expand capacity, or has the team caught up?

If it's still an issue, let's talk. If not, I'll follow up next quarter.
```

## Customization Notes

### Industry Personalization
- Research their current marketing/site/brand
- Reference specific observations
- Use relevant similar clients

### Proof Points
- Include portfolio links
- Quantify results where possible
- Name clients (with permission)

### CTA Adjustments
- Agencies often benefit from offering audits/analyses
- Lower commitment asks work well initially
- Build to proposal/scope discussions

---

*These agency sequences work best with genuine research and personalization. Templates are starting points.*
