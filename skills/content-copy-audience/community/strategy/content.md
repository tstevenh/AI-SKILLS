# Community Content Strategy

## Overview

Content is the fuel that keeps communities alive. Without consistent, valuable content, communities become ghost towns. This guide covers everything you need to create a sustainable content strategy that engages members, drives value, and scales with your community.

---

## Part 1: Content Fundamentals

### The Content Equation

```
Valuable Content + Consistent Delivery + Member Relevance = Engaged Community
```

**Three Questions to Answer**:
1. What does my community need to learn/experience?
2. How often can I realistically deliver value?
3. How do I make content relevant to different member types?

### Content vs Conversation

Communities need both:

**Content** (One-to-Many):
- Educational posts
- Tutorials and guides
- Announcements
- Resources and tools
- Expert interviews

**Conversation** (Many-to-Many):
- Discussion threads
- Q&A sessions
- Member introductions
- Wins and celebrations
- Casual chat

**Ideal Ratio**: 20% Creator Content / 80% Member Conversation

Your job is to spark conversations, not dominate them.

---

## Part 2: Content Pillars

### Defining Your Pillars

Content pillars are 3-5 core themes that all your content revolves around. They ensure consistency and help members know what to expect.

**How to Choose Pillars**:
1. What transformation do members want?
2. What problems do they face?
3. What are you uniquely qualified to teach?
4. What topics generate most engagement?

**Example: Online Business Community**:
```
Pillar 1: Marketing & Traffic
Pillar 2: Sales & Conversion
Pillar 3: Systems & Automation
Pillar 4: Mindset & Productivity
Pillar 5: Member Success Stories
```

**Example: Fitness Community**:
```
Pillar 1: Workout Programming
Pillar 2: Nutrition & Diet
Pillar 3: Recovery & Mobility
Pillar 4: Mindset & Motivation
Pillar 5: Progress & Transformations
```

### Pillar Content Matrix

Create content for each pillar across formats:

| Pillar | Educational | Discussion | Resource | Event |
|--------|-------------|------------|----------|-------|
| Marketing | How-to guide | "What's working?" | Template | Workshop |
| Sales | Framework | Objection handling | Scripts | Role-play |
| Systems | Tutorial | Tool recommendations | Checklist | Demo |
| Mindset | Lesson | Daily prompt | Book list | Coaching |
| Success | Case study | Win sharing | Interview | Spotlight |

## Part 3: Content Types

### Educational Content

**Purpose**: Teach members what they need to know

**Formats**:
- Written tutorials
- Video lessons
- Framework breakdowns
- Tool comparisons
- How-to guides
- Industry updates

**Best Practices**:
- One concept per piece
- Actionable takeaways
- Visual aids when helpful
- Link to related content
- Invite questions

**Example Post Structure**:
```
🎯 [Clear Title]

The problem: [What pain point this solves]

The solution: [The concept/framework]

How to implement:
1. Step one
2. Step two
3. Step three

💡 Key takeaway: [Summary]

⬇️ Questions? Drop them below!
```

### Discussion Content

**Purpose**: Spark member-to-member conversation

**Formats**:
- Open-ended questions
- Polls
- Debates
- "What would you do?" scenarios
- Recommendation requests

**Best Practices**:
- Ask one clear question
- Make it easy to answer
- Respond to comments
- Tag relevant members
- Share your perspective too

**Example Discussion Prompts**:
```
"What's one thing you wish you knew when starting?"
"Hot take: [controversial opinion]. Agree or disagree?"
"What's your biggest challenge this week?"
"If you could only use 3 tools, which would you keep?"
"What was your biggest win this month?"
```

### Resource Content

**Purpose**: Provide tools, templates, and shortcuts

**Formats**:
- Templates (docs, spreadsheets)
- Checklists
- Swipe files
- Tool recommendations
- Book/course lists
- Curated links

**Best Practices**:
- Make immediately usable
- Explain how to use
- Update regularly
- Organize accessibly
- Credit sources

**Resource Post Example**:
```
📥 Free Template: [Name]

What it is: [Brief description]

Who it's for: [Target member]

How to use it:
1. Download the file
2. Make a copy
3. Fill in [sections]

🔗 [Link to download]

P.S. Let me know if you have questions!
```

### Social/Connection Content

**Purpose**: Build relationships and community culture

**Formats**:
- Member introductions
- Win celebrations
- Milestone recognition
- Fun prompts
- Team building

**Best Practices**:
- Celebrate often
- Be personal
- Encourage tagging
- Create traditions
- Show vulnerability

**Examples**:
```
🎉 Win Wednesday: Share your biggest win this week!

👋 New members this week: [tags]. Welcome!

🎂 Community birthdays: [names]. Help us celebrate!

🔥 Consistency streak: [name] has been active 30 days!

📸 Show us your workspace/setup/routine!
```

### News/Updates Content

**Purpose**: Keep members informed about community and industry

**Formats**:
- Community announcements
- Industry news
- Platform updates
- Schedule reminders
- Policy changes

**Best Practices**:
- Clear and concise
- Pin important updates
- Send notifications for critical info
- Recap regularly
- Archive old news

## Part 4: Content Cadence

### Finding Your Rhythm

Content cadence depends on:
- Community size
- Your bandwidth
- Member expectations
- Platform norms
- Content type

### Recommended Cadences

**Minimum Viable Cadence**:
```
Weekly:
- 1 educational piece
- 2-3 discussion prompts
- 1 resource or tool share
- Member highlights as appropriate
```

**Optimal Cadence**:
```
Daily:
- Discussion prompt or question

Weekly:
- 2-3 educational pieces
- 1 resource
- 1 member spotlight
- 1 live event

Monthly:
- 1 in-depth training
- 1 challenge or intensive
- Community recap
```

### Content Calendar Template

**Monday**: Week kickoff + educational content
**Tuesday**: Discussion prompt + resource share
**Wednesday**: Mid-week wins + Q&A
**Thursday**: Educational content + tool spotlight
**Friday**: Week recap + weekend discussion
**Weekend**: Light engagement (optional)

### Planning Ahead

**Monthly Planning Session**:
1. Review last month's performance
2. Identify upcoming events/themes
3. Plan pillar coverage
4. Assign content to days
5. Prepare key pieces in advance

**Quarterly Planning**:
1. Major themes/campaigns
2. Guest expert schedules
3. Challenge timelines
4. Content refresh needs
5. Resource updates

## Part 5: Member-Generated Content

### The UGC Advantage

User-generated content (UGC):
- Reduces your workload
- Increases engagement
- Builds ownership
- Provides social proof
- Surfaces diverse perspectives

**Goal**: Eventually 80%+ of content comes from members

### Encouraging UGC

**Lower the Barrier**:
- Create simple prompts
- Provide templates
- Feature good examples
- Celebrate contributors
- Make it optional

**Structured UGC Opportunities**:
```
Introductions: "Tell us about yourself"
Wins: "Share your success"
Questions: "What do you need help with?"
Resources: "What tools do you love?"
Feedback: "What would you improve?"
```

### UGC Formats

**1. Success Stories**
```
Prompt: "Share a win you've had since joining!"

Template:
- Where I started:
- What I tried:
- My result:
- Biggest lesson:
```

**2. Implementation Posts**
```
Prompt: "Show us what you built/created/achieved!"

Template:
- What I created:
- How I did it:
- Time it took:
- What I'd do differently:
```

**3. Resource Shares**
```
Prompt: "What tool/book/resource has helped you most?"

Template:
- The resource:
- Why I love it:
- How I use it:
- Link:
```

**4. Questions**
```
Prompt: "What's your biggest challenge right now?"

The community answers → valuable for everyone
```

### UGC Quality Control

**Encourage Quality**:
- Pin exemplary posts
- Feature best contributions
- Provide feedback privately
- Create quality guidelines
- Reward thoughtful posts

**Manage Low Quality**:
- Gentle redirection
- Private feedback
- Content guidelines
- Moderation when needed
- Focus on positive examples

## Part 6: Expert Content

### Leveraging Expertise

Expert content elevates community value through:
- Your expertise (founder content)
- Guest experts
- Member expertise
- External resources

### Founder Content Strategy

**Your Unique Content**:
- Frameworks and methodologies
- Behind-the-scenes insights
- Personal experiences
- Industry connections
- Curated expertise

**Content Ratio by Stage**:
```
0-100 members: 80% founder content
100-500 members: 50% founder content
500-1000 members: 30% founder content
1000+ members: 20% founder content
```

### Guest Expert Strategy

**Benefits**:
- Fresh perspectives
- Expanded expertise
- Cross-promotion
- Reduced founder burden
- Member value

**Guest Expert Formats**:
- AMAs (Ask Me Anything)
- Workshop/training
- Interview
- Co-hosted event
- Written feature

**Finding Guest Experts**:
- Your network
- Industry connections
- Member recommendations
- Complementary creators
- Book authors
- Tool founders

**Guest Expert Template**:

## Part 7: Evergreen vs Timely Content

### Evergreen Content

**Definition**: Content that remains valuable over time

**Examples**:
- Fundamental frameworks
- How-to guides
- FAQs
- Resource libraries
- Best practices

**Benefits**:
- Compound value over time
- New member onboarding
- Reduced repetition
- SEO value (if public)
- Time investment pays off

**Evergreen Best Practices**:
- Create once, reference often
- Organize accessibly
- Update periodically
- Link from timely content
- Include in onboarding

### Timely Content

**Definition**: Content relevant to current moment

**Examples**:
- Industry news
- Trending topics
- Event announcements
- Seasonal content
- Current challenges

**Benefits**:
- High engagement
- Demonstrates relevance
- Creates urgency
- Builds authority
- Drives conversation

**Timely Content Best Practices**:
- Move quickly on trends
- Have opinion/perspective
- Connect to member needs
- Don't force relevance
- Archive when outdated

### Content Ratio

**Recommended Split**:
```
70% Evergreen: Foundations that last
30% Timely: Current relevance

Example Week:
Mon: Evergreen framework
Tue: Timely discussion (current event)
Wed: Evergreen resource
Thu: Timely tool update
Fri: Evergreen recap + timely wins
```

### Platform-Specific Organization

**Skool**:
- Categories for content types
- Classroom for courses
- Pinned posts for key content

**Discord**:
- Channels per topic
- Pinned messages

**Help members find content**:

1. **Start Here**: Clear onboarding path
2. **Resource Index**: Organized links
3. **Search**: Ensure content is searchable

**Regular Maintenance Tasks**:
```
Weekly:
- Review new content performance
- Respond to comments

- Archive outdated content
- Update top resources
- Review organization
- Identify gaps

Quarterly:
- Major content audit
- Refresh evergreen content
```

## Part 9: Content Performance

### Metrics to Track

**Engagement Metrics**:
- Likes/reactions
- Comments
- Shares
- Time on content
- Return visits

**Value Metrics**:
- Questions answered
- Problems solved
- Actions taken
- Results reported
- Testimonials generated

### Content Analysis

**What to Measure**:
```
For each content piece:
- Views/reach
- Engagement rate
- Comment quality
- Saves/bookmarks
- Follow-up questions
```

**What to Learn**:
- Which topics resonate?
- Which formats perform?
- What length works?
- When to post?
- Who engages most?

### Iteration Process

**Monthly Content Review**:
1. Identify top 3 performing pieces
2. Identify bottom 3 performing pieces
3. Analyze differences
4. Create more of what works
5. Fix or abandon what doesn't

## Part 10: Content Systems

### Content Creation Workflow

**Phase 1: Ideation**
```
Sources:
- Member questions
- Industry news
- Competitor analysis
- Personal experience
- Content gaps
```

**Phase 2: Planning**
```
For each piece:
- Topic
- Format
- Pillar
- Target date
- Resources needed
```

**Phase 3: Creation**
```
Batching:
- Write multiple pieces at once
- Record multiple videos
- Create templates in bulk
- Schedule in advance
```

**Phase 4: Publication**
```
Checklist:
- Proofread
- Add visuals
- Include CTA
- Schedule time
- Notify relevant members
```

**Phase 5: Engagement**
```
Post-publication:
- Respond to comments
- Answer questions
- Reshare as needed
- Track performance
```

### Batching Strategy

**Weekly Content Batch** (2-4 hours):
```
1. Review upcoming week
2. Create 5-7 discussion prompts
3. Draft 2-3 educational pieces
4. Prepare 1 resource share
5. Schedule everything
```

**Monthly Content Batch** (1 day):
```
1. Plan month's calendar
2. Create major pieces
3. Record videos
4. Prepare resources
5. Schedule recurring content
```

**Creation**:
- Google Docs (writing)
- Canva (graphics)
- Loom (video)
- Descript (editing)

**Scheduling**:
- Platform native scheduling
- Zapier (automation)
- Buffer/Hootsuite (if cross-posting)

## Summary

### Content Strategy Principles

1. **Consistency Over Perfection**: Regular good content beats sporadic great content
2. **Value First**: Every piece should help members
3. **Conversation Catalyst**: Your content should spark discussion
4. **Member-Centric**: Create what they need, not what you want to share
5. **Sustainable Pace**: Build systems you can maintain

### Quick Start Guide

**Week 1**: Define 3-5 content pillars
**Week 2**: Create content calendar template
**Week 3**: Batch create first 2 weeks of content
**Week 4**: Establish rhythm, measure, adjust

### Content Success Formula

```
Clear Pillars + Consistent Cadence + Quality Execution + Member Contribution = Thriving Content
```
