# Community Sponsorships: Complete Revenue Guide

## Overview

Sponsorships represent a significant revenue opportunity for community builders. Brands pay to access your engaged audience, and when done right, sponsorships add value for both sponsors and members. This guide covers how to build, price, and manage a successful sponsorship program.

---

## Part 1: Sponsorship Fundamentals

### What Is Community Sponsorship?

**Definition**: A business relationship where a brand pays for exposure to and association with your community audience.

**What Sponsors Get**:
```
EXPOSURE: Access to your audience
CREDIBILITY: Association with your brand
LEADS: Potential customers
DATA: Market insights
RELATIONSHIPS: Network access
```

**What You Get**:
```
REVENUE: Direct income
RESOURCES: Products/services for members
CONTENT: Co-created value
CREDIBILITY: Association with known brands
```

### Sponsorship vs Advertising

**Sponsorship**:
- Relationship-based
- Brand association
- Often exclusive
- Longer-term
- Integrated experience

**Advertising**:
- Transaction-based
- Impression-focused
- Non-exclusive
- Short-term
- Separate from content

### When You're Ready for Sponsors

**Prerequisites**:
```
□ Established audience (500+ engaged members minimum)
□ Clear audience demographics
□ Consistent engagement metrics
□ Professional presentation
□ Brand sponsors would want to associate with
□ Bandwidth to manage relationships
```

## Part 3: Pricing Sponsorships

### Pricing Factors

**What Determines Price**:
```
AUDIENCE SIZE:
- Number of members
- Email list size
- Event attendance
- Social reach

AUDIENCE QUALITY:
- Demographics
- Buying power
- Decision-making authority
- Engagement level

ENGAGEMENT METRICS:
- Open rates
- Click rates
- Participation rates
- Response rates

EXCLUSIVITY:
- Category exclusivity
- Competitive restrictions
- Priority placement

YOUR BRAND:
- Industry reputation
- Thought leadership
- Quality perception
```

### Pricing Benchmarks

**Newsletter Sponsorships**:
```
By CPM (Cost Per 1,000 subscribers):
- General audience: $20-50 CPM
- Engaged niche: $50-100 CPM
- Premium B2B: $100-200+ CPM

Examples:
5,000 subscribers × $50 CPM = $250/send
10,000 subscribers × $75 CPM = $750/send
25,000 subscribers × $100 CPM = $2,500/send
```

**Community Sponsorships**:
```
By member count (monthly):
- 500 members: $500-1,000
- 2,500 members: $1,500-3,000
- 10,000 members: $5,000-10,000
- 50,000 members: $15,000-30,000

Factors that increase pricing:
- High engagement rates
- Premium demographics
- Exclusive access
- Multiple touchpoints
```

**Event Sponsorships**:
```
Virtual Events:
- 100 attendees: $500-2,000
- 500 attendees: $2,000-5,000
- 1,000+ attendees: $5,000-15,000

In-Person Events:
- 50 attendees: $1,000-3,000
- 200 attendees: $5,000-15,000
- 500+ attendees: $15,000-50,000+
```

### Value-Based Pricing

**Calculate Based on Lead Value**:
```
Sponsor's customer LTV: $10,000
Expected conversion rate: 1%
Members exposed: 2,500
Expected leads: 25
Expected customers: 0.25

Customer value to sponsor: $2,500

Charge 20-50% of value: $500-1,250/month
```

## Part 4: Finding Sponsors

### Ideal Sponsor Profile

**Characteristics of Good Sponsors**:
```
✓ Serves your audience
✓ Non-competitive with you
✓ Quality product/service
✓ Good reputation
✓ Marketing budget
✓ Long-term mindset
✓ Values alignment
```

### Sponsor Sources

**Where to Find Sponsors**:
```
1. TOOLS YOUR MEMBERS USE
   - Software they mention
   - Services they recommend
   - Products they buy

2. INDUSTRY ADVERTISERS
   - Who advertises in trade publications
   - Who sponsors industry events
   - Who has affiliate programs

3. BRANDS SPONSORING SIMILAR COMMUNITIES
   - Competitor sponsors
   - Adjacent community sponsors
   - Industry podcast sponsors

4. YOUR NETWORK
   - Companies you've worked with
   - Products you personally use
   - Connections' companies

5. INBOUND INTEREST
   - Companies that reach out
   - PR pitches (pivot to sponsorship)
   - Ad inquiries
```

### Research Process

**Before Outreach**:
```
□ Research company and product
□ Understand their target customer
□ Find marketing decision-maker
□ Review their current marketing
□ Identify alignment with your audience
□ Prepare relevance talking points
```

## Part 5: Sponsorship Outreach

### Cold Outreach Template

```
Subject: Sponsorship opportunity: [X] engaged [audience type]

Hi [Name],

I run [Community Name], a community of [X] [audience description].

I noticed [Company] serves a similar audience with [product/service]. 
Our members are actively discussing [relevant topic] and 
[evidence of interest].

We're opening up sponsorship opportunities, and I think 
[Company] could be a great fit.

Our members:
- [Relevant demographic/behavior]
- [Evidence of buying intent]
- [Engagement stat]

Sponsorship includes:
- [Key benefit 1]
- [Key benefit 2]
- [Key benefit 3]

Would you be open to a quick call to explore if there's a fit?

[Your Name]

P.S. [Social proof or urgency element]
```

### Warm Introduction Request

```
Subject: Intro request - [Company Name] sponsorship

Hi [Connector Name],

I'm looking to connect with someone at [Company] about 
sponsoring [Community Name].

Our community of [X] [audience] would be a great fit for 
their [product], and I think it could be a valuable 
partnership for both sides.

Would you be able to introduce me to the right person there?

Here's a quick blurb you can forward:

---
"[Your Name] runs [Community Name], a community of [X] 
[audience]. They're exploring sponsorship opportunities 
and thought [Company] might be a great fit given [reason]."
---

Thanks for considering!

[Your Name]
```

### Follow-Up Sequence

```
Day 0: Initial outreach
Day 5: Follow-up #1
Day 12: Follow-up #2 (new angle)
Day 25: Final follow-up

FOLLOW-UP #1:
"Following up on my note about sponsoring [Community Name]. 
Wanted to make sure it didn't get lost. Would love to 
chat briefly to see if there's a fit."

FOLLOW-UP #2:
"One more note - we just [recent milestone/news], and 
sponsors are getting great results. [Example]. Would love 
to discuss how [Company] could benefit."

FINAL:
"I'll assume the timing isn't right. If things change, 
we'd love to have [Company] as a sponsor. Feel free to 
reach out anytime."
```

## Part 8: Ethical Sponsorships

### Maintaining Trust

**Sponsorship Principles**:
```
1. TRANSPARENCY
   - Clearly label sponsored content
   - Disclose relationships
   - Don't disguise ads as editorial

2. RELEVANCE
   - Only accept relevant sponsors
   - Products members would actually use
   - Real value for audience

3. QUALITY
   - Vet sponsors before accepting
   - Stand behind recommendations
   - Remove bad sponsors

4. BALANCE
   - Don't over-commercialize
   - Maintain content quality
   - Members first, sponsors second

5. AUTHENTICITY
   - Don't fake endorsements
   - Be honest about experiences
   - Disclose if you haven't used product
```

### What to Avoid

**Red Flags in Potential Sponsors**:
```
✗ Products you wouldn't recommend
✗ Companies with bad reputations
✗ Direct competitors to your business
✗ Misleading or scammy offerings
✗ Poor fit for audience
✗ Excessive demands
✗ Payment problems
```

### Disclosure Requirements

**Legal Considerations**:
```
FTC GUIDELINES (US):
- Clear and conspicuous disclosure
- "Ad," "Sponsored," "Paid partnership"
- Near the beginning of content
- Not hidden in hashtags or small print

EXAMPLE DISCLOSURES:
"This post is sponsored by [Company]"
"Partner content with [Company]"
"[Company] is a sponsor of [Community]"
"Ad: Check out our sponsor [Company]"
```

## Summary

### Sponsorship Principles

1. **Audience First**: Only accept sponsors that add value
2. **Transparency**: Always disclose sponsorships
3. **Professionalism**: Deliver what you promise
4. **Relationships**: Treat sponsors as partners
5. **Results**: Focus on sponsor ROI

### Sponsorship Checklist

```
□ Sponsorship inventory defined
□ Packages and pricing set
□ Media kit created
□ Outreach templates ready
□ Onboarding process documented
□ Reporting templates prepared
□ Disclosure practices established
□ Pipeline tracking in place
```

### Sponsorship Revenue Formula

```
Sponsorship Revenue = Sponsors × Average Deal Size × Retention

Increase sponsors: Better outreach
Increase deal size: Better packaging
Increase retention: Better service
```
