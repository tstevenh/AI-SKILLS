# Pre-Launch: Building Before You Build

## Overview

The pre-launch phase is where community success is determined. Skip it, and you launch to crickets. Nail it, and you have engaged founding members ready to bring your community to life from day one.

---

## Part 1: Pre-Launch Fundamentals

### Why Pre-Launch Matters

**The Ghost Town Problem**:
Communities that launch without pre-work face:
- Empty discussion feeds
- No engagement momentum
- Founder posting into void
- New members see dead community
- Negative first impressions

**The Solution**: Build anticipation, recruit founding members, and launch with momentum.

### Pre-Launch Timeline

```
8 Weeks Before Launch:
- Define community concept
- Validate with target audience
- Start building waitlist

4 Weeks Before:
- Finalize platform and setup
- Create founding member offer
- Begin outreach

2 Weeks Before:
- Invite founding members
- Set up platform
- Create initial content

1 Week Before:
- Onboard founding members
- Test everything
- Build anticipation

Launch Day:
- Open doors with momentum
- Community already active
```

## Part 2: Validating Your Community

### Before Building, Validate

**Questions to Answer**:
1. Does this audience want community?
2. Will they pay for it?
3. What specific value do they want?
4. Where do they currently gather?
5. Why would they choose your community?

### Validation Methods

**Survey Your Audience**:
```
"I'm considering creating a community for [audience].

Quick questions:
1. Would you be interested? (1-10)
2. What would make it valuable to you?
3. What would you pay monthly?
4. What would prevent you from joining?

Reply or comment - your input shapes this!"
```

**Pre-Sell Test**:
- Create founding member offer
- Accept payments before building
- If no one pays, reconsider
- If people pay, you have validation AND revenue

**1:1 Conversations**:
- Talk to 10-20 ideal members
- Deep dive on needs
- Understand objections
- Validate price points
- Get commitment

### Validation Metrics

**Green Lights**:
- 50+ survey responses with high interest
- 5+ people pre-pay without seeing product
- Consistent feedback on desired features
- Enthusiastic volunteers to help

**Yellow Lights**:
- Low survey response rate
- "Interested" but won't commit money
- Conflicting feedback on value
- Lukewarm enthusiasm

**Red Lights**:
- No one responds
- Zero pre-sales
- "I'd never pay for community"
- No clear value proposition emerges

## Part 3: Founding Member Recruitment

### Who Are Founding Members?

Founding members are your first members who:
- Join before or at launch
- Get special benefits/pricing
- Help shape community culture
- Provide early feedback
- Often become advocates

### The Founding Member Offer

**Standard Structure**:
```
🎉 FOUNDING MEMBER INVITATION

I'm launching [Community Name] and want YOU as a Founding Member.

WHAT YOU GET:
✅ Founding Member pricing: $[X]/month (locked forever)
   (Regular price will be $[Y]/month)
✅ Direct access to me during build phase
✅ Input on community features and direction
✅ Exclusive Founding Member badge
✅ Lifetime benefits reserved for founders

WHAT I NEED FROM YOU:
- Commit to engaging actively for first 90 days
- Provide honest feedback
- Help welcome new members
- Share your expertise

FOUNDING MEMBER SPOTS: [X] (limited)
LAUNCH DATE: [Date]

[Link to join]

Questions? Reply directly.
```

### Finding Founding Members

**From Your Existing Audience**:
- Email list
- Social media followers
- YouTube subscribers
- Podcast listeners
- Course students

**Personal Network**:
- Friends in target audience
- Past clients/customers
- Professional connections
- Mastermind/community peers

**Targeted Outreach**:
- Cold DMs (personalized)
- Referrals from contacts
- LinkedIn connections
- Forum/group members

### Founding Member Criteria

**Ideal Founding Members**:
- Highly engaged with your content
- Fit target member profile
- Willing to contribute actively
- Can provide quality feedback
- Likely to renew/refer

**Red Flags**:
- Only interested in discount
- No history of engagement
- Complaint-prone
- Not actually in target audience
- Unlikely to participate

### Outreach Template

**Email/DM to Known Contact**:
```
Hey [Name],

Quick question - I'm launching [Community Name], a [brief description].

Given your work in [their focus], I immediately thought of you for the founding member group.

Founding members get [key benefit] at [price] locked forever (will be [higher price] later).

But more importantly, you'd help shape what this becomes. Your input would be invaluable.

Interested? I can send details.

[Your name]

P.S. Only taking [X] founding members, and I'd love you to be one.
```

**Email to List**:
```
Subject: Invitation: Be a Founding Member of [Community]

[Name],

I've been working on something exciting, and you're getting first access.

[1-2 sentences on why you're creating this]

I'm looking for [X] Founding Members to help launch [Community Name].

As a Founding Member, you'll get:
• $[X]/month locked forever (will be $[Y])
• Direct access during the build phase
• Input on features and direction
• Exclusive Founding Member status

What I need from you:
• Active participation for first 90 days
• Honest feedback
• Help welcome new members

Spots are limited. First [X] who apply.

[Apply Button]

Thanks for being part of this journey.

[Your name]

P.S. Have questions? Just reply to this email.
```

## Part 4: Building Anticipation

### Waitlist Strategy

**Why Waitlist**:
- Captures interested audience
- Creates scarcity
- Enables launch communication
- Gauges demand

**Waitlist Page Elements**:
```
HEADLINE: [What they get]
SUBHEAD: [Why it matters]

[Email capture form]

WHAT TO EXPECT:
• [Benefit 1]
• [Benefit 2]
• [Benefit 3]

LAUNCHING: [Date/timeframe]

[Join Waitlist Button]

[Social proof: "X people on the waitlist"]
```

### Waitlist Nurture Sequence

**After Signup**:
```
Email 1 (Immediate): Welcome + What to Expect
Email 2 (Day 3): Why This Community
Email 3 (Day 7): Sneak Peek / Behind the Scenes
Email 4 (Day 14): Founding Member Offer
Email 5 (Pre-Launch): Last Chance + Countdown
```

### Content Marketing Pre-Launch

**Build Audience While Building Community**:
- Share your process publicly
- "Building in public" content
- Tease features and content
- Share member stories (early adopters)
- Create value before launch

**Pre-Launch Content Ideas**:
```
- "Why I'm building this community"
- "What [Community] will help you achieve"
- Behind-the-scenes build updates
- Early member spotlights
- Content previews
- Launch countdown posts
```

## Part 5: Beta Testing

### The Beta Phase

Before full launch, run a beta with founding members:

**Beta Goals**:
- Test platform and features
- Identify bugs/issues
- Refine onboarding
- Gather feedback
- Create initial content
- Build engagement momentum

### Beta Timeline

```
Week 1: Invite founding members
Week 2: Core feature testing
Week 3: Content and engagement testing
Week 4: Feedback and refinement
Week 5-6: Prepare for public launch
```

### Beta Feedback System

**What to Ask**:
```
BETA FEEDBACK FORM

Help us make [Community] amazing!

1. How easy was it to get started? (1-10)
   What could be clearer?

2. What features do you love?
   What's missing?

3. How likely are you to recommend? (1-10)
   Why or why not?

4. What would make this a "must-have"?

5. What questions do new members ask?

6. Anything else we should know?
```

**Feedback Channels**:
- Dedicated feedback channel
- Weekly feedback calls
- 1:1 conversations
- Anonymous surveys
- Usage analytics

### Iterating in Beta

**Quick Fixes**:
- Confusing navigation
- Onboarding gaps
- Missing content
- Technical bugs
- Communication issues

**Note for Later**:
- Feature requests
- Major platform changes
- Content expansion ideas
- Pricing feedback

**Must-Have Before Launch**:
1. Welcome/Getting Started guide
2. Community guidelines
3. Introduction post prompt
4. First week discussion content
5. At least one valuable resource
6. FAQ


## Part 10: Pre-Launch Metrics

### What to Track

**Waitlist Metrics**:
- Total signups
- Daily signup rate
- Source tracking
- Email open rates

**Founding Member Metrics**:
- Invited vs accepted
- Payment completion
- Activation rate (intro posted, etc.)
- Feedback scores

**Engagement Metrics** (Beta):
- Daily active users
- Posts per member
- Comments per post
- Event attendance

### Success Indicators

**Ready to Launch**:
- 50+ waitlist (small launch) or 500+ (big launch)
- 20-100 founding members active
- 70%+ founding member satisfaction
- Core content complete
- Platform functioning smoothly

**Consider Delaying**:
- Very low waitlist numbers
- Founding members disengaged
- Critical features broken
- Major negative feedback
- You're not ready

---

## Summary

### Pre-Launch Principles

1. **Validate Before Building**: Make sure people want this
2. **Launch with Momentum**: Never open to an empty room
3. **Founding Members Are Gold**: Treat them exceptionally
4. **Feedback Is Fuel**: Listen and iterate
5. **Don't Rush**: Better to delay than launch broken

### Pre-Launch Timeline Recap

```
Week -8 to -6: Validate concept, build waitlist
Week -6 to -4: Create founding member offer, outreach
Week -4 to -2: Set up platform, invite founding members
Week -2 to -1: Beta testing, feedback, iteration
Week 0: LAUNCH with momentum
```

### Pre-Launch Success Formula

```
Validated Concept + Engaged Founding Members + Ready Platform = Successful Launch
```
