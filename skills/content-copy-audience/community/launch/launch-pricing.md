# Community Pricing Strategy

## Overview

Pricing is one of the most impactful decisions you'll make for your community. Price too low, and you attract uncommitted members while undervaluing your offering. Price too high, and you limit accessibility and growth. This guide covers everything you need to price your community confidently.

---

## Part 1: Pricing Fundamentals

### The Pricing Mindset

**Common Mistakes**:
- Pricing based on insecurity, not value
- Copying competitors without strategy
- Racing to the bottom
- Ignoring willingness to pay
- Not testing prices

**Pricing Principles**:
1. Price for the value delivered, not time invested
2. Higher prices attract more committed members
3. You can always lower, hard to raise
4. Price signals positioning
5. Test and iterate

### The Value Equation

```
Perceived Value = (Dream Outcome × Perceived Likelihood) / (Time to Achieve × Effort Required)

Your job: Maximize the top, minimize the bottom
```

Members pay for:
- **Transformation**: Getting from A to B
- **Access**: People, content, resources
- **Connection**: Peers, network, belonging
- **Accountability**: Staying on track
- **Support**: Help when stuck

## Part 2: Market Research

### Competitor Analysis

**What to Research**:
- Direct competitors (same audience, same offer)
- Indirect competitors (same audience, different offer)
- Adjacent communities (different audience, similar model)

**Competitor Matrix**:
```
| Competitor | Price | Features | Audience | Positioning |
|------------|-------|----------|----------|-------------|
| A          | $X    | ...      | ...      | ...         |
| B          | $X    | ...      | ...      | ...         |
| C          | $X    | ...      | ...      | ...         |
```

### Audience Research

**Ask Your Audience**:
```
"I'm creating a community for [audience] that includes:
- [Feature 1]
- [Feature 2]
- [Feature 3]

What would you expect to pay monthly for something like this?
A) Under $29
B) $29-49
C) $49-99
D) $99-199
E) Over $199"
```

**Willingness to Pay Indicators**:
- Current spending on similar solutions
- Income/budget of target audience
- Urgency of problem being solved
- Value of transformation

### Price Sensitivity Analysis

**Van Westendorp Method**:
Ask four questions:
1. At what price is this too expensive to consider?
2. At what price does this seem expensive but still acceptable?
3. At what price is this a good deal?
4. At what price is this too cheap (suspicious quality)?

The overlap reveals optimal price range.

## Part 3: Pricing Models

### Monthly vs Annual

**Monthly Subscription**:
```
$49/month

Pros:
- Lower barrier to entry
- Easier to start
- Cash flow earlier
- Easy to cancel (lower commitment)

Cons:
- Higher churn risk
- Less predictable revenue
- More payment processing
```

**Annual Subscription**:
```
$399/year (vs $588 if paid monthly)

Pros:
- Better cash flow
- Lower churn (committed)
- Higher LTV
- More predictable revenue

Cons:
- Higher barrier
- Refund complexity
- Need to deliver for 12 months
```

**Best Practice**: Offer both with annual discount
```
Monthly: $49/month
Annual: $399/year (save $189!)
```

### Pricing Tiers

**Single Tier**:
```
Pro: $49/month
- All features
- All access
- Simple decision
```

**Two Tiers**:
```
Member: $29/month
- Community access
- Basic content
- Monthly calls

VIP: $99/month
- Everything in Member
- Premium content
- Weekly calls
- Direct access
```

**Three Tiers**:
```
Essential: $19/month
- Community access
- Limited content

Professional: $49/month
- Full community
- All content
- Weekly calls

Premium: $149/month
- Everything
- 1:1 access
- Priority support
- Exclusive events
```

### One-Time vs Recurring

**One-Time (Lifetime Access)**:
```
$997 one-time = lifetime access

When to use:
- Course + community bundle
- Want quick cash injection
- Can deliver without ongoing costs
- Willing to have freeloaders

Risks:
- No recurring revenue
- Members may disengage
- Hard to fund ongoing operations
```

**Recurring (Subscription)**:
```
$49/month ongoing

When to use:
- Need sustainable revenue
- Ongoing value delivery
- Want engaged members
- Building business (not side project)

Risks:
- Constant delivery pressure
- Churn management required
- Longer to reach revenue goals
```

**Hybrid**:
```
Course: $497 one-time
+ Community: $29/month after 12 months

or

Community: $49/month
+ One-time upgrade: $297 (lifetime access)
```

## Part 5: Pricing Psychology

### Anchoring

**Use a Higher Anchor**:
```
Regular price: $99/month
Your price: $49/month
You save: $600/year

or

Similar programs cost: $2000+
[Community Name]: $99/month
```

### Charm Pricing

```
$49 feels cheaper than $50
$99 feels cheaper than $100
$997 feels cheaper than $1000
```

### Value Stacking

**Show Component Values**:
```
What You Get:

✓ Complete course library ($1,997 value)
✓ Weekly group calls ($1,200/year value)
✓ Resource templates ($497 value)
✓ Expert interviews ($997 value)
✓ Community access ($1,200/year value)

Total Value: $5,891

Your Investment: $99/month
```

### Loss Aversion

**Frame the Cost of NOT Joining**:
```
"What's it costing you to stay stuck?"
"How much longer can you afford to figure this out alone?"
"Members save an average of $X by [benefit]"
```

### Social Proof

```
"Join 2,500+ members who've invested in themselves"
"Our members have collectively achieved $X in results"
"Average member ROI: 10x their investment"
```

## Part 6: Founding Member Pricing

### The Founding Member Strategy

**Why Offer Lower Founding Pricing**:
- Reduces risk for early adopters
- Creates urgency
- Builds initial momentum
- Rewards early believers
- Generates testimonials

### Founding Member Offer Structure

```
FOUNDING MEMBER SPECIAL

Regular Price: $99/month

Founding Member Price: $49/month
(Locked for life!)

Limited to: First 50 members
Deadline: [Date]

Founding Members also get:
✓ Price locked forever
✓ Direct founder access
✓ Input on community direction
✓ Exclusive Founding Member badge
✓ [Special bonus]
```

### How to Lock Prices

**Platform Methods**:
- Coupon codes that persist
- Separate pricing tiers
- Manual price protection
- Grandfather clause policy

**Communication**:
```
"Your founding member price of $49/month is locked forever.
Even when we raise prices, you keep your rate as long as 
your membership is active."
```

### Transitioning to Full Price

```
Launch: $49 Founding (50 spots)
Week 2: Founding closes, $69 Early Bird
Week 4: Early Bird closes, $99 Regular

or

Founding: $49 (first 50)
Regular: $99 (everyone after)
```

## Part 7: Pricing Strategies

### Value-Based Pricing

**Calculate Based on Value**:
```
If your community helps members:
- Save 5 hours/week (worth $50/hour) = $1,000/month value
- Price at 5-10% of value = $50-100/month

If your community helps members:
- Generate $10,000 in additional revenue
- Price at 1-5% of result = $100-500
```

### Competitor-Based Positioning

**Price Relative to Market**:
```
Budget Position: 50-70% of market average
Value Position: 80-120% of market average
Premium Position: 150-300%+ of market average
```

### Cost-Plus Pricing

**Minimum Viable Price**:
```
Monthly costs:
- Platform: $100
- Your time (10 hrs × $100): $1,000
- Tools/software: $100
- Content: $200
Total: $1,400/month

Need 50 members at $28 just to break even
Double for profit: $56/month minimum
```

## Part 8: Discounts and Promotions

### Types of Discounts

**Limited-Time Discounts**:
```
"Launch special: 30% off first 3 months"
"Black Friday: 50% off annual"
"Flash sale: 24 hours only"
```

**Annual Discount**:
```
Monthly: $49/month ($588/year)
Annual: $399/year (Save $189!)
```

**Promotional Codes**:
```
LAUNCH50 - 50% off first month
ANNUAL20 - 20% off annual plan
FRIEND25 - 25% off referral code
```

### When to Discount

**Good Reasons**:
- Launches and promotions
- Annual vs monthly incentive
- Referral rewards
- Strategic partnerships
- Re-engagement campaigns

**Bad Reasons**:
- Desperation
- Competing on price
- Devaluing offering
- Constant discounting

### Discount Best Practices

```
DO:
- Set clear expiration
- Limit availability
- Track usage
- Test different amounts
- Use for specific goals

DON'T:
- Discount constantly
- Discount more than 50%
- Discount without reason
- Train audience to wait for sales
- Undercut your positioning
```

## Part 9: Price Increases

### When to Raise Prices

**Good Indicators**:
- Strong demand (waitlist)
- High retention
- Positive feedback
- Added value
- Below market rate
- Business needs growth

### How to Raise Prices

**Grandfather Existing Members**:
```
Email to current members:

"Good news and important update:

Good news: We're adding [new features] in the coming months.

Important: Starting [date], pricing will increase to $[new price]/month for new members.

Because you're a current member, you're grandfathered at your current rate of $[old price]/month forever.

Thanks for being part of [Community]!"
```

**Raise for Everyone**:
```
Email:

"Starting [date], our membership investment is changing.

Current price: $49/month
New price: $69/month

WHY: [Explanation - added value, costs, etc.]

What this means for you:
- If you're a current member on monthly, your rate will adjust on [date]
- If you're annual, you're locked until renewal
- Want to lock in current rate? Upgrade to annual before [date]

Questions? Reply here.

Thanks for understanding."
```

### Price Increase Frequency

```
Recommended:
- Review pricing every 6-12 months
- Small increases (10-20%) annually
- Larger increases (30%+) with major value adds
- Grandfather loyal members when possible
```

## Part 10: Testing and Iteration

### A/B Testing Prices

**Test Methods**:
- Different landing pages
- Time-based pricing
- Segment testing
- Geography-based pricing

**What to Test**:
- Monthly vs annual ratio
- Number of tiers
- Price points
- Discount amounts
- Free trial length

### Price Optimization Metrics

**Watch These Metrics**:
```
Conversion Rate: % of visitors who buy
- Low = price might be too high
- High = might be leaving money

Churn Rate: % who cancel
- High immediate churn = price mismatch
- High later churn = value mismatch

LTV: Total revenue per customer
- Higher price × lower retention vs
- Lower price × higher retention

CAC Ratio: LTV / Acquisition Cost
- Need 3:1 or better for healthy business
```

### Common Pricing Tests

**Test 1: Price Point**
```
Variant A: $49/month
Variant B: $79/month

Measure: Revenue per visitor
```

**Test 2: Annual Discount**
```
Variant A: Annual = 2 months free
Variant B: Annual = 20% off

Measure: Annual conversion rate
```

**Test 3: Tier Structure**
```
Variant A: Single tier at $49
Variant B: Two tiers at $29 and $79

Measure: Total revenue and engagement
```

## Summary

### Pricing Principles

1. **Value Over Cost**: Price based on transformation delivered
2. **Committed Members**: Higher prices = more committed
3. **Test and Learn**: Your first price is a hypothesis
4. **Positioning**: Price signals quality
5. **Simplicity**: Don't overcomplicate tiers

### Pricing Quick Start

```
1. Research competitors (what do they charge?)
2. Survey audience (what will they pay?)
3. Calculate minimum (what do you need?)
4. Choose position (budget/value/premium)
5. Set founding price (20-40% off regular)
6. Launch and learn
7. Adjust based on data
```

### Pricing Formula

```
Starting Price = Market Average × Position Multiplier

Budget: Market × 0.7
Value: Market × 1.0
Premium: Market × 1.5+

Then adjust based on:
- Audience feedback
- Conversion rates
- Retention rates
- Business needs
```
