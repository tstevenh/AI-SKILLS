# Community Moderation Guide

## Overview

Moderation is the immune system of your community. Good moderation protects culture, maintains safety, and ensures positive experiences without being heavy-handed or creating fear. This guide covers everything from guidelines creation to handling difficult situations.

---

## Part 1: Community Guidelines

### Purpose of Guidelines

Guidelines serve to:
- Set expectations clearly
- Protect community culture
- Give moderators authority
- Create accountability
- Prevent problems proactively

### Creating Effective Guidelines

**Guidelines Framework**:
```
1. Community Values (who we are)
2. Expected Behaviors (what we do)
3. Prohibited Behaviors (what we don't do)
4. Consequences (what happens if violated)
5. Reporting (how to flag issues)
```

### Guidelines Template

```
# [COMMUNITY NAME] COMMUNITY GUIDELINES

## Our Values
[Community Name] is built on:
- **Respect**: We treat everyone with dignity
- **Generosity**: We give before we take
- **Growth**: We're here to improve together
- **Support**: We help each other succeed

## Expected Behaviors
✅ Be respectful and constructive
✅ Share generously and support others
✅ Stay on topic in designated channels
✅ Give credit where due
✅ Ask for help when needed
✅ Celebrate others' successes

## What We Don't Allow
❌ Harassment, discrimination, or hate speech
❌ Spam or excessive self-promotion
❌ Sharing confidential member information
❌ Illegal content or activities
❌ Disrespectful or aggressive behavior
❌ Trolling or intentional disruption

## Self-Promotion Policy
We welcome sharing your work with context:
- Share in designated promotional areas
- Provide value before promoting
- No unsolicited DM selling
- Ask permission for affiliate links
- Focus on helping, not pitching

## Consequences
1st offense: Warning (private)
2nd offense: Temporary restriction
3rd offense: Removal from community

Severe violations (harassment, hate speech, illegal activity) 
result in immediate removal.

## Reporting Issues
If you see something concerning:
- Flag the post/message
- DM a moderator
- Email: [contact]

All reports are confidential.

## Agreement
By participating in [Community Name], you agree to 
follow these guidelines. We reserve the right to 
remove any member who violates them.

Questions? Contact [admin/moderator].
```

## Part 2: Moderation Team

### Moderation Roles

**Community Owner/Admin**:
- Final decision authority
- Policy creation
- Moderator management
- Escalation handling

**Senior Moderator**:
- Day-to-day moderation
- New moderator training
- Escalation first line
- Policy suggestions

**Moderator**:
- Content monitoring
- Basic enforcement
- Member support
- Issue escalation

**Community Ambassador**:
- Positive engagement
- New member welcome
- Culture modeling
- Issue reporting (no enforcement)

### Recruiting Moderators

**Ideal Moderator Traits**:
- Active and engaged member
- Level-headed demeanor
- Good communication skills
- Time availability
- Alignment with values
- Respected by community

**Outreach Template**:
```
Hey [Name],

I've noticed you're consistently helpful and engaged 
in [Community Name]. Your contributions really 
exemplify our values.

Would you be interested in becoming a moderator?

Responsibilities:
- [List key duties]
- Time commitment: [X] hours/week

Benefits:
- [What they get]

No pressure if timing isn't right—just thought 
you'd be great for the role!

Let me know your thoughts.
[Your name]
```

### Training Moderators

**Training Topics**:
1. Guidelines interpretation
2. Escalation procedures
3. Communication standards
4. Tool usage
5. Common scenarios
6. When to act vs observe

**Moderator Guidelines**:
```
MODERATOR HANDBOOK

Your Role:
- Maintain safe, welcoming environment
- Enforce guidelines fairly and consistently
- Support members with questions
- Escalate when unsure

Decision Framework:
1. Does it violate guidelines?
2. Is it harmful to members/community?
3. Can it be redirected/educated?
4. Does it need escalation?

Communication Standards:
- Always be respectful
- Assume good intent first
- Be clear about the issue
- Explain the why
- Offer path forward

Escalation Triggers:
- Harassment or threats
- Repeated violations
- Legal concerns
- Uncertain situations
- Member disputes

When in doubt: DON'T act, DO escalate
```

## Part 3: Handling Issues

### Issue Categories

**Low Severity**:
- Off-topic posts
- Minor rule bending
- Misunderstanding
- First-time mistakes

**Medium Severity**:
- Repeated minor violations
- Disrespectful behavior
- Inappropriate promotion
- Complaints about members

**High Severity**:
- Harassment
- Hate speech
- Threats
- Illegal activity
- Doxxing

### Response Framework

**The CARE Framework**:
```
C - Clarify: Understand what happened
A - Assess: Determine severity
R - Respond: Take appropriate action
E - Educate: Help prevent recurrence
```

### Response Templates

**Gentle Redirect (Low)**:
```
Hey [Name], thanks for sharing!

This post might fit better in [channel]. Mind moving 
it there so the right people see it?

Thanks!
```

**Policy Reminder (Medium)**:
```
Hey [Name],

Quick heads up - [specific behavior] goes against our 
community guidelines on [policy].

Here's our policy: [link/quote]

Would you mind [specific action]?

Thanks for understanding!
```

**Formal Warning (Medium-High)**:
```
[Name],

Your recent [post/behavior] violates our community 
guidelines regarding [specific policy].

This is a formal warning. Further violations may 
result in [consequence].

Please review our guidelines: [link]

If you have questions, happy to discuss privately.

[Moderator name]
```

**Removal Notice (High)**:
```
[Name],

After review, we've determined that your [behavior] 
violates our community guidelines regarding [policy].

Your membership has been [suspended/terminated].

[If suspension: You may return on [date] if you 
agree to follow our guidelines.]

This decision is final.

[Admin name]
```

## Part 4: Common Situations

### Self-Promotion

**Scenario**: Member constantly promotes their services
**Response**:
1. Private message explaining policy
2. Point to appropriate channels
3. Offer guidance on value-first approach
4. Warning if continues
5. Removal for repeat offense

### Conflict Between Members

**Scenario**: Two members arguing publicly
**Response**:
1. Take conversation private if escalating
2. Hear both sides separately
3. Mediate if possible
4. Set expectations for future
5. Monitor relationship

### Negativity/Complaining

**Scenario**: Member consistently negative
**Response**:
1. Private check-in (is something wrong?)
2. Acknowledge feelings
3. Redirect to constructive action
4. Set expectations
5. Consider if fit is right

### Trolling

**Scenario**: Member disrupting intentionally
**Response**:
1. Quick assessment (troll or misunderstanding?)
2. Remove content if harmful
3. Warning or immediate removal
4. Don't engage publicly
5. Document incident

### Spam/Bot Attacks

**Scenario**: Spam posts flooding community
**Response**:
1. Remove content immediately
2. Ban offending accounts
3. Check for additional compromised accounts
4. Communicate with community if needed
5. Review security settings

## Part 5: Proactive Moderation

### Prevention > Reaction

**Proactive Measures**:
- Clear, visible guidelines
- Consistent enforcement
- Positive culture modeling
- Community education
- Quality onboarding

### Culture Building

**Set the Tone**:
- Founders and mods model behavior
- Celebrate positive contributions
- Redirect gently before enforcing
- Create psychological safety
- Address issues early

### Community Norms

**Reinforce Positive Norms**:
```
"I love how [Name] asked this question - exactly 
the kind of curiosity that makes this community great!"

"Shoutout to everyone helping in the questions 
channel today. This is what we're all about! 🙌"
```

## Part 7: Difficult Decisions

### When to Remove Members

**Removal Criteria**:
- Pattern of violations after warnings
- Severe single violation
- Harmful to other members
- Refuses to follow guidelines
- Damages community culture

**Removal Process**:
1. Review all evidence
2. Consult with team if available
3. Make decision
4. Execute removal
5. Document thoroughly
6. Communicate if necessary

### Appeals Process

**Simple Appeals**:
```
If you believe your [warning/removal] was in error, 
you may appeal by emailing [email].

Please include:
- What happened from your perspective
- Why you believe the decision was incorrect
- What you would do differently

We'll review and respond within [X] days.
```

### When You Made a Mistake

**If You Over-Moderated**:
1. Acknowledge the error
2. Apologize genuinely
3. Reverse the action
4. Learn from it
5. Update processes if needed

## Part 8: Legal Considerations

### Member Agreement

Ensure members agree to terms that include:
- Guidelines compliance
- Removal rights
- Liability limitations
- Content ownership
- Data usage

### Content Issues

**Copyright Violations**:
- Remove infringing content
- Educate member
- Repeat = removal

**Illegal Content**:
- Remove immediately
- Document everything
- Report if required
- Consider legal counsel

### Privacy

**Protect Member Privacy**:
- Don't share reports publicly
- Keep disciplinary actions private
- Handle data responsibly
- Have clear privacy policy

## Part 9: Moderation at Scale

### Scaling Strategies

**0-500 Members**:
- Founder handles moderation
- Guidelines clear and enforced
- Personal touch possible

**500-2000 Members**:
- Add 2-3 moderators
- Create mod handbook
- Regular mod check-ins
- Escalation paths

**2000+ Members**:
- Full moderation team
- Clear hierarchy
- Automation tools
- Regular training
- Community reports dashboard

### Team Communication

**Moderator Channel**:
```
#mod-team (Private)

Purpose:
- Discuss situations
- Request input
- Share updates
- Coordinate coverage

Etiquette:
- Check before acting on ambiguous cases
- Share learnings
- Support each other
```

## Part 10: Mental Health of Moderators

### Moderator Burnout

**Signs**:
- Cynicism toward members
- Quick to punish
- Emotional reactions
- Avoidance
- Complaints about role

**Prevention**:
- Reasonable expectations
- Time limits on mod work
- Emotional support
- Regular breaks
- Appreciation and recognition

### Supporting Your Mod Team

```
Moderator Support Practices:
- Regular check-ins
- Clear boundaries on hours
- Permission to step away
- Recognition and appreciation
- Resources for difficult situations
- Team support structure
```

## Summary

### Moderation Principles

1. **Clarity**: Clear guidelines everyone understands
2. **Consistency**: Same rules for everyone
3. **Compassion**: Assume good intent first
4. **Culture**: Model the behavior you want
5. **Communication**: Explain the why

### Moderation Checklist

```
[ ] Guidelines created and visible
[ ] Moderation team trained
[ ] Escalation process defined
[ ] Documentation system in place
[ ] Appeals process available
[ ] Regular team check-ins scheduled
```

### Moderation Philosophy

```
Prevention > Correction > Removal

Education > Punishment

Private > Public (for discipline)

Quick > Perfect (for harmful content)
```
