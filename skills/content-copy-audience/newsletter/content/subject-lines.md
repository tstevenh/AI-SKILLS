# 100+ Subject Line Formulas That Drive Opens

Your subject line determines whether your email gets opened or ignored. A great subject line can increase opens by 50%+. This guide provides 100+ proven formulas with examples.

---

## Subject Line Fundamentals

### The Open Rate Equation

```
Opens = (Subject Line Appeal × Preview Text Support) × Deliverability × Timing
```

Your subject line is the biggest lever you control.

### Subject Line Benchmarks

| Open Rate | Assessment |
|-----------|------------|
| <20% | Poor - needs work |
| 20-30% | Below average |
| 30-40% | Average |
| 40-50% | Good |
| 50-60% | Excellent |
| >60% | Outstanding |

### The 4 U's of Subject Lines

1. **Useful** - Provides clear value
2. **Urgent** - Creates time sensitivity
3. **Ultra-Specific** - Concrete, not vague
4. **Unique** - Stands out in inbox

## Preview Text Optimization

### What Preview Text Is

The secondary line visible in inbox after subject line.

### Preview Text Best Practices

1. **Extend the subject line** - Continue the thought
2. **Add information** - Context not in subject
3. **Create intrigue** - Second hook
4. **Avoid repetition** - Don't repeat subject

### Preview Text Formulas

**Continuation:**
- Subject: "The secret to 50% open rates"
- Preview: "(and it's not what you think)"

**Addition:**
- Subject: "5 growth tactics"
- Preview: "Plus: the tool I use daily"

**Question:**
- Subject: "Your opens are dropping"
- Preview: "Here's why..."

**Tease:**
- Subject: "Big news"
- Preview: "We've been working on something..."

## A/B Testing Subject Lines

### What to Test

1. Length (short vs. long)
2. Personalization (name vs. no name)
3. Emoji (with vs. without)
4. Question vs. statement
5. Number vs. no number

### Testing Process

1. Create two variants
2. Send to 20% of list each
3. Wait 2-4 hours
4. Send winner to remaining 60%
5. Record results

### Sample Size Guidelines

| List Size | Test Size | Confidence |
|-----------|-----------|------------|
| 5,000 | 500 each | Low |
| 10,000 | 1,000 each | Medium |
| 25,000 | 2,500 each | Good |
| 50,000+ | 5,000 each | High |

## Subject Line Mistakes to Avoid

### Mistake 1: ALL CAPS
- Looks spammy
- Triggers spam filters
- Damages trust

### Mistake 2: Excessive Punctuation
- "URGENT!!!" looks desperate
- Use sparingly if at all

### Mistake 3: Misleading Content
- Subject doesn't match email
- Damages trust permanently

### Mistake 4: Too Long
- Gets cut off on mobile
- Under 50 characters ideal

### Mistake 5: Too Vague
- "Newsletter #47" tells nothing
- "Update" is meaningless

### Mistake 6: Spam Trigger Words
- "FREE!!!", "Act now", "Limited time"
- Use naturally, not manipulatively
