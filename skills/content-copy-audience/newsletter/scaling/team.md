# Building Your Newsletter Team: Complete Hiring Guide

At some point, you can't do it all yourself. This guide covers when to hire, who to hire first, and how to build a team that scales.

---

## When to Hire

### Signs You're Ready

**Time Signals:**
- Working 50+ hours on newsletter alone
- Missing deadlines or quality slipping
- No time for growth activities
- Burning out

**Financial Signals:**
- Revenue can support contractor ($500-2k/month)
- Predictable income stream
- Clear ROI on hired help
- Budget cushion exists

**Growth Signals:**
- Subscriber growth stalling
- Opportunities being missed
- Can't execute growth ideas
- Reader requests going unanswered

### When NOT to Hire

**Hold off if:**
- Revenue unpredictable
- Haven't systematized workflows
- Unclear on what to delegate
- Hiring to solve unclear problems

### The Rule of Thumb

**Hire when:**
```
Time saved × Your hourly value > Hire cost + Management time
```

**Example:**
- Task takes you 10 hours/week
- Your effective hourly: $100
- Value of that time: $1,000/week
- Contractor cost: $500/week
- Management: 2 hours × $100 = $200/week
- Net gain: $1,000 - $500 - $200 = $300/week ✓

## First Hires Roadmap

### Common First Hire Paths

**Path 1: Writer/Editor First**
- You focus on growth and business
- Hire handles content production
- Good if you're better at business than writing

**Path 2: Growth/Operations First**
- You focus on content creation
- Hire handles growth, sponsors, operations
- Good if you're best at content

**Path 3: Virtual Assistant First**
- Handles administrative tasks
- Cheapest option to start
- Frees up significant time

### First Hire by Revenue

| Monthly Revenue | First Hire | Budget |
|----------------|-----------|--------|
| $2-5k | VA (part-time) | $500-1k |
| $5-10k | Writer or VA | $1-2k |
| $10-20k | Writer + VA | $2-4k |
| $20-50k | Full team member | $4-8k |
| $50k+ | Multiple specialists | $10k+ |

## Hiring Process

### Step 1: Define the Role

**Write a Job Description:**
```
[Role Title]

About [Newsletter]:
[Brief description of newsletter and audience]

The Role:
[What this person will do]

Responsibilities:
• [Responsibility 1]
• [Responsibility 2]
• [Responsibility 3]

Requirements:
• [Skill 1]
• [Skill 2]
• [Experience requirement]

Nice to Have:
• [Optional skill]
• [Optional experience]

Compensation: [Range or structure]

How to Apply: [Instructions]
```

### Step 2: Source Candidates

**Where to Post:**
- LinkedIn
- Newsletter-specific job boards
- Twitter/X
- Industry communities
- Your own newsletter
- Referrals (always best)

### Step 3: Screen Applications

**Review for:**
- Relevant experience
- Writing quality (for writers)
- Results shown (for growth roles)
- Attention to detail
- Cultural fit signals

**Red Flags:**
- Generic application
- Spelling/grammar errors
- No relevant experience
- Over-promises results
- Poor communication

### Step 4: Test Project

**For Writers:**
- Paid test article
- Edit existing content
- Create content brief

**For Growth:**
- Growth strategy for your newsletter
- Campaign plan
- Analysis of current performance

**For Operations:**
- Process documentation
- Problem-solving exercise
- Tool/system walkthrough

### Step 5: Interview

**Questions to Ask:**

**All Roles:**
- Walk me through your experience with [relevant area]
- What's your approach to [key responsibility]?
- Tell me about a time you [faced relevant challenge]
- What would you do in [specific scenario]?
- What questions do you have about this role?

**Writers:**
- How do you approach learning a new subject?
- Show me something you're proud of writing
- How do you handle feedback/edits?

**Growth:**
- What would you do in first 30 days?
- Walk me through a campaign you ran
- How do you prioritize growth tactics?

### Step 6: Onboarding

**First Week:**
- Access to all tools/systems
- Documentation review
- 1:1 meetings daily
- First small tasks

**First Month:**
- Increasing responsibility
- Regular feedback
- Adjust expectations as needed
- Check cultural fit

## Managing Contractors and Team

### Communication Systems

**Recommended Tools:**
| Function | Tool |
|----------|------|
| Daily communication | Slack |
| Task management | Notion, Asana |
| Documentation | Notion, Google Docs |
| Meetings | Zoom |
| File sharing | Google Drive, Dropbox |

### Meeting Cadence

**Solo Creator + 1-2 Contractors:**
- Weekly sync (30 min)
- Async daily updates
- Ad-hoc as needed

**Small Team (3-5):**
- Weekly team meeting (1 hour)
- 1:1s bi-weekly (30 min)
- Daily standup (async or 15 min)

### Feedback and Development

**Regular Feedback:**
- Weekly check-ins on work
- Monthly performance discussion
- Quarterly goal review
- Annual planning

**Give Feedback:**
- Specific and timely
- Balanced (positive + constructive)
- Focused on behavior/output
- Actionable suggestions

### Documentation

**Document Everything:**
- Processes and workflows
- Style guides
- Templates
- Decision history
- Lessons learned

**Why:**
- Enables delegation
- Maintains consistency
- Onboards new people faster
- Creates institutional knowledge

**Best Practices:**
1. Clear brief every time
2. Payment terms upfront

4. Regular feedback

**Contract Essentials:**
- Scope of work
- Deliverables

- Payment terms
- Rights/ownership
- Termination clause

|-----------|-------------|

## Team Scaling Stages

### Stage 1: Solo ($0-5k/month revenue)

**Team:** Just you
**Focus:** Establish content and find fit
**Leverage:** Templates, automation, batch creation

### Stage 2: Solo + Support ($5-15k/month)

**Team:** You + 1 contractor
**Common Setup:**
- Writer (you write, they assist)
- OR VA (you focus on content)

### Stage 3: Core Team ($15-50k/month)

**Team:** You + 2-3 people
**Common Setup:**
- Writer/Editor
- Growth or Operations
- VA/Administrative

### Stage 4: Full Team ($50k+/month)

**Team:** 4-8 people
**Common Setup:**
- Managing Editor
- 1-2 Writers
- Growth Lead
- Sponsor Sales
- Operations
- You: Strategy and vision

## Common Mistakes

### Mistake 1: Hiring Too Early

**Problem:** Spend money before revenue supports it
**Solution:** Wait until clear ROI, start with contractors

### Mistake 2: Hiring Too Late

**Problem:** Burnout, missed opportunities
**Solution:** Plan ahead, hire before you're desperate

### Mistake 3: Poor Role Definition

**Problem:** Unclear expectations, missed deliverables
**Solution:** Detailed job descriptions and success metrics

### Mistake 4: Not Documenting

**Problem:** Everything lives in your head
**Solution:** Document processes before hiring

### Mistake 5: Micromanaging

**Problem:** Still doing everything, frustrating team
**Solution:** Hire people you trust, give them autonomy

### Mistake 6: Under-Communicating

**Problem:** Team doesn't know priorities or feedback
**Solution:** Regular check-ins, clear feedback
