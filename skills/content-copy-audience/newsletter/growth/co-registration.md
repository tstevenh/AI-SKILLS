# Co-Registration: Complete Guide to Partner Acquisition

Co-registration is when subscribers opt-in to multiple newsletters at once. It can be a cost-effective growth channel, but quality requires careful management.

---

## What Is Co-Registration?

### Definition

Co-registration (co-reg) happens when a subscriber signs up for one newsletter and simultaneously opts into receiving your newsletter.

**How It Works:**
```
User visits Partner's site
    ↓
Subscribes to Partner's newsletter
    ↓
Thank you page shows: "You may also like..."
    ↓
Checkboxes for partner newsletters (including yours)
    ↓
User selects additional newsletters
    ↓
User receives your welcome email
```

### Co-Reg vs. Other Acquisition

| Method | CPA | Quality | Control |
|--------|-----|---------|---------|
| Co-registration | $0.25-1.50 | Variable | Low |
| Newsletter ads | $1.50-4.00 | High | Medium |
| Referral | $0.50-2.00 | Highest | Medium |
| Paid social | $2.00-5.00 | Medium | High |

## Types of Co-Registration

### Type 1: Thank You Page Recommendations

**How It Works:**
After someone subscribes to a partner newsletter, they see recommendations for related newsletters on the thank you page.

**Example:**
```
Thanks for subscribing to Marketing Brew!

You might also like:
☐ TLDR Marketing - Daily marketing news
☐ Ariyh - Research-backed marketing tactics
☐ [Your Newsletter] - [Your value prop]

[Subscribe to selected]
```

**Quality Level:** Medium-High
- User actively chooses
- Intent is clear
- Quality varies by partner

### Type 2: Checkbox During Signup

**How It Works:**
Additional newsletter options appear during the primary signup process.

**Example:**
```
Subscribe to [Partner Newsletter]

Email: [________________]

☐ Also subscribe me to partner newsletters:
  ☐ [Your Newsletter] - [Value prop]
  ☐ [Other Newsletter] - [Value prop]

[Subscribe]
```

**Quality Level:** Medium
- Less intentional than post-signup
- Can feel sneaky
- Often pre-checked (lower quality)

### Type 3: Bundled Recommendations

**How It Works:**
Multiple newsletters promoted together, often through shared landing pages or networks.

**Example:**
```
"The Best Newsletters for Marketers"

Subscribe to receive:
☐ Marketing Brew
☐ TLDR Marketing
☐ [Your Newsletter]
[... more options]

[Subscribe to all selected]
```

**Quality Level:** Variable
- Depends on bundle positioning
- Context matters
- Can be high quality if curated

### Type 4: Co-Registration Networks

**How It Works:**
Automated networks matching newsletters with publishers who have thank you page real estate.

**Examples:**
- SparkLoop Upscribe (built into referral product)
- Letterhead (newsletter recommendations)
- Publisher-specific programs

**Quality Level:** Variable
- Network handles matching
- Quality controls vary
- Can be hands-off

## Co-Registration Economics

### Pricing Models

**Cost Per Subscriber (CPS):**
Most common model—pay for each subscriber acquired.

| Quality Tier | Typical CPS |
|-------------|-------------|
| Low (pre-checked, broad) | $0.25-0.50 |
| Medium (opt-in, related) | $0.50-1.00 |
| High (curated, engaged) | $1.00-2.00 |

**Revenue Share:**
Some partners charge % of subscriber value.

**Flat Fee:**
Fixed cost for placement period.

### Quality vs. Cost Tradeoff

```
                Higher Cost
                    │
    ┌───────────────┼───────────────┐
    │               │               │
    │   Sweet Spot  │    Premium    │
    │   (Target)    │   Partners    │
    │               │               │
    ├───────────────┼───────────────┤
    │               │               │
    │  Low Quality  │   Good Value  │
    │   (Avoid)     │   (Test)      │
    │               │               │
    └───────────────┴───────────────┘
                Lower Cost
```

### ROI Calculation

**Simple ROI:**
```
Subscriber LTV - Acquisition Cost = Net Value

Example:
LTV: $3.00
CPS: $0.75
Net Value: $2.25 per subscriber
ROI: 300%
```

**Quality-Adjusted ROI:**
```
(LTV × Quality Factor) - CPS = Adjusted Net Value

Example:
LTV: $3.00
Quality Factor: 0.6 (60% as engaged as organic)
Adjusted LTV: $1.80
CPS: $0.75
Adjusted Net Value: $1.05
```

## Finding Co-Reg Partners

### Direct Partnerships

**Where to Find:**
- Complementary newsletters in your niche
- Newsletters that already cross-promote
- Conference speakers/partners
- Industry associations

**Outreach Template:**
```
Subject: Cross-promotion partnership?

Hi [Name],

I run [Your Newsletter], a [description] with [X] subscribers.

I noticed our audiences seem complementary - your focus on [topic] aligns well with our readers interested in [related topic].

I wanted to explore a co-registration partnership where we recommend each other to new subscribers.

Here's what I'm thinking:
- We add you to our thank you page
- You add us to yours
- Track results for [time period]
- Optimize based on performance

Interested in exploring this?

Best,
[Your name]
```

### Co-Reg Networks

**SparkLoop:**
- Built into referral program
- Upscribe feature for acquiring
- Partner program for earning
- Quality controls

**Letterhead:**
- Recommendation network
- Curated partnerships
- Editorial focus

**Custom Networks:**
- Industry-specific
- Publication groups
- Media company networks

### Evaluating Partners

**Good Partner Signals:**
- Similar audience demographics
- High engagement rates
- Quality content
- Transparent practices
- Good reputation

**Red Flags:**
- Very cheap pricing (<$0.25)
- No quality controls
- Pre-checked boxes
- Unrelated content
- No verification process

## Quality Management

### The Quality Problem

Co-reg subscribers are often lower quality because:
- Less intentional signup
- May not remember choosing
- Divided attention during signup
- Lower engagement expectations

### Quality Benchmarks

**Compare to organic subscribers:**

| Metric | Organic | Good Co-Reg | Poor Co-Reg |
|--------|---------|-------------|-------------|
| 7-day open rate | 60% | 40-50% | <30% |
| 30-day open rate | 50% | 30-40% | <20% |
| 90-day retention | 85% | 70-80% | <60% |
| Spam complaints | 0.01% | 0.05% | >0.1% |

### Quality Controls

**Before Partnering:**
1. Review partner's signup flow
2. Check how your newsletter is presented
3. Verify opt-in process (no pre-checked)
4. Understand their audience

**Ongoing:**
1. Track quality per source
2. Compare engagement rates
3. Monitor spam complaints
4. Review retention

**Disqualify If:**
- Engagement <50% of organic
- Spam complaints increase
- High bounce rates
- Can't verify opt-in

### Source Tagging

**Tag every subscriber by source:**
```
Tags:
- source: coreg
- partner: [partner_name]
- date: [acquisition_date]
```

**Track per source:**
- Open rates
- Click rates
- Unsubscribes
- Spam complaints
- Revenue (if monetizing)

## Running a Co-Reg Program

### As an Acquirer

**Setup:**
1. Identify potential partners
2. Negotiate terms
3. Provide creative/copy
4. Set up tracking
5. Monitor quality

**Management:**
1. Weekly quality review
2. Monthly partner assessment
3. Scale good partners
4. Cut underperformers

**Scaling:**
1. Add new partners
2. Increase budget with winners
3. Test new networks
4. Optimize conversion points

### As a Publisher (Earning from Co-Reg)

**Setup:**
1. Add recommendation section to thank you page
2. Partner with complementary newsletters
3. Set pricing
4. Implement tracking

**Optimization:**
1. Test recommendation copy
2. A/B test positioning
3. Rotate partners
4. Track conversion rates

**Revenue Potential:**
```
1,000 new subscribers/month
× 30% select additional newsletters
× $0.50 per selection
= $150/month passive

At scale (10,000/month):
$1,500/month from recommendations
```

## Best Practices

### For Acquirers

**Do:**
- Start small with each partner
- Track quality religiously
- Build direct relationships
- Pay for quality, not volume

**Don't:**
- Prioritize cheap over quality
- Ignore engagement data
- Scale before validating
- Work with sketchy partners

### For Publishers

**Do:**
- Only recommend newsletters you'd read
- Be transparent with audience
- Rotate to stay fresh
- Track conversions for partners

**Don't:**
- Pre-check boxes
- Recommend irrelevant content
- Overload with options
- Sacrifice trust for money

## Co-Reg Compliance

### Legal Requirements

**CAN-SPAM:**
- Clear opt-in required
- Can't send without consent
- Unsubscribe must work

**GDPR (if applicable):**
- Explicit consent needed
- Clear what they're signing up for
- Easy to withdraw

### Best Practices

1. Clear labeling of partner signups
2. Unchecked boxes by default
3. Transparent value proposition
4. Immediate welcome email
5. Easy unsubscribe

---

## Co-Reg vs. Alternatives

### When to Use Co-Reg

**Good for:**
- Testing new audiences
- Cost-efficient growth
- Supplementing other channels
- Passive publisher revenue

**Not good for:**
- Primary growth strategy
- Building engaged audience
- High-value monetization
- Quality-sensitive lists

### Recommended Mix

```
Growth Channel Mix:

Organic/SEO: 30-40%
Referral: 20-30%
Paid (social, ads): 20-30%
Co-Reg: 5-15%
Other: 5-10%
```

**Co-reg should supplement, not replace, quality channels.**
