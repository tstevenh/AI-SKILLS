# Substack: Complete Platform Guide

Substack pioneered the modern newsletter renaissance and remains the dominant platform for writers focused on paid subscriptions. Its unique network effects and writer-first philosophy make it a distinct choice.

---

## Platform Overview

### What Substack Is

Substack is a newsletter platform designed for:
- Writers prioritizing paid subscriptions
- Independent journalists and commentators
- Creators seeking audience discovery
- Publishers wanting minimal tech friction

### History and Positioning

Founded in 2017 by Chris Best, Hamish McKenzie, and Jairaj Sethi, Substack launched with a simple premise: let writers earn from subscriptions with zero upfront cost. The platform grew explosively during 2020-2021, attracting high-profile writers from traditional media.

**Key Philosophy:**
- Writers keep most of the money
- Simplicity over features
- Network discovery drives growth
- Subscription-first model

### The Substack Ecosystem

Unlike other platforms, Substack is a network, not just a tool:
- **Substack App:** Reader app with recommendations
- **Notes:** Twitter-like social features
- **Recommendations:** Cross-promotion system
- **Leaderboards:** Category rankings
- **Discovery:** Algorithmic and curated

## Pricing Model

### How Substack Makes Money

**Revenue Model:**
- Free to start and use
- Substack takes 10% of paid subscription revenue
- Stripe processing fees additional (~2.9% + 30¢)
- Total creator take: ~86-87% of subscription revenue

**No fees on:**
- Free newsletters
- Advertising revenue
- Affiliate revenue
- External product sales

### Cost Comparison

| Revenue | Substack Take | Stripe | Net to Creator |
|---------|--------------|--------|----------------|
| $1,000/mo | $100 | ~$35 | ~$865 |
| $5,000/mo | $500 | ~$175 | ~$4,325 |
| $10,000/mo | $1,000 | ~$350 | ~$8,650 |
| $50,000/mo | $5,000 | ~$1,750 | ~$43,250 |

### Substack vs. Beehiiv Economics

| Revenue | Substack Net | Beehiiv Net (Scale) |
|---------|--------------|---------------------|
| $1,000/mo | $865 | $965 + $43 plan = $922 |
| $5,000/mo | $4,325 | $4,825 + $43 = $4,782 |
| $10,000/mo | $8,650 | $9,650 + $43 = $9,607 |
| $50,000/mo | $43,250 | $48,250 + $43 = $48,207 |

**Key Insight:** At higher revenue, Substack's 10% becomes significant. A $50k/mo newsletter loses $5,000/mo to Substack that would be retained on Beehiiv.

## Paid Subscription Features

### Setting Up Paid

**Pricing Options:**
- Monthly: Typically $5-15/month
- Annual: Typically $50-150/year
- Founding: Custom tier for supporters

**Recommended Pricing:**
| Category | Monthly | Annual |
|----------|---------|--------|
| Personal/lifestyle | $5 | $50 |
| Professional/business | $10-15 | $100-150 |
| Premium analysis | $15-30 | $150-300 |

### Free vs. Paid Content

**Common Approaches:**

**1. Some Free, Some Paid**
- Weekly free post (growth)
- 1-2 additional paid posts
- Most common approach

**2. Free Preview, Paid Full**
- Every post starts free
- Full content behind paywall
- Reader can sample

**3. All Paid, Free Archive**
- All new posts paid
- Old posts eventually free
- Evergreen strategy

**4. Paywalled Sections**
- Main content free
- Bonus sections paid
- Analysis, Q&A, etc.

### Founding Members

**What It Is:** Premium tier for biggest supporters

**Features:**
- Higher price point ($100-500+/year)
- Special recognition
- Additional perks (calls, community)
- Supporter messaging

**Best Practices:**
- Offer meaningful differentiation
- Personal touch (thank you notes)
- Exclusive content or access
- Limited availability creates urgency

## Network Effects and Discovery

### The Substack Network Advantage

Substack's unique value proposition is network-driven discovery.

**Discovery Channels:**
1. **Recommendations** - Other writers recommend you
2. **Substack App** - Algorithm suggests publications
3. **Notes** - Social layer for visibility
4. **Leaderboards** - Category rankings
5. **Staff picks** - Curated by Substack team
6. **Search** - In-platform search

### Recommendations

**How It Works:**
1. Writers recommend other Substacks
2. Shown to new subscribers after signup
3. Reciprocal relationship building
4. Algorithmic matching

**Best Practices:**
- Recommend genuinely good writers
- Ask writers you admire for recommendations
- Check your recommendation analytics
- Rotate recommendations based on performance

**Typical Impact:**
- 5-20% of new subscribers from recommendations
- Quality varies by recommending publication size
- Reciprocity generally expected

### Substack Notes

**What It Is:** Twitter-like social layer on Substack

**Features:**
- Short-form posts
- Reposts
- Comments
- Following
- Notifications

**Growth Strategy:**
- Share excerpts from newsletters
- Engage with other writers
- Build relationships
- Drive to subscription

**Effectiveness:**
- Growing in importance
- Less effective than Twitter for now
- Best for Substack-native audience
- Worth using, not relying on

### The Leaderboards

**Categories:**
- Overall
- Culture
- Politics
- Technology
- Business
- Faith
- Sports
- Many more

**How Ranking Works:**
- Based on subscriber count and engagement
- Paid subscribers weighted more heavily
- Exact algorithm not public
- Updated regularly

**Impact:**
- Top positions drive discovery
- Category leadership builds credibility
- Competitive dynamic

## Migration Considerations

### To Substack

**Pros:**
- Easy setup
- Network effects immediately
- Simple migration

**Cons:**
- Lose advanced features
- Limited customization
- 10% ongoing cost

### From Substack

**Common Reasons:**
- Want to keep more revenue
- Need advanced features
- Want better growth tools
- Customization requirements

**Migration Process:**
1. Export subscriber list from Substack
2. Export content archive
3. Import to new platform
4. Set up redirects for SEO
5. Announce move to subscribers
6. Handle paid subscriber migration

**Paid Subscriber Migration:**
- Can migrate Stripe subscriptions
- Some manual work required
- Risk of churn during transition
- Communicate clearly with paid subs

## Substack Tips and Best Practices

### Quick Wins

1. **Ask for recommendations immediately** - Network effect is free
2. **Enable Notes** - Additional visibility channel
3. **Set competitive pricing** - Research your category
4. **Engage in comments** - Builds retention
5. **Cross-promote** - Leverage the ecosystem

### Growth Tactics

1. **Recommendation swaps** - Ask writers directly
2. **Notes engagement** - Comment on popular writers
3. **Guest posts** - Write for larger Substacks
4. **External promotion** - Twitter/X, LinkedIn
5. **Consistent publishing** - Algorithm rewards regularity

### Maximizing Paid Conversions

1. **Strategic free vs. paid balance** - Hook then paywall
2. **Preview paid content** - Show what they're missing
3. **Annual incentive** - Discount for commitment
4. **Founding member tier** - For superfans
5. **Launch promotion** - Time-limited offers

### Common Mistakes

1. **All content paid** - Need free for growth
2. **Ignoring Notes** - Free distribution channel
3. **Not asking for recommendations** - Leaving growth on table
4. **Underpricing** - Substack audience pays premium
5. **Irregular publishing** - Consistency matters
