# Milk Road Growth Breakdown: 0 to 250K in 10 Months

Milk Road is the fastest-growing newsletter case study in history. Shaan Puri and Ben Levy built a crypto newsletter to 250,000 subscribers in under a year, then sold it. Here's exactly how they did it.

---

| Growth rate | ~25,000/month average |

## The Growth Strategy

### Phase 1: Launch with Audience (Month 1)

**The Unfair Advantage:**
Shaan promoted Milk Road to his existing audience:
- 500K+ Twitter followers
- My First Million podcast listeners
- Previous newsletter subscribers

**Launch Numbers:**
- 25,000 subscribers in first week
- 50,000 by end of month 1
- Entirely from existing audience

**Lesson:** Starting with an audience compresses years of growth into days.

**Strategy:** Pour money into paid growth while crypto was hot

**Channels:**

|---------|-----------|-----|
| SparkLoop/Newsletter ads | Heavy | $1-2 |
| Twitter ads | Moderate | $2-3 |
| Podcast sponsorships | Heavy | $3-5 |
| Cross-promotions | Free | $0 |

**Monthly Growth:**
- Month 2: 75,000 total
- Month 3: 100,000 total
- Month 4: 125,000 total
- Month 5: 150,000 total
- Month 6: 175,000 total

### Phase 3: Referral Acceleration (Months 4-10)

**Referral Program:**
Implemented aggressive referral rewards:

| Referrals | Reward |
|-----------|--------|
| 1 | Exclusive content |
| 3 | Milk Road merch |
| 5 | Premium newsletter access |
| 10 | Crypto NFT |
| 25 | Major reward |

**Referral Impact:**
- 20-30% of growth from referrals
- Lower blended CAC
- Higher quality subscribers

### Phase 4: Content Virality (Ongoing)

**Viral Content Strategy:**
- Create shareable, meme-worthy content
- Screenshot-worthy sections
- Twitter-native formatting
- Controversy and hot takes

**Social Amplification:**
- Shaan shared every issue
- Readers shared screenshots
- Twitter threads about content
- Natural viral loops

## Content Strategy

### The Milk Road Format

**Daily Email Structure:**
1. **Hook** - Attention-grabbing opening
2. **Top Stories** - 3-4 crypto news items
3. **Deep Dive** - One analysis piece
4. **Quick Hits** - Brief news items
5. **Fun Section** - Memes, jokes
6. **Sponsor** - Native integration

### Tone and Voice

**Characteristics:**
- Irreverent and fun
- Meme-heavy
- Accessible to crypto beginners
- Strong opinions
- Cultural references

**Sample Opening:**
"GM. The SEC just did something dumb again. Shocking, we know. But this time it actually matters for your bags. Let's dive in."

### Differentiation

**What Made It Different:**
- NOT trying to be serious
- Embraced crypto culture
- Made complex topics simple
- Entertainment first, information second

## Monetization

### Sponsor Revenue

**Crypto Sponsor Demand:**
During the bull market, crypto companies had massive marketing budgets:
- Exchanges (FTX, Crypto.com)
- DeFi protocols
- NFT projects
- Web3 tools

**Pricing:**
- CPMs: $50-100 (premium crypto audience)
- Primary sponsor: $10,000-25,000 per send
- Secondary: $5,000-10,000

**Monthly Revenue (estimated at scale):**
- 250k subs × 50% open × $75 CPM = ~$9,375 per send
- 5 sends/week = ~$46,875/week
- Monthly: ~$187,500

### Why Sponsors Paid Premium

1. **Hard-to-reach audience** - Crypto-interested but not crypto-native
2. **High engagement** - 50%+ open rates
3. **Buying intent** - Audience actively investing
4. **Trust** - Shaan's reputation transferred

## What Made Milk Road Different

### 1. Founder Audience Leverage

Most newsletters start from zero. Milk Road started with 500K+ potential readers from Shaan's audience.

**Replicable?** Only if you have audience. Otherwise, build audience first.

### 2. Perfect Timing

Launched during crypto boom when:
- Audience interest at peak
- Sponsor budgets unlimited
- Competition still building
- Growth channels cheap

**Replicable?** Timing can't be planned. Be ready when opportunity appears.

### 3. Operations Excellence

Ben's Morning Brew experience meant:
- No learning curve
- Optimized from day one
- Right tools and processes
- Efficient execution

**Replicable?** Yes, if you study and implement best practices.

### 4. Aggressive Capital Deployment

Didn't wait to "figure out" paid growth:
- Spent heavily early
- Accepted short-term losses
- Optimized while scaling
- Reinvested revenue

**Replicable?** Yes, with capital and confidence in unit economics.

### 5. Brand and Voice

The Milk Road brand was immediately distinctive:
- Memorable name
- Irreverent tone
- Visual identity
- Cultural fit with audience

**Replicable?** Yes, with intentional brand development.

---

## Lessons for Newsletter Operators

### Lesson 1: Audience Accelerates Everything

If you don't have audience, build it first (Twitter, podcast, YouTube). This is the biggest growth unlock.

### Lesson 2: Timing Matters

Look for emerging niches with:
- Growing audience interest
- Sponsor demand
- Low competition
- Clear monetization

### Lesson 3: Spend Money to Make Money

If unit economics work, scale aggressively. Many newsletters stay small because they won't invest.

### Lesson 4: Voice Creates Moats

A distinctive voice is hard to copy. Invest in developing one.

### Lesson 5: Consider Exit from Day One

Milk Road was built to sell:
- Clean metrics
- Scalable systems
- Diversified revenue
- Strategic value

## What You Can Replicate

### Without Shaan's Audience

1. **Build audience first** - 6-12 months on Twitter/podcast
2. **Start small** - Prove concept before scaling
3. **Partner with influencers** - Guest posts, cross-promos
4. **Use paid growth** - SparkLoop, newsletter ads

### The Playbook

1. Find emerging niche with sponsor demand
2. Build or borrow initial audience
3. Create distinctive brand and voice
4. Implement referral program immediately
5. Deploy capital aggressively on paid
6. Optimize for engagement and retention
7. Build for exit optionality

| Factor | Milk Road | Typical Newsletter |
