# Key Metrics: What to Track and Why

## Overview

Analytics transform guesswork into strategy. By understanding what metrics matter and what they tell you, you can systematically improve your short-form video performance.

## Primary Metrics

### Views

**What It Measures:**
Total number of times your video was watched (platform-specific definitions vary).

**What It Tells You:**
- Reach of your content
- Algorithm distribution
- Topic/hook appeal at scale

**Benchmarks:**
- Views relative to your follower count
- Views compared to your average
- Views compared to niche benchmarks

**Caveats:**
- High views don't always mean quality
- Different platforms count views differently
- Views alone don't indicate engagement

### Watch Time / Average View Duration

**What It Measures:**
How long people watch before leaving or scrolling.

**What It Tells You:**
- Content retention quality
- Hook effectiveness
- Pacing appropriateness
- Overall engagement

**Benchmarks:**
- 50%+ average view duration: Good
- 70%+ average view duration: Excellent
- 90%+ average view duration: Exceptional

**This Is THE Most Important Metric:**
Watch time directly impacts algorithmic distribution on all platforms. A video with fewer views but higher retention will often outperform a video with more views but lower retention.

### Completion Rate

**What It Measures:**
Percentage of viewers who watch to the end.

**What It Tells You:**
- Content quality throughout
- Appropriate length
- Ending strength

**Benchmarks:**
- 30%+: Acceptable
- 50%+: Good
- 70%+: Excellent

**Optimizing Completion:**
- Tighten content (remove filler)
- Improve hooks (stop early drop-off)
- Create anticipation for ending
- Make videos loop-friendly

### Engagement Rate

**What It Measures:**
Total engagements (likes, comments, shares, saves) divided by views or reach.

**Formula:**
`(Likes + Comments + Shares + Saves) / Views × 100`

**What It Tells You:**
- Content resonance
- Audience connection
- Viral potential

**Benchmarks by Platform:**
- TikTok: 5-10% is good, 15%+ is excellent
- Instagram Reels: 3-6% is good, 10%+ is excellent
- YouTube Shorts: 3-5% is good, 8%+ is excellent

## Secondary Metrics (Weighted by Algorithm Value)

### Shares

**What It Measures:**
How many times viewers shared your content.

**What It Tells You:**
- Content worth recommending
- Highest form of endorsement
- Strong algorithmic signal

**Why Shares Matter Most:**
Shares indicate viewers found content valuable enough to put their own reputation behind it by recommending to others.

### Saves

**What It Measures:**
How many viewers saved/bookmarked your content.

**What It Tells You:**
- Reference value
- Intent to return
- Strong algorithmic signal (especially for educational content)

**Content Types with High Saves:**
- Tutorials
- Recipes
- Tips and hacks
- Templates
- Reference guides

### Comments

**What It Measures:**
Number of comments on your video.

**What It Tells You:**
- Conversation stimulation
- Emotional engagement
- Community building

**Quality vs. Quantity:**
More isn't always better. Quality conversations > spam comments.

**Comment Sentiment:**
Positive comments are better signals than negative comments, even if total volume is lower.

### Likes

**What It Measures:**
Viewers who tapped like/heart.

**What It Tells You:**
- Basic approval signal
- Lowest-effort engagement
- Weakest algorithmic signal

**Reality:**
Likes are the easiest engagement action and therefore weighted less heavily by algorithms than shares, saves, or comments.

## Conversion Metrics

### Profile Visits

**What It Measures:**
Viewers who visited your profile after watching.

**What It Tells You:**
- Interest in more content
- Potential followers
- Content driving curiosity

**Optimizing:**
- Clear value proposition in content
- Mention "more on my page" type CTAs
- Consistent content theme

### Follower Growth

**What It Measures:**
New followers gained.

**What It Tells You:**
- Content converting viewers to followers
- Sustainable audience building
- Long-term growth trajectory

**Track:**
- Followers per video
- Follower conversion rate (followers gained ÷ views)
- Net followers (gained - lost)

### Link Clicks (Where Available)

**What It Measures:**
Clicks on links in bio or content.

**What It Tells You:**
- Content driving action
- Funnel effectiveness
- External conversion potential

## Analytics Interpretation

### Reading Retention Curves

**Ideal Curve:**
Flat with slight decline—viewers watching consistently through the video.

**Hook Problem:**
Sharp drop in first 3-5 seconds—hook isn't working.

**Content Problem:**
Gradual steep decline—content isn't holding attention.

**Ending Problem:**
Drop-off before end—video is too long or ending isn't compelling.

**Loop Spike:**
Uptick at end (from loops)—content is being rewatched.

### Correlation Analysis

**High Views + Low Engagement:**
- Hook is working, content isn't
- Topic has interest, execution is weak
- Possibly inauthentic or clickbait

**Low Views + High Engagement:**
- Content is great, distribution is limited
- Niche audience loves it
- Keep making similar content

**High Views + High Engagement:**
- Winning content
- Study and replicate
- Double down on this format/topic

**Low Views + Low Engagement:**
- Content or topic isn't resonating
- Consider different approach
- Don't replicate

## Setting Up Tracking

### Weekly Tracking Template

Create a spreadsheet with:

|------|----------|-------------|-------|-----------------|-----------------|--------|-------|----------|---------------|

### What to Compare

**To Your Average:**
Is this video performing above or below your normal?

**To Similar Content:**
How does this compare to videos on similar topics?

**To Timing:**
Did posting time affect performance?

**To Format:**
Do certain formats consistently perform better?

### Analysis Frequency

**Daily:**
- Quick check on new video performance
- Note any viral or underperforming content

**Weekly:**
- Compare all videos from the week
- Identify patterns
- Adjust next week's strategy

**Monthly:**
- Full analytics review
- Content audit
- Strategy adjustment

**Quarterly:**
- Deep analysis
- Goal setting
- Major strategy decisions

## Common Analytics Mistakes

### Mistake 1: Only Looking at Views
Views without context are meaningless. A video with 10K views and 80% completion rate is often better than one with 50K views and 20% completion.

### Mistake 2: Comparing Across Niches
Different niches have different benchmarks. Compare yourself to similar creators, not random viral content.

### Mistake 3: Short Time Horizons
One video's performance isn't a trend. Look at patterns over 10+ videos.

### Mistake 4: Ignoring Underperformers
Failed content teaches as much as successes. Understand why things don't work.

### Mistake 5: Vanity Metrics Focus
Likes and views are less important than saves, shares, and conversion to followers.

## Actionable Analytics Framework

### When a Video Outperforms

1. **Identify why**
   - Hook style?
   - Topic?
   - Format?
   - Timing?
   
2. **Document learnings**
   - Add to successful templates
   - Note in content planning
   
3. **Replicate strategically**
   - Create similar content
   - Don't copy exactly
   - Apply lessons to other topics

### When a Video Underperforms

1. **Identify why**
   - Hook failure (check retention curve start)
   - Content failure (check middle retention)
   - Topic/niche mismatch
   
2. **Document learnings**
   - Add to "avoid" list
   - Note patterns in failures
   
3. **Adjust approach**
   - Don't repeat same mistakes
   - Test alternatives

## Summary: Metrics That Matter Most

### For Algorithm Performance
1. Watch time / completion rate
2. Shares
3. Saves
4. Comments
5. Likes

### For Growth
1. Follower conversion rate
2. Profile visits
3. Net follower growth

### For Monetization
1. Engagement rate (brand deals)
2. Link clicks (conversions)
3. Views on branded content

**Remember:** Analytics inform strategy, but don't let numbers kill creativity. The best content often comes from intuition and experimentation, with analytics helping refine the approach over time.
