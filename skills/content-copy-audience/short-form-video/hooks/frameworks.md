# Hook Frameworks: 10 Proven Structures That Stop the Scroll

## Introduction to Hook Frameworks

A hook framework is a reusable structure that can be filled with different content to create attention-grabbing openings. Rather than inventing a new hook from scratch for every video, master these frameworks and adapt them to your specific topic.

Each framework below includes:
- The psychological principle behind why it works
- The structure explained
- Examples across different niches
- When to use it
- Tips for maximum effectiveness

---

## Framework 1: The Question Hook

### Structure
Open with a question that your target audience desperately wants answered.

### Why It Works
Questions create an "open loop" in the brain. Once a question is posed, the mind seeks resolution. This drives continued viewing to find the answer.

Questions also serve as relevance filters—the right question immediately tells your target audience "this is for you."

### Formula
"Have you ever wondered [curious thing]?"
"Why does [frustrating thing] happen?"
"What would you do if [scenario]?"
"Do you know why [unexpected fact]?"

### Examples by Niche

**Personal Finance:**
- "Why do rich people always say to invest in real estate?"
- "Have you ever wondered why your savings account is basically worthless?"
- "What would you do with an extra $1,000 a month?"

**Fitness:**
- "Why do some people get abs and others just get frustrated?"
- "Have you ever wondered why you're not seeing results?"
- "What if I told you you're doing squats wrong?"

**Business:**
- "Why do 90% of businesses fail in the first year?"
- "Have you ever wondered how some people make 6 figures from home?"
- "What separates successful entrepreneurs from everyone else?"

**Cooking:**
- "Why does restaurant pasta always taste better than homemade?"
- "Have you ever wondered why your cookies turn out flat?"
- "What if I told you you've been cooking rice wrong?"

### When to Use
- Educational content
- Myth-busting content
- Tutorial content
- Problem-solving content

### Tips for Maximum Effectiveness
- Ask questions your audience actually has (not ones you made up)
- The question should feel urgent or important
- Follow with the answer relatively quickly
- Don't over-tease—deliver on the promise

## Framework 2: The Bold Claim Hook

### Structure
Open with a surprising, specific statement that challenges conventional wisdom or promises an exceptional outcome.

### Why It Works
Bold claims trigger a mixture of curiosity and skepticism. The viewer wants to see if you can back it up, and stays to find out. Specificity adds credibility—vague claims feel like hype.

### Formula
"[Specific action] changed my entire [outcome]."
"This is the [best/worst/only] way to [achieve result]."
"I made [specific number] doing [thing]."
"[X] is a complete scam, and here's proof."

### Examples by Niche

**Personal Finance:**
- "I paid off $50,000 in debt in 18 months with this one strategy."
- "This investing mistake cost me $27,000."
- "The stock market is designed to take your money."

**Fitness:**
- "I lost 30 pounds eating more than I ever have."
- "This 10-minute routine replaced my entire gym workout."
- "Everything you've been told about cardio is wrong."

**Business:**
- "I built a 6-figure business working 4 hours a day."
- "Cold email is the most underrated marketing channel."
- "Your morning routine is why you're not successful."

**Productivity:**
- "This app added 2 hours to my day."
- "I quit social media for 30 days and here's what happened."
- "The to-do list is killing your productivity."

### When to Use
- When you have genuinely impressive results
- Myth-busting content
- Controversial takes
- Transformation stories

### Tips for Maximum Effectiveness
- Be specific (numbers, timeframes, metrics)
- Only make claims you can actually support
- Be prepared for skepticism (address it in the video)
- Don't overuse—loses impact

## Framework 3: The Controversy Hook

### Structure
Open with an opinion or statement that goes against popular belief or stirs debate.

### Why It Works
Controversy activates tribal psychology. People either feel validated ("finally someone said it!") or challenged ("that's wrong and I'll prove it"). Either reaction is engagement.

### Formula
"Unpopular opinion: [contrarian take]"
"I don't care what anyone says, [strong position]"
"[Common advice] is terrible advice, and here's why"
"I'm going to get hate for this, but..."

### Examples by Niche

**Personal Finance:**
- "Unpopular opinion: buying a house is financially stupid for most people."
- "I don't care what anyone says—the 4% rule is outdated."
- "Budgeting is a waste of time."

**Fitness:**
- "Unpopular opinion: you don't need to track calories to lose weight."
- "Cardio is the worst way to lose fat."
- "I'm going to get hate for this, but protein is overrated."

**Career:**
- "Unpopular opinion: college degrees are becoming worthless."
- "Your dream job doesn't exist."
- "Hustle culture isn't toxic—you're just lazy."

**Relationships:**
- "Unpopular opinion: staying friends with your ex is a red flag."
- "Compatibility matters more than chemistry."
- "Stop listening to relationship advice from single people."

### When to Use
- When you have a genuinely held contrarian belief
- To spark discussion/comments
- To establish thought leadership
- To attract like-minded people (and filter others)

### Tips for Maximum Effectiveness
- Actually believe what you're saying (authenticity shows)
- Be prepared to defend your position
- Don't be contrarian just for controversy
- Have evidence or logic to support your take
- Don't punch down or attack people

## Framework 4: The Story Hook

### Structure
Open in the middle of a compelling story that creates immediate dramatic tension.

### Why It Works
Humans are wired for narrative. When a story begins, the brain enters "narrative transportation"—a state where we lose awareness of our surroundings and become absorbed in the story. Starting in the middle (in medias res) skips exposition and drops viewers into action.

### Formula
"I was [normal situation] when [unexpected thing happened]"
"Last week something happened that changed everything"
"So there I was, [dramatic situation]"
"The day I [life event], everything changed"

### Examples by Niche

**Personal Finance:**
- "I was checking my bank account when I noticed $15,000 was missing."
- "Last Tuesday, I quit my 6-figure job without a backup plan."
- "So there I was, $100,000 in debt, staring at a termination letter."

**Business:**
- "I was about to close my biggest deal when I made a mistake."
- "Six months ago, my business was dying. I had 30 days to save it."
- "My business partner called me at 2 AM with news that changed everything."

**Lifestyle:**
- "I was on a normal Tuesday morning run when I found it."
- "Last month I moved across the country without knowing anyone."
- "So there I was, in a foreign country, and my phone just died."

**Fitness:**
- "I stepped on the scale and couldn't believe the number."
- "The doctor told me something I'll never forget."
- "Six months ago, I couldn't walk up stairs without getting winded."

### When to Use
- Personal stories and experiences
- Transformation content
- Cautionary tales
- Inspiration content
- Case studies

### Tips for Maximum Effectiveness
- Start at a moment of tension, not the beginning
- Make the stakes clear immediately
- Create curiosity about what happens next
- Keep the story relevant to your main content
- Resolve the story satisfyingly

## Framework 5: The Demonstration Hook

### Structure
Open by immediately showing a result, process, or demonstration that visually captures interest.

### Why It Works
"Show, don't tell" is powerful because visual processing is faster than verbal. A compelling demonstration answers "what am I going to learn?" before the viewer can form the question.

### Formula
[Show result] "Here's how I did it"
[Show process] "Watch this"
[Show transformation] "Before vs. after"
[Show action] "This is what happens when you..."

### Examples by Niche

**Cooking:**
- [Showing finished dish] "This took 15 minutes to make"
- [Showing technique] "Watch what happens when you add this"
- [Showing result] "This is the fluffiest pancake I've ever made"

**Fitness:**
- [Showing exercise] "This one move targets your entire core"
- [Showing transformation] "6 months. Same person."
- [Showing technique] "See the difference in my form?"

**Beauty:**
- [Showing finished look] "5 minutes for this look"
- [Showing product] "Watch what this does to my skin"
- [Showing before/after] "Same day. Same lighting."

**DIY/Crafts:**
- [Showing finished product] "I made this for $20"
- [Showing process] "Watch how easy this is"
- [Showing transformation] "I found this on the side of the road"

### When to Use
- Tutorial content
- Before/after content
- Product demonstrations
- How-to content
- Results-focused content

### Tips for Maximum Effectiveness
- Show the most impressive visual first
- Make the demonstration clear and visible
- Use close-ups for detail work
- Keep movement in frame
- Let the visual speak before excessive explanation

## Framework 6: The List Hook

### Structure
Open by promising a specific number of items, tips, mistakes, or things.

### Why It Works
Lists satisfy the brain's desire for organization and completeness. A numbered list creates a contract—viewers know exactly what they're getting and can anticipate completion. Lists also imply that someone has done the curation work for you.

### Formula
"[Number] things you need to know about [topic]"
"[Number] mistakes you're making with [thing]"
"[Number] signs that [situation]"
"[Number] reasons why [claim]"

### Examples by Niche

**Personal Finance:**
- "5 money mistakes that keep people poor"
- "3 things rich people never buy"
- "7 signs you'll be wealthy"

**Fitness:**
- "4 exercises you're doing wrong"
- "3 things killing your gains"
- "5 signs you need a rest day"

**Career:**
- "6 red flags in a job interview"
- "3 things successful people do before 7 AM"
- "5 habits that got me promoted twice"

**Relationships:**
- "4 signs they're not the one"
- "3 things you should never say to your partner"
- "5 green flags that relationship will last"

### When to Use
- Educational content
- Tip compilations
- Advice content
- Comparison content

### Tips for Maximum Effectiveness
- Odd numbers often perform better (psychological research supports this)
- Keep numbers manageable (3-7 optimal for short-form)
- Make sure each item delivers value
- Consider numbering on screen for tracking
- The first item should be your strongest or most surprising

## Framework 7: The Mistake/Myth Hook

### Structure
Open by calling out a common mistake or widely-believed myth that your audience probably makes or believes.

### Why It Works
Nobody wants to be doing something wrong. This hook triggers mild anxiety—"Am I making this mistake?"—which drives viewing to find out. It also positions you as an authority who knows better.

### Formula
"You're probably [wrong action] wrong"
"Stop doing [common behavior]"
"The myth of [common belief] needs to die"
"Everyone thinks [thing] but actually..."

### Examples by Niche

**Personal Finance:**
- "You're probably wasting money on insurance you don't need"
- "Stop paying off your smallest debts first"
- "The myth of 'good debt' needs to die"

**Fitness:**
- "You're probably doing push-ups wrong"
- "Stop stretching before your workout"
- "The myth of spot reduction needs to die"

**Business:**
- "You're probably using LinkedIn completely wrong"
- "Stop focusing on your weaknesses"
- "The myth of 'passion' is ruining careers"

**Productivity:**
- "You're probably using to-do lists wrong"
- "Stop trying to be productive in the morning"
- "The myth of multitasking needs to die"

### When to Use
- Educational content
- Myth-busting
- Authority positioning
- Course/product leads

### Tips for Maximum Effectiveness
- Target a real, common mistake (not a strawman)
- Show you understand why people make the mistake
- Provide the correct alternative
- Be helpful, not condescending
- Back claims with evidence

## Framework 8: The Relatable Hook

### Structure
Open with a scenario, feeling, or experience that your target audience deeply relates to.

### Why It Works
When viewers see themselves in content, they feel understood. This builds instant rapport and trust. Relatable hooks also trigger the response "yes, that's me!" which is a form of engagement.

### Formula
"POV: You're [relatable situation]"
"When you [relatable behavior] and [relatable outcome]"
"Tell me you [characteristic] without telling me you [characteristic]"
"Me when [relatable scenario]"

### Examples by Niche

**Work/Career:**
- "POV: You're pretending to work while your boss walks by"
- "When you have 4 meetings and still need to do your actual job"
- "Me calculating how many vacation days I need to survive"

**Fitness:**
- "POV: You're at the gym and every machine you need is taken"
- "When you skip leg day but tell yourself you'll go tomorrow"
- "Me convincing myself one cheat meal won't hurt"

**Parenting:**
- "POV: You told your kids you have to leave in 5 minutes"
- "When you finally get the kids to sleep and remember you haven't eaten"
- "Me pretending to look for the toy I actually threw away"

**Adulting:**
- "POV: You're making a doctor's appointment for the first time as an adult"
- "When you have to do something your parents always did for you"
- "Me asking my mom how to cook a basic meal"

### When to Use
- Comedy/entertainment content
- Community building
- Lifestyle content
- Building trust before educating

### Tips for Maximum Effectiveness
- Know your audience deeply
- Be specific (specificity creates relatability)
- Don't try to be relatable to everyone
- Time the humor well
- Can lead into educational content

## Framework 9: The Transformation Hook

### Structure
Open by immediately showing or describing a dramatic before and after—whether yours or someone else's.

### Why It Works
Transformations are visual proof of possibility. They trigger aspiration ("I want that") and curiosity ("how did they do that?"). The bigger and more believable the transformation, the stronger the hook.

### Formula
"[Time period]. Same person."
"I went from [before state] to [after state]"
"This is what [action] for [time period] looks like"
"Before vs. after [specific thing]"

### Examples by Niche

**Fitness:**
- "365 days. Same person."
- "I went from 280 to 180 pounds in one year"
- "This is what working out every day for a year looks like"

**Business:**
- "$0 to $10,000/month. Same business."
- "I went from cubicle to CEO in 3 years"
- "This is what consistent content creation for 2 years looks like"

**Home/DIY:**
- "Same room. Same budget. Big difference."
- "I transformed this $5,000 house"
- "This is what $200 can do to a space"

**Skills:**
- "1 year of learning [skill]. Day 1 vs. Day 365."
- "I practiced for 30 days straight. Here's the progress."
- "Same drawing prompt, 5 years apart"

### When to Use
- Transformation/progress content
- Before/after reveals
- Motivational content
- Product/service results

### Tips for Maximum Effectiveness
- Show both states clearly
- Use similar lighting/conditions for authenticity
- Include the timeframe
- Be honest about the journey
- Follow with how it was achieved

## Framework 10: The Behind-the-Scenes Hook

### Structure
Open with an insider look at something normally hidden—a process, a secret, or exclusive access.

### Why It Works
Humans are naturally curious about what's hidden from them. Behind-the-scenes content satisfies the desire to see "how things really work" and creates a sense of exclusivity for the viewer.

### Formula
"What [thing] looks like behind the scenes"
"I'll show you something most people never see"
"Here's what actually happens when [process]"
"The reality of [thing everyone thinks they know about]"

### Examples by Niche

**Business:**
- "What a 7-figure business looks like behind the scenes"
- "I'll show you what my inbox actually looks like"
- "The reality of being your own boss"

**Food/Restaurant:**
- "What actually happens in a restaurant kitchen"
- "I'll show you how this dish is really made"
- "The reality of working in the food industry"

**Content Creation:**
- "What making content actually looks like"
- "I'll show you the 100 takes before the final video"
- "The reality of being a full-time creator"

**Industry:**
- "What most people don't know about [industry]"
- "I'll show you what really happens at [company type]"
- "Here's what they don't teach you in [field]"

### When to Use
- Transparency content
- Industry insight
- Day-in-the-life content
- Building trust and authenticity

### Tips for Maximum Effectiveness
- Show something genuinely interesting
- Be authentic (polished BTS defeats the purpose)
- Add context for why this is worth knowing
- Balance vulnerability with value
- Don't reveal anything damaging

## Combining Frameworks

The most effective hooks often combine multiple frameworks:

**Question + Bold Claim:**
"Why did this simple change add $1,000 to my monthly income?"

**Story + Transformation:**
"I was $100,000 in debt. Here's the same person three years later."

**Mistake + List:**
"5 money mistakes I made that kept me broke for years"

**Controversy + Demonstration:**
"Everyone says this doesn't work. Watch me prove them wrong."

**Relatable + Behind-the-Scenes:**
"POV: You're watching what working from home actually looks like"

## Choosing the Right Framework

Match framework to content type:

| Content Type | Best Frameworks |
|-------------|-----------------|
| Tutorial | Demonstration, List, Mistake |
| Story | Story, Transformation, BTS |
| Entertainment | Relatable, Story, Demonstration |
| Controversy | Controversy, Bold Claim, Mistake |
| Education | Question, List, Mistake, Bold Claim |
| Motivation | Transformation, Story, Bold Claim |
| Product | Demonstration, Transformation, BTS |

## Building Your Hook Library

1. **Save hooks that stop YOU**: Build a swipe file
2. **Categorize by framework**: Identify which framework each uses
3. **Test systematically**: Try different frameworks for similar content
4. **Track performance**: Note which frameworks work for your audience
5. **Iterate**: Refine and combine based on data
