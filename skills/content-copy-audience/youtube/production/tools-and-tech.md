## Camera and Video

### Smartphone Filming

**Why smartphones work**:
- 4K video standard on modern phones
- Excellent auto-focus
- Good low-light (recent models)
- Always with you
- Improving rapidly

**Smartphone tips**:
- Clean the lens (seriously)
- Use back camera (better quality)
- Lock focus and exposure
- Use 4K 30fps
- Horizontal orientation for YouTube
- Stabilize (tripod or gimbal)

### Dedicated Cameras

**Webcams**:
- Best for: Streaming, quick videos
- Options: Logitech C920 ($70), Logitech Brio ($200)
- Limitation: Quality ceiling

**Compact cameras**:
- Best for: Vlogging, general use
- Options: Sony ZV-1 ($650), Canon G7X ($600)
- Benefit: All-in-one, portable

**Mirrorless cameras**:
- Best for: Professional quality
- Options: Sony A6400 ($900), Canon M50 ($600), Sony A7C ($1,800)
- Benefit: Interchangeable lenses, best quality

### Camera Settings for YouTube

| Setting | Recommended |
|---------|-------------|
| Resolution | 4K (downscale to 1080p in edit) |
| Frame rate | 24fps (cinematic) or 30fps (standard) |
| Shutter speed | 1/50 at 24fps, 1/60 at 30fps |
| ISO | As low as possible |
| Aperture | f/2.8-f/4 (background blur) |
| Picture profile | Flat/log for flexibility |

## Audio Equipment

### Audio Priority Principle

**Audio quality matters more than video quality.**

Viewers forgive mediocre video but won't tolerate poor audio.

### Microphone Types

**Lavalier (lapel) mics**:
- Clips to clothing
- Good for movement
- Affordable
- Options: Rode SmartLav+ ($80), Rode Wireless Go ($200)

**USB microphones**:
- Direct to computer
- Great for desk setups
- Options: Blue Yeti ($130), Audio-Technica AT2020 ($100), Shure MV7 ($250)

**XLR microphones**:
- Require audio interface
- Professional quality
- Options: Shure SM7B ($400), Rode PodMic ($100), Electro-Voice RE20 ($450)

**Shotgun microphones**:
- Directional, on-camera
- Good for run-and-gun
- Options: Rode VideoMic ($150), Rode NTG ($250)

### Audio Interfaces

For XLR microphones:
- Focusrite Scarlett Solo ($110)
- MOTU M2 ($180)
- Universal Audio Volt 276 ($300)

### Audio Environment

**Room treatment options**:
- Acoustic foam panels ($50-200)
- Moving blankets (cheap hack)
- Portable vocal booth ($100-300)
- Dedicated recording space

**Quick wins**:
- Record away from walls
- Add soft furnishings (rugs, curtains)
- Close windows, stop HVAC
- Get closer to microphone

## Lighting

### Lighting Basics

**Three-point lighting**:
1. **Key light**: Main light source (brightest)
2. **Fill light**: Softens shadows (dimmer)
3. **Back light**: Separates from background

### Lighting Options

**Natural light**:
- Free
- Flattering
- Inconsistent
- Best: Large window, indirect light

**Ring lights**:
- Even, flattering light
- Good for close-up
- Creates ring reflection in eyes
- Options: $30-150 range

**LED panels**:
- Versatile, adjustable
- Color temperature control
- Can use for 2-3 point setup
- Options: Elgato Key Light ($200), Neewer panels ($50-100)

**Softboxes**:
- Traditional video lighting
- Very soft, flattering
- Require space
- Options: $100-300 kits

### Lighting Setup Recommendations

**Beginner (one light)**:
- Large light source at 45° angle
- Position slightly above eye level
- Use reflector or white wall for fill

**Intermediate (two lights)**:
- Key light at 45°
- Fill light at 45° other side (dimmer)
- Control shadows

**Professional (three lights)**:
- Key, fill, and back light
- Background lighting if needed
- Full control over look

## Editing Software

### Free Options

**DaVinci Resolve**:
- Industry professional tool (free version)
- Best free option, period
- Color grading excellence
- Steep learning curve
- Best for: Serious creators willing to learn

**CapCut**:
- Very beginner-friendly
- Mobile and desktop
- Good effects/templates
- Best for: Beginners, Shorts

**iMovie (Mac)**:
- Simple, intuitive
- Limited features
- Best for: Mac beginners

### Paid Options

**Adobe Premiere Pro**:
- Industry standard
- Best integration (After Effects, etc.)
- $22/month (or full Creative Cloud)
- Best for: Professional workflow

**Final Cut Pro (Mac)**:
- One-time purchase ($300)
- Excellent performance on Mac
- Magnetic timeline
- Best for: Mac users wanting pro features

**Filmora**:
- Beginner-friendly
- Affordable ($80/year)
- Good templates/effects
- Best for: Intermediate creators

## Teleprompter Setups

### Why Use a Teleprompter

**Benefits**:
- Natural eye contact with camera
- Consistent delivery
- Faster filming (fewer takes)
- Better scripted content

### Teleprompter Options

**Smartphone apps**:
- Free to cheap
- Use any smartphone
- Apps: PromptSmart, Teleprompter Premium
- Limitation: Small screen, no camera alignment

**Tablet teleprompter rigs**:
- iPad/tablet in prompter housing
- Beam splitter aligns with camera
- $100-400 for rig + tablet
- Good quality, more affordable

**Professional teleprompters**:
- Dedicated teleprompter monitors
- Better brightness and readability
- $500-2000+
- For high-volume creators

### DIY Teleprompter Tips

**Budget option**:
1. Phone/tablet with prompter app
2. Position just below camera (as close as possible)
3. Adjust speech to glance naturally
4. Practice until it's invisible

**Key settings**:
- Font size: Large enough to read easily
- Scroll speed: Match your natural speaking pace
- Margins: Center text, narrow width
- Background: High contrast (white on black or vice versa)

## AI Tools for YouTube

### Content Creation AI

**Script writing**:
- Claude / ChatGPT: Script outlining, hook generation
- Jasper: Marketing copy, descriptions
- Use for: First drafts, ideas, not final content

**Thumbnail generation**:
- Midjourney: Creative concepts
- DALL-E: Quick iterations
- Ideogram: Text-heavy thumbnails
- Use for: Concepts, backgrounds, not final thumbnails

### Editing AI

**Auto-editing**:
- Descript: AI cuts, filler word removal
- Opus Clip: Automatic short clips from long videos
- Vidyo.ai: AI clip extraction

**Audio enhancement**:
- Adobe Podcast: Background noise removal
- Descript: Studio sound effect
- Cleanvoice: Filler word removal

**Captions**:
- YouTube auto-captions (improving)
- Descript: Accurate transcription
- Rev: Professional accuracy

### Research and Analytics AI

**Keyword research**:
- vidIQ AI Coach: Topic suggestions
- TubeBuddy AI: Title optimization
- ChatGPT: Keyword brainstorming

**Trend analysis**:
- Exploding Topics: Trend prediction
- Google Trends + AI interpretation
- Social listening tools

### AI Tool Workflow

**Research phase**:
1. AI for topic brainstorming
2. AI for keyword variations
3. AI for competitive analysis

**Creation phase**:
1. AI for script outline (not final script)
2. AI for description drafts
3. AI for thumbnail concepts

**Post-production phase**:
1. AI for transcription
2. AI for clip extraction (Shorts)
3. AI for audio enhancement

**Always**: Human review and refinement. AI assists, doesn't replace.

## Tool Selection Principles

### Buy vs Free

**Buy when**:
- Free version has critical limitations
- Time savings justify cost
- Tool directly impacts revenue

**Stay free when**:
- Learning/experimenting
- Free version meets needs
- Budget is tight

### Upgrade Path

**Start minimal** → **Upgrade when hitting limits** → **Don't upgrade preemptively**

Better to master free tools than accumulate unused paid subscriptions.

---

*The best tool is the one you'll actually use consistently. Start simple, upgrade when needed.*
