# Digital PR Strategy

## What Is Digital PR?

Digital PR is the practice of earning media coverage and links through newsworthy content, expert positioning, and journalist relationships. Unlike traditional link building (where you pitch for links), digital PR earns links as a byproduct of media coverage.

### Digital PR vs. Traditional Link Building

| Aspect | Digital PR | Traditional Link Building |
|--------|------------|---------------------------|
| Primary goal | Media coverage | Backlinks |
| Approach | Newsworthy stories | Link opportunities |
| Targets | Journalists, publications | Webmasters, bloggers |
| Links earned | Byproduct of coverage | Direct objective |
| Scale | Fewer, higher impact | More, varied impact |
| Timeline | Unpredictable | More predictable |
| Cost | Higher upfront | More predictable |
| Authority | Very high | Variable |

### Why Digital PR Matters for SEO

**Link quality:**
Links from major publications (DR 80+) are extremely valuable. One link from Forbes or TechCrunch can outweigh dozens of standard guest posts.

**Brand authority:**
Media coverage establishes credibility that supports all marketing efforts.

**Natural link profile:**
Editorial links from journalism are exactly what Google wants to see.

**Traffic diversity:**
Major publications drive significant referral traffic.

## Newsjacking

### What Is Newsjacking?

Newsjacking involves inserting your brand or expertise into breaking news stories. When a relevant story breaks, you position yourself as an expert source.

### The Newsjacking Process

**Step 1: Monitor for opportunities**
- Set up Google Alerts for industry keywords
- Follow industry journalists on Twitter
- Monitor news aggregators
- Track competitor mentions

**Step 2: Assess fit**
Questions to answer:
- Is this story relevant to your expertise?
- Can you add genuine value?
- Is the story still developing (not stale)?
- Do you have a unique angle?

**Step 3: Create response content**
- Expert commentary
- Data that adds context
- Contrarian perspective
- Industry insider view

**Step 4: Rapid outreach**
- Contact journalists covering the story
- Offer your expertise
- Respond within hours, not days
- Be available for quick turnaround

### Newsjacking Examples

**Example 1: Industry regulation change**
- News: New privacy regulation announced
- Your company: Data security software
- Angle: Expert analysis of what it means for businesses
- Pitch: "As a data security expert, here's what businesses need to do to comply"

**Example 2: Company crisis**
- News: Major competitor has data breach
- Your company: Competing security product
- Angle: Industry analysis (not attack on competitor)
- Pitch: "Here's what this means for the industry and how businesses can protect themselves"

**Example 3: Industry trend**
- News: Report shows remote work is permanent
- Your company: Remote work software
- Angle: Supporting data from your user base
- Pitch: "Our data from 10,000 companies confirms this trend. Here's what we're seeing..."

### Newsjacking Best Practices

**Speed is critical:**
- News cycle is fast
- Respond within hours
- Have templates ready
- Know your talking points

**Add genuine value:**
- Don't just promote
- Provide real insight
- Share actual data if possible
- Be genuinely helpful

**Stay appropriate:**
- Don't capitalize on tragedies
- Don't attack competitors directly
- Stay in your area of expertise
- Be tasteful

## Data-Driven Content

### The Power of Original Data

Original research and data generate links because:
- Journalists need sources
- Data can't be found elsewhere
- Statistics get cited repeatedly
- Creates ongoing link opportunities

### Types of Data Content

**1. Industry surveys**
Survey your audience or industry:
- "State of [Industry] Report"
- Customer/user behavior studies
- Industry benchmark reports

**2. Internal data analysis**
Analyze your own data:
- Platform usage trends
- Customer behavior patterns
- Product/service insights

**3. Public data aggregation**
Compile and analyze public data:
- Government statistics
- Industry databases
- Scrape-able public information

**4. Original research**
Commission or conduct studies:
- Scientific research
- Consumer studies
- Market analysis

### Creating Linkable Data Content

**Step 1: Identify compelling questions**
- What does your audience wonder about?
- What would journalists want to cite?
- What data doesn't exist yet?

**Step 2: Gather data**
- Survey your audience
- Analyze internal metrics
- Compile public data
- Commission research

**Step 3: Find insights**
- Look for surprising findings
- Identify trends
- Find quotable statistics
- Create visualizations

**Step 4: Package for media**
- Press release with key findings
- Embeddable graphics
- Full report for depth
- Expert quotes for context

**Step 5: Pitch strategically**
- Identify relevant journalists
- Offer exclusive early access
- Provide ready-to-use assets
- Follow up with additional angles

### Data Content Success Metrics

**Leading indicators:**
- Journalist interest
- Media inquiries
- Social shares

**Lagging indicators:**
- Links earned
- Brand mentions
- Referral traffic
- Secondary citations (links to coverage)

## Expert Commentary

### Positioning as an Expert Source

Journalists constantly need expert quotes. Position yourself as a go-to source.

**Building expert positioning:**

1. **Define your expertise**
   - Specific niche focus
   - Demonstrable experience
   - Unique perspective

2. **Create thought leadership content**
   - Blog posts with strong opinions
   - LinkedIn articles
   - Industry presentations
   - Podcast appearances

3. **Make yourself findable**
   - Optimize LinkedIn for expertise
   - Create an "As Seen In" page
   - List yourself in expert directories
   - Register with HARO and alternatives

4. **Be reliably available**
   - Respond quickly to journalists
   - Provide quotable soundbites
   - Meet deadlines without fail
   - Build relationships over time

### Responding to Expert Requests

**When a journalist reaches out:**

**Do:**
- Respond within hours
- Provide clear, quotable answers
- Offer additional context
- Be available for follow-up
- Say if you can't help (and refer someone)

**Don't:**
- Delay response
- Give vague answers
- Promote excessively
- Overcommit and miss deadline
- Ghost after initial response

### Building Journalist Relationships

**Initial contact:**
- Respond helpfully to requests
- No ask in first interactions
- Provide value freely

**Ongoing relationship:**
- Follow their work
- Share helpful information
- Congratulate on good pieces
- Be a reliable source

**Long-term:**
- Become a go-to for your topic
- Get proactive outreach from journalists
- Contribute to stories before publication

## Awards and Rankings

### Why Awards Matter

Awards generate coverage and links:
- Award announcements are newsworthy
- Rankings pages link to winners
- PR opportunities around awards
- Social proof for other coverage

### Types of Awards

**Industry awards:**
- "Best [Product Category]"
- Industry association awards
- Peer-nominated awards

**Business awards:**
- Inc. 5000 / Fast Growth lists
- "Best Places to Work"
- Regional business awards

**Expertise awards:**
- "Top [Role] Under 40"
- Industry influencer lists
- Thought leader rankings

### Award Strategy

**1. Identify relevant awards**
- Search "[your industry] awards"
- Check competitor PR (what did they win?)
- Industry publications' annual lists
- Regional business awards

**2. Prioritize applications**
- Link value of award/listing
- Brand value
- Application effort
- Cost (some require fees)

**3. Prepare strong applications**
- Gather supporting data
- Collect testimonials
- Create compelling narrative
- Meet all requirements

**4. Maximize wins**
- Press release for wins
- Social media announcement
- Add to website/email signature
- Include in future PR pitches

### Award Application Tips

**Stand out:**
- Lead with impressive metrics
- Use specific numbers
- Include brief customer quotes
- Keep it scannable

**Follow instructions:**
- Read criteria carefully
- Submit all required materials
- Meet deadlines
- Answer what's asked (not what you want to say)

## Building a Digital PR Program

### Team and Skills

**Required skills:**
- PR and media relations
- Content creation
- Data analysis (for data content)
- Outreach and relationship building

**Team structure options:**
- In-house PR person/team
- PR agency
- Freelance PR specialist
- Hybrid approach

### Annual PR Calendar

**Quarterly focus areas:**

**Q1:**
- Year-in-review data content
- Industry predictions
- Award applications (many due Q1)

**Q2:**
- Original research publication
- Conference season PR
- Mid-year trend pieces

**Q3:**
- Seasonal content for your industry
- "Best of" list positioning
- Back-to-school/business angles

**Q4:**
- Year-end roundups
- Holiday-related angles
- Planning for next year's campaigns

### Budget Considerations

**DIY digital PR:**
- Time cost only
- Requires skills and relationships
- Slower to build momentum

**Agency digital PR:**
- $3,000-15,000+/month
- Established journalist relationships
- Faster results potential
- Not guaranteed

**Hybrid approach:**
- Project-based agency work
- In-house for ongoing efforts
- Best for growing companies

## Measuring Digital PR Success

### Link Metrics

- Links earned (quantity)
- DR/DA of linking sites
- Referral traffic from links
- Secondary links (links to coverage)

### Brand Metrics

- Media mentions (linked and unlinked)
- Share of voice vs. competitors
- Brand search volume
- Social mentions

### ROI Calculation

**Value of PR links:**
Traditional method: What would these links cost to build otherwise?

Example:
- 5 links from DR70+ publications
- Estimated cost to build equivalent: $4,000-8,000
- PR campaign cost: $6,000
- Additional value: Brand exposure, referral traffic

## Summary

Digital PR builds links through media coverage:

1. **Newsjacking** - Insert expertise into breaking stories
2. **Data content** - Create original, citable research
3. **Expert positioning** - Become a go-to source
4. **Awards** - Earn recognition and links

Digital PR links are:
- Highest authority (DR 70+)
- Most natural (editorial)
- Best for brand building
- Less predictable than other tactics

For comprehensive link building, digital PR complements guest posting and niche edits at the top of the quality pyramid.
