# Guest Post Content Creation

## Content That Gets Published and Drives Results

Guest post content serves dual purposes: it must satisfy the publisher's editorial standards AND advance your link building goals. This chapter covers how to create content that achieves both.

## Content Guidelines: Understanding Publisher Expectations

### Standard Publisher Requirements

Most quality sites have similar baseline expectations:

**Length:**
- Minimum: 800-1,000 words
- Standard: 1,500-2,000 words
- Premium: 2,500+ words

**Format:**
- Clear headings (H2, H3)
- Short paragraphs (2-4 sentences)
- Bullet points and lists
- Images where appropriate

**Quality:**
- Original content (not published elsewhere)
- Grammatically correct
- Well-researched
- Actionable insights

**Restrictions:**
- No promotional language
- Limited self-references
- No competitor disparagement
- No duplicate content

### Reading Guidelines Carefully

Before writing, read (and follow) the site's guidelines:

**Check for:**
- Word count requirements
- Formatting preferences
- Topic restrictions
- Link policies
- Image requirements
- Author bio format
- Submission process

**Red flags in guidelines:**
- "We do not include links" (why bother?)
- "All links are nofollow" (lower value)
- "No promotional content" (be subtle)

**Negotiate before writing:**
If guidelines conflict with your goals (e.g., no links in body), clarify before investing in content.

### Adapting to Site Voice

Quality sites want content that fits their style:

**Research their voice:**
- Read 5-10 recent articles
- Note tone (formal vs casual)
- Observe sentence structure
- Check use of first/second person
- Note formatting conventions

**Match their style:**
- Use similar vocabulary
- Mirror their paragraph length
- Follow their heading conventions
- Match their use of examples
- Adopt similar imagery

## Quality Standards

### The Quality Hierarchy

**Level 1: Acceptable**
- Meets basic requirements
- Grammatically correct
- Provides some value
- Gets published

**Level 2: Good**
- Well-researched
- Unique insights
- Engaging writing
- Publisher is happy to publish

**Level 3: Excellent**
- Genuinely valuable content
- Publisher excited to share
- Generates engagement
- Earns additional links

**Level 4: Exceptional**
- Best content on the topic
- Publisher promotes actively
- Goes viral/gets picked up
- Creates lasting relationship

**Aim for Level 2-3 consistently.** Level 4 is expensive but valuable for key targets.

### Content Quality Checklist

Before submission, verify:

**Research quality:**
✓ Facts are accurate
✓ Statistics have sources
✓ Claims are supported
✓ Examples are relevant

**Writing quality:**
✓ Clear and concise
✓ No grammar/spelling errors
✓ Flows logically
✓ Engaging throughout

**Structure quality:**
✓ Compelling introduction
✓ Clear section headings
✓ Logical progression
✓ Strong conclusion

**Value quality:**
✓ Reader learns something
✓ Actionable takeaways
✓ Not just common knowledge
✓ Worth reading

### What Makes Content Genuinely Valuable

**Unique insights:**
- Original research or data
- Expert perspective from experience
- Novel framework or approach
- Counter-intuitive conclusions

**Practical application:**
- Step-by-step instructions
- Templates or tools
- Real examples
- Checklists readers can use

**Depth:**
- Goes beyond surface level
- Addresses edge cases
- Anticipates questions
- Comprehensive coverage

**Freshness:**
- Current information
- Addresses recent changes
- Up-to-date examples
- New angle on old topics

## Anchor Text Strategy

### The Anchor Text Balance

Your anchor text must appear natural while supporting your SEO goals.

**The golden rule:** No more than 5-10% exact match anchors across your profile.

### Anchor Text Distribution in Guest Posts

**For a typical campaign (10 guest posts):**

| Anchor Type | Count | Examples |
|-------------|-------|----------|
| Branded | 4-5 | "[Company Name]" |
| Naked URL | 2-3 | "https://example.com" |
| Generic | 1-2 | "click here," "this resource" |
| Partial match | 1-2 | "helpful guide to [keyword]" |
| Exact match | 0-1 | "[exact target keyword]" |

### Natural Anchor Text Examples

**Branded anchors:**
- "According to [Company Name]..."
- "...as explained by [Company Name]"
- "The team at [Company Name] suggests..."

**Naked URL:**
- "Learn more at https://example.com"
- "Source: https://example.com/data"

**Generic anchors:**
- "Learn more about this here"
- "Check out this resource"
- "Read the full guide"
- "This comprehensive article"

**Partial match:**
- "This guide to email marketing"
- "A helpful project management resource"
- "The team's approach to content strategy"

**Exact match (use sparingly):**
- "The best CRM software for small business"
- Only when contextually perfect

### Contextual Integration

Links should flow naturally within content:

**Bad integration:**
"Email marketing is important. [Exact Match Anchor] is the best choice."

**Good integration:**
"When selecting an email marketing platform, consider factors like automation capabilities and deliverability rates. [This comprehensive guide to email marketing tools] breaks down the key differences."

### Anchor Text Red Flags

**Avoid:**
- Multiple exact match anchors in one post
- Forced, unnatural link placements
- Link at start of article (looks planted)
- Link with no surrounding context
- Over-optimized anchors ("best cheap affordable...")

## Internal Linking in Guest Posts

### The Opportunity

Guest posts can include internal links to the publisher's content—a value-add that:
- Makes your post more useful
- Shows you know their content
- Increases acceptance chances
- Builds publisher relationship

### How to Internal Link

**Step 1: Research their content**
Before writing, identify 3-5 relevant articles on their site.

**Step 2: Note linking opportunities**
As you outline, mark where you can reference their content.

**Step 3: Integrate naturally**
Link where genuinely relevant:
- "As [Site Name] covered in [their article]..."
- "For more on this topic, see [their article]"
- "This builds on concepts explained in [their article]"

### Strategic Benefits

**For the publisher:**
- Keeps readers on site longer
- Signals you understand their content
- Makes their job easier
- Shows collaborative intent

**For you:**
- Increases acceptance rate
- Positions your link among quality links
- Demonstrates professionalism
- Builds relationship

## Author Bio Optimization

### The Author Bio Real Estate

Your bio is guaranteed link placement. Optimize it.

**Standard bio structure:**
1. Name and title
2. Brief credential/expertise
3. Company mention with link
4. Optional: Social media

**Example bio:**
"[Name] is the [Title] at [Company Name](link), where [brief description of what company does/what you do]. When not [professional activity], [personal touch]. Connect on [Twitter/LinkedIn](link)."

### Bio Link Strategy

**Primary link:** Your target page or homepage
**Secondary link:** Social profile or other relevant page

**When allowed one link:**
- Link to most valuable target page
- Or link to homepage if brand building

**When allowed multiple links:**
- Primary: Target page
- Secondary: Social or "about" page
- Tertiary: Another valuable page

### Bio Anchor Text

Bio links are typically branded, which is fine:
- Company name
- Your name
- "Company Name's website"

**Don't:**
- Try to stuff keywords in bio
- Make bio links look unnatural
- Include promotional language

### Bio Writing Tips

**Be credible:**
- Mention relevant experience
- Include credentials if impressive
- Reference known publications

**Be human:**
- Brief personal detail
- Not just corporate speak
- Personality appropriate to site

**Be brief:**
- 50-100 words typically
- Longer bios may be cut
- Get key info first

## Content Creation Workflow

### Step 1: Topic Development

**Brainstorm based on:**
- Publisher's existing content
- Gaps in their coverage
- Your expertise
- Audience needs

**Validate topic:**
- Search volume (is there interest?)
- Competition (can you add value?)
- Relevance (fits the site?)
- Link opportunity (where can you link?)

### Step 2: Outline Creation

**Standard outline structure:**

```
# Title (with hook)

## Introduction (150-200 words)
- Hook
- Context/problem
- What reader will learn

## Section 1 (300-400 words)
- Key point
- Supporting evidence
- Practical application
[Link opportunity]

## Section 2 (300-400 words)
- Key point
- Supporting evidence
- Practical application

## Section 3 (300-400 words)
- Key point
- Supporting evidence
- Practical application
[Link opportunity]

## Section 4 (300-400 words)
- Key point
- Supporting evidence
- Practical application

## Conclusion (150-200 words)
- Summary
- Key takeaway
- Call to action

## Author Bio
```

### Step 3: Research and Writing

**Research phase:**
- Gather sources and data
- Find examples and case studies
- Identify expert quotes
- Note statistics to include

**Writing phase:**
- Follow outline structure
- Write in site voice
- Include evidence for claims
- Integrate links naturally

**First draft goal:** Complete, not perfect

### Step 4: Editing and Polish

**Self-editing checklist:**
- Read aloud for flow
- Check grammar/spelling
- Verify facts and links
- Ensure guidelines met
- Reduce word count (tighten)

**Quality check:**
- Would you share this?
- Does it provide real value?
- Is it better than existing content?
- Are links natural?

### Step 5: Formatting for Submission

**Standard format:**
- Google Doc (preferred by most)
- Clean formatting
- Include images with credits
- Author bio at end
- Links already embedded

**Submission package:**
- Content document
- Featured image (if required)
- Author headshot (if required)
- Short bio (if separate)

## Working with Writers

### When to Write vs. Outsource

**Write yourself when:**
- Establishing expertise/voice
- Key relationships
- Complex topics requiring deep knowledge
- Highest-tier placements

**Outsource when:**
- Scaling operations
- Standard placements
- Topics with clear requirements
- Time constraints

### Finding Quality Writers

**Sources:**
- Content agencies
- Freelance platforms (Upwork, Contently)
- Writer networks
- Referrals from other SEOs

**Vetting process:**
1. Review portfolio/samples
2. Assign paid test article
3. Evaluate against standards
4. Provide detailed feedback
5. Decide on ongoing relationship

### Writer Briefing Template

Provide writers with:

```
PROJECT BRIEF

Target Site: [Site name and URL]
Site Voice: [Notes on style]
Target Audience: [Who reads this site]
Guidelines URL: [Link to their guidelines]

Article Topic: [Working title]
Target Keywords: [If any]
Word Count: [Minimum-maximum]

Link Requirements:
- Our link: [URL and preferred anchor]
- Internal links: [Include 2-3 to their content]

Outline: [Detailed outline]

Reference Content:
- [Similar article on their site]
- [Example of desired quality]

Deadline: [Date]
Revision Policy: [Details]
```

### Quality Control for Outsourced Content

**Before submission:**
1. Read entire piece
2. Verify against guidelines
3. Check link integration
4. Ensure brand/keyword alignment
5. Edit for quality issues

**Never submit without review.**

## Handling Revisions

### Common Revision Requests

**Content issues:**
- "Need more depth" → Add examples, data
- "Too promotional" → Soften language
- "Doesn't fit our audience" → Refocus angle
- "Missing key points" → Expand sections

**Structural issues:**
- "Too long/short" → Adjust accordingly
- "Needs better flow" → Reorganize
- "More subheadings" → Break up text

**Link issues:**
- "Remove product mention" → Reframe link context
- "Change anchor text" → Select alternative
- "Links don't fit" → Find better placement

### Handling Revision Requests Gracefully

**Do:**
- Respond promptly
- Make requested changes
- Maintain quality standard
- Ask clarifying questions if needed
- Thank them for feedback

**Don't:**
- Argue or push back (unless egregious)
- Delay response
- Make partial changes
- Submit lower quality revision

**Remember:** Relationship matters more than any single article.

## Summary

Quality content is the foundation of successful guest posting:

1. **Understand guidelines** - Read and follow publisher requirements

4. **Add internal links** - Value-add for publishers
5. **Polish bios** - Maximize guaranteed link opportunity
6. **Systematize workflow** - Consistent process for consistent results
7. **Handle revisions well** - Professional relationship management
