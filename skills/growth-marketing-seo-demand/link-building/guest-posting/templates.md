# Guest Post Email Templates (20+)

## How to Use These Templates

These templates are starting points, not copy-paste solutions. For best results:

1. **Personalize** the opening with specific references to their site
2. **Adapt** the pitch to your actual topic and expertise
3. **Match** the tone to the site's style
4. **Test** different approaches and measure results

**Variables to replace:**
- `[Site Name]` - Their website name
- `[Name]` - Editor's name (or "Hi there" if unknown)
- `[Topic]` - Your proposed topic
- `[Title]` - Your proposed article title
- `[Your Name]` - Your name
- `[Company]` - Your company name
- `[Publication]` - Sites you've written for
- `[Credential]` - Your relevant expertise

## Initial Pitch Templates

### Template 1: Classic Professional Pitch

**Subject:** Guest post contribution for [Site Name]

```
Hi [Name],

I've been following [Site Name] for a while and really appreciate your coverage of [topic area]. Your recent article on [specific article] was particularly insightful.

I'd love to contribute a guest post on:

"[Proposed Title]"

This piece would cover:
• [Point 1]
• [Point 2]
• [Point 3]

The article would be around [word count] words with actionable takeaways your readers can implement immediately.

A bit about me: I'm [brief credential] with [X years] experience in [relevant field]. My work has been featured in [Publication 1] and [Publication 2].

Would this topic be of interest? I'm happy to send a detailed outline or adjust the angle based on your feedback.

Best regards,
[Your Name]
[Title] at [Company]
```

## Quick Tips for Template Success

1. **Personalize the first sentence** - Always reference something specific
2. **Keep it short** - Under 200 words for initial pitch
3. **One clear ask** - Don't confuse with multiple requests
4. **Make it easy** - Reduce friction to say yes
5. **Professional signature** - Name, title, company
6. **Proofread** - Errors kill credibility
7. **Follow up** - But not more than 3-4 times
8. **Track results** - Know what works for your audience
