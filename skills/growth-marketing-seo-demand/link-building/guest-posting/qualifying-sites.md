# Qualifying Guest Post Sites

## The Quality Control Checkpoint

Finding prospects is only half the battle. Qualifying them—determining which sites are actually worth pursuing—separates successful link builders from those who waste time and money on worthless placements.

This chapter provides a systematic framework for evaluating guest post opportunities, from quick initial filters to deep due diligence.

## Quick Filter: The 30-Second Test

Before investing time in deep evaluation, apply these instant filters:

### Filter 1: Domain Rating (DR) / Domain Authority (DA)

**Check:** Ahrefs DR or Moz DA

**Minimum thresholds:**
- Standard campaigns: DR 25+
- Quality campaigns: DR 40+
- Premium campaigns: DR 55+

**Pass/Fail:** If below your threshold, skip immediately.

### Filter 2: Organic Traffic

**Check:** Ahrefs or SEMrush organic traffic estimate

**Minimum thresholds:**
- Standard campaigns: 500+ monthly organic visitors
- Quality campaigns: 5,000+ monthly organic visitors
- Premium campaigns: 20,000+ monthly organic visitors

**Pass/Fail:** Low traffic with high DR is a red flag—investigate or skip.

### Filter 3: Language and Geography

**Check:** Site language and target audience location

**Considerations:**
- Is content in your target language?
- Is traffic from relevant countries?
- Does audience match your target market?

**Pass/Fail:** Skip irrelevant language/geo unless specifically targeting that market.

### Filter 4: Obvious Spam Signals

**Quick visual check for:**
- Casino/gambling content
- Pharma/drug content
- Adult content
- Obvious PBN characteristics
- No real content (just placeholder posts)
- Every post is a "guest post" or "sponsored"

**Pass/Fail:** Any obvious spam = immediate skip.

### The 30-Second Checklist

✓ DR above threshold?
✓ Traffic validates DR?
✓ Language appropriate?
✓ No obvious spam?

**If all yes:** Proceed to deep evaluation
**If any no:** Skip and move to next prospect

## DR/DA Threshold Guidelines

### Understanding What DR Means

Domain Rating measures backlink authority, not quality. A site can have high DR from:
- Genuine editorial links
- PBN manipulation
- Expired domain purchase
- Link schemes

**Therefore, DR alone is insufficient.**

### Setting Appropriate Thresholds

**Factors affecting your threshold:**

1. **Client expectations** - Higher-end clients expect higher DR
2. **Campaign goals** - Brand building needs higher DR than velocity building
3. **Niche availability** - Some niches have fewer high-DR sites
4. **Budget** - Higher DR sites often cost more
5. **Competition** - Match or exceed competitor link quality

**Recommended threshold matrix:**

| Campaign Type | Minimum DR | Target DR | Premium DR |
|---------------|------------|-----------|------------|
| Budget/Startup | 20 | 30-40 | 50+ |
| Standard | 30 | 40-55 | 60+ |
| Premium | 45 | 55-70 | 75+ |
| Enterprise | 55 | 65-80 | 85+ |

### DR vs. Traffic Correlation

**Expected traffic by DR (rough guidelines):**

| DR Range | Expected Organic Traffic |
|----------|--------------------------|
| 20-30 | 200-2,000 |
| 30-40 | 1,000-10,000 |
| 40-50 | 5,000-50,000 |
| 50-60 | 20,000-100,000 |
| 60-70 | 50,000-500,000 |
| 70+ | 200,000+ |

**Red flags:**
- DR 50+ with under 5,000 traffic = likely manipulated
- DR 40+ with under 1,000 traffic = definitely investigate

### DR Decay and Inflation

**DR can be artificially inflated by:**
- Buying links from high-DR domains
- Expired domain schemes
- PBN connections
- Link exchanges

**Signs of inflated DR:**
- Recent DR spike without corresponding content/traffic growth
- High DR but thin content
- High DR but minimal social presence
- High DR but site looks unprofessional

**Check for natural DR:**
- Use Ahrefs DR history graph
- Look for gradual growth patterns
- Sudden jumps warrant investigation

## Traffic Verification

### Why Traffic Matters

Traffic serves as validation that a site provides real value to users. Google rewards sites that people actually visit, so traffic is a proxy for legitimacy.

### Verification Methods

**Method 1: Ahrefs Organic Traffic**
- Most commonly used
- Based on keyword rankings × CTR estimates
- Good directional indicator

**Method 2: SEMrush Traffic Analytics**
- Similar methodology
- Can show different numbers than Ahrefs
- Cross-reference for accuracy

**Method 3: SimilarWeb**
- Estimates total traffic (not just organic)
- Shows traffic sources breakdown
- Can catch sites with only direct/referral traffic

**Method 4: Manual investigation**
- Check Alexa rank (deprecated but sometimes useful)
- Look for social engagement
- Check comment activity
- Review recent content performance

### Traffic Pattern Analysis

**Healthy traffic patterns:**
- Gradual growth over time
- Seasonal variations that make sense
- Multiple traffic sources
- Rankings for relevant keywords

**Unhealthy patterns:**
- Spike then plateau (bought expired domain)
- Traffic only from obscure countries
- Traffic doesn't match content topics
- All traffic from one keyword (potentially gaming that term)

### Traffic Source Analysis

**Use SimilarWeb or SEMrush to check:**

**Organic search:** Primary validation—should be majority of traffic
**Direct:** Indicates brand recognition
**Referral:** Shows other sites link/refer visitors
**Social:** Indicates engagement
**Paid:** Not inherently bad but shouldn't dominate

**Ideal distribution:**
- 40-60% organic
- 15-30% direct
- 10-20% referral
- 5-15% social
- 0-10% paid

**Red flags:**
- 90%+ from one source
- Significant traffic from countries that don't match content language
- Zero social traffic (no one shares?)

## Content Quality Assessment

### Visual Assessment

**Professional indicators:**
- Clean, modern design
- Easy navigation
- Mobile responsive
- No intrusive ads (or reasonable ad placement)
- Branded elements (logo, consistent styling)

**Unprofessional indicators:**
- Outdated design (looks like 2005)
- Broken layouts
- Overwhelming ads
- Pop-ups everywhere
- Generic/template appearance

### Content Evaluation Checklist

**Examine 5-10 recent posts:**

**Writing quality:**
✓ Grammar and spelling correct?
✓ Well-structured with headings?
✓ Substantial content (1000+ words)?
✓ Original insights or just rehashed?
✓ Would you actually read this?

**Content uniqueness:**
✓ Original research or data?
✓ Unique perspective?
✓ Not obviously AI-generated?
✓ Author expertise evident?

**Content freshness:**
✓ Regular publishing schedule?
✓ Recent posts (within last month)?
✓ Topics timely and relevant?

**Engagement:**
✓ Comments from real readers?
✓ Social shares?
✓ Evidence of audience?

### Guest Post Content Quality

**Review existing guest posts specifically:**

Questions to answer:
- Do they maintain quality standards for guest content?
- Are guest posts given same treatment as staff content?
- Are guest posts clearly valuable (or obvious link vehicles)?
- How are guest posts marked/labeled?

**Warning signs:**
- Every post is a guest post
- Guest posts are thin (under 500 words)
- Guest posts cover random topics
- Obvious keyword stuffing in guest posts

### Editorial Standards Signals

**Strong editorial standards:**
- Detailed submission guidelines
- Content requirements specified
- Revision requests are common
- Some pitches get rejected
- Editor contact available

**Weak editorial standards:**
- Accepts any submission
- No guidelines or vague guidelines
- Everything published as submitted
- No editorial contact
- "Submit here and it's live!"

## Link Profile Check

### Analyzing the Site's Inbound Links

The sites linking TO your target site reveal a lot about its quality.

**Using Ahrefs Site Explorer:**
1. Enter target domain
2. Go to Backlinks → Referring domains
3. Review top linking domains
4. Check DR distribution
5. Look for spam patterns

**What to look for:**

**Positive signals:**
- Links from recognizable brands
- Links from sites in same niche
- Natural DR distribution (some high, some low)
- Links from diverse countries (if international)
- Editorial links from quality content

**Negative signals:**
- Links primarily from PBN-looking sites
- Links from completely unrelated niches
- Links from gambling/pharma/adult sites
- Many links from link directories
- Links from same network of sites

### Analyzing Outbound Links

Check what sites this target links to (their link neighborhood).

**Review 5-10 articles:**
- What sites do they link to?
- Are outbound links to quality resources?
- Do they link to spammy sites?
- Are there excessive outbound links per post?

**Red flags:**
- Links to casinos, pharma, adult sites
- Links to sites with nothing to do with content
- 20+ outbound links per post
- All links go to the same few sites

### Link Profile Health Score

Create a mental (or actual) score:

| Factor | Score |
|--------|-------|
| Inbound links from quality sites | /10 |
| No spam in inbound links | /10 |
| Outbound links to quality sites | /10 |
| Reasonable link ratios | /10 |
| Natural linking patterns | /10 |

**Total /50**
- 40-50: Excellent link profile
- 30-39: Acceptable
- 20-29: Concerning
- Below 20: Skip

## Red Flags: What to Avoid

### Instant Rejection Criteria

These findings mean immediate skip—no exceptions:

**1. Deindexed from Google**
Check: `site:domain.com` returns no results
Action: Skip immediately

**2. Manual penalty indicators**
Check: No organic traffic despite good content
Action: Skip

**3. PBN characteristics**
- Thin or no "About" page
- Hidden ownership (no real people)
- Same template as known PBNs
- Only exists to publish guest posts
Action: Skip

**4. Spam content**
- Gambling, pharma, adult content
- Payday loans, essay mills
- Illegal or harmful content
Action: Skip

**5. No real site**
- Just a parked domain
- Under construction forever
- No substantial content
Action: Skip

### Major Warning Signs

These require investigation or likely skip:

**1. Accepts everything**
- No editorial standards visible
- Your pitch accepted instantly
- No revisions requested ever
Warning: May be link farm

**2. Every post is sponsored/guest**
- No staff content
- Site exists only for guest posts
Warning: Google may discount all links

**3. Recent domain history change**
- Check archive.org
- Content completely different 1-2 years ago
Warning: Purchased expired domain

**4. Pricing way below market**
- DR50 site for $30
- "Bulk discounts" offered unprompted
Warning: Quality issues or scam

**5. Hidden ownership**
- No about page
- No author information
- WHOIS hidden and no company info
Warning: Lack of accountability

### Minor Concerns (Investigate Further)

These warrant additional research:

**1. High DR, low traffic**
- May be legitimate niche site
- May be manipulated
Investigation: Check content quality, link profile

**2. New site (under 2 years)**
- May be legitimate new venture
- May be burned domain replacement
Investigation: Check founders, content trajectory

**3. Inconsistent publishing**
- May be hobby blog with real authority
- May be abandoned site
Investigation: Check last update, response to outreach

**4. Heavy advertising**
- May be monetized professionally
- May indicate low standards
Investigation: Check ad-to-content ratio

## The Complete Qualification Scorecard

### Scoring Framework

Rate each factor 1-5:

| Factor | Weight | Score (1-5) | Weighted |
|--------|--------|-------------|----------|
| Domain Rating | 20% | ___ | ___ |
| Organic Traffic | 20% | ___ | ___ |
| Content Quality | 20% | ___ | ___ |
| Relevance to Niche | 15% | ___ | ___ |
| Editorial Standards | 15% | ___ | ___ |
| Link Profile Health | 10% | ___ | ___ |
| **Total** | 100% | | ___ |

**Score interpretation:**
- 4.5-5.0: Premium opportunity—prioritize
- 3.5-4.4: Quality opportunity—pursue
- 2.5-3.4: Acceptable for standard campaigns
- Below 2.5: Skip or reconsider standards

### Sample Evaluations

**Example 1: Strong Opportunity**
- DR: 55 (5)
- Traffic: 45,000/month (5)
- Content: Well-written, regular posts (4)
- Relevance: Exact niche match (5)
- Editorial: Clear guidelines, editor responsive (4)
- Link Profile: Clean, quality inbound (4)
- **Weighted Score: 4.5** → Premium opportunity

**Example 2: Questionable Opportunity**
- DR: 62 (5)
- Traffic: 800/month (2)
- Content: Thin, sporadic (2)
- Relevance: Related niche (3)
- Editorial: Accepts anything (2)
- Link Profile: Many spammy inbound (2)
- **Weighted Score: 2.6** → Skip despite high DR

### Batch Processing

For efficiency, process prospects in batches:

**Step 1: Quick filter (30 seconds each)**
- Apply 30-second test to all prospects
- Mark as "Pass," "Fail," or "Review"
- Process 50+ per hour

**Step 2: Standard evaluation (5 minutes each)**
- Deep dive on "Pass" and "Review" prospects
- Complete scorecard
- Process 10-12 per hour

**Step 3: Final decision**
- Accept prospects scoring 3.0+
- Note any concerns for outreach
- Move to outreach queue

## Building Your Standards

### Documenting Your Criteria

Create a written qualification policy for your team:

**Hard requirements:**
- Minimum DR: ___
- Minimum traffic: ___
- Required relevance: ___
- Prohibited content types: ___

**Soft requirements:**
- Preferred DR range: ___
- Preferred traffic: ___
- Content quality expectations: ___
- Editorial standard expectations: ___

**Red flags (auto-reject):**
- List all instant rejection criteria

### Training Team Members

**Calibration exercises:**
1. Evaluate same prospects independently
2. Compare scores and discuss differences
3. Align on interpretation of criteria
4. Review edge cases together

**Ongoing quality control:**
- Random spot checks on qualified sites
- Review of rejected sites (ensuring nothing missed)
- Regular criteria review and updates

### Adjusting Over Time

Your standards should evolve:

**Raise standards when:**
- Too many low-value links
- Client complaints about quality
- Seeing diminishing returns

**Lower standards when:**
- Can't find enough opportunities
- Niche has limited quality sites
- Budget constraints require trade-offs

Review and adjust quarterly.

## Summary

Site qualification protects your campaigns from wasted effort and risky placements:

1. **Quick filter first** - Save time by eliminating obvious misses
2. **Verify traffic** - DR without traffic is meaningless
3. **Assess content** - Would you actually read this site?
4. **Check link profiles** - Both inbound and outbound links matter
5. **Know red flags** - Some signals mean instant rejection
6. **Score systematically** - Remove subjectivity with frameworks
7. **Document standards** - Ensure team consistency

With qualified prospects in hand, you're ready for outreach—the subject of our next chapter.
