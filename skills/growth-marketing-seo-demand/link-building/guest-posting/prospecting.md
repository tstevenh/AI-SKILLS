# Prospecting for Guest Posts

## The Art and Science of Finding Opportunities

Prospecting is where link building campaigns succeed or fail. The best outreach in the world won't help if you're targeting the wrong sites. This chapter covers every method for finding guest post opportunities, from basic search operators to advanced competitive analysis.

## Competitor Backlink Analysis

### Why Competitor Analysis Works

Your competitors have already done the work of finding relevant sites. By analyzing their backlinks, you can:

1. Find sites that accept content from your industry
2. Identify proven guest post opportunities
3. Discover sites you'd never find through search
4. Understand what content earns links in your niche

### Step-by-Step Competitor Backlink Analysis

**Step 1: Identify competitors to analyze**

Choose 5-10 competitors based on:
- Direct competitors (same product/service)
- Content competitors (rank for your keywords)
- Aspirational competitors (where you want to be)

**Step 2: Pull their backlink profiles**

Using Ahrefs:
1. Enter competitor URL
2. Go to Backlinks → Backlinks report
3. Filter: One link per domain
4. Sort by DR (highest first)
5. Export to CSV

Using Semrush:
1. Enter competitor URL
2. Go to Backlink Analytics
3. Filter: One per root domain
4. Sort by Authority Score
5. Export

**Step 3: Identify guest post links**

Look for patterns indicating guest posts:
- Author bios with links
- "Guest post by [name]"
- Links from blog sections
- Multiple competitor links from same domain (they're all guest posting there)

**Step 4: Evaluate and add to prospecting list**

For each promising site:
- Check DR/traffic
- Verify relevance
- Find contact info
- Add to outreach list

### Identifying Guest Posts in Backlink Profiles

**Signals of guest post links:**

URL patterns:
- /guest-post/
- /author/[name]/
- /contributor/[name]/
- /blog/ URLs with external author

Anchor text patterns:
- Person's name
- Company name in bio context
- "Written by [name]"

Page title patterns:
- Contains "guest post"
- Contains author name not associated with site

**Pro tip:** Look at the page content for links that appear to be from author bios—usually at the end of articles with a brief author description.

### Finding Common Link Targets

**The intersection method:**

1. Export backlinks from 3-5 competitors
2. Find domains linking to multiple competitors
3. These sites clearly accept your industry content
4. Prioritize for outreach

**Using Ahrefs Link Intersect:**
1. Go to Ahrefs → Link Intersect
2. Enter your competitors
3. Enter your site (to exclude sites already linking to you)
4. Review sites linking to competitors but not you

### Reverse Engineering Competitor Strategies

**Questions to answer:**

1. What types of sites are they targeting?
2. What content topics earn links?
3. What anchor text do they use?
4. How many guest posts per month?
5. What quality levels do they pursue?

**Build a competitor link profile:**

| Competitor | Total RDs | Guest Posts | Avg DR | Top Link Sources |
|------------|-----------|-------------|--------|------------------|
| Comp A | 500 | ~100 | 45 | Industry blogs |
| Comp B | 800 | ~150 | 38 | General business |
| Comp C | 300 | ~50 | 55 | High-end publications |

This helps you understand the competitive landscape and set appropriate targets.

## Guest Post Databases and Lists

### Curated Guest Post Lists

Various SEOs maintain lists of guest post opportunities:

**Free resources:**
- Google "guest post sites [your industry]"
- Search for "guest post database"
- GitHub repositories with link lists
- Reddit r/SEO threads

**Paid databases:**
- Ahrefs Content Explorer (search for "guest post")
- BuzzSumo (find sites accepting contributions)
- Various SEO tools with built-in databases

### Using These Resources Effectively

**Cautions:**
- Public lists mean these sites are heavily contacted
- Quality may have degraded from over-submission
- Verify each site meets your standards
- Many may be outdated

**Best practices:**
1. Use lists as starting points, not final lists
2. Always verify current quality
3. Personalize outreach (they receive many pitches)
4. Look for sites not on public lists (less competition)

### Building Your Own Database

Over time, build proprietary prospect lists:

**Track for each site:**
- URL
- DR/DA
- Organic traffic
- Contact info
- Acceptance history
- Content topics
- Pricing (if paid)
- Notes

**Update regularly:**
- Remove sites that stop accepting posts
- Add new discoveries
- Update metrics quarterly
- Track acceptance rates

## Organizing Your Prospecting Workflow

### The Prospecting Funnel

**Stage 1: Discovery (100%)**
Find all potentially relevant sites through various methods.

**Stage 2: Initial Screening (50%)**
Quick filter based on:
- DR above minimum
- Relevant to niche
- Has real content

**Stage 3: Deep Evaluation (25%)**
Detailed review of:
- Traffic verification
- Content quality
- Guest post history
- Contact availability

**Stage 4: Outreach Ready (15%)**
Sites that pass all filters and have:
- Verified contact info
- Clear submission process
- Good fit for your content

### Spreadsheet Structure

Create a master prospecting spreadsheet:

| Column | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| Domain | Target website |
| DR | Ahrefs Domain Rating |
| Traffic | Monthly organic traffic |
| Relevance | 1-5 score |
| Contact Email | Primary contact |
| Contact Name | Editor/manager name |
| Contact Method | Email/form/social |
| Guest Post Page | URL of submission guidelines |
| Topics Accepted | What they publish |
| Status | New/Contacted/Replied/Accepted |
| Notes | Additional details |
| Date Added | When prospected |
| Date Contacted | When outreach sent |

### Weekly Prospecting Routine

**Monday: Competitor analysis (2 hours)**
- Analyze 2 competitor backlink profiles
- Add new targets to list

**Tuesday: Search operator prospecting (2 hours)**
- Run 10-15 search queries
- Vet and add results

**Wednesday: Social prospecting (1 hour)**
- Monitor Twitter/LinkedIn
- Engage with editors

**Thursday: Database review (1 hour)**
- Update existing prospects
- Remove outdated entries

**Friday: Outreach list prep (2 hours)**
- Move qualified prospects to outreach queue
- Research contacts
- Personalization prep

### Prospecting Metrics

Track these numbers:

**Volume metrics:**
- Prospects discovered per week
- Prospects after initial filter
- Prospects ready for outreach

**Quality metrics:**
- Average DR of prospects
- Average traffic
- Relevance score distribution

**Efficiency metrics:**
- Time per qualified prospect
- Discovery channel effectiveness
- Pass-through rates at each stage

**Target benchmarks:**
- 50+ new prospects discovered/week
- 15-20 qualified for outreach/week
- Average DR 35+
- 30% pass initial screening
- 15% reach outreach stage

## Summary

Effective prospecting combines multiple methods:

1. **Search operators** provide direct guest post opportunities
2. **Competitor analysis** reveals proven targets
3. **Databases** offer starting points (but verify)
4. **Social media** uncovers hidden opportunities
5. **Tools** scale and systematize discovery

The goal is building a continuous pipeline of qualified prospects. With consistent effort, you'll never run out of sites to target.
