# Performance Metrics Guide

## Why Metrics Matter

In link building, what gets measured gets improved. Metrics provide:
- Objective performance evaluation
- Identification of bottlenecks
- Data for optimization
- Accountability and transparency
- Forecasting capability

## Metric Categories

### Activity Metrics

Measure effort and throughput—what people are doing.

**Prospecting metrics:**
| Metric | Definition | Target |
|--------|------------|--------|
| Prospects found | New opportunities identified | 100+/week |
| Prospects qualified | Passing quality thresholds | 50+/week |
| Database additions | Added to outreach pipeline | 40+/week |
| Qualification rate | Qualified ÷ Found | 40-60% |

**Outreach metrics:**
| Metric | Definition | Target |
|--------|------------|--------|
| Emails sent | Initial outreach emails | 50+/week |
| Follow-ups sent | Follow-up emails | 30+/week |
| Total touches | All outreach communications | 80+/week |
| Contacts made | Unique contacts reached | 50+/week |

**Content metrics:**
| Metric | Definition | Target |
|--------|------------|--------|
| Articles written | Content pieces completed | 3-5/week |
| Words produced | Total content output | 5,000+/week |
| Revisions required | Edits after submission | <30% |
| Acceptance rate | Published ÷ Submitted | 85%+ |

### Performance Metrics

Measure effectiveness—how well activities convert to results.

**Response metrics:**
| Metric | Definition | Benchmark |
|--------|------------|-----------|
| Response rate | Responses ÷ Emails sent | 10-20% |
| Positive response rate | Interested ÷ Emails sent | 5-12% |
| Negotiation success | Deals closed ÷ Interested | 40-60% |

**Conversion metrics:**
| Metric | Definition | Benchmark |
|--------|------------|-----------|
| Link acquisition rate | Links ÷ Emails sent | 3-8% |
| Links per week | Total links secured | Based on role |
| Cost per link | Total cost ÷ Links | Varies by DR |
| Time to link | Days from outreach to live | 21-45 days |

**Quality metrics:**
| Metric | Definition | Target |
|--------|------------|--------|
| Average DR | Mean DR of links | 40+ |
| Quality pass rate | Links meeting standards | 95%+ |
| First-time acceptance | No revisions needed | 70%+ |

### Outcome Metrics

Measure business impact—what actually matters.

**Client/campaign metrics:**
| Metric | Definition | Target |
|--------|------------|--------|
| Links delivered vs. goal | Actual ÷ Target | 100%+ |
| Average DR vs. promised | Actual ÷ Promised | 100%+ |
| Client satisfaction | Survey scores | 8+/10 |
| Client retention | Retained ÷ Total | 85%+ |

**SEO impact metrics:**
| Metric | Definition | Measurement |
|--------|------------|-------------|
| Referring domains growth | RD increase over time | Monthly tracking |
| Ranking improvements | Position changes | By keyword |
| Organic traffic impact | Traffic change | Monthly tracking |
| Revenue attribution | Revenue from organic | When trackable |

## Individual Performance Scorecards

### Link Builder Scorecard

**Weekly targets:**

| Metric | Target | Actual | Score |
|--------|--------|--------|-------|
| Prospects qualified | 40 | ___ | ___% |
| Outreach sent | 50 | ___ | ___% |
| Response rate | 15% | ___% | ___% |
| Links secured | 5 | ___ | ___% |
| Quality score | 4.0 | ___ | ___% |
| **Overall** | | | **___**% |

**Score calculation:**
(Actual ÷ Target) × 100 = Score %
Capped at 150% to prevent gaming

**Performance ratings:**
- 120%+: Exceeds expectations
- 100-119%: Meets expectations
- 80-99%: Developing
- <80%: Needs improvement

## Using Metrics for Improvement

### Identifying Problems

**Low response rates (<10%):**
- Check email quality
- Review subject lines
- Assess personalization
- Verify targeting

**Low conversion rates (<3%):**
- Review site quality
- Check relevance
- Assess pitch value
- Evaluate negotiation

**Quality issues:**
- Review qualification process
- Check QA procedures
- Assess content quality
- Evaluate vendor quality

### A/B Testing

**What to test:**
- Subject lines
- Opening sentences
- Value propositions
- Call-to-action
- Follow-up timing

**Testing process:**
1. Identify metric to improve
2. Create two variants
3. Split sample randomly
4. Measure results
5. Implement winner
6. Repeat

### Continuous Improvement Cycle

```
Measure → Analyze → Hypothesize → Test → Implement → Measure
```

## Metric Pitfalls

### Gaming Metrics

**Problem:** Optimizing for metrics at expense of actual results

**Examples:**
- Sending low-quality outreach to hit volume
- Accepting poor sites to hit link count
- Inflating numbers through creative counting

**Prevention:**
- Balance volume and quality metrics
- Spot-check for quality
- Align incentives with outcomes

### Vanity Metrics

**Problem:** Tracking metrics that don't matter

**Examples:**
- Total emails ever sent
- Total prospects in database
- Social shares of content

**Better alternatives:**
- Conversion rates
- Active pipeline
- Links actually acquired

### Analysis Paralysis

**Problem:** Too many metrics, no action

**Prevention:**
- Focus on 5-7 key metrics
- Clear ownership
- Required actions for underperformance
- Regular review cadence

## Summary

Effective performance measurement requires:

1. **Right metrics** - Activity, performance, and outcomes
2. **Clear targets** - Benchmarks for each metric
3. **Regular tracking** - Daily, weekly, monthly
4. **Analysis** - Understanding what numbers mean
5. **Action** - Using data to improve

Metrics are tools, not goals. Use them to drive better results.
