# Getting Cited by ChatGPT

> ChatGPT has 200+ million users. When it cites your content, you get visibility, traffic, and authority. Here's how to optimize for it.

## How ChatGPT Citations Work

### With Web Browsing Enabled

When users enable web browsing (Browse with Bing), ChatGPT:
1. Searches the web for relevant information
2. Reads and processes content from top sources
3. Synthesizes an answer
4. Cites sources with links

### Citation Selection Factors

ChatGPT appears to prioritize:
- **Relevance** to the query
- **Recency** of content (heavily weighted)
- **Authority** of the source
- **Structure** that's easy to extract
- **Accuracy** of information

## Jacky's Research: What Gets Cited

From Jacky Chou's analysis of ChatGPT citations:

### 43.8% Come from "Best X" Listicles

The dominant citation format. Examples:
- "Best email marketing tools"
- "Best CRM software for small business"
- "Best SEO plugins for WordPress"

### 79.1% Were Updated in Current Year

Freshness is critical. ChatGPT strongly prefers:
- Content updated within the last 12 months
- Content with visible "last updated" dates
- Content with current-year references

### Authority Signals Still Matter

Sites with strong backlink profiles and brand recognition get cited more frequently. ChatGPT seems to factor in:
- Domain authority
- Brand mentions across the web
- Topical authority in the subject area

## Optimizing Existing Content

### Step 1: Identify High-Potential Content

Look for content that's:
- Already ranking well (page 1-2)
- Targeting queries people ask ChatGPT
- Could be restructured as a listicle

### Step 2: Add Listicle Elements

If your content is a guide, add a "Top Picks" summary:

```markdown
# Email Marketing Guide

## Top 3 Email Marketing Tools (Quick Picks)

1. **ConvertKit** — Best for creators
2. **Mailchimp** — Best free option
3. **ActiveCampaign** — Best for automation

[Rest of guide content...]
```

### Step 3: Add Clear Definitions

For any term you discuss, add a definition section:

```markdown
## What is Email Marketing?

Email marketing is the practice of sending promotional or 
informational messages to a list of subscribers via email. 
It remains one of the highest-ROI marketing channels, with 
an average return of $42 for every $1 spent.
```

### Step 4: Update Freshness Signals

- Add or update "Last updated: [Date]"
- Include current year in title
- Update any outdated information
- Add recent statistics or examples

### Step 5: Improve Structure

- Add clear H2/H3 headers
- Use bullet points and numbered lists
- Create comparison tables
- Add FAQ sections

## Freshness Strategy

### Update Schedule

| Content Type | Update Frequency |
|--------------|------------------|
| "Best X" listicles | Monthly check, update as needed |
| Statistics/data posts | Quarterly |
| How-to guides | Bi-annually |

### Update Checklist

When updating:
- [ ] Change "Last updated" date
- [ ] Update year in title if applicable
- [ ] Check all pricing is current
- [ ] Verify product features haven't changed
- [ ] Add any new relevant products
- [ ] Remove discontinued products
- [ ] Update screenshots if UI changed
- [ ] Add recent statistics or data

## Authority Building for ChatGPT

### Backlinks Still Matter

ChatGPT appears to factor in domain authority:

1. **Build relevant backlinks** to your content
2. **Get mentioned by authoritative sites** in your niche
3. **Create linkable assets** (original research, tools, templates)

### Brand Recognition

ChatGPT may prefer recognized brands:

1. **Consistent branding** across the web
2. **Press mentions** and media coverage
3. **Social proof** (reviews, testimonials)
4. **Active presence** in your industry

### Topical Authority

ChatGPT seems to favor sites with demonstrated expertise:

1. **Comprehensive content clusters** on your topics
2. **Multiple pieces** covering related subjects
3. **Consistent publishing** over time
4. **Expert authorship** with credentials

## Common Mistakes

### 1. Ignoring Freshness

Old content rarely gets cited. Update regularly.

### 2. Burying the Answer

ChatGPT extracts content from the beginning. Put key information up front.

### 3. Walls of Text

Unstructured content is hard to extract. Use formatting.

### 4. No Listicle Content

If you don't have "Best X" content, you're missing 43.8% of citation opportunities.

### 5. Missing Definitions

When ChatGPT needs to define something, it looks for clear definitions. Provide them.
