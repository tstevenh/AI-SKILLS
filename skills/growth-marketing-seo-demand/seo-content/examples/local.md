## Example 3: Local Blog Post

**Target keyword**: "how to find a good plumber in Vancouver"

### Title Tag
```
How to Find a Good Plumber in Vancouver (2026 Guide)
```

### Content

```markdown
# How to Find a Good Plumber in Vancouver (Local's Guide)

Finding a reliable plumber in Vancouver can feel like a gamble. 
Here's how to find one you can trust.

---

## What to Look For in a Vancouver Plumber

### 1. Proper Licensing

All plumbers in BC must be licensed. Ask for their license 
number and verify with BC Housing.

**Red flag:** Anyone who hesitates to provide licensing info.

### 2. Insurance

A reputable plumber carries liability insurance. This protects 
you if something goes wrong.

**Ask for:** Certificate of insurance.

### 3. Reviews & Reputation

Check multiple sources:
- Google reviews
- Better Business Bureau
- HomeStars
- Neighborhood Facebook groups

**Look for:** Consistent 4+ star ratings with specific feedback.

### 4. Upfront Pricing

Good plumbers provide quotes before starting work. Avoid 
anyone who won't commit to pricing.

### 5. Emergency Availability

In Vancouver's older housing stock, emergencies happen. Make 
sure your plumber offers 24/7 service.

---

## Questions to Ask Before Hiring

1. "Are you licensed and insured?"
2. "Can you provide a written estimate?"
3. "How long have you worked in Vancouver?"
4. "Do you offer warranties on your work?"
5. "What's your availability for emergencies?"

---

## Average Plumber Costs in Vancouver (2026)

| Service | Typical Cost |
|---------|-------------|
| Service call | $75-$150 |
| Drain cleaning | $150-$300 |
| Leak repair | $200-$500 |
| Toilet replacement | $300-$600 |
| Water heater install | $1,500-$3,500 |

*Prices vary by neighborhood and complexity.*

---

## Best Times to Call a Plumber

**Avoid:** Evenings and weekends cost more (emergency rates)

**Best:** Tuesday-Thursday, morning appointments

**Plan ahead:** Regular maintenance prevents emergencies

---

## Vancouver-Specific Tips

### Heritage Homes

Vancouver has many older homes with galvanized or lead pipes. 
If you have a pre-1970s home, get a pipe inspection before 
problems start.

### Earthquake Preparedness

Know your main water shut-off valve location. A good plumber 
can add an earthquake valve for extra protection.

### Strata Properties

Living in a condo? Know what plumbing you're responsible for 
vs. strata. Usually, anything inside your unit is yours.

---

## When to Call for Emergency Service

Some issues can't wait:

- ❗ Burst pipes
- ❗ No water to the home
- ❗ Sewage backup
- ❗ Gas smell (call gas company first!)
- ❗ Flooding

---

## Conclusion

A good Vancouver plumber is:
- Licensed and insured
- Transparent about pricing
- Well-reviewed by locals
- Available when you need them

Take time to find one before you have an emergency.
```

## Local SEO Content Best Practices

### Schema Markup (Required)

```json
{
  "@context": "https://schema.org",
  "@type": "LocalBusiness",
  "name": "ABC Plumbing",
  "address": {
    "@type": "PostalAddress",
    "streetAddress": "123 Main Street",
    "addressLocality": "Vancouver",
    "addressRegion": "BC",
    "postalCode": "V6B 1A1",
    "addressCountry": "CA"
  },
  "telephone": "+1-604-555-0123",
  "openingHours": "Mo-Su 00:00-23:59",
  "geo": {
    "@type": "GeoCoordinates",
    "latitude": "49.2827",
    "longitude": "-123.1207"
  },
  "areaServed": {
    "@type": "City",
    "name": "Vancouver"
  }
}
```

### NAP Consistency
- Name, Address, Phone must match exactly across all pages
- Include full address with postal code
- Use local phone number (not 1-800)

### Local Signals to Include
- City/neighborhood names in content
- Local landmarks or references
- Service area maps
- Local testimonials with neighborhood mentions
- Local phone number
- Physical address

### What to Avoid
- Thin location pages with only changed city names
- Fake reviews or testimonials
- Keyword stuffing with city names
- Doorway pages targeting every small town
