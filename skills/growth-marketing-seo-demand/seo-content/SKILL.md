# SEO Content

Good SEO content does three things at once:

- satisfies search intent
- earns trust from humans
- makes extraction easy for search engines and AI answer systems

## Required inputs

Before drafting, gather:

1. primary keyword
2. supporting keyword cluster
3. search intent
4. content type
5. target audience
6. unique angle or proprietary advantage
7. desired CTA
8. brand voice constraints, if any

## Content types

Default content-type mapping:

- informational -> guide, explainer, how-to
- commercial -> comparison, listicle, review
- transactional -> landing page or product-led content

If the SERP says "product page," do not force a blog post.

## Workflow

Use this sequence:

`RESEARCH -> BRIEF -> OUTLINE -> DRAFT -> HUMANIZE -> OPTIMIZE -> REVIEW`

## Phase 1: Research

Review the live search landscape first.

For top competing results, capture:

- content type
- rough depth
- structure
- freshness
- strengths
- missing angles
- weak explanations or generic sections

Also capture:

- People Also Ask questions
- featured snippet format
- AI Overview tendencies, if present

## Phase 2: Brief

Create a short brief before drafting:

- target keyword and secondaries
- intent
- article type
- target reader
- word-count tier
- unique angle
- questions to answer
- internal links to add
- CTA goal

## Word-count tiers

Use practical tiers rather than arbitrary lengths:

- `Compact`: 500-700 words
- `Standard`: 1200-1600 words
- `Comprehensive`: 2200-2800 words
- `Authority`: 3000-3500+ words

Pick the shortest tier that fully satisfies intent.

## Outline rules

Build the outline around search intent, not a generic blog template.

Typical structure:

1. fast answer / hook
2. context and stakes
3. core sections
4. practical application or decision guidance
5. FAQ or objection handling
6. conclusion and CTA

## Human-first writing rules

- answer the query early
- use concrete examples
- sound like a credible practitioner, not a content farm
- remove generic filler
- admit limits where appropriate
- prefer clarity over "SEO-sounding" wording

## E-E-A-T layer

Every draft should strengthen:

- Experience: what has actually been done, seen, tested, or learned
- Expertise: accurate, specific guidance
- Authoritativeness: proof, references, or original insight
- Trust: honesty, clarity, and no inflated claims

If you have no firsthand edge, be explicit about what the content is and is not.

## AI-search and snippet layer

Write so the piece can be lifted cleanly into:

- featured snippets
- AI Overviews
- chatbot citations

Do that by including:

- crisp definitions
- direct answer blocks
- clear comparison tables where useful
- short summary bullets
- updated facts and current framing
- structured FAQ sections when appropriate

Do not keyword-stuff for AI systems. Make extraction easy by being precise.

## Optimization checklist

Before finalizing, confirm:

- title matches intent
- keyword is used naturally in title, intro, and relevant headers
- sections answer real sub-questions
- headings are useful, not decorative
- internal links are relevant
- CTA matches reader stage
- tone fits the brand
- the article says something a reader would actually save or share

## Deliverable format

Return:

1. brief summary
2. title options
3. outline
4. full draft
5. metadata suggestions if needed
6. optimization notes or proof gaps

## Rule of thumb

If the piece is technically optimized but forgettable, it is still weak SEO content.

Search wins compound when the article is both useful enough to rank and strong enough to build trust after the click.
