# Keyword Research

Most keyword research fails because it produces a spreadsheet instead of a strategy.

The job of this skill is to turn business context into a ranked content plan, then optionally validate that plan with SERP and competitor data.

## Core output

Produce:

- keyword clusters
- pillar topics
- priority ranking
- content recommendations
- a clear "start here" sequence

## Default workflow

Use this baseline path first:

`SEED -> EXPAND -> CLUSTER -> PRIORITIZE -> MAP`

## Intake

Gather:

1. What is being sold?
2. Who is the audience?
3. What website already exists?
4. What is the business goal: traffic, leads, sales, authority, retention?
5. Who are the main competitors?
6. Is the user looking for quick wins, long-term authority, or both?

## Phase 1: Seed generation

Generate seeds across six lenses.

### 1. What you sell

Direct product, service, or category terms.

### 2. Problems you solve

Pain-driven queries and frustration language.

### 3. Outcomes you deliver

Result-oriented search language.

### 4. Your positioning

Terms tied to your unique angle or method.

### 5. Adjacent topics

Places your audience spends attention before or around the core problem.

### 6. Entities to associate with

Tools, frameworks, people, brands, or concepts relevant to authority-building.

## Phase 2: Expansion

Expand each seed using:

- question variations
- modifiers
- comparisons
- alternatives
- audience-specific variants
- beginner / advanced variants

If no external tools are available, do this manually from logic and known patterns.

## Phase 3: Clustering

Group keywords into clusters and then into pillar topics.

A pillar should support:

- one comprehensive hub page or guide
- multiple supporting articles
- a durable business topic, not just a single keyword

Validate each proposed pillar with four checks:

1. Is there enough demand?
2. Is it market language rather than internal product language?
3. Is it realistically winnable?
4. Do we have a real advantage on this topic?

If a pillar fails two or more checks, demote or remove it.

## Phase 4: Prioritization

Score clusters by:

- business value
- relevance to the offer
- likelihood of ranking
- content effort
- proprietary advantage
- speed to impact

Simple priority labels are enough:

- `P1`: publish first
- `P2`: important but not urgent
- `P3`: later or only after authority grows

## Phase 5: Map to content

For each cluster, define:

- primary keyword
- supporting keywords
- search intent
- recommended content type
- CTA goal
- internal linking targets

## Advanced mode: tool-assisted validation

Use this layer when data or SERP tooling is available.

### Competitor and gap analysis

Check:

- what competitors rank for that you do not
- where they have weak or outdated coverage
- where they dominate so strongly that the topic should be deprioritized

### SERP intent validation

Before prioritizing a term, confirm the SERP matches the intended asset.

Examples:

- informational SERP -> article, guide, how-to
- commercial SERP -> comparison, listicle, review
- transactional SERP -> landing page, product page, signup page

Do not force blog content onto obviously transactional SERPs.

### SERP weakness scoring

When comparing opportunities, look for weakness signals:

- thin or outdated content
- weak heading structure
- poor intent match
- weak authority in top results
- heavy forum / UGC presence
- poor freshness for freshness-sensitive topics

Treat weakness scoring as a prioritization input, not a substitute for judgment.

### Cannibalization checks

For existing sites, make sure multiple pages are not already competing for the same intent cluster.

If they are:

- merge
- re-scope
- redirect
- or assign clearer intent boundaries

## Deliverable format

Return:

1. business summary
2. seed themes
3. keyword clusters
4. pillar decisions
5. priority table
6. recommended first 5-10 content pieces
7. optional competitor / SERP notes

## Rule of thumb

Do not optimize for search volume alone.

The best keywords are the ones where:

- the right people search
- the intent matches the right page
- you can say something better than the current winners
