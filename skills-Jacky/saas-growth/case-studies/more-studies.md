# More SaaS Case Studies

## Figma

**Category:** Collaborative design
**Exit:** $20B acquisition by Adobe (later cancelled)

**Growth Drivers:**
- Browser-based (no download)
- Real-time collaboration
- Free tier for individuals
- Designers → Teams → Companies

**Key Lesson:** Multi-player beats single-player. Collaboration features are viral features.

---

## Zoom

**Category:** Video conferencing
**Result:** IPO, $4B+ ARR at peak

**Growth Drivers:**
- "It just works" reliability
- Free tier (40-min limit)
- Every meeting = product demo
- Pandemic accelerant

**Key Lesson:** Reliability and ease of use can beat feature-rich competitors.

---

## Dropbox

**Category:** File storage
**Result:** IPO, $2B+ ARR

**Growth Drivers:**
- Famous referral program (2-sided)
- Freemium model
- Shared folders = viral spread
- Simple, clear value prop

**Key Lesson:** Referral incentives can supercharge growth when product already has value.

---

## Atlassian

**Category:** Developer/team tools
**Result:** $3B+ ARR, public company

**Growth Drivers:**
- No sales team (historically)
- Self-serve purchasing
- Multiple complementary products
- Ecosystem of add-ons

**Key Lesson:** PLG can work at massive scale. Let the product sell itself.

---

## HubSpot

**Category:** Marketing, Sales, Service
**Result:** $2B+ ARR, public company

**Growth Drivers:**
- Invented "inbound marketing" category
- Free CRM as wedge
- Content marketing excellence
- Freemium → Premium → Suite

**Key Lesson:** Category creation + free product + content = powerful combination.

---

## Airtable

**Category:** Flexible database/spreadsheet
**Result:** $11B valuation

**Growth Drivers:**
- Templates drive adoption
- Flexible use cases
- Freemium for individuals
- Bottom-up enterprise adoption

**Key Lesson:** Flexibility enables many entry points; templates show what's possible.

---

## Intercom

**Category:** Customer messaging
**Result:** $1.3B valuation

**Growth Drivers:**
- In-app messenger as product demo
- "Powered by Intercom" widget
- Multiple products from one wedge
- Strong brand and content

**Key Lesson:** Your product can advertise itself in customer-facing contexts.

---

## Common Patterns Across Successful SaaS

### PLG Fundamentals
- Free tier enables trial
- Virality built into usage
- Value demonstrated quickly
- Upgrade triggers are clear

### Category Creation
- Define new category
- Become the default
- Own the narrative
- Educate the market

### Bottom-Up Enterprise
- Land with team/individual
- Expand to department
- Enterprise deal follows
- Reduce CAC dramatically

### Ecosystem Building
- Integrations increase value
- Community drives content
- Partners extend reach
- Lock-in through breadth

### Timing Matters
- Right product, right moment
- External trends accelerate
- Patience before acceleration
- First-mover (or fast-follower) advantage
