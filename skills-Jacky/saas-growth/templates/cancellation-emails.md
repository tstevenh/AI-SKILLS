# Cancellation Email Templates

## Introduction

Cancellation emails are your last touchpoint with departing customers. Done well, they can save accounts, collect valuable feedback, and leave the door open for return. This template collection covers the entire cancellation journey—from exit confirmation to long-term win-back campaigns.

---

## 1. Exit Survey Email

**Sent: When cancellation initiated**

```
Subject: Quick question before you go

Hi [Name],

I'm sorry to see you cancel your [Product] account.

Before you go, would you take 30 seconds to tell us why?

[Button: Share Feedback]

Your honest feedback helps us improve for everyone.

Thanks for being a customer.

[Your Name]
[Product] Team
```

## 2. Cancellation Confirmation

**Sent: When cancellation processed**

```
Subject: Your [Product] cancellation is confirmed

Hi [Name],

This confirms that your [Product] subscription has been cancelled.

Here's what you should know:
• Last billing date: [Date]
• Access continues until: [Date]
• Data retention: [X days] — you can export anytime

What's next:
• Your data is safe for [X days]
• You can reactivate anytime: [Link]
• Questions? Just reply

We'd love to have you back someday. Thanks for being a customer.

[Your Name]
[Product] Team

P.S. If you have a moment, we'd really value your feedback:
[Feedback link]
```

## 3. Save Offer Emails

### Save Offer: Discount (Too Expensive)

```
Subject: Before you go—a special offer

Hi [Name],

I noticed you're leaving because of pricing. Before you go, I wanted to offer you something special.

How about 30% off for the next 3 months?

Your new price: $[Discounted Price]/month
(Normally $[Regular Price]/month)

[Button: Accept Offer & Stay]

This is my way of saying we value your business and want to make it work.

If you still need to cancel, I understand. But I hope you'll give us another chance.

[Your Name]
```

### Save Offer: Feature (Missing Features)

```
Subject: About those features you mentioned

Hi [Name],

You mentioned [Product] was missing features you need.

I wanted to share something:

[Feature they mentioned] is on our roadmap for [Quarter/Timeline].

Would you consider staying if we:
• Give you early access when it launches
• Set up a call to discuss workarounds in the meantime
• Add you to our feedback group to shape the feature

[Button: Let's Talk]

I'd hate to lose you right before we build what you need.

[Your Name]
```

### Save Offer: Pause (Timing/Temporary)

```
Subject: Need a break instead?

Hi [Name],

Instead of cancelling, would a pause work better?

Pause your subscription:
• No charges for up to 3 months
• Your data stays safe
• Resume anytime

[Button: Pause My Account]

Many customers find this helpful when timing isn't right but they want to come back.

Let me know what works best for you.

[Your Name]
```

### Save Offer: Downgrade

```
Subject: What if you paid less instead?

Hi [Name],

Before you cancel completely, would a lower tier work?

Our Starter plan is just $[Price]/month and includes:
✓ [Feature 1]
✓ [Feature 2]
✓ [Feature 3]

Your data stays exactly where it is.

[Button: Downgrade to Starter]

This way, you keep access to the basics without the full commitment.

[Your Name]
```

### Save Offer: Support (Bad Experience)

```
Subject: I want to make this right

Hi [Name],

I saw you mentioned having a poor experience with [Product]. I'm genuinely sorry about that.

Before you leave, would you give me a chance to fix it?

I'd like to:
• Understand what went wrong
• Make it right if possible
• Ensure it doesn't happen to others

[Button: Schedule a Call]

Even if you still decide to leave, your feedback will help us improve.

[Your Name]
```

## 4. Win-Back Email Sequence

### Day 7: First Win-Back

```
Subject: We miss you, [Name]

Hi [Name],

It's been a week since you left [Product]. I hope you found what you were looking for.

Just so you know:
• Your data is still here
• You can log back in anytime
• Reactivating takes 30 seconds

[Button: Come Back to [Product]]

If anything changes, we'd love to have you back.

[Your Name]
```

### Day 21: What's New

```
Subject: A lot has changed at [Product]

Hi [Name],

Since you've been gone, we've been busy:

✨ NEW: [Feature 1]
🚀 IMPROVED: [Feature 2]
🔧 FIXED: [Common issue]

Some of these might address what led you to leave.

[Button: See What's New]

Worth another look?

[Your Name]
```

### Day 30: The Offer

```
Subject: Come back with 30% off

Hi [Name],

I'd love to welcome you back to [Product].

For the next 48 hours, use code COMEBACK30 for 30% off your first 3 months.

Your previous work is still there—you can pick up right where you left off.

[Button: Reactivate with 30% Off]

Hope to see you again.

[Your Name]
```

### Day 60: Specific Use Case

```
Subject: [Specific thing] just got easier

Hi [Name],

Remember when you used [Product] for [their use case]?

We just made it 10x better:
• [Improvement 1]
• [Improvement 2]
• [Improvement 3]

[Customer Name] just told us: "[Positive quote about improvement]"

Thought you might want to know.

[Button: Try It Again]

[Your Name]
```

### Day 90: Final Direct Attempt

```
Subject: Last note from me

Hi [Name],

This is my last win-back email. I want to respect your inbox.

Here's where we stand:
• Your data is stored for [X more days]
• Reactivating is easy: [Link]
• We'd love to have you back

After this, I'll only send occasional product updates (quarterly at most).

If you ever want to return, you know where to find us.

Thanks for being a customer.

[Your Name]
```

## 7. Email Sequence Summary

| Day | Email | Goal |
|-----|-------|------|
| 0 | Exit survey | Understand reason |
| 0 | Save offer | Retain if possible |
| 0 | Confirmation | Close cleanly |
| 7 | First win-back | Prompt return |
| 21 | What's new | Show improvements |
| 30 | Discount offer | Incentivize return |
| 60 | Use case | Relevance reminder |
| 90 | Final | Last direct attempt |
| Pre-deletion | Data warning | Prevent data loss |

---

## 8. Best Practices

**Do:**
- Personalize based on churn reason
- Make reactivation easy (one click)
- Time win-backs strategically
- Show genuine improvement
- Respect inbox limits

**Don't:**
- Beg or guilt trip
- Over-email
- Ignore their feedback
- Make cancellation hard
- Burn the bridge
