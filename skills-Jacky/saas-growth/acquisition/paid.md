# Paid Acquisition for SaaS

## Introduction

Paid acquisition offers what organic channels can't: immediate, scalable traffic. For SaaS companies, paid channels are often essential for accelerating growth once product-market fit is established. But paid acquisition requires discipline—without strong unit economics, you can burn through capital quickly with nothing to show for it.

This guide covers Google Ads, Facebook/Meta Ads, LinkedIn Ads, retargeting strategies, and CAC benchmarks by channel.

---

## 1. Google Ads for SaaS

### Google Ads Campaign Types

**Search Campaigns (Most Important)**
- User actively searching
- High intent
- Keyword-targeted
- Text ads in search results

**Display Campaigns**
- Banner ads across web
- Lower intent
- Audience-targeted
- Good for awareness/retargeting

**Performance Max**
- AI-driven across all placements
- Less control, more automation
- Mixed results for SaaS

**YouTube Ads**
- Video ads on YouTube
- Awareness and retargeting
- Growing channel for B2B

### Search Campaign Strategy

**Keyword Types:**

| Type | Example | Intent | CPC | Volume |
|------|---------|--------|-----|--------|
| Branded | "[your brand]" | Very High | Low | Low |
| Competitor | "[competitor] alternative" | Very High | Med-High | Low |
| Category | "CRM software" | High | Very High | High |
| Problem | "how to track sales" | Medium | Medium | High |
| Feature | "sales pipeline tool" | High | High | Medium |

**Priority Order:**
1. Branded (protect your brand)
2. Competitor (high intent, capture switchers)
3. Category (high intent, but expensive)
4. Feature (qualified intent)
5. Problem (informational, lower conversion)

### Google Ads Best Practices

**1. Start with Search**
- Highest intent
- Most control
- Clearest attribution

**2. Use SKAGs or Theme Groups**
- Single Keyword Ad Groups (SKAG)
- Or tightly themed groups
- Better quality scores
- More relevant ads

**3. Landing Page Alignment**
- Match ad to landing page
- Keyword in headline
- Clear CTA
- Fast load time

**4. Negative Keywords**
- Exclude irrelevant searches
- "free," "jobs," "salary"
- Industry-specific exclusions

**5. Bid Strategies**
- Manual CPC initially (control)
- Target CPA once data exists
- Maximize conversions for volume

### Google Ads Metrics

| Metric | Good | Great |
|--------|------|-------|
| Click-through Rate (CTR) | >3% | >5% |
| Quality Score | 7+ | 9+ |
| Conversion Rate (Search) | >3% | >5% |
| Cost per Click (SaaS) | $2-10 | <$5 |
| Cost per Trial | $50-200 | <$75 |

## 2. Facebook/Meta Ads for SaaS

### Why Facebook/Meta for B2B SaaS?

**Advantages:**
- Massive reach
- Sophisticated targeting
- Strong creative options
- Retargeting capabilities
- Often cheaper than Google

**Challenges:**
- Lower intent (not searching)
- B2B targeting imprecise
- iOS privacy changes impacted
- Requires strong creative

### Facebook Campaign Structure

**Campaign Types:**

| Objective | Use Case | Stage |
|-----------|----------|-------|
| Awareness | Brand building | TOFU |
| Traffic | Blog/content | TOFU |
| Lead Gen | Form fills | MOFU |
| Conversions | Signups/trials | BOFU |
| Retargeting | Previous visitors | BOFU |

### B2B Targeting on Facebook

**Job Title Targeting:**
- "Marketing Manager"
- "Founder"
- "Product Manager"
- Limited but useful

**Interest Targeting:**
- Business publications (HBR, TechCrunch)
- B2B tools (Salesforce, HubSpot)
- Industry associations
- Competitors

**Lookalike Audiences:**
- Upload customer list
- Create 1%, 2%, 5% lookalikes
- Often best performing
- Requires 1000+ source list

**Retargeting:**
- Website visitors
- Pricing page visitors
- Trial users who didn't convert
- Video viewers

### Facebook Creative Best Practices

**Creative Types:**

| Format | Best For | Notes |
|--------|----------|-------|
| Image | Quick tests | Simple, fast to produce |
| Video | Explaining product | 15-30 seconds optimal |
| Carousel | Multiple features | Tell a story |
| UGC-style | Authenticity | Feels less like an ad |

**Copy Formula:**
```
Hook (problem/pain point)
↓
Agitate (consequences)
↓
Solution (your product)
↓
Proof (social proof)
↓
CTA (clear action)
```

**Example:**
```
"Tired of losing leads in spreadsheets? 😫

Our CRM customers close 30% more deals.

Join 10,000+ sales teams who switched this year.

[Try Free for 14 Days]"
```

### Facebook Metrics

| Metric | Good | Great |
|--------|------|-------|
| CTR | >1% | >2% |
| CPM | <$20 | <$10 |
| Cost per Click | <$3 | <$1.50 |
| Cost per Lead | <$50 | <$25 |
| Hook Rate (Video) | >30% | >50% |

## 3. LinkedIn Ads for SaaS

### Why LinkedIn for B2B

**Advantages:**
- Precise B2B targeting
- Professional context
- Decision-maker access
- High-value audiences

**Challenges:**
- Expensive (3-10x Facebook)
- Smaller audience
- Limited creative options
- Lower engagement

### LinkedIn Campaign Types

**Sponsored Content:**
- Posts in feed
- Images, video, carousels
- Highest reach

**Message Ads:**
- Direct messages
- High engagement
- Can feel spammy

**Text Ads:**
- Sidebar placement
- Cheapest
- Low volume

**Conversation Ads:**
- Interactive messaging
- Multiple CTA options
- Higher engagement

### LinkedIn Targeting

**Best Targeting Options:**

| Target | Precision | Cost |
|--------|-----------|------|
| Job Title | High | High |
| Job Function | Medium | Medium |
| Seniority | High | Medium |
| Industry | Medium | Medium |
| Company Size | High | Medium |
| Company Name | Very High | High |
| Skills | Medium | Medium |

**Example Targeting:**
```
Job Function: Marketing
Seniority: Director+
Company Size: 51-200 employees
Industry: Technology
```

### LinkedIn Best Practices

**1. Lead Gen Forms**
- Pre-filled from profile
- Higher conversion than website
- Lower quality than website
- Good for gated content

**2. Content That Works**
- Thought leadership
- Industry insights
- Comparison content
- Case studies

**3. ABM Campaigns**
- Target specific companies
- Upload company list
- Reach decision-makers

### LinkedIn Metrics

| Metric | Good | Great |
|--------|------|-------|
| CTR | >0.5% | >1% |
| CPM | <$50 | <$30 |
| Cost per Click | <$10 | <$5 |
| Cost per Lead | <$150 | <$75 |
| Conversion Rate | >10% | >20% |

## 4. Retargeting Strategies

### Why Retargeting Works

**The Stats:**
- 97% of visitors don't convert first visit
- Retargeted visitors 70% more likely to convert
- Average person needs 7+ touchpoints

### Retargeting Audience Segments

**1. All Website Visitors**
- Broadest audience
- Lowest intent
- Good for awareness

**2. Pricing Page Visitors**
- High intent
- Close to decision
- High-value segment

**3. Trial Users (Not Converted)**
- Very high intent
- Just need nudge
- Highest value

**4. Blog/Content Viewers**
- Lower intent
- Educational retargeting
- Move down funnel

**5. Cart/Checkout Abandoners**
- Highest intent
- Technical or friction issue
- Strong ROI

### Retargeting by Funnel Stage

```
Awareness (All Visitors):
- Brand video ads
- Social proof content
- Thought leadership

Consideration (Engaged Visitors):
- Product demos
- Feature highlights
- Case studies

Decision (Pricing/Trial):
- Free trial offer
- Limited-time discount
- Competitor comparison

Recovery (Abandoned):
- Reminder ads
- Objection handling
- Support offer
```

### Retargeting Best Practices

**1. Frequency Caps**
- Don't stalk people
- 3-5 impressions per day max
- Rotate creative

**2. Exclude Converters**
- Stop showing ads to customers
- Waste of spend
- Annoying for them

**3. Creative Sequencing**
- Story across touchpoints
- Different messages over time
- Not same ad repeatedly

**4. Cross-Platform**
- Facebook + Google
- Don't rely on one
- Reach across web

## 5. CAC by Channel Benchmarks

### CAC Benchmarks

| Channel | SMB CAC | Mid-Market CAC | Enterprise CAC |
|---------|---------|----------------|----------------|
| Organic/SEO | $100-300 | $500-1,500 | $2,000-5,000 |
| Google Search | $200-500 | $800-2,000 | $3,000-8,000 |
| Google Display | $100-300 | $500-1,500 | $2,000-5,000 |
| Facebook | $150-400 | $600-1,500 | $2,500-6,000 |
| LinkedIn | $300-700 | $1,000-3,000 | $4,000-10,000 |
| Outbound | $500-1,500 | $2,000-5,000 | $10,000-30,000 |
| Referral | $50-150 | $300-800 | $1,000-3,000 |

### CAC Ratio Guidelines

| Ratio | Interpretation |
|-------|---------------|
| LTV:CAC < 1:1 | Losing money |
| LTV:CAC 1:1-2:1 | Marginal |
| LTV:CAC 3:1 | Healthy |
| LTV:CAC 5:1+ | Very efficient |

### Channel Selection Framework

**Choose Google When:**
- Users search for your solution
- High intent keywords exist
- You can afford competitive CPCs

**Choose Facebook When:**
- Visual product
- Broad audience appeal
- Strong creative capability
- Lower CPCs needed

**Choose LinkedIn When:**
- Very specific B2B targeting needed
- High ACV justifies cost
- Enterprise focus
- ABM strategy

**Choose Retargeting When:**
- Good website traffic exists
- Conversion rates low
- Long consideration cycle
- Multi-touch needed

## Summary: Paid Acquisition Framework

### Getting Started

**Phase 1: Foundation**
- Google branded (protect brand)
- Basic retargeting
- Small budgets, test everything

**Phase 2: Expansion**
- Google competitor/category
- Facebook prospecting
- LinkedIn tests (if B2B)

**Phase 3: Scale**
- Optimize winning channels
- Kill losing channels
- Increase budgets on winners
- Advanced retargeting

### Paid Checklist

- [ ] Conversion tracking set up
- [ ] Attribution model defined
- [ ] Landing pages optimized
- [ ] Branded search protected
- [ ] Retargeting audiences built
- [ ] Budget allocation by channel
- [ ] Creative testing process
- [ ] Negative keywords added
