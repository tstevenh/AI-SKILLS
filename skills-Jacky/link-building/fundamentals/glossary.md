# Link Building Glossary

## A

### Anchor Text
The visible, clickable text of a hyperlink. Anchor text helps search engines understand what the linked page is about.
**Example:** In `<a href="example.com">best running shoes</a>`, "best running shoes" is the anchor text.

### Anchor Text Distribution
The mix of different anchor text types in a site's backlink profile. A natural distribution includes branded anchors, naked URLs, generic phrases, and limited exact-match keywords.

### Authority
A measure of a website's or page's credibility and trustworthiness in the eyes of search engines. Higher authority typically correlates with better rankings.

### Authority Transfer
The concept of authority or "link equity" passing from one page to another through hyperlinks. Also known as link juice or PageRank flow.

### Ahrefs
A popular SEO tool used for backlink analysis, keyword research, and competitor analysis. Known for having one of the largest link databases.

## B

### Backlink
A link from one website to another. Backlinks are a major ranking factor as they serve as "votes" for the linked page.

### Black Hat SEO
SEO tactics that violate search engine guidelines. These include link schemes, cloaking, and manipulative practices that can result in penalties.

### Broken Link
A hyperlink that leads to a page that no longer exists (typically returns a 404 error). Finding and replacing broken links is a link building technique.

### Broken Link Building
A link building tactic where you find broken links on websites, create replacement content, and ask the site owner to link to your content instead.

### BuzzStream
An outreach and link building management platform used for organizing contacts and managing outreach campaigns.

## C

### Canonical URL
The preferred version of a URL when multiple URLs can access the same content. Important for ensuring link equity goes to the right page.

### Citation
A mention of a business's name, address, and phone number (NAP) on the web. Important for local SEO.

### Citation Flow (CF)
A Majestic metric measuring how influential a URL is based on how many sites link to it. Measures quantity of links.

### Contextual Link
A link placed within the main body content of a page, surrounded by relevant text. Generally more valuable than sidebar or footer links.

### Content Explorer
A tool (available in Ahrefs and others) that allows you to search for content by topic and see its backlink data.

### Crawl
The process by which search engines discover and index web pages by following links.

## D

### DA (Domain Authority)
A score developed by Moz that predicts how well a website will rank in search engines. Scored from 0-100.

### Dead Link
Another term for a broken link—a hyperlink that no longer works.

### Deep Link
A link to an interior page of a website rather than the homepage.

### Directory
A website that lists and categorizes other websites. Directory submissions are a basic link building tactic.

### Disavow
The process of telling Google to ignore certain backlinks when assessing your site. Done through Google Search Console when you have spammy links.

### Dofollow
A link that passes authority (link equity) to the linked page. The default state of links without any rel attributes.

### Domain Rating (DR)
Ahrefs' metric showing the strength of a website's backlink profile on a 0-100 scale.

### DR
See Domain Rating.

## E

### E-E-A-T
Experience, Expertise, Authoritativeness, and Trustworthiness. Google's framework for evaluating content quality, mentioned in their Search Quality Rater Guidelines.

### Editorial Link
A link given freely by the site owner because they found your content valuable, without payment or request.

### Exact Match Anchor
Anchor text that exactly matches the target keyword you want to rank for. Should be used sparingly to avoid over-optimization.

### Expired Domain
A domain that the previous owner let lapse. Some retain backlinks and authority that can be valuable for SEO.

## F

### Featured Snippet
The special box at the top of some search results showing a direct answer to a query. Earning one can significantly increase traffic.

### Follow Link
See Dofollow.

### Footer Link
A link placed in the footer of a website, which appears on every page. Generally considered lower value than contextual links.

### Free Link
A link earned without payment, typically through relationships, content quality, or earning coverage.

## G

### Guest Blogging
See Guest Posting.

### Guest Post
An article written by someone outside the website's regular team and published on that site. A common link building tactic when done for quality.

### Guest Posting
The practice of writing articles for other websites in exchange for a link, byline, and exposure.

### Gray Hat SEO
SEO tactics that fall between white hat and black hat—not explicitly against guidelines but still manipulative. Most commercial link building operates here.

## H

### HARO (Help A Reporter Out)
A service connecting journalists with expert sources. SEOs use it to earn links by providing quotes for articles.

### Homepage Link
A link pointing to a website's main page rather than an interior page.

### HTTP/HTTPS
Protocols for transferring web pages. HTTPS (secure) is preferred by Google and users.

### Hunter.io
An email finding tool commonly used in link building outreach.

## I

### Inbound Link
A link from another website to yours. Same as backlink.

### Index
Google's database of web pages. A page must be indexed to appear in search results.

### Internal Link
A link from one page on a website to another page on the same website.

### IP Address
The unique numerical address assigned to devices and servers. Relevant for link building as same-IP links may be devalued.

## J

### Journalist Outreach
The practice of building relationships with journalists to earn coverage and links through digital PR.

## K

### Keyword
A word or phrase that describes content and that users type into search engines.

### Keyword Anchor
Anchor text that includes target keywords rather than branded terms or generic phrases.

## L

### Link
A hyperlink connecting one web page to another.

### Link Attribute
HTML attributes on links that provide additional information, such as rel="nofollow" or rel="sponsored".

### Link Bait
Content specifically designed to attract backlinks, such as original research, tools, or comprehensive guides. Now often called "linkable assets."

### Link Building
The practice of acquiring hyperlinks from other websites to your own.

### Link Burst
A sudden, rapid increase in backlinks. Can appear unnatural if too extreme.

### Link Diversity
Having backlinks from many different domains, IP addresses, and geographic locations rather than concentrated sources.

### Link Equity
The value or authority passed through a link from one page to another. Also called link juice.

### Link Exchange
An arrangement where two sites agree to link to each other. Excessive exchanges violate Google's guidelines.

### Link Farm
A website or network of websites created solely to build links. A black hat tactic.

### Link Insertion
See Niche Edit.

### Link Juice
Colloquial term for the authority or value passed through links. Also called link equity.

### Link Neighborhood
The other sites that a page links to and receives links from. Links from bad neighborhoods can hurt your site.

### Link Profile
The complete collection of backlinks pointing to a website.

### Link Reclamation
The process of finding and recovering links that should point to your site but don't (broken links, unlinked mentions, etc.).

### Link Removal
The process of asking other sites to remove links pointing to you, typically as part of cleaning up a spammy profile.

### Link Rot
The gradual loss of links over time as pages are removed, domains expire, or sites change.

### Link Scheme
Any pattern of links intended to manipulate PageRank or a site's ranking. Prohibited by Google's guidelines.

### Link Spam
Low-quality, manipulative links created solely to influence search rankings.

### Link Velocity
The rate at which a website acquires new backlinks over time.

### Linkable Asset
Content specifically designed to earn links, such as original research, tools, infographics, or comprehensive guides.

### Linkless Mention
A mention of your brand or website without a hyperlink. Often a link building opportunity.

### Local Citation
A mention of a business's name, address, and phone number online. Important for local SEO.

### Lost Links
Backlinks that previously pointed to your site but no longer do.

## M

### Manual Action
A penalty manually applied by Google's webspam team for violating guidelines. More severe than algorithmic demotion.

### Majestic
An SEO tool known for its link analysis and proprietary Trust Flow/Citation Flow metrics.

### Mention
A reference to a brand, person, or website, with or without a hyperlink.

### Money Site
The main website you're trying to rank, as opposed to satellite sites or PBNs.

### Moz
An SEO software company known for Domain Authority metric and educational resources.

## N

### NAP
Name, Address, Phone number. Consistency across citations is important for local SEO.

### Natural Link
A link given organically because someone found your content valuable, without solicitation or payment.

### Negative SEO
Attempting to harm a competitor's rankings through techniques like building spammy links to their site.

### New Link
A recently acquired backlink.

### Niche
A specific topic or market segment.

### Niche Edit
Adding a link to an existing published article on another website. Also called link insertion or curated link.

### Nofollow
A link attribute (rel="nofollow") telling search engines not to pass link equity. Google now treats as a "hint" rather than directive.

## O

### Organic Link
A link earned naturally without payment or explicit request.

### Organic Search
Unpaid search results, as opposed to paid advertisements.

### Organic Traffic
Website visitors who arrive through unpaid search results.

### Outbound Link
A link from your website to another website.

### Outreach
The process of contacting website owners to request links or coverage.

## P

### PA (Page Authority)
A Moz metric predicting how well a specific page will rank. Scored 0-100.

### PageRank
Google's original algorithm for ranking web pages based on backlinks. The foundational concept behind link building.

### Paid Link
A link obtained through payment. Should be marked with rel="sponsored" according to Google's guidelines.

### Parasite SEO
Using high-authority third-party platforms (Medium, LinkedIn, etc.) to rank content.

### Partial Match Anchor
Anchor text that includes some of your target keywords along with other words.

### PBN (Private Blog Network)
A network of websites used solely to build links to a main site. A black hat tactic that violates Google's guidelines.

### Penguin
A Google algorithm update (originally 2012) targeting link spam and manipulative link building.

### Pitchbox
An outreach platform used for managing link building campaigns at scale.

### Placement
The location where a link appears on a page (contextual, sidebar, footer, etc.).

## Q

### Quality Link
A backlink from a relevant, authoritative, trustworthy source with natural anchor text and contextual placement.

### Qwoted
A platform connecting journalists with expert sources, similar to HARO.

## R

### Reciprocal Link
A link exchange where Site A links to Site B and Site B links back to Site A.

### Redirect
A way to send users and search engines from one URL to another. 301 redirects pass link equity.

### Referral Traffic
Website visitors who arrive by clicking links on other websites.

### Referring Domain
A unique domain that links to your website. The count of referring domains is often more important than total backlinks.

### Relevance
How closely related the linking page's topic is to the linked page's topic. Highly relevant links are more valuable.

### Resource Page
A page specifically created to link out to useful resources on a topic. A common target for link building.

### Return on Investment (ROI)
The ratio of profit to investment. For link building, comparing the value of links earned to the cost of acquiring them.

## S

### Schema Markup
Structured data that helps search engines understand your content. Can earn rich snippets in search results.

### Scholarship Link Building
Creating a scholarship program to earn links from educational institution scholarship pages.

### Screaming Frog
A website crawler tool used for technical SEO audits and finding broken links.

### SERP (Search Engine Results Page)
The page displayed by search engines showing results for a query.

### SEMrush
A popular SEO tool offering keyword research, competitive analysis, and backlink data.

### Sidebar Link
A link placed in a website's sidebar, which may appear on multiple pages. Generally less valuable than contextual links.

### Site-Wide Link
A link that appears on every page of a website, such as in the footer or sidebar.

### Skyscraper Technique
A link building strategy where you find popular content, create something better, and reach out to those who linked to the original.

### Spam Score
A metric (used by Moz and others) indicating how likely a site is to be penalized for spam.

### Sponsored Attribute
The rel="sponsored" link attribute indicating a paid placement. Required for paid links per Google's guidelines.

### Syndication
Publishing the same content on multiple websites. Can dilute link value and create duplicate content issues.

## T

### Three-Way Link Exchange
An exchange where Site A links to Site B, Site B links to Site C, and Site C links to Site A. Harder to detect than direct exchanges.

### Tier 1 Links
Links pointing directly to your money site.

### Tier 2 Links
Links pointing to your Tier 1 links, indirectly boosting your site.

### Tiered Link Building
Building links in layers, with Tier 2 links pointing to Tier 1 links.

### Toxic Links
Harmful backlinks that could trigger a penalty. Typically from spammy or irrelevant sources.

### Traffic
Visitors to a website. Can be organic, paid, referral, direct, or social.

### Trust Flow (TF)
A Majestic metric measuring how trustworthy a URL is based on links from trusted seed sites.

### Trustworthiness
A quality signal indicating how reliable and credible a website is.

## U

### UGC (User Generated Content)
Content created by users rather than the site owner. The rel="ugc" attribute is used for links in UGC.

### Unlinked Mention
A reference to your brand without a hyperlink. An opportunity to request a link be added.

### UR (URL Rating)
Ahrefs' metric measuring the strength of a specific URL's backlink profile on a 0-100 scale.

### URL
The web address of a page (Uniform Resource Locator).

## V

### Velocity
See Link Velocity.

## W

### Web 2.0
User-generated content platforms like Blogger, WordPress.com, Tumblr. Sometimes used for building links.

### Webmaster
A person who manages a website.

### White Hat SEO
SEO tactics that fully comply with search engine guidelines. Lower risk but often slower results.

### Wikipedia
The online encyclopedia. Wikipedia links are nofollow but highly valued for referral traffic and credibility.

### Write For Us Page
A page on a website explaining how to submit guest posts. A common prospecting target.

## X, Y, Z

### XML Sitemap
A file listing a website's pages to help search engines crawl the site.

### Yoast
A popular WordPress SEO plugin.

### Zero-Click Search
A search where the user's query is answered directly on the search results page without clicking through.
