# The Skyscraper Technique

## What Is the Skyscraper Technique?

The Skyscraper Technique, coined by Brian Dean of Backlinko in 2013, is a content-based link building strategy with three steps:

1. **Find content that has earned many links**
2. **Create something significantly better**
3. **Reach out to people who linked to the original**

The metaphor: When you're next to a tall building (highly-linked content), you build a skyscraper (better content) and people notice.

### Why the Technique Works

**Proven demand:**
If content has earned 100 links, there's proven appetite for that topic.

**Improvement is possible:**
Most content has room for improvement—more depth, better design, updated information.

**Linkers are identifiable:**
You know exactly who to reach out to—people who already link to similar content.

**Value proposition is clear:**
"You linked to X, here's something better."

### The Evolution of Skyscraper

**2013-2016: The golden era**
- Novel approach
- High success rates
- Few competitors using it

**2017-2020: Maturation**
- More people using the technique
- Higher quality bar required
- "10x content" became the standard

**2021-Present: Refinement needed**
- "Better" must be significantly better
- Outreach must be more sophisticated
- Promotion strategy matters more
- Relationship building increasingly important

## Finding Content to Improve

### Step 1: Identify Highly-Linked Content

**Using Ahrefs Content Explorer:**
1. Enter topic keyword
2. Sort by Referring Domains
3. Filter by DR of linking domains
4. Identify content with 50+ referring domains

**Using Ahrefs Site Explorer on competitors:**
1. Enter competitor domain
2. Go to Top Pages
3. Sort by Referring Domains
4. Find their most-linked content

**Criteria for good targets:**
- 50+ referring domains (proves link demand)
- Topic relevant to your expertise
- Content is improvable
- Published 1+ years ago (has had time to earn links)
- Not from a major publication (harder to outcompete)

### Step 2: Analyze Why It Earned Links

**Questions to answer:**
- What need does it fulfill?
- What makes it linkable?
- Why would someone reference it?
- What value does it provide linkers?

**Common link magnets:**
- Original data or research
- Comprehensive coverage
- Unique angle or perspective
- Visual assets
- Practical tools or templates
- Controversy or strong opinions

### Step 3: Identify Improvement Opportunities

**Types of improvements:**

**Depth:**
- More comprehensive coverage
- Additional subtopics
- More examples/case studies
- Expert quotes

**Freshness:**
- Updated statistics
- Current examples
- Recent developments
- 2026-specific information

**Design:**
- Better visual design
- Custom graphics
- Interactive elements
- Better UX

**Format:**
- Video + text
- Downloadable resources
- Interactive tools
- Better organization

**Originality:**
- Original research
- New data
- Unique perspective
- Expert contributions

### Red Flags: Content Not Worth Competing With

**Skip when:**
- Content is from major publications (NYT, Forbes)
- Content is nearly perfect already
- Topic is outside your expertise
- Would require massive resource investment
- Link profile is heavily branded (links for the brand, not content)

## Creating 10x Content

### What "10x" Actually Means

"10x content" doesn't mean 10 times longer. It means:
- Significantly more valuable to the reader
- Noticeably better experience
- Clearly superior in some important dimension
- Worth linking to instead of (or in addition to) alternatives

### Content Improvement Framework

**Dimension 1: Comprehensiveness**
| Original | Your Version |
|----------|--------------|
| Covers 5 points | Covers 15 points |
| 1,500 words | 5,000 words |
| Surface treatment | Deep dive |

**Dimension 2: Freshness**
| Original | Your Version |
|----------|--------------|
| Data from 2020 | Data from 2026 |
| Outdated examples | Current examples |
| Old screenshots | New screenshots |

**Dimension 3: Design/UX**
| Original | Your Version |
|----------|--------------|
| Wall of text | Visual hierarchy |
| No graphics | Custom infographics |
| Difficult navigation | Clear sections |

**Dimension 4: Originality**
| Original | Your Version |
|----------|--------------|
| Aggregated info | Original research |
| Generic advice | Expert insights |
| Common knowledge | Unique data |

### Content Creation Checklist

Before publishing:

**Value:**
✓ More comprehensive than competitors?
✓ Contains unique information?
✓ Provides actionable takeaways?
✓ Answers questions readers have?

**Quality:**
✓ Well-written and edited?
✓ Professionally designed?
✓ Properly sourced?
✓ Free of errors?

**Format:**
✓ Easy to scan?
✓ Includes visuals?
✓ Mobile-friendly?
✓ Fast-loading?

**Differentiation:**
✓ Clear improvement over alternatives?
✓ Something unique to offer?
✓ Reason to link to THIS over others?

## Outreach to Linkers

### Building Your Outreach List

**Export linkers from original content:**
1. In Ahrefs, find the original content
2. Go to Backlinks
3. Filter: One link per domain
4. Export to spreadsheet
5. Remove irrelevant or low-quality domains

**Qualify linkers:**
- DR 20+ (or highly relevant)
- Actual content pages (not directories)
- Likely to update content
- Contact findable

### Outreach Approach

**The pitch:**
You're not asking for a link—you're sharing a resource they might find valuable.

**Framework:**
1. Reference their content and the link
2. Share your improved resource
3. Explain why it might be useful
4. No hard ask (or soft ask only)

### Outreach Templates

**Template 1: Soft approach**

```
Subject: Regarding your [topic] article

Hi [Name],

I was reading your article on [topic] and noticed you referenced [original content] as a resource.

I recently put together an updated guide on the same topic: [Your URL]

It includes [key improvement - updated data, more comprehensive coverage, etc.].

Thought it might be useful for you or your readers.

[Your Name]
```

**Template 2: Direct approach**

```
Subject: Updated resource for your [topic] page

Hi [Name],

In your post about [topic], you linked to [original content].

I've created an updated version that includes:
• [Improvement 1]
• [Improvement 2]
• [Improvement 3]

Here it is: [Your URL]

If you think it's valuable, feel free to reference it. Either way, hope you find it useful.

[Your Name]
```

**Template 3: Value-first approach**

```
Subject: Your [topic] article - quick resource

Hi [Name],

Your article on [topic] is one of the better resources I've found. The point you made about [specific thing] was especially helpful.

I recently finished a comprehensive guide on [related topic] that goes even deeper: [Your URL]

Thought you might find it interesting given your coverage of this area.

Best,
[Your Name]
```

### Response Rate Expectations

**Realistic benchmarks:**

| Metric | Typical | Good | Excellent |
|--------|---------|------|-----------|
| Response rate | 5-10% | 12-18% | 20%+ |
| Link rate | 2-5% | 5-8% | 10%+ |

**Factors affecting success:**
- How much better your content actually is
- Relevance of your outreach list
- Quality of your pitch
- Timing and persistence

### Why Skyscraper Outreach Often Fails

**Common problems:**

1. **Content isn't actually better**
   - Solution: Be honest about whether you've improved significantly

2. **Outreach is too generic**
   - Solution: Personalize based on their content

3. **Asking too directly**
   - Solution: Share as a resource, not a request

4. **Wrong audience**
   - Solution: Better qualify your outreach list

5. **No follow-up**
   - Solution: 2-3 touch follow-up sequence

## Updated Approach for 2026

### Beyond Basic Skyscraper

The original technique is now table stakes. Modern improvements:

**Build relationships first:**
Don't pitch cold. Engage with linkers' content before reaching out.

**Offer exclusive value:**
Give linkers something extra—data, quotes, early access.

**Think distribution:**
Content promotion matters as much as creation.

**Consider formats:**
Video, interactive, audio—not just articles.

**Focus on unique angles:**
Original research or perspectives, not just "more complete."

### Content Promotion Strategy

**Pre-launch:**
- Tease on social media
- Alert relationships about upcoming content
- Line up promotional partners

**Launch:**
- Social media push
- Email to list
- Outreach to linkers
- Paid promotion if budget allows

**Post-launch:**
- Continue outreach over weeks
- Repurpose into other formats
- Monitor for additional link opportunities
- Update and re-promote periodically

### Integration with Other Tactics

Skyscraper works best combined with:

**Guest posting:**
Create skyscraper content, then write guest posts that link to it.

**Digital PR:**
Pitch unique findings to journalists.

**Resource page outreach:**
Submit to relevant resource pages.

**Broken link building:**
Your content replaces dead resources.

## Summary

The Skyscraper Technique remains viable but requires:

1. **Significant improvement** - "Better" must be obviously better
2. **Quality outreach** - Personalized, value-focused
3. **Realistic expectations** - 5-10% link rate is good
4. **Modern promotion** - Multi-channel distribution
5. **Integration** - Part of broader link building program

When executed well, skyscraper content becomes a lasting asset that earns links over time, not just from initial outreach.
