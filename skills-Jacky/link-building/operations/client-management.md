# Client Management

## Client Onboarding

### Pre-Sale Qualification

**Before accepting a client:**

Questions to answer:
- Is their niche buildable? (Can you find opportunities?)
- Are expectations realistic?
- Do they have budget for meaningful work?
- Is timeline reasonable?
- Will they be good to work with?

**Red flags:**
- Wants 50 links for $500
- Expects page 1 rankings in 30 days
- Bad-mouths previous agencies
- Won't commit to reasonable term
- Unrealistic quality expectations

### Onboarding Process

**Week 1: Discovery**

1. **Kickoff call**
   - Introductions
   - Goals discussion
   - Expectations alignment
   - Process overview

2. **Information gathering**
   - Target URLs for links
   - Keyword targets
   - Competitor list
   - Brand guidelines
   - Restrictions (sites to avoid, etc.)

3. **Access setup**
   - Analytics access
   - Search Console (if needed)
   - Brand assets
   - Content approval process

**Week 2: Strategy**

4. **Link audit**
   - Current link profile
   - Competitor analysis
   - Opportunity assessment

5. **Strategy presentation**
   - Recommended approach
   - Target site types
   - Timeline
   - Expected outcomes

6. **Approval and launch**
   - Final sign-off
   - Begin prospecting
   - First month plan

## Setting Expectations

### What to Communicate

**Timeline expectations:**
- Links start appearing: 4-6 weeks
- SEO impact visible: 3-6 months
- Full campaign effect: 6-12 months

**Quality vs. quantity:**
- Explain why quality matters
- Set realistic volume expectations
- Manage "more is better" thinking

**Results expectations:**
- Links are one factor in rankings
- Can't guarantee specific positions
- Ongoing effort required

### Managing Expectations

**During sales:**
- Be realistic about outcomes
- Explain the process
- Set timeline expectations
- Discuss what success looks like

**During onboarding:**
- Reinforce expectations
- Document agreements
- Establish communication cadence
- Create feedback loops

**Ongoing:**
- Regular check-ins
- Proactive communication
- Address concerns early
- Celebrate wins

### The "Under-Promise, Over-Deliver" Rule

**Promise:**
- Slightly below what you expect to deliver
- Conservative timeline
- Minimum quality thresholds

**Deliver:**
- More links when possible
- Higher quality when available
- Faster when able

## Handling Complaints

### Common Complaints

**"Not enough links"**
- Review delivery vs. agreement
- Explain quality vs. quantity trade-off
- Discuss pipeline and coming deliveries
- Adjust expectations or package if needed

**"Links aren't working"**
- Explain SEO timeline
- Show link profile improvement
- Discuss other ranking factors
- Set realistic timeline for impact

**"Quality isn't good enough"**
- Review quality standards agreed
- Show metrics for delivered links
- Discuss specific concerns
- Adjust strategy if legitimate

**"Communication is poor"**
- Acknowledge the concern
- Adjust communication frequency
- Establish preferred channels
- Follow through on changes

### Complaint Resolution Process

**Step 1: Listen**
- Let them express concern fully
- Don't interrupt or defend
- Take notes

**Step 2: Acknowledge**
- Show you understand
- Validate their feelings
- Don't dismiss concerns

**Step 3: Investigate**
- Review the facts
- Check your delivery
- Identify any gaps

**Step 4: Respond**
- Present findings honestly
- Propose solution
- Get agreement on path forward

**Step 5: Follow through**
- Implement agreed actions
- Follow up proactively
- Document resolution

### When to Fire a Client

Sometimes clients aren't a good fit:

**Warning signs:**
- Constantly moving goalposts
- Abusive to team members
- Won't pay on time
- Unrealistic demands
- Bad faith behavior

**Exit process:**
- Document issues
- Have honest conversation
- Offer graceful transition
- Complete committed work
- Part professionally

## Client Retention

### Building Long-Term Relationships

**Deliver consistently:**
- Meet or exceed deliverables
- Maintain quality standards
- Hit deadlines

**Communicate proactively:**
- Share wins immediately
- Surface issues early
- Provide strategic input

**Add value beyond scope:**
- Share relevant insights
- Make recommendations
- Be a strategic partner

**Build personal relationships:**
- Know their business
- Remember personal details
- Be genuinely invested

### Retention Tactics

**Quarterly business reviews:**
- Review performance
- Discuss goals
- Plan ahead
- Strengthen relationship

**Annual strategy sessions:**
- Big picture planning
- Contract renewal discussion
- Upsell opportunities
- Relationship deepening

**Value-adds:**
- Bonus links when available
- Free audits/reports
- Industry insights
- Referral benefits

### Measuring Retention

**Track:**
- Client tenure
- Retention rate
- Expansion revenue
- NPS or satisfaction scores

**Benchmark:**
- 80%+ annual retention = good
- 90%+ = excellent
- Below 70% = investigate issues

## Summary

Client management success requires:

1. **Thorough onboarding** - Set up for success
2. **Clear expectations** - Avoid misunderstandings
3. **Regular reporting** - Demonstrate value
4. **Complaint handling** - Resolve issues professionally
5. **Retention focus** - Build lasting relationships

Happy clients refer others, expand their business with you, and are a pleasure to work with.
