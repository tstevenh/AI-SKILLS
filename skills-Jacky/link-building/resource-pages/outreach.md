# Resource Page Outreach

## The Resource Page Outreach Approach

Resource page link building has a straightforward value proposition: your resource helps their page, and their link helps you. The key is presenting your resource as genuinely valuable to their audience.

## Finding the Right Contact

### Who to Contact

**Priority order:**
1. Page author/creator (if identifiable)
2. Webmaster
3. Site owner
4. Department head (for .edu/.gov)
5. General contact

### Finding Contacts

**On-page methods:**
- Author attribution
- Contact link on page
- "Contact us" in footer
- About page

**Email finding:**
- Hunter.io for domain emails
- Pattern guessing (first@domain.com, etc.)
- LinkedIn search for site team

**For .edu sites:**
- Look for librarian or departmental contacts
- Faculty directory
- Webmaster email (often webmaster@university.edu)

## Crafting the Pitch

### Value Proposition

Your pitch must answer: "Why should they add your resource?"

**Strong value propositions:**
- Fills a gap in their list
- More comprehensive than similar resources
- More current/updated
- Free resource their audience needs
- Unique tool or perspective

### Email Structure

**Element 1: Personalized opening (show you've seen the page)**
"I was looking for resources on [topic] and found your page..."

**Element 2: Your suggestion (what you want added)**
"I thought your readers might also find [your resource] helpful..."

**Element 3: Why it fits (value proposition)**
"It covers [specific value] which complements [what they already have]..."

**Element 4: Easy ask (make it simple)**
"Would you consider adding it to your list?"

### Tone and Approach

**Do:**
- Be genuinely helpful
- Focus on their audience's needs
- Be concise and respectful
- Make it easy to say yes

**Don't:**
- Be demanding
- Make it about your needs
- Send obvious templates
- Oversell your resource

## Email Templates

**Subject:** Resource suggestion for your [Topic] page

```

Would you consider adding it to your list?

Best,
[Your Name]
```

## Handling Responses

### Positive Responses

**"Yes, we'll add it"**
Response: Thank them, confirm details if needed, ask about preferred description.

**"Send more info"**
Response: Provide a brief description, key benefits, and the link. Offer to answer questions.

**"We need to review it first"**
Response: Completely understand. Share the link and any supporting information. Offer to follow up.

### Neutral Responses

**"Maybe later"**
Response: Thank them, ask if you can follow up in a specific timeframe.

**"We'll consider it"**
Response: Thank them, offer to provide any additional information, follow up in 2-3 weeks.

### Negative Responses

**"No, doesn't fit"**
Response: Thank them for considering, ask if they have suggestions for where it might fit.

**"We don't accept suggestions"**
Response: Thank them, respect the decision, don't argue.

**No response**
Action: Send one follow-up after 5-7 days. If no response, move on.

## Follow-Up Strategy

### Follow-Up Timing

- First follow-up: 5-7 days after initial email
- Second follow-up: 10-14 days after initial (if needed)
- Stop after second follow-up

### Follow-Up Templates

**Follow-up 1:**
```

[Your Name]
```

```

[Your Name]
```

## Success Optimization

### A/B Testing

Test variations of:
- Subject lines
- Opening sentences
- Value propositions
- Call to actions

### Response Rate Benchmarks

**Typical response rates:**
- Cold outreach: 8-15%
- With personalization: 15-25%
- .edu sites: 5-12%
- Link inclusion rate: 5-15% of sent emails

### Improving Results

**Higher response rates:**
- More personalization
- Better resource quality
- Clearer value proposition
- More relevant targeting

**Higher conversion rates:**
- Truly useful resources
- Perfect fit for their page
- Professional presentation
- Easy to implement

## Summary

Resource page outreach succeeds when:

1. **Your resource genuinely fits** - Don't force mismatches
2. **You show you've visited** - Personalization matters
3. **Value is clear** - Why should they add you?
4. **The ask is easy** - Simple to say yes
5. **You follow up appropriately** - Persistent but respectful

With the right resources and approach, resource page link building provides consistent, high-quality links.
