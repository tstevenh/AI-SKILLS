# Broken Link Outreach

## The Broken Link Pitch

Broken link outreach has a built-in value proposition: you're helping fix a problem. This makes it potentially more effective than cold guest post pitches, but only if executed properly.

### The Psychology of Broken Link Outreach

**Why it works:**
- You're solving their problem (broken link)
- No one wants broken links on their site
- You're offering value, not just asking

**Why it sometimes fails:**
- Site owner doesn't care
- Your replacement isn't actually relevant
- The ask isn't clear
- Too much friction to implement

## Crafting Effective Broken Link Pitches

### Email Structure

**Element 1: The helpful alert**
"I was reading [article] and noticed a broken link..."

**Element 2: The specific problem**
"The link to [resource name] in your section about [topic] isn't working anymore."

**Element 3: The solution**
"I have a similar resource that covers [topic]: [URL]"

**Element 4: The easy ask**
"Would you consider updating the link?"

### What to Include

**Essential:**
- Specific page with the broken link
- What the broken link was (topic/original URL)
- Your suggested replacement
- Brief description of why it's relevant

**Optional but helpful:**
- Screenshot of the broken link
- Brief description of your replacement
- Why you think it's a good fit

**Avoid:**
- Long explanations about your site
- Multiple asks in one email
- Promotional language
- Generic templates without specifics

## Email Templates

### Template 1: Standard Broken Link Report

**Subject:** Broken link on your [Topic] page

```
Hi [Name],

I was reading your article on [topic] and noticed a broken link.

The link to [original resource or "a resource about X"] in the section about [specific section] returns a 404.

I have a resource that covers the same topic: [Your URL]

If you'd like to update the broken link, this might be a good replacement. Let me know if you'd like more details.

Thanks,
[Your Name]
```

### Template 2: Helpful Fix with Context

**Subject:** Quick heads up - broken link found

```
Hi [Name],

Found a broken link while reading your excellent post on [topic].

The broken link:
• Location: [Section/context where it appears]
• Original: [Original URL if known, or describe what it linked to]
• Error: Returns 404

Suggested replacement:
• [Your URL]
• Covers: [Brief description of content]

Would this work as a replacement?

Best,
[Your Name]
```

### Template 3: Multiple Broken Links

**Subject:** Found some broken links on your site

```
Hi [Name],

I was going through your [topic] resources and found a few broken links:

1. [Page URL] - link to [resource] is dead
2. [Page URL] - link to [resource] is dead

I have resources that could replace one (or both):
• [Your URL 1] covers [topic 1]
• [Your URL 2] covers [topic 2]

Happy to help fix these if you'd like suggestions.

Thanks,
[Your Name]
```

### Template 4: Educational Site (.edu)

**Subject:** Broken link report - [Page/Department]

```
Hi [Name],

I'm reaching out about a broken link on your [department/topic] resources page.

The link to [resource description] no longer works. I thought you'd want to know since students might be trying to access it.

We have a free resource at [Your URL] that covers similar content: [brief description].

If you're updating the page, this might be a suitable replacement.

Thank you for maintaining such valuable resources.

[Your Name]
```

### Template 5: Resource Page Focus

**Subject:** Broken link on your resources page

```
Hi [Name],

Your [topic] resource page has been helpful, but I noticed one of the links isn't working anymore.

The link to [resource name/description] returns a 404 error.

I have a resource that might be a good replacement: [Your URL]

It covers [topic] and would fit with the other resources on your page.

Would you consider swapping it in?

Thanks,
[Your Name]
```

### Template 6: Short and Direct

**Subject:** Broken link fix

```
Hi,

Quick note: your page [URL] has a broken link to [resource topic].

Possible replacement: [Your URL]

Let me know if you want more info.

[Your Name]
```

### Template 7: With Screenshot

**Subject:** Broken link spotted (screenshot attached)

```
Hi [Name],

I noticed a broken link on your [topic] page - screenshot attached showing where it appears.

The link to [resource] isn't working anymore. 

We have a resource at [Your URL] that covers the same topic if you'd like to update it.

Let me know if this would be helpful.

[Your Name]

[Attach screenshot]
```

### Template 8: Offering Alternative Options

**Subject:** Broken link on [Page] - some options

```
Hi [Name],

I found a broken link on your page about [topic]. The link to [resource] returns a 404.

A few options that might work as replacements:
1. [Your URL] - [brief description]
2. [Archive.org link if available] - archived version
3. [Alternative similar resource] - different take

Any of these work for you?

Best,
[Your Name]
```

### Template 9: Follow-Up

**Subject:** Re: Broken link on your [Topic] page

```
Hi [Name],

Following up on my note about the broken link on your [topic] page.

Quick recap: the link to [resource] isn't working, and I suggested [Your URL] as a replacement.

Let me know if you'd like any more information.

Thanks,
[Your Name]
```

### Template 10: Final Follow-Up

**Subject:** Re: Broken link on your [Topic] page

```
Hi [Name],

Last follow-up on the broken link I found. No worries if you don't have time to update it - just wanted to make sure you saw my note.

The broken link and suggested fix are in my previous email.

Thanks either way.

[Your Name]
```

## Response Handling

### Positive Responses

**"Thanks, I'll update it"**
Response: Great! Thank them, confirm they have the correct URL.

**"Can you tell me more about your resource?"**
Response: Provide brief, relevant description. Focus on why it's a good replacement.

**"I'll check it out"**
Response: Thank them. Offer to answer any questions. Follow up in a week if no update.

### Neutral Responses

**"I don't manage that page"**
Response: Ask who does, or ask to be forwarded.

**"We'll look into it"**
Response: Thank them. Follow up in 1-2 weeks.

### Negative Responses

**"We removed it intentionally"**
Response: Thank them for letting you know. Ask if there are other pages where your resource might fit.

**"We don't update old content"**
Response: Thank them. Move on.

**No response (after 2 follow-ups):**
Action: Move on. Maybe try again in 6+ months.

## Follow-Up Strategy

### Timeline

- **Initial email:** Day 0
- **First follow-up:** Day 5-7
- **Second follow-up:** Day 12-14
- **Stop:** After second follow-up

### Follow-Up Principles

- Keep follow-ups shorter than original
- Reference the specific broken link
- Provide value (not just "checking in")
- Be respectful of their time
- Stop after 2 follow-ups

## Conversion Optimization

### Improving Response Rates

**Higher response rates with:**
- Specific page and section references
- Clear problem-solution framing
- Genuinely helpful tone
- Professional presentation
- Easy-to-implement suggestion

**Lower response rates with:**
- Vague references
- Long, promotional emails
- Multiple asks
- Poor quality replacement
- Generic templates

### Success Rate Benchmarks

| Metric | Minimum | Good | Excellent |
|--------|---------|------|-----------|
| Response rate | 10% | 15-20% | 25%+ |
| Positive response rate | 5% | 10% | 15%+ |
| Link conversion | 3% | 8% | 12%+ |

### Why Campaigns Fail

**Common problems:**
1. Replacement content isn't actually good
2. Not enough volume (need many attempts)
3. Targeting wrong sites (low quality/irrelevant)
4. Templates too generic
5. Not following up

## Summary

Broken link outreach succeeds when:

1. **You find real broken links** - Verify before reaching out
2. **Your replacement is legitimate** - Actually covers the topic
3. **Your pitch is helpful** - Solving their problem, not selling
4. **You're specific** - Reference exact page and section
5. **You follow up** - But respectfully

The built-in value proposition of broken link building makes it one of the more reliable outreach methods when executed well.
