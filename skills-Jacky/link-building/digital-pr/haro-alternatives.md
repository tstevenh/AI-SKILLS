# HARO & Alternatives

## What Is HARO?

HARO (Help A Reporter Out) was the original journalist-source matching service. Journalists post queries; sources (you) respond with expert commentary. When quoted, you often get a backlink.

**The 2023-2024 changes:** Cision acquired HARO and rebranded it as "Connectively" with significant changes to the model, including paid tiers. The principles remain the same, but the platforms have evolved.

## How HARO-Style Services Work

### The Basic Flow

1. Journalist needs expert quote or source
2. Posts query to the platform
3. Query distributed to registered sources
4. Sources (you) submit responses
5. Journalist selects best responses
6. Story published with quotes and links

### Why It Works for Link Building

**High authority links:**
Journalists work for publications—often high-DR sites you couldn't otherwise access.

**Editorial legitimacy:**
These are genuine editorial links, exactly what Google values.

**Relationship building:**
One good response can lead to repeat opportunities.

**Cost effective:**
Free or low-cost access to high-quality link opportunities.

## Platform Breakdown

### Connectively (formerly HARO)

**Website:** connectively.us

**How it works:**
- Three daily emails (5:35 AM, 12:35 PM, 5:35 PM ET)
- Queries organized by category
- Respond via email to journalist's anonymous address
- Free tier available, paid tiers for more features

**Categories:**
- Business and Finance
- High Tech
- Biotech and Healthcare
- General
- Lifestyle and Fitness
- Entertainment and Media
- Government and Politics
- Travel
- Education

**Best for:**
Broad range of topics, established name recognition

**Tips:**
- Respond within 2-3 hours of query
- Lead with credentials
- Answer the specific question
- Keep responses under 300 words

### Qwoted

**Website:** qwoted.com

**How it works:**
- Platform for journalists to find sources
- Create detailed expert profile
- Receive matched queries
- Respond through platform

**Best for:**
Building ongoing journalist relationships, specific industries

**Paid options:**
Premium features for better visibility

### SourceBottle

**Website:** sourcebottle.com

**How it works:**
- Similar query distribution model
- Australian-based but international reach
- Daily digest of opportunities

**Best for:**
Australian market, international coverage

### Featured.com

**Website:** featured.com

**How it works:**
- Expert response platform
- Submit through their interface
- They match responses to journalist queries
- Success-based payment model

**Best for:**
Those willing to pay for successful placements

### Help a B2B Writer

**Website:** helpab2bwriter.com

**How it works:**
- Focused on B2B content writers (not journalists)
- Writers need expert quotes for content marketing
- Similar response model

**Best for:**
B2B expertise, content marketing links (lower DR but relevant)

### PressPlugs

**Website:** pressplugs.co.uk

**How it works:**
- UK-focused journalist matching
- Daily email digests
- Response through platform

**Best for:**
UK market, British publications

### MuckRack Journalist Queries

**Website:** muckrack.com

- PR platform with journalist query feature

Serious PR operations, premium placements

## HARO Response Strategy

### Anatomy of a Winning Response

**Element 1: Subject line**
Include the query number and a clear identifier:
"[Query #123456] Social Media Expert - Former Facebook Marketing Lead"

**Element 2: Credentials (first)**
Lead with why you're qualified:
"As a [Title] at [Company] with [X years] experience in [relevant area], I've [relevant accomplishment]..."

**Element 3: Direct answer**
Answer exactly what they asked:
"To answer your question about [topic]: [clear, quotable statement]"

**Element 4: Supporting detail**
Add context and examples:
"For example, [brief case study or data point]..."

**Element 5: Availability**
Make follow-up easy:
"I'm happy to elaborate or provide additional context. Best reached at [email/phone]."

### Response Templates

**Template 1: Expert Quote Response**

```
Subject: [Query #] - [Your expertise] - [Credential]

Hi [Journalist name if known],

[One sentence credential]. Here's my response to your query:

[Direct, quotable answer to their specific question - 2-3 sentences]

[Brief supporting example or data - 1-2 sentences]

[Optional: Additional angle they might find interesting]

I'm available for follow-up questions at [email] or [phone].

Best,
[Name]
[Title], [Company]
[Website]
```

**Template 2: Data/Statistics Response**

```
Subject: [Query #] - Data on [Topic] from [Company]

Hi,

I'm [Name], [Title] at [Company]. We have data directly relevant to your query:

[Specific statistic or finding]

This comes from [source/methodology].

Additional context: [Brief explanation of what it means]

Happy to provide the full data set or connect for an interview.

[Name]
[Contact info]
```

**Template 3: Contrarian Take Response**

```
Subject: [Query #] - Alternative perspective on [Topic]

Hi,

While many experts say [conventional wisdom], my experience suggests otherwise.

As [credential], I've found that [contrarian insight].

For example: [Brief supporting evidence]

If you're interested in a different angle for your piece, I'm happy to elaborate.

[Name]
[Title], [Company]
```

### Response Best Practices

**Do:**
- Respond FAST (within 2 hours if possible)
- Lead with credentials
- Answer the specific question asked
- Be concise (200-400 words)
- Provide quotable soundbites
- Include contact information
- Be genuinely helpful

**Don't:**
- Respond to queries outside your expertise
- Write a dissertation
- Be promotional
- Include attachments (unless requested)
- Use excessive formatting
- Mass-respond to everything

### Query Selection

**Respond to queries when:**
- You have genuine expertise
- Your response adds unique value
- Publication is relevant to your goals
- Deadline allows quality response

**Skip queries when:**
- Topic is outside your expertise
- You can only give a generic answer
- Deadline is too tight
- Publication isn't worth the effort
- Query is too vague to answer well

### Success Rate Optimization

**Typical success rates:**
- Amateur responders: 2-5%
- Good responses: 8-15%
- Expert responders: 15-25%

**Increase success by:**
- Responding quickly (ideally first hour)
- Having unique credentials
- Providing exclusive data
- Being more helpful than others
- Building relationships with repeat journalists

### Tracking HARO Efforts

**Track:**
- Queries responded to
- Responses selected
- Links earned
- DR of publications
- Time spent

**Calculate:**
- Response success rate
- Average DR of wins
- Time per link
- Cost per link (if using paid platforms)

## Building a HARO Routine

### Daily Process

**Morning (after 5:35 AM email):**
1. Review queries (5 minutes)
2. Identify good fits (immediate)
3. Draft responses to top 2-3 (30-45 minutes)
4. Send before noon

**Afternoon (after 12:35 PM email):**
1. Quick review
2. Respond to urgent/perfect matches only
3. Save others for evening

**Evening (after 5:35 PM email):**
1. Review queries
2. Respond to best opportunities
3. May be less competitive (fewer responders)

### Weekly Time Commitment

**Minimum viable:** 3-5 hours/week
**Recommended:** 5-8 hours/week
**Aggressive:** 10+ hours/week

### Team Approach

If multiple people can respond:

**Divide by expertise:**
- Person A: Technical queries
- Person B: Business/strategy queries
- Person C: Industry-specific queries

**Coordinate to avoid duplicate responses from same organization.**

## Measuring HARO ROI

### Value Calculation

**Example month:**
- Queries responded to: 30
- Successful placements: 4
- Average DR: 65

**Estimated link value:**
- 4 links × $400 average DR65 link = $1,600 value

**Time invested:**
- 20 hours × $50/hour opportunity cost = $1,000

**ROI:** (1,600 - 1,000) / 1,000 = 60%

Plus: Brand exposure, relationship building, referral traffic

### When HARO Makes Sense

**Good investment when:**
- You have genuine, unique expertise
- Your time cost is reasonable
- You can commit consistently
- Target publications match your needs

**Poor investment when:**
- Generic expertise only
- Inconsistent availability
- High opportunity cost of time
- Can't respond quickly

## Summary

HARO and alternatives offer high-authority link opportunities:

1. **Choose platforms** matching your market (Connectively, Qwoted, etc.)
2. **Respond strategically** - Quality over quantity
3. **Lead with credentials** - Why should they quote you?
4. **Answer quickly** - Speed correlates with success
5. **Track and optimize** - Improve response quality over time

HARO links are:
- High authority (often DR 60+)
- Completely editorial
- Relationship-building
- Time-intensive but cost-effective

For building natural link profiles with premium links, HARO is an essential tool.
