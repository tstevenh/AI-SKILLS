# Scholarship Link Building

## What Is Scholarship Link Building?

Scholarship link building involves creating a scholarship program and reaching out to educational institutions (.edu) to list your scholarship on their financial aid or scholarship pages.

### Why It Works

**High-authority links:**
.edu domains are trusted by Google, often DR 60-90+.

**Natural link opportunity:**
Schools want to share scholarship resources with students.

**Scalable:**
Many schools have scholarship pages accepting submissions.

### The Basic Process

1. Create legitimate scholarship
2. Build scholarship page on your site
3. Find .edu scholarship pages
4. Reach out to be listed
5. Award scholarship annually

## Setting Up a Scholarship

### Scholarship Requirements

**Essential elements:**
- Clear eligibility criteria
- Award amount
- Application process
- Deadline
- Selection criteria
- Contact information

**Legal considerations:**
- Follow FTC guidelines
- No discriminatory criteria
- Clear terms and conditions
- Privacy policy for applications
- Actually award the scholarship

### Creating Your Scholarship Page

**Page structure:**

```
[SCHOLARSHIP NAME] Scholarship

Award Amount: $X

Eligibility:
• [Criterion 1]
• [Criterion 2]
• [Criterion 3]

How to Apply:
[Application instructions]

Deadline: [Date]

Selection Criteria:
[How winners are chosen]

About [Your Company]:
[Brief company description]

Previous Winners:
[If applicable]

Contact:
[Email/form for questions]
```

### Award Amount Guidelines

**Minimum credible amounts:**
- $500: Entry level
- $1,000: Standard
- $2,500: Strong
- $5,000+: Premium

**Budget consideration:**
Including marketing value, $1,000-2,500 is often optimal.

### Eligibility Design

**Common criteria:**
- Currently enrolled students
- Specific field of study
- GPA requirements
- Essay requirements
- Demographic focus (optional)

**Relevance to your business:**
Try to connect scholarship to your industry:
- Marketing company → Marketing students
- Tech company → Computer science students
- Finance company → Business students

### Finding Contact Information

**Look for:**
- Financial aid office contact
- Scholarship coordinator email
- Webmaster (for link updates)
- Submission forms

**Common contacts:**
- scholarships@[university].edu
- finaid@[university].edu
- financialaid@[university].edu

### Building Your List

**Target volume:**
100-300 schools for a campaign

**Organize by:**
- School name
- Scholarship page URL
- Contact email
- Submission method
- Status

## Outreach Strategy

### Email Template

**Subject:** Scholarship listing for [School Name] students

```
Hi,

I'm reaching out from [Company Name] regarding our [Scholarship Name] scholarship program.

We're offering a $[Amount] scholarship for [eligibility criteria], and we'd love for your students to have the opportunity to apply.

Scholarship details:
• Award: $[Amount]
• Eligibility: [Brief criteria]
• Deadline: [Date]
• Apply at: [URL]

Would it be possible to add this to your scholarship resources page? I've included all the details above, but happy to provide anything else you need.

Thank you for considering this opportunity for your students.

Best regards,
[Your Name]
[Company Name]
[Contact Information]
```

### Follow-Up Approach

**Timing:**
- First follow-up: 10-14 days
- Second follow-up: 21-28 days
- Stop after second follow-up

**Follow-up template:**

```
Hi,

I wanted to follow up on my email about the [Scholarship Name] scholarship for your students.

The deadline is approaching on [date], so I wanted to make sure students have a chance to apply.

Here's the link again: [URL]

Let me know if you need any additional information.

Thank you,
[Your Name]
```

### Response Handling

**Common responses:**

"Send more information"
→ Provide full scholarship details, deadline, link

"We'll add it"
→ Thank them, confirm details, request notification when live

"Fill out this form"
→ Complete their submission process

"We don't list outside scholarships"
→ Thank them, remove from list

## Cost and ROI

### Annual Costs

**Scholarship award:** $1,000-5,000
**Page creation:** $200-500 (one-time)
**Outreach time/cost:** $500-1,500
**Ongoing management:** $200-500

**Total first year:** $2,000-7,500
**Total ongoing:** $1,500-6,000/year

### Expected Results

**Realistic outcomes:**
- Outreach to: 200 schools
- Response rate: 10-20%
- Link rate: 5-15%
- Links earned: 10-30

### ROI Calculation

**Investment:** $3,000
**Links earned:** 20
**Average DR:** 70
**Cost per link:** $150
**Comparable .edu link value:** $300-500+

**ROI: 100-200%** (on link value alone)

Additional benefits:
- Brand visibility
- Student engagement
- Corporate responsibility image
- PR opportunities

## Maintaining Your Scholarship

### Annual Requirements

**Before deadline:**
- Promote scholarship
- Collect applications
- Review submissions

**Selection:**
- Choose winner(s) based on criteria
- Notify winner(s)
- Obtain permission for publicity

**After selection:**
- Award funds
- Update website with winner(s)
- Press release (optional)
- Thank participating schools

### Link Maintenance

**Annual outreach:**
- Re-contact schools for new year
- Update deadline information
- Reach out to new schools

**Link monitoring:**
- Check existing links annually
- Request reinstatement if removed
- Update details if pages change

## Ethical Considerations

### Legitimate Scholarship Requirements

**You must:**
- Actually award the scholarship
- Follow through on commitments
- Provide promised funds
- Maintain program year over year

**Unethical approaches:**
- Creating fake scholarships
- Never awarding
- Stopping program after links earned
- Misleading eligibility criteria

### Sustainability

**Plan for long-term:**
- Budget annually
- Build into marketing spend
- Consider multi-year commitment
- Genuinely help students

## Scaling Considerations

### Multiple Scholarships

**Options:**
- Different amounts for different criteria
- Named scholarships for different audiences
- Regional scholarships
- Field-specific scholarships

### Team Involvement

**Engage employees:**
- Scholarship committee
- Application review
- Winner selection
- Award presentation

### PR Integration

**Maximize exposure:**
- Press releases for awards
- Winner spotlights
- Social media coverage
- Local news opportunities

## Summary

Scholarship link building works when:

1. **Scholarship is legitimate** - Real award, actually given
2. **Outreach is professional** - Helpful, not spammy
3. **Commitment is long-term** - Annual program
4. **ROI justifies investment** - Links + brand value
5. **Ethics maintained** - Genuine benefit to students

A $1,000-2,500 scholarship with proper execution can generate 10-30 high-authority .edu links annually—one of the most cost-effective tactics for high-quality links.
