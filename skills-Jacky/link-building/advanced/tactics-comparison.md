## When to Use Each Tactic

### Guest Posting

**Best for:**
- Building consistent link volume
- Content marketing integration
- Establishing thought leadership
- Targeting specific niches
- Long-term relationship building

**Avoid when:**
- Need immediate results
- Very limited budget
- No content creation capacity
- Targeting only DR 70+ sites

**Ideal client profiles:**
- SaaS companies
- B2B services
- Content-driven businesses
- Companies with subject matter experts

**Resource requirements:**
- Content writer or budget for content
- Outreach capacity
- 4-8 weeks timeline
- $150-400 per placement

### Niche Edits

**Best for:**
- Quick wins needed
- Budget efficiency
- Supplementing guest posts
- Aged link profile building
- Specific page authority boosting

**Avoid when:**
- Client has strict quality requirements
- Building long-term relationships
- Need content control
- Want author byline visibility

**Ideal client profiles:**
- E-commerce sites
- Affiliate sites
- Local businesses
- Companies needing quick results

**Resource requirements:**
- Prospecting capacity
- Outreach skills
- 2-4 weeks timeline
- $100-300 per placement

### Digital PR

**Best for:**
- Building top-tier authority
- Brand building
- Competitive industries
- Companies with news/data
- Long-term SEO strategy

**Avoid when:**
- Need predictable results
- Limited budget
- No newsworthy angles
- Impatient timeline expectations

**Ideal client profiles:**
- Funded startups
- Established brands
- Companies with unique data
- Consumer-facing businesses

**Resource requirements:**
- PR skills or agency
- Newsworthy content/angles
- 6-12 weeks timeline
- $3000-15000/month

### HARO/Expert Responses

**Best for:**
- Earning high-authority links
- Building expert credibility
- Low-budget campaigns
- Companies with expertise
- Supplementing other tactics

**Avoid when:**
- No genuine expertise
- Can't respond quickly
- Need guaranteed volume
- Time is more valuable than money

**Ideal client profiles:**
- Professional services
- Industry experts
- B2B companies
- Anyone with genuine expertise

**Resource requirements:**
- Subject matter expertise
- Quick response capability
- 3-5 hours/week minimum
- Patience (low conversion rate)

### Resource Page Link Building

**Best for:**
- Building foundational links
- Lower-competition niches
- .edu and .gov opportunities
- Companies with useful resources
- Supplementing other tactics

**Avoid when:**
- Need high-DR links only
- Resource pages are saturated in niche
- Don't have genuinely useful content
- Need quick volume

**Ideal client profiles:**
- Educational services
- Non-profits
- Tool providers
- Content publishers

**Resource requirements:**
- Quality resource content
- Prospecting capacity
- 4-8 weeks timeline
- $50-200 per placement

### Broken Link Building

**Best for:**
- Win-win value proposition
- Earning goodwill while building links
- Finding opportunities others miss
- Replacing competitor links
- Building to specific pages

**Avoid when:**
- Need high volume
- Few broken links exist in niche
- Don't have replacement content
- Limited prospecting resources

**Ideal client profiles:**
- Companies with comprehensive content
- Those willing to create content
- Niches with aging resources
- Patient campaign managers

**Resource requirements:**
- Content (existing or to create)
- Prospecting tools and time
- 3-6 weeks timeline
- $75-200 per placement

### Skyscraper Technique

**Best for:**
- Creating lasting linkable assets
- Competitive differentiation
- Niches where content quality varies
- Long-term authority building
- Combining with other promotion

**Avoid when:**
- Can't invest in exceptional content
- Need quick results
- Niche already has excellent content
- No promotion capability

**Ideal client profiles:**
- Content-focused brands
- Companies with expertise
- Well-funded campaigns
- Patient long-term thinkers

**Resource requirements:**
- Significant content investment
- Promotion capacity
- 8-16 weeks timeline
- $200-500 per link (amortized)

### Local Link Building

**Best for:**
- Local businesses
- Multi-location companies
- Local SEO focus
- Community-oriented brands
- Building NAP consistency

**Avoid when:**
- National/global focus only
- No local presence
- Can't participate in community
- Need high-DR links

**Ideal client profiles:**
- Local service businesses
- Restaurants and retail
- Professional services with offices
- Franchises

**Resource requirements:**
- Local community involvement
- Directory management
- Sponsorship budget (optional)
- Ongoing maintenance

### Scholarship Link Building

**Best for:**
- Earning .edu links
- Corporate social responsibility
- Long-term link building
- Brand reputation
- Tax benefits

**Avoid when:**
- Very limited budget
- Need quick results
- Can't commit to annual program
- Ethics concerns about approach

**Ideal client profiles:**
- Established businesses
- Companies with CSR focus
- Those valuing .edu links
- Brands targeting young demographics

**Resource requirements:**
- $1000-5000+ annual award
- Scholarship page creation
- Outreach to schools
- Annual management

### Parasite SEO

**Best for:**
- Quick visibility
- Diversifying link profile
- Testing content before investing
- Building platform presence
- Thought leadership

**Avoid when:**
- Need dofollow links only
- Don't want platform dependency
- Strict "owned media" preference
- Concerns about platform risk

**Ideal client profiles:**
- Thought leaders
- SaaS companies
- Professional services
- Anyone with expertise to share

**Resource requirements:**
- Quality content creation
- Platform presence building
- Ongoing content commitment
- 1-4 weeks timeline

## Combining Tactics for Maximum Impact

### The Foundation Layer (60% of effort)

**Primary tactics:**
- Guest posting (consistent volume)
- Niche edits (efficiency and speed)

**Why:**
- Predictable delivery
- Scalable processes
- Controllable outcomes
- Relationship building

### The Authority Layer (30% of effort)

**Premium tactics:**
- Digital PR campaigns
- HARO responses
- Scholarship programs

**Why:**
- High-authority links
- Brand building
- Natural link profile
- Credibility establishment

### The Supplement Layer (10% of effort)

**Opportunistic tactics:**
- Broken link building
- Resource page outreach
- Local opportunities
- Skyscraper promotion

**Why:**
- Fills gaps
- Diversifies profile
- Takes advantage of opportunities
- Varies link sources

## Risk Management by Tactic

### Low Risk Tactics (< 2% penalty chance)

- Digital PR (earned coverage)
- HARO responses
- Scholarship links
- Local directories
- Resource page outreach
- Broken link building

**Characteristics:**
- Provide genuine value
- Would exist without SEO
- Editorial in nature
- Natural acquisition pattern

### Medium Risk Tactics (2-10% penalty chance)

- Guest posting (quality focused)
- Niche edits (quality sites)
- Skyscraper outreach
- Parasite SEO

**Risk mitigation:**
- Maintain quality standards
- Diversify sources
- Natural anchor distribution
- Reasonable velocity

### Higher Risk Tactics (> 10% penalty chance)

- Link exchanges at scale
- Expired domain schemes
- PBN usage
- Low-quality guest posts

**Risk mitigation:**
- Limit to small percentage of profile
- Diversify extensively
- Monitor for footprints
- Have recovery plan

## Making Your Tactical Selection

### Decision Framework

**Step 1: Assess your situation**
- Budget available
- Timeline expectations
- Risk tolerance
- Resource availability
- Niche competitiveness

**Step 2: Identify primary tactic**
Based on situation, select 1-2 primary tactics for 60% of effort.

**Step 3: Add authority layer**
Select 1-2 tactics for high-authority, lower-volume links.

**Step 4: Plan supplements**
Identify opportunistic tactics to diversify.

**Step 5: Allocate resources**
Distribute budget, time, and people across selected tactics.

**Step 6: Set metrics**
Define success for each tactic and overall campaign.

**Step 7: Review and adjust**
Monthly review of what's working; quarterly strategic adjustment.

## Summary

Choosing the right tactics requires:

1. **Understanding each tactic** - Strengths, weaknesses, requirements
2. **Matching to situation** - Budget, timeline, risk tolerance
3. **Combining strategically** - Foundation + authority + supplements
4. **Allocating resources** - Based on priorities and capabilities
5. **Managing risk** - Diversification and quality focus
6. **Continuous optimization** - Adjust based on results

No single tactic is universally best. The right approach depends on your specific situation, and successful link building usually combines multiple tactics.
