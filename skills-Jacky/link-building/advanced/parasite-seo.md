# Parasite SEO for Links

## What Is Parasite SEO?

Parasite SEO involves publishing content on high-authority third-party platforms to benefit from their domain authority. For link building purposes, this means creating content on platforms like Medium, LinkedIn, or other high-DR sites that can link to your main site.

### The Concept

Instead of building authority to your own domain (slow), you "borrow" authority from established platforms (fast) by:
1. Publishing on high-DR platforms
2. Getting that content to rank
3. Linking to your site from that content

### Why "Parasite"?

You're leveraging the host platform's authority—they do the hard work of building domain trust, you benefit from it.

## Using High-DR Platforms

### Platform Options

**Content platforms:**
- Medium (DR 96)
- LinkedIn Articles (DR 99)
- Substack (DR 90+)
- HubPages (DR 83)
- Vocal Media (DR 75)

**Web 2.0:**
- WordPress.com (DR 94)
- Blogger/Blogspot (DR 98)
- Tumblr (DR 95)
- Weebly (DR 92)

**Q&A and Forums:**
- Quora (DR 93)
- Reddit (DR 92)

**Document sharing:**
- SlideShare (DR 95)
- Scribd (DR 92)
- Issuu (DR 94)

### Platform Considerations

| Platform | DR | Link Follow? | Ease of Use | Risk Level |
|----------|----|--------------| ------------|------------|
| Medium | 96 | Nofollow | Easy | Low |
| LinkedIn | 99 | Nofollow | Easy | Low |
| WordPress.com | 94 | Dofollow (free tier limited) | Easy | Low |
| Quora | 93 | Nofollow | Moderate | Low |
| Reddit | 92 | Nofollow | Hard | Moderate |
| Web 2.0s | 90+ | Often dofollow | Easy | Moderate-High |

### Content Strategy on Platforms

**Quality requirements:**
- Content must provide genuine value
- Not just a link vehicle
- Follows platform guidelines
- Engages platform's audience

**Optimization:**
- Keyword-optimize for platform search
- Optimize for Google (platform pages rank)
- Internal links within platform
- Strategic links to your site

## Medium Strategy

### Setting Up

1. Create Medium account
2. Complete profile professionally
3. Connect to relevant publications
4. Establish posting history

### Content Approach

**What works on Medium:**
- Thought leadership pieces
- Personal experience stories
- How-to guides
- Industry analysis
- Curated insights

**Link placement:**
- Natural within content
- As reference/source
- In author bio section
- Don't over-link

### Ranking Medium Content

**Medium SEO factors:**
- Content quality/engagement
- Claps and comments
- Read time
- Publication placement
- External links to article

**Strategy:**
1. Publish quality content
2. Promote externally for engagement
3. Build links to Medium article
4. Let authority flow through

### Medium Limitations

- Links are nofollow
- Requires ongoing content
- Platform can change policies
- Limited control
- Competes with your own site

## LinkedIn Article Strategy

### Publishing on LinkedIn

**Requirements:**
- Professional profile
- Some connections/followers
- "Newsletter" feature access (optional)

**Content types:**
- Professional insights
- Industry commentary
- Career advice
- Business analysis

### Link Building with LinkedIn

**In articles:**
- Link to resources you've created
- Reference your site as source
- Include in relevant context

**In newsletter:**
- Build subscriber base
- Ongoing link opportunities
- Direct traffic + visibility

### LinkedIn Advantages

- Massive professional audience
- High trust signal
- Content stays visible
- Drives direct traffic
- Relationship building

### LinkedIn Limitations

- Nofollow links
- Professional context only
- Audience expects certain content
- Limited SEO impact

## Risks and Rewards

### Potential Rewards

**Ranking benefits:**
- Platform content can rank quickly
- Passes some authority via links
- Diversifies link profile

**Traffic benefits:**
- Platform audiences
- Search traffic to platform content
- Click-throughs to your site

**Brand benefits:**
- Visibility on known platforms
- Credibility association
- Thought leadership

### Potential Risks

**Platform risks:**
- Content can be removed
- Platform policies change
- Accounts can be banned
- Nofollow may become stricter

**SEO risks:**
- Google's stance unclear
- May become devalued
- Could be seen as manipulation
- Competes with own content

**Business risks:**
- Dependency on third parties
- Limited control
- Work lost if removed
- Building value elsewhere

### Risk Mitigation

1. **Diversify** - Don't rely on one platform
2. **Quality focus** - Genuinely useful content
3. **Follow guidelines** - Stay within platform rules
4. **Backup content** - Keep copies of everything
5. **Balance** - Use alongside owned properties

## Best Practices

### Do's

- Create genuinely valuable content
- Follow platform guidelines
- Build real audience on platform
- Link naturally and sparingly
- Use as complement, not replacement

### Don'ts

- Spam with low-quality content
- Over-optimize for links
- Violate platform terms
- Rely entirely on parasites
- Ignore platform audiences

### Quality Guidelines

**Content should:**
- Stand alone as valuable
- Engage platform's audience
- Follow platform norms
- Include links naturally
- Be something you're proud of

### Link Guidelines

**Best practices:**
- 1-3 links per substantial article
- Contextually relevant
- Mixed anchor text
- To genuinely relevant pages
- Not every article needs links

## Strategy Integration

### Parasite SEO in Link Building Program

**As supplementary tactic (recommended):**
- 10-20% of link building effort
- Supplement owned content
- Diversify link sources
- Quick wins while building authority

**As primary tactic (not recommended):**
- Too much dependency
- Limited control
- Risk concentration
- Not building owned assets

### Workflow

**Monthly allocation:**
- 2-4 platform articles
- Quality content on each
- Strategic link placement
- Promotion for engagement

**Build genuine presence:**
- Engage on platforms
- Build followers
- Create valuable content
- Establish thought leadership

## Summary

Parasite SEO for links offers:

**Benefits:**
- Quick authority borrowing
- High-DR link sources
- Traffic and visibility
- Diversification

**Limitations:**
- Mostly nofollow
- Platform dependency
- Limited control
- Risk of removal

**Best use:**
- Supplement to main strategy
- Quality content focus
- Diversification play
- Thought leadership building

Use judiciously as part of a broader, diversified link building strategy.
