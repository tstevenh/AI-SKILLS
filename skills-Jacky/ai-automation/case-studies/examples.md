## Case Study 1: SaaS Company (50 Employees)

### Company Profile
- **Industry:** B2B SaaS (Project Management)
- **Stage:** Series A, $5M ARR
- **Team:** 50 employees
- **Challenges:** Support scaling, lead quality, content velocity

### Automation Stack

**Infrastructure:**
- n8n (self-hosted) - $50/month
- Claude API - $400/month
- GPT-4o-mini - $100/month

**Marketing Automation:**
- Blog post pipeline: Topic → Research → Draft → Edit
- Social media repurposing: Blog → 10 social posts
- Email personalization at scale
- **Monthly cost:** $200

**Sales Automation:**
- Inbound lead enrichment (Apollo + Clay)
- Lead scoring with AI
- Personalized outreach sequences
- Meeting prep automation
- **Monthly cost:** $500

**Support Automation:**
- Ticket classification and routing
- AI response drafting (40% of tickets)
- Auto-resolution for FAQs (25% of tickets)
- **Monthly cost:** $300

### Results (6 months)

| Metric | Before | After | Change |
|--------|--------|-------|--------|
| Support tickets/agent/day | 35 | 55 | +57% |
| First response time | 4 hours | 30 min | -88% |
| Content pieces/month | 4 | 16 | +300% |
| Qualified leads/month | 50 | 120 | +140% |
| Sales team capacity | 100% | 60% | Better focus |

### Financial Impact

```
Monthly costs:
- Infrastructure: $50
- AI APIs: $500
- Tools (Clay, Apollo): $450
Total: $1,000/month

Monthly savings:
- Support (2 FTEs avoided): $8,000
- Content (1 FTE avoided): $4,000
- Sales (time savings): $2,000
Total: $14,000/month

Net ROI: 13x
Payback: <1 month
```

### Key Learnings

1. **Start with support** - Highest volume, clearest ROI
2. **Quality gates matter** - 10% of AI responses needed human correction initially
3. **Model routing saves money** - Using mini for simple tasks cut costs 60%
4. **Team adoption takes time** - 3 months to full comfort

## Common Patterns Across Cases

### What Works

1. **Start with high-volume, low-risk tasks**
   - Support ticket triage
   - Content drafting
   - Data enrichment

2. **Build human-in-the-loop first**
   - AI drafts, humans review
   - Builds trust and catches errors
   - Gradual autonomy increase

3. **Invest in quality systems**
   - Review workflows
   - Feedback loops
   - Performance monitoring

4. **Document everything**
   - Prompts that work
   - Edge cases
   - Cost per task

### What Doesn't Work

1. **Automating without understanding**
   - Process must be clear first
   - AI amplifies existing systems

2. **Ignoring quality**
   - Bad output = lost trust
   - Start conservative

3. **Over-engineering early**
   - Simple > complex
   - Add sophistication as needed

4. **Forgetting maintenance**
   - Automations need updates
   - APIs change, models improve
