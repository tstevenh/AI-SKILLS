# Cold Email Glossary

A comprehensive reference of terms, acronyms, and concepts used in cold email.

---

## A

### A/B Testing
The practice of testing two variations of an email element (subject line, CTA, etc.) against each other to determine which performs better.

### ABM (Account-Based Marketing)
A strategic approach that concentrates sales and marketing resources on a specific set of target accounts, using personalized campaigns designed to engage each account.

### ACV (Annual Contract Value)
The annualized revenue from a customer contract. Used to prioritize prospects and calculate ROI.

### Automation
The use of software to perform repetitive tasks without manual intervention, such as sending scheduled emails or updating CRM records.

### Auto-Responder
An automatic reply sent when someone emails a specific address or triggers a specific action.

---

## B

### B2B (Business-to-Business)
Commerce or communication between businesses, as opposed to between a business and individual consumers.

### BDR (Business Development Representative)
A sales role focused on outbound prospecting and qualifying leads before handing them to account executives.

### Blacklist
A database of IP addresses or domains that have been flagged for sending spam. Being on a blacklist significantly impacts deliverability.

### Bounce
When an email fails to be delivered to the recipient's inbox.

### Bounce Rate
The percentage of sent emails that fail to be delivered.

### Breakup Email
The final email in a sequence, acknowledging that you'll stop emailing and leaving the door open for future contact.

### Bump Email
A short follow-up email designed to bring a previous message back to the top of the recipient's inbox.

### Buyer Persona
A semi-fictional representation of your ideal customer based on market research and real data about your existing customers.

---

## C

### CAC (Customer Acquisition Cost)
The total cost of acquiring a new customer, including sales and marketing expenses.

### Cadence
The timing and frequency pattern of emails in a sequence.

### CAN-SPAM
The Controlling the Assault of Non-Solicited Pornography And Marketing Act, the US law governing commercial email.

### CASL
Canada's Anti-Spam Legislation, one of the strictest email regulations globally.

### Catch-All Domain
A domain configured to accept emails sent to any address, making verification difficult.

### Clay
A popular tool for data enrichment and AI-powered personalization at scale.

### Click-Through Rate (CTR)
The percentage of email recipients who click on one or more links in an email.

### Cold Email
Unsolicited email sent to prospects with no prior relationship, typically for business development purposes.

### Conversion Rate
The percentage of recipients who complete a desired action (reply, book meeting, etc.).

### CRM (Customer Relationship Management)
Software for managing company relationships and interactions with customers and prospects.

### CTA (Call to Action)
The specific action you want the email recipient to take, typically expressed as a question or instruction.

---

## D

### Deliverability
The ability of an email to reach the recipient's inbox (as opposed to spam folder or being blocked).

### DKIM (DomainKeys Identified Mail)
An email authentication method that allows receivers to verify that an email was authorized by the domain owner.

### DMARC (Domain-based Message Authentication, Reporting & Conformance)
An email authentication protocol that builds on SPF and DKIM to protect domains from unauthorized use.

### DNS (Domain Name System)
The system that translates domain names to IP addresses, including records for email authentication.

### Domain Reputation
A score assigned by email providers based on sending history, affecting deliverability.

### Domain Warming
The process of gradually increasing email volume from a new domain to build positive reputation.

### Double Opt-In
A process where subscribers confirm their subscription by clicking a link in a confirmation email.

### Drip Campaign
A marketing strategy that sends a pre-written set of emails to prospects over time.

---

## E

### Email Deliverability
See Deliverability.

### Email Service Provider (ESP)
A company that offers email marketing services, including sending infrastructure and tools.

### Engagement Rate
A measure of how recipients interact with emails (opens, clicks, replies).

### Enrichment
The process of adding additional data to existing prospect records (company info, social profiles, etc.).

### Enterprise
Large organizations, typically 1,000+ employees, with complex buying processes and longer sales cycles.

---

## F

### First-Party Data
Data collected directly from your own sources (website, CRM, etc.) rather than purchased or scraped.

### Follow-Up
Subsequent emails sent after an initial outreach, typically part of a sequence.

### Funnel
The stages a prospect goes through from first contact to becoming a customer.

---

## G

### Gateway Filter
The first layer of spam filtering that checks emails at the server level before they reach the inbox.

### GDPR (General Data Protection Regulation)
The EU's comprehensive data protection law with significant implications for email marketing.

### GTM (Go-to-Market)
The strategy and tactics used to bring a product to market, including sales and marketing approaches.

---

## H

### Hard Bounce
A permanent email delivery failure, typically due to an invalid email address.

### Hybrid Sequence
A cold email sequence that incorporates multiple channels (email, LinkedIn, phone).

---

## I

### ICP (Ideal Customer Profile)
A detailed description of the type of company that would get the most value from your product or service.

### Inbox Placement
When an email arrives in the primary inbox rather than spam folder or promotions tab.

### Inbound
Leads or prospects who come to you (through content, ads, referrals) rather than outbound prospecting.

### Intent Data
Information indicating that a company is actively researching topics related to your solution.

### IP Address
A unique numerical address assigned to devices on the internet, including email servers.

### IP Reputation
The trustworthiness score associated with an IP address used for sending email.

### IP Warming
The process of gradually increasing email volume from a new IP address to build positive reputation.

---

## L

### Lead
A potential customer who has shown some interest or fits your target profile.

### Lead Magnet
Content offered in exchange for contact information (ebooks, guides, templates).

### Lead Scoring
Assigning numerical values to leads based on their likelihood to convert.

### Legitimate Interest
Under GDPR, a legal basis for processing personal data without explicit consent in certain B2B scenarios.

### LTV (Lifetime Value)
The total revenue expected from a customer over the entire relationship.

---

## M

### Mail Merge
The process of personalizing mass emails with individual data (names, company names, etc.).

### Mailbox Provider (MBP)
Companies that provide email services (Gmail, Outlook, Yahoo).

### Marketing Automation
Technology that automates marketing processes like email campaigns, lead scoring, and segmentation.

### Meeting Rate
The percentage of positive replies that convert to scheduled meetings.

### Merge Field/Variable
A placeholder in an email template that gets replaced with personalized data.

### MQL (Marketing Qualified Lead)
A lead deemed more likely to become a customer based on marketing engagement.

### MX Record
DNS record that specifies the mail server responsible for receiving email for a domain.

### Multi-Threading
The practice of reaching out to multiple contacts at the same target company.

---

## N

### Negative Reply
A response indicating disinterest or requesting to be removed from future communications.

### Nurture Sequence
A series of emails designed to maintain contact with leads not yet ready to buy.

---

## O

### Objection
A reason given by a prospect for not proceeding (price, timing, existing solution, etc.).

### Open Rate
The percentage of delivered emails that are opened by recipients.

### Opt-In
When a user agrees to receive communications.

### Opt-Out
When a user requests to stop receiving communications.

### Outbound
Proactive prospecting where you reach out to potential customers (cold email, cold calling).

### OTE (On-Target Earnings)
Total expected compensation for a sales role when quota is met (base + commission).

---

## P

### PAS (Problem-Agitate-Solution)
A copywriting framework that identifies a problem, amplifies its impact, then presents a solution.

### PECR
Privacy and Electronic Communications Regulations, UK rules governing electronic marketing.

### Personalization
Customizing email content based on recipient data to increase relevance and engagement.

### Pipeline
The total value of active sales opportunities at various stages of the sales process.

### Plain Text Email
Email containing only text without HTML formatting.

### Positive Reply
A response indicating interest in learning more or continuing the conversation.

### Postmaster Tools
Free tools from email providers (like Google) that show sender reputation and deliverability metrics.

### Prospect
A potential customer who fits your ICP but hasn't yet engaged with your outreach.

### Prospecting
The process of identifying and reaching out to potential customers.

### PTR Record
A DNS record used for reverse DNS lookups, connecting IP addresses to domain names.

---

## Q

### Qualification
The process of determining whether a prospect is a good fit for your product or service.

### Quota
A target set for sales performance, typically measured in meetings, pipeline, or revenue.

---

## R

### Ramp
The period during which a new salesperson or mailbox gradually increases to full capacity.

### Reply Rate
The percentage of delivered emails that receive any response.

### Reputation
See Domain Reputation and IP Reputation.

### ROI (Return on Investment)
The ratio of net profit to the cost of investment.

---

## S

### SaaS (Software as a Service)
Software delivered over the internet on a subscription basis.

### Sales Navigator
LinkedIn's premium tool for sales prospecting with advanced search and tracking features.

### SDR (Sales Development Representative)
A sales role focused on outbound prospecting and qualifying leads.

### Seed Testing
Sending test emails to accounts you control to check inbox placement.

### Segmentation
Dividing your prospect list into groups based on shared characteristics.

### Sender Reputation
The overall trustworthiness score associated with your sending identity.

### Sequence
A series of pre-planned emails sent to a prospect over time.

### SMB (Small and Medium Business)
Companies typically under 500 employees with shorter sales cycles.

### SMTP (Simple Mail Transfer Protocol)
The standard protocol for sending emails across the internet.

### Soft Bounce
A temporary email delivery failure, often due to a full mailbox or server issue.

### Spam
Unsolicited email, typically sent in bulk, that recipients don't want.

### Spam Complaint
When a recipient marks an email as spam, negatively affecting sender reputation.

### Spam Filter
Technology that identifies and blocks or quarantines suspected spam emails.

### Spam Trap
Email addresses designed to catch senders with poor list practices.

### SPF (Sender Policy Framework)
An email authentication method that specifies which servers can send email for a domain.

### SQL (Sales Qualified Lead)
A lead that has been vetted by sales and is deemed ready for direct sales engagement.

### Subject Line
The text that appears in the recipient's inbox before opening the email.

### Suppression List
A list of email addresses that should never be contacted.

---

## T

### TAM (Total Addressable Market)
The total market demand for a product or service.

### Technographics
Data about the technology stack a company uses.

### Third-Party Data
Data purchased from external sources rather than collected firsthand.

### Touchpoint
Any interaction between your company and a prospect.

### Tracking Pixel
A small image embedded in emails to track opens.

### Trigger Event
A change or event that creates a sales opportunity (funding, hiring, news).

---

## U

### Unsubscribe
The action of opting out of future email communications.

### Unsubscribe Rate
The percentage of recipients who unsubscribe from an email campaign.

---

## V

### Value Proposition
The unique benefit your product or service offers to customers.

### Verification
The process of confirming that email addresses are valid and deliverable.

---

## W

### Warm-Up
The process of gradually establishing positive reputation for a new mailbox or domain.

### Waterfall Enrichment
Trying multiple data sources sequentially to maximize coverage.

### Webinar
An online seminar or presentation, often used for lead generation.

---

## Z

### ZoomInfo
A leading B2B contact and company database provider.
