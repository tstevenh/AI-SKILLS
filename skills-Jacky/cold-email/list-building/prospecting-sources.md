## Prospecting Source Overview

### Categories of Prospecting Sources

**Direct Databases**: Platforms with contact information
- Apollo.io
- ZoomInfo
- Cognism
- Lusha

**Professional Networks**: Social platforms with business data
- LinkedIn Sales Navigator
- LinkedIn (free)

**Technology Detection**: Tools revealing tech stacks
- BuiltWith
- Wappalyzer
- HG Insights

**Signal Sources**: Platforms revealing buying signals
- Job boards (Indeed, LinkedIn Jobs)
- Funding databases (Crunchbase, PitchBook)
- News sources

**Review/Comparison Sites**: Platforms showing evaluation behavior
- G2
- Capterra
- TrustRadius

### Source Quality Hierarchy

| Source Type | Data Freshness | Accuracy | Signals | Cost |
|-------------|---------------|----------|---------|------|
| LinkedIn (manual) | Excellent | Excellent | Low | Time |
| Sales Navigator | Excellent | Excellent | Medium | $$$ |
| Apollo | Good | Good | Medium | $$ |
| ZoomInfo | Good | Excellent | High | $$$$ |
| Job postings | Excellent | N/A | High | Free/$ |
| Funding news | Excellent | N/A | High | Free/$ |

## LinkedIn Sales Navigator

### Overview

LinkedIn Sales Navigator is the premium LinkedIn tool for sales professionals. It's the gold standard for B2B prospecting, offering advanced search, lead tracking, and direct messaging.

### Pricing

| Plan | Monthly | Annual | Features |
|------|---------|--------|----------|
| Core | $99/mo | $79/mo billed annually | Lead search, 50 InMail/mo |
| Advanced | $149/mo | $125/mo billed annually | + TeamLink, CRM sync |
| Advanced Plus | Custom | Custom | + Data validation, ROI reporting |

### Key Features

**Lead Search Filters**:
- Job title (current, past, changed)
- Company (current, past)
- Geography (HQ, regional)
- Industry
- Company size
- Seniority level
- Years in position
- Keywords in profile

**Account Search Filters**:
- Industry
- Company size
- Headquarters region
- Company type
- Technologies used
- Revenue (limited)
- Follower count

**Lead Lists**:
- Save leads to lists
- Track lead activity
- Get alerts on changes
- Export (limited)

**InMail**:
- Direct message non-connections
- 50 credits/month (Core)
- Better response rates than email for some audiences

### Effective Sales Navigator Workflows

**Workflow 1: Title + Company Search**
1. Search for specific titles ("VP of Sales")
2. Filter by company size (51-200)
3. Filter by industry (SaaS)
4. Save leads to list
5. Research and reach out

**Workflow 2: Account-First Search**
1. Build account list matching ICP
2. Within each account, find relevant leads
3. Multi-thread (2-3 contacts per account)
4. Track engagement across account

**Workflow 3: Changed Jobs**
1. Filter for "Changed jobs in last 90 days"
2. New role = new budget, new initiatives
3. Reach out referencing the transition
4. Example: "Congrats on the new role at [Company]..."

**Workflow 4: Posted Content**
1. Monitor leads' posting activity
2. Engage with posts (like, comment)
3. Reference posts in outreach
4. Build familiarity before email

### Sales Navigator Best Practices

**Do**:
- Save searches for repeated use
- Use Boolean operators for precision
- Check lead activity before outreach
- Engage before pitching
- Export to CRM/sequencing tools

**Don't**:
- Mass InMail without personalization
- Ignore profile research opportunities
- Rely solely on Navigator (supplement with other sources)
- Violate LinkedIn ToS with excessive automation

### Exporting from Sales Navigator

Sales Navigator doesn't allow direct CSV export. Options:

**Manual Export**:
- Copy data manually (time-consuming)
- Use for small, high-value lists

**Chrome Extensions**:
- Tools like Evaboot, Phantombuster, Dripify
- Automate data extraction
- Check LinkedIn ToS compliance

**CRM Sync**:
- Sync leads to CRM (Advanced plans)
- Use CRM integrations for enrichment

## Apollo.io

### Overview

Apollo.io is the most popular all-in-one prospecting platform, combining a massive database with email sequences, enrichment, and CRM features.

### Pricing

| Plan | Monthly | Credits | Key Limits |
|------|---------|---------|------------|
| Free | $0 | 50/mo | Basic search |
| Basic | $49/mo | 900/yr | 200 exports/mo |
| Professional | $99/mo | 12,000/yr | Unlimited emails |
| Organization | $119/mo | 24,000/yr | + Team features |

### Database Size

- 275M+ contacts
- 73M+ companies
- 150M+ verified emails
- Regular updates

### Key Features

**Search Filters**:
- Job titles and seniority
- Department
- Company size
- Industry
- Location
- Technologies
- Funding status
- Revenue
- Keywords

**Enrichment**:
- Email addresses
- Phone numbers
- Company data
- Technology data

**Sequences**:
- Built-in email sequences
- A/B testing
- Basic automation

**Intent Data**:
- Buying signals
- Topic interest
- Web activity

### Effective Apollo Workflows

**Workflow 1: Basic ICP Search**
1. Set industry filters
2. Set company size range
3. Set job title keywords
4. Set location
5. Review results
6. Save to list
7. Verify emails
8. Export or add to sequence

**Workflow 2: Technology-Based Search**
1. Filter by technology (e.g., "Uses Salesforce")
2. Add other ICP criteria
3. Export contacts
4. Personalize based on tech stack

**Workflow 3: Funding Signal Search**
1. Filter by "Funding raised in last 6 months"
2. Filter by funding amount
3. Add role filters
4. Reach out referencing funding

**Workflow 4: Competitor Replacement**
1. Filter by competitor technology
2. Find relevant decision-makers
3. Craft replacement messaging
4. Execute competitive campaign

### Apollo Data Quality

**Strengths**:
- Large database
- Good tech/small company coverage
- Reasonable accuracy (85-90%)
- Regular updates

**Weaknesses**:
- Enterprise data less complete than ZoomInfo
- Some outdated records
- Phone numbers less reliable than email
- Verification recommended before large campaigns

### Maximizing Apollo Credits

**Credit Conservation**:
- Use search filters before revealing contacts
- Verify necessity before using credits
- Export in batches, not one-by-one
- Use free verification before export

**Credit Optimization**:
- Higher plans = better cost per credit
- Annual billing = bonus credits
- Credits roll over (check terms)

## ZoomInfo

### Overview

ZoomInfo is the enterprise-grade B2B intelligence platform with the most accurate data and extensive features. It's the gold standard for data quality but comes with enterprise pricing.

### Pricing

Custom pricing, typically:
- $15,000-$25,000/year (small teams)
- $25,000-$50,000/year (mid-market)
- $50,000-$100,000+/year (enterprise)

### Key Features

**Database**:
- 100M+ contacts
- High accuracy (95%+ for email)
- Deep company intelligence
- Org charts

**Intent Data**:
- Buyer intent signals
- Website visitor tracking
- Content consumption tracking

**Enrichment**:
- Real-time enrichment
- CRM integration
- Form enrichment

**Automation**:
- Workflows
- Automated enrichment
- Alerts

### When ZoomInfo Makes Sense

**Good Fit**:
- Enterprise sales targets
- High deal values ($50K+)
- Data accuracy critical
- Budget available
- Phone outreach important

**Not Ideal**:
- SMB targets (Apollo sufficient)
- Tight budgets
- Low-volume outreach
- Primarily email-only outreach

### Alternatives to ZoomInfo

If ZoomInfo pricing is prohibitive:

**Cognism**: Strong for Europe, good phone data
**Lusha**: Good for quick lookups
**Apollo**: Best value for most cases
**Multiple sources**: Combine cheaper tools

## Hunter.io

### Overview

Hunter.io is a focused email finding and verification tool. It's simpler than full platforms but excellent for email discovery.

### Pricing

| Plan | Monthly | Searches | Verifications |
|------|---------|----------|---------------|
| Free | $0 | 25/mo | 50/mo |
| Starter | $49/mo | 500/mo | 1,000/mo |
| Growth | $149/mo | 5,000/mo | 10,000/mo |
| Business | $499/mo | 50,000/mo | 100,000/mo |

### Key Features

**Domain Search**:
Find all emails associated with a domain.

Example: Search "acme.com" → Returns all known emails at Acme.

**Email Finder**:
Find specific person's email given name and domain.

Example: "John Smith" + "acme.com" → john.smith@acme.com

**Email Verifier**:
Check if email is valid and deliverable.

**Bulk Operations**:
Process lists at scale.

**API Access**:
Integrate into workflows.

### Effective Hunter Workflows

**Workflow 1: Company → Contacts**
1. Have list of target companies
2. Run domain search on each
3. Filter by job title keywords
4. Verify emails
5. Add to outreach list

**Workflow 2: LinkedIn → Email**
1. Find contact on LinkedIn
2. Note name and company
3. Use Email Finder
4. Verify result
5. Add to sequence

**Workflow 3: Bulk Verification**
1. Import list from any source
2. Bulk verify all emails
3. Remove invalid/risky
4. Export clean list

### Hunter vs. Full Platforms

**Use Hunter When**:
- Already have company/name data
- Need simple email finding
- Want affordable verification
- Building custom workflows

**Use Full Platform When**:
- Need prospecting search
- Want additional data (phone, company info)
- Need built-in sequences
- Want intent data

## BuiltWith and Wappalyzer

### Overview

Technology detection tools reveal what technologies websites use. This data is invaluable for targeting based on tech stack.

### BuiltWith

**Pricing**:
- Free: Limited searches
- Basic: $295/mo
- Pro: $495/mo
- Team: $995/mo

**Features**:
- Extensive technology detection
- Historical data
- Industry statistics
- Lead lists by technology
- API access

**Best For**: Finding companies using specific technologies.

### Wappalyzer

**Pricing**:
- Browser extension: Free
- API: From $250/mo

**Features**:
- Real-time technology detection
- Browser extension
- API access
- Bulk lookup

**Best For**: Quick lookups, integration into workflows.

### Using Technology Data for Prospecting

**Strategy 1: Integration Targeting**
Your product integrates with Salesforce → Find companies using Salesforce.

**Strategy 2: Competitor Replacement**
You compete with Mailchimp → Find companies using Mailchimp.

**Strategy 3: Technology Indicators**
Companies using Stripe likely process payments → SaaS companies.

**Strategy 4: Gap Filling**
Company uses marketing automation but no CRM → Sell CRM.

### Example Workflow

1. Identify technologies that indicate ICP
2. Pull list from BuiltWith/Wappalyzer
3. Enrich with contact data (Apollo, Hunter)
4. Verify emails
5. Personalize based on tech stack
6. Execute campaign

**Personalization Example**:
"I noticed [Company] is using HubSpot for marketing. Most teams on HubSpot eventually want [feature we provide] but have to use [workaround]. We solve that..."

## Job Postings as Signals

### Why Job Postings Matter

Job postings reveal:
- **Budget**: They're spending on a role
- **Priorities**: They care about this function
- **Pain points**: Job descriptions reveal challenges
- **Timing**: They're actively building capability
- **Decision-makers**: Who they're hiring for/reporting to

### Job Posting Sources

**LinkedIn Jobs**: Largest professional job board
**Indeed**: Broad coverage
**Glassdoor**: With company reviews
**Company Career Pages**: Direct source
**Angel.co (Wellfound)**: Startups
**Industry-Specific Boards**: Niche roles

### Using Job Postings for Prospecting

**Signal 1: Hiring for Role Your Product Supports**

They're hiring SDRs → They might need sales tools.
They're hiring data engineers → They might need data tools.

**Signal 2: Job Description Language**

Look for pain points in requirements:
"Must manage multiple tools..." → Consolidation opportunity
"Experience with manual reporting..." → Automation opportunity

**Signal 3: Volume of Hiring**

Company posting 20 sales roles → Rapid growth, budget available.

**Signal 4: Specific Tool Mentions**

"Experience with Salesforce required" → Salesforce ecosystem plays.

### Job Posting Workflow

1. Set up alerts for relevant job titles
2. Monitor daily/weekly
3. Research company when signal appears
4. Find relevant decision-maker
5. Reference hiring in outreach

**Example Email**:
"Noticed [Company] is hiring several account executives—congrats on the growth. As you scale the team, [challenge we solve] usually becomes a bottleneck. We helped [similar company] address this by..."

### Job Posting Monitoring Tools

**Google Alerts**: Free, basic
**LinkedIn Job Alerts**: Role-specific
**Indeed Alerts**: Broad coverage
**Clay**: Automated enrichment workflows
**Custom scrapers**: For advanced users

## Funding Announcements

### Why Funding Matters

Funding announcements signal:
- **Available budget**: They just received capital
- **Growth mode**: They're planning to scale
- **Timing**: They're investing in growth areas
- **Validation**: Investors believe in them
- **Decision-makers**: Leadership is likely evaluating tools

### Funding Data Sources

**Crunchbase**:
- Most comprehensive funding database
- Company profiles
- Investment details
- API available
- Free tier limited, Pro from $49/mo

**PitchBook**:
- Enterprise-grade data
- Deep financial details
- Expensive ($thousands/year)

**LinkedIn News**:
- Funding announcements
- Free but manual

**TechCrunch, VentureBeat, etc.**:
- News coverage
- Free
- Not comprehensive

**Apollo Funding Filters**:
- Built into prospecting
- Filter by funding stage/recency

### Using Funding Signals

**Strategy 1: Recent Funding Filter**
Filter for companies that raised in last 3-6 months.

**Strategy 2: Funding Stage Targeting**
- Seed: Early stage, limited budget, founder-led sales
- Series A: Growth mode, building teams, budget opening
- Series B+: Established, significant budget, defined processes

**Strategy 3: Funding Amount**
Larger rounds = larger budgets.
$5M round → different budget than $50M round.

### Funding Outreach Workflow

1. Set up alerts for funding in target segments
2. When funding announced, research company
3. Find relevant stakeholders
4. Craft funding-relevant outreach
5. Execute within 2-4 weeks of announcement

**Example Email**:
"Congrats on the Series A—exciting milestone. As you scale from [current state] to [likely goals], most companies at your stage run into [challenge]. We help with that—worked with [similar recently-funded company] on exactly this. Worth a quick chat?"

## Review Sites (G2, Capterra)

### Why Review Sites Matter

**G2**, **Capterra**, and similar sites reveal:
- **Active evaluation**: Companies comparing solutions
- **Technology stack**: What they're using
- **Pain points**: What they complain about in reviews
- **Satisfaction levels**: Unhappy customers = replacement opportunity

### G2 for Prospecting

**G2 Buyer Intent**:
- Shows companies researching your category
- Integrates with sales tools
- Requires paid plan

**G2 Competitor Tracking**:
- See who reviews competitors
- Identify unhappy competitor users
- Find comparison shoppers

**How to Use**:
1. Set up G2 intent tracking
2. Get alerts when companies research your category
3. Identify specific contacts at those companies
4. Reach out while they're actively evaluating

### Capterra and Others

**Capterra**:
- Gartner-owned
- Strong SMB coverage
- Review data visible

**TrustRadius**:
- In-depth reviews
- Enterprise focus
- Buyer intent data

**Software Advice**:
- Capterra sister site
- SMB focus

### Review-Based Prospecting Workflow

**Workflow 1: Intent-Based**
1. Subscribe to category intent data
2. Receive alerts when companies research
3. Research the company
4. Reach out while evaluating

**Workflow 2: Competitor Review Mining**
1. Read competitor reviews
2. Identify common complaints
3. Find companies with those complaints
4. Reach out addressing the specific pain point

**Example Email**:
"I noticed in your G2 review of [competitor] you mentioned [specific complaint]. That's actually why [similar company] switched to us last quarter—we specifically addressed [the issue]. Would it be worth a quick comparison?"

## Industry Directories

### Overview

Industry-specific directories list companies and contacts in particular verticals. They're excellent for niche targeting.

### Types of Directories

**Industry Associations**:
- Member directories
- Often requires membership
- High-quality, targeted data

**Trade Publication Lists**:
- "Top 100" lists
- Ranked companies
- Usually public

**Government Databases**:
- SEC filings (public companies)
- State business registrations
- SBA databases

**Specialized Databases**:
- Healthcare: Definitive Healthcare
- Legal: Martindale-Hubbell
- Finance: Bloomberg
- Manufacturing: ThomasNet

### Using Directories Effectively

**Strategy 1: Association Member Lists**
Join industry associations, access member directories, filter for ICP.

**Strategy 2: "Best of" Lists**
Use published lists of top companies in your target segment.

**Strategy 3: Regulatory Filings**
Public filings reveal company information, leadership, and financials.

### Example Directory Workflow

1. Identify relevant industry directory
2. Access or scrape company list
3. Enrich with contact data (Apollo, Hunter)
4. Verify emails
5. Craft industry-specific messaging
6. Execute targeted campaign

## Multi-Source Prospecting Strategy

### The Multi-Source Approach

No single source has perfect data. The best prospecting combines multiple sources.

**Why Multi-Source**:
- Better coverage (no gaps)
- Better accuracy (cross-reference)
- Better signals (combine intent with data)
- Better personalization (more context)

### Combining Sources

**Example Multi-Source Workflow**:

1. **Start with Signals**: Identify companies showing buying signals
   - Job postings → Building team
   - Funding → Budget available
   - G2 intent → Actively evaluating

2. **Verify ICP Fit**: Check company matches ICP
   - Apollo → Company size, industry
   - BuiltWith → Technology stack

3. **Find Contacts**: Identify decision-makers
   - Sales Navigator → Find relevant titles
   - Apollo → Get email addresses

4. **Enrich for Personalization**: Gather context
   - LinkedIn → Recent posts, profile details
   - News → Company announcements
   - Reviews → Pain points

5. **Verify Data**: Ensure deliverability
   - Hunter → Verify emails
   - Check for duplicates

6. **Execute Campaign**: Send to verified, enriched list

### Source Prioritization by Use Case

| Use Case | Primary Source | Secondary | Enrichment |
|----------|---------------|-----------|------------|
| General B2B | Apollo | Sales Navigator | Hunter |
| Enterprise | ZoomInfo | Sales Navigator | Clearbit |
| Tech companies | Apollo | BuiltWith | Hunter |
| Recently funded | Crunchbase | Apollo | Hunter |
| Actively evaluating | G2 Intent | Apollo | Sales Navigator |

## Summary: Prospecting Principles

**Quality Over Quantity**: 1,000 well-researched prospects beat 10,000 random contacts.

**Multiple Sources**: No single source is complete or accurate enough alone.

**Signal First**: Prioritize prospects showing buying signals.

**Verify Before Sending**: Never send to unverified lists.

**Personalize from Research**: Use prospecting data to personalize outreach.

**Continuous Improvement**: Track which sources produce best results.
