# Objection Handling

Objections are not rejections—they're requests for more information. This chapter covers the most common objections and how to address them.

## Objection Handling Framework

### The LAER Method

**L**isten: Understand the objection fully
**A**cknowledge: Show you heard and respect their concern
**E**xplore: Ask questions to understand root cause
**R**espond: Address the real issue

### Key Principles

1. **Don't be defensive**: Objections are normal
2. **Ask before answering**: Understand the real concern
3. **Be honest**: If it's not a fit, say so
4. **Focus on value**: Not just overcoming resistance

## Price/Budget Objections

### "It's too expensive"

**Response 1: Understand the comparison**
```
Totally fair—mind if I ask what you're comparing it to?

Understanding your budget helps me see if we have options that work, or if there's a clear ROI case that changes the math.
```

**Response 2: ROI focus**
```
I get that—no one likes spending money.

Quick question: what would it be worth to [achieve outcome your product enables]?

[Similar Company] was hesitant too, but after calculating [specific ROI], the investment made sense.
```

### "We don't have budget"

**Response 1: Timeline check**
```
Completely understand. When does your budget cycle reset?

Happy to follow up then with a proposal ready to go.
```

**Response 2: Business case offer**
```
Makes sense. Would it help if I put together ROI data you could use to make a case internally?

Sometimes showing the numbers helps unlock budget.
```

## Timing Objections

### "Not the right time"

**Response 1: Understand the timeline**
```
Totally respect that. Quick question: is it the timing around [specific thing], or more about bandwidth?

Just want to understand when would be the right time to reconnect.
```

**Response 2: Plant the seed**
```
No worries—timing is everything.

When would make sense to circle back? I'll make a note and follow up then.
```

### "We just started with [competitor]"

**Response 1: Future evaluation**
```
Makes sense—give them a fair shot.

When does your contract renew? And what would success look like for you?

I'll check back before renewal in case you want to compare.
```

## Solution Objections

### "We already have a solution"

**Response 1: Understand satisfaction**
```
Got it—what are you using, and how's it working?

Not trying to push you to switch—just curious how it compares.
```

**Response 2: Complementary angle**
```
Makes sense. Most companies using [their solution] also use us for [different use case].

We're complementary, not competitive. Worth a look?
```

### "We built something in-house"

**Response**
```
Impressive—building in-house shows serious commitment.

One question: how much time does your team spend maintaining it?

[Similar Company] switched to us specifically to free up engineering for core product work.
```

## Authority Objections

### "I'm not the decision maker"

**Response**
```
Appreciate you letting me know.

Who should I talk to about this? Happy to reach out to them directly.

Or, if helpful, I could send you something to share internally?
```

### "Need to check with my team/boss"

**Response**
```
Of course. What questions do you think they'll have?

I can put together materials that address those—make it easier to pitch internally.
```

---

## Interest Objections

### "Not interested"

**Response 1: Understand why**
```
Appreciate the directness.

Mind if I ask—is it the product itself, the timing, or something else?

Not trying to change your mind, just helpful for me to understand.
```

**Response 2: Graceful exit**
```
Totally fair. Thanks for letting me know.

If anything changes, feel free to reach out. Best of luck.
```

### "Send me more info"

**Response**
```
Happy to.

Quick question so I send the right stuff—what specifically would be most useful?

Or, if easier, I can tailor it live in a 15-minute call?
```

## Skepticism Objections

### "I've heard this before"

**Response**
```
Ha—totally get it. Everyone claims to solve this.

Here's what's different: [specific differentiator].

But honestly, the best way to see is [proof point or demo].

Worth 15 minutes to judge for yourself?
```

### "Sounds too good to be true"

**Response**
```
Fair skepticism. Here's the reality:

We don't achieve these results for everyone. It works best when [conditions].

For companies that fit, results are typically [realistic range].

Does [Company]'s situation match those conditions?
```

## Summary: Objection Principles

**Objections = Interest**: They're engaging, not ignoring you.

**Ask Before Answering**: Understand the real concern.

**Be Honest**: If it's not a fit, say so.

**Stay Professional**: Never argue or get defensive.

**Know When to Stop**: Some objections mean "no."
