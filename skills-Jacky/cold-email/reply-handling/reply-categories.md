# Reply Categories

Not all replies are equal. Proper categorization helps you prioritize effectively and respond appropriately.

## The Six Reply Categories

### 1. Positive (Interested)

**What it looks like**:
- "Yes, let's chat"
- "Tell me more"
- "This sounds interesting"
- "What does this cost?"
- "How does it work?"

**Priority**: Highest
**Response Time**: Within 2 hours
**Goal**: Book the meeting immediately

**Response Strategy**:
- Express enthusiasm (briefly)
- Provide calendar link
- Suggest specific times
- Make booking frictionless

### 2. Objection

**What it looks like**:
- "Too expensive"
- "We already have a solution"
- "Not the right time"
- "I'm not the right person"

**Priority**: High
**Response Time**: Within 4 hours
**Goal**: Address objection and move toward meeting

**Response Strategy**:
- Acknowledge the objection
- Ask clarifying question or provide counter
- See [Objection Handling](objection-handling.md)

### 3. Not Now / Timing

**What it looks like**:
- "Check back in Q3"
- "Not a priority right now"
- "We're swamped this quarter"
- "Maybe next year"

**Priority**: Medium
**Response Time**: Within 6 hours
**Goal**: Understand timeline and set follow-up

**Response Strategy**:
- Respect their timing
- Ask when to follow up
- Set reminder/task for future
- Leave door open

### 4. Wrong Person / Referral

**What it looks like**:
- "I'm not the right person"
- "You should talk to [Name]"
- "Try reaching out to our [Department]"

**Priority**: High
**Response Time**: Within 4 hours
**Goal**: Get warm introduction to right person

**Response Strategy**:
- Thank them for the redirect
- Ask if they can make intro
- Request right contact's email
- Follow up referencing their name

### 5. Negative / Unsubscribe

**What it looks like**:
- "Not interested"
- "Stop emailing me"
- "Take me off your list"
- "This is spam"

**Priority**: Medium (for processing)
**Response Time**: Within 24 hours
**Goal**: Honor request and exit gracefully

**Response Strategy**:
- Acknowledge immediately
- Remove from sequences
- Add to suppression list
- Brief, professional response
- Never argue

### 6. Out of Office / Auto-Reply

**What it looks like**:
- OOO auto-replies
- "I'm out until [date]"
- Vacation notices

**Priority**: Low
**Response Time**: N/A (wait)
**Goal**: Note return date, re-engage then

**Response Strategy**:
- Mark in CRM with return date
- Set task to follow up after return
- May indicate good contact (they have OOO set up)

## Categorization Workflow

### Manual Process
1. Check inbox multiple times daily
2. Read and categorize each reply
3. Prioritize by category
4. Respond in priority order
5. Log in CRM

### Automated/Semi-Automated
1. Replies flow into unified inbox
2. AI/rules pre-categorize
3. Human reviews and adjusts
4. Prioritized queue presented
5. Respond from queue
6. Auto-log to CRM

---

## Response Prioritization

### Immediate (0-2 hours)
- Positive/Interested replies
- Questions about product/pricing

### Same Day (2-6 hours)
- Objections
- Referrals
- Timing questions

### Next Day (6-24 hours)
- Negative/Unsubscribe
- Out of office (note and wait)

---

## Reply Rate by Category (Benchmarks)

Of all replies received:
- Positive: 40-60%
- Objections: 15-25%
- Not Now: 10-15%
- Wrong Person: 5-10%
- Negative: 10-20%
- OOO: 5-10%

---

## Summary

**Categorize Quickly**: Know what type of reply you're dealing with.

**Prioritize Correctly**: Positive replies first, always.

**Respond Appropriately**: Match your response to the category.

**Track Everything**: Log for future reference and analysis.
