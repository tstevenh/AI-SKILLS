# Personalization Hierarchy

Not all personalization is created equal. This chapter covers the hierarchy of personalization—from table stakes to truly impressive.

## The Five Levels of Personalization

### Level 1: Basic Merge Fields (Table Stakes)
**What it is**: Name, company, job title inserted via mail merge.

**Examples**:
- "Hi {{first_name}},"
- "I noticed {{company}} is..."
- "As a {{job_title}}, you probably..."

**Effectiveness**: Minimal. Everyone does this. It's expected, not impressive.

**When to use**: Always include, but never rely on alone.

### Level 2: Segment-Based Personalization
**What it is**: Content tailored to their segment (industry, company size, role type).

**Examples**:
- "Most SaaS founders at your stage..." (segment: SaaS, Series A)
- "Marketing agencies typically struggle with..." (segment: agencies)
- "VPs of Sales in fintech are dealing with..." (segment: role + industry)

**Effectiveness**: Better. Shows you understand their category. 

**When to use**: Good baseline for volume campaigns.

### Level 3: Company-Specific Personalization
**What it is**: References specific to their company—news, products, positioning.

**Examples**:
- "Congrats on the Series B announcement last week"
- "Saw your new pricing page—the value prop is clear"
- "Noticed Acme is expanding to Europe"

**Effectiveness**: Good. Demonstrates research beyond a database.

**When to use**: For ICP-matched prospects where company data is accessible.

### Level 4: Individual-Specific Personalization
**What it is**: References specific to the individual—their content, background, activities.

**Examples**:
- "Your LinkedIn post about scaling challenges resonated"
- "Your talk at SaaStr last month was excellent"
- "Saw you came from [Previous Company]—that background is relevant here"

**Effectiveness**: Excellent. Shows genuine individual research.

**When to use**: For high-value prospects worth significant research investment.

### Level 5: Deep Research Personalization
**What it is**: Insights that combine multiple data points into genuine understanding.

**Examples**:
- "Given your background at [Company], your recent post about [topic], and [Company]'s expansion plans, I think..."
- "Your approach to [topic] in your podcast combined with [Company]'s positioning suggests..."

**Effectiveness**: Outstanding. Feels like a personal note, not a campaign.

**When to use**: For top-tier prospects, enterprise deals, or when high response rate matters more than volume.

## Personalization ROI by Level

| Level | Time Investment | Response Lift | Volume Feasibility |
|-------|----------------|---------------|-------------------|
| 1: Merge Fields | Seconds | 0% (baseline) | Unlimited |
| 2: Segment | Minutes | 10-20% | High (1000+/day) |
| 3: Company | 2-5 min | 30-50% | Medium (100-200/day) |
| 4: Individual | 5-15 min | 75-150% | Low (25-50/day) |
| 5: Deep Research | 15-30 min | 200%+ | Very Low (10-20/day) |

## Personalization Data Points

### Level 2: Segment Data
- Industry vertical
- Company size (employees, revenue)
- Geography
- Business model
- Funding stage
- Technology stack

### Level 3: Company Data
- Recent news/announcements
- Funding events
- Product launches
- Hiring activity
- Leadership changes
- Website changes
- Competitive moves

### Level 4: Individual Data
- LinkedIn posts/articles
- Podcast appearances
- Conference talks
- Social media activity
- Published content
- Professional background
- Education
- Mutual connections

### Level 5: Combined Insights
- Patterns across multiple data points
- Inferences from combined signals
- Connections between company and individual data
- Timing and relevance convergence

## Choosing Your Level

### For High-Volume Campaigns (500+ emails)
**Target Level**: 2-3
**Approach**: Segment-based with light company personalization
**How**: Use tools to pull company data automatically

### For Standard Campaigns (100-500 emails)
**Target Level**: 3
**Approach**: Company-specific opening lines
**How**: Semi-automated research with human review

### For Targeted Campaigns (25-100 emails)
**Target Level**: 3-4
**Approach**: Individual-specific personalization
**How**: Manual research with template structure

### For Enterprise/High-Value (< 25 emails)
**Target Level**: 4-5
**Approach**: Deep research, custom emails
**How**: Fully manual, significant research per prospect

## Personalization Quality Checklist

### Minimum Viable Personalization
- [ ] First name spelled correctly
- [ ] Company name spelled correctly
- [ ] Job title is current
- [ ] Segment-appropriate messaging

### Good Personalization
- [ ] References something specific to their company
- [ ] Timing relates to something current
- [ ] Message connects personalization to value prop
- [ ] Sounds like you actually researched them

### Great Personalization
- [ ] References their specific work or content
- [ ] Demonstrates genuine understanding of their situation
- [ ] Connects multiple data points
- [ ] Feels like a personal note, not a campaign

## Common Personalization Mistakes

### Over-Personalization
**Problem**: So much personalization it feels creepy or time-wasting.
**Example**: A 3-paragraph opening about their LinkedIn activity.
**Fix**: Keep personalization brief. 1-2 sentences max.

### Fake Personalization
**Problem**: Generic compliments that could apply to anyone.
**Example**: "I love what you're doing at [Company]!"
**Fix**: Be specific. What exactly do you love? Why?

### Irrelevant Personalization
**Problem**: Personalization that doesn't connect to your value prop.
**Example**: "Saw you went to Stanford!" (when it's irrelevant)
**Fix**: Personalize on something that bridges to your pitch.

### Broken Merge Fields
**Problem**: {{first_name}} shows in the email.
**Fix**: Test emails before sending. Use fallbacks.

### Outdated Information
**Problem**: Referencing old news or former roles.
**Fix**: Check dates. Verify current status.

---

## Summary: Personalization Principles

**Quality Over Quantity**: Better to personalize fewer emails well than many emails poorly.

**Connect to Value**: Personalization should bridge to why you're reaching out.

**Be Genuine**: If you can't find something real to personalize, don't fake it.

**Match Investment to Value**: High-value prospects deserve high personalization investment.

**Scale Strategically**: Use tools to scale personalization, not skip it.
