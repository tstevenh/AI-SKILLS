# Agency Model

Cold email as a service is a growing business model. This chapter covers how to offer cold email services to clients.

## The Agency Opportunity

### Why Cold Email Agencies Work

**Client Pain Points**:
- Don't know how to do cold email
- Don't have time/resources
- Need to scale quickly
- Want predictable lead flow

**Agency Advantages**:
- Expertise and systems
- Infrastructure already built
- Can spread costs across clients
- Continuous optimization

---

## Service Models

### Model 1: Done-For-You (DFY)
**What You Do**: Everything—strategy, copy, infrastructure, sending, reply handling

**Client Involvement**: Minimal (provide info, take meetings)

**Pricing**: $3,000-$10,000+/month

**Best For**: Busy clients, premium positioning

### Model 2: Done-With-You (DWY)
**What You Do**: Strategy, copy, coaching—client sends

**Client Involvement**: Significant (they execute)

**Pricing**: $1,500-$4,000/month

**Best For**: Clients who want control, learning

### Model 3: Setup + Training
**What You Do**: Build infrastructure, train team, hand off

**Client Involvement**: Take over after training

**Pricing**: $5,000-$15,000 one-time

**Best For**: Companies building internal capability

---

## Pricing Models

### Retainer
**Structure**: Fixed monthly fee

**Pros**: Predictable revenue, stable relationship
**Cons**: Client may feel underserved if slow months

**Example**: $5,000/month for DFY service

### Performance-Based
**Structure**: Base + per-meeting/lead fee

**Pros**: Aligned incentives, can scale
**Cons**: Revenue variability, quality pressure

**Example**: $2,000 base + $200 per qualified meeting

### Hybrid
**Structure**: Retainer + performance bonus

**Pros**: Stability + alignment
**Cons**: More complex

**Example**: $3,500/month + $100 per meeting over 10

### Pay-Per-Lead
**Structure**: Pure performance, pay per result

**Pros**: Low risk for client, high upside for agency
**Cons**: Quality control, pricing complexity

**Example**: $300 per qualified meeting

## Client Management

### Onboarding Process

**Week 1: Discovery**
- Understand their business
- Define ICP
- Review existing assets
- Set expectations

**Week 2: Setup**
- Configure infrastructure
- Build target lists
- Write copy
- Approve messaging

**Week 3-4: Launch**
- Begin sending (ramping)
- Daily monitoring
- Adjust based on data
- Report progress

### Ongoing Management

**Weekly**:
- Metrics report
- Reply review
- Campaign optimization
- Client check-in

**Monthly**:
- Full performance review
- Strategy adjustment
- New campaign planning
- Success stories

### Client Expectations

**Set Clear Expectations**:
- Typical results timeline (4-8 weeks)
- Realistic metrics benchmarks
- What's included/excluded
- Response times

**Manage Relationships**:
- Regular communication
- Proactive updates
- Quick issue resolution
- Celebrate wins together

## Scaling the Agency

### Client Capacity

| Team Size | Clients | Monthly Volume |
|-----------|---------|----------------|
| 1 person | 3-5 | 5,000-15,000 |
| 2-3 people | 8-15 | 20,000-50,000 |
| 5-7 people | 20-35 | 60,000-150,000 |
| 10+ people | 40+ | 200,000+ |

### Operations

**Tools for Agencies**:
- Smartlead (whitelabel)
- Client management system
- Reporting dashboards
- Communication tools

**Processes Needed**:
- Standardized onboarding
- Templated workflows
- Quality control
- Client communication cadence

### Profitability

**Unit Economics**:
- Revenue per client: $5,000/month
- Cost per client: $1,500-2,500/month
- Margin: 50-70%

**Scale Considerations**:
- Tool costs scale with volume
- People costs scale with clients
- Infrastructure is relatively fixed

## Common Challenges

### Client Expectations
**Problem**: Client expects instant results
**Solution**: Set clear timeline expectations upfront

### Reply Quality
**Problem**: Meetings booked but not qualified
**Solution**: Define qualification criteria, improve targeting

### Deliverability Issues
**Problem**: Emails landing in spam
**Solution**: Proper infrastructure, conservative sending, monitoring

### Scope Creep
**Problem**: Client wants more than agreed
**Solution**: Clear contracts, change order process
