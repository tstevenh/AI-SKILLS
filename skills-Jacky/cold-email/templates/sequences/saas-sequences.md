# SaaS Sales Sequences (5 Complete Sequences)

Ready-to-use complete sequences for SaaS sales outreach. Customize bracketed elements for your specific product and prospects.

---

## Sequence 1: Standard SaaS Prospecting

**Use Case**: Cold outreach to ICP-matched prospects without specific trigger
**Length**: 6 emails over 28 days
**Goal**: Book demo/discovery call

### Email 1 - Day 1: Introduction
```
Subject: Question about [Company]'s [function]

[First name],

[Personalized opening based on research—their content, company news, or observation]

Most [titles] I talk to are struggling with [specific challenge your product solves].

We built [Product] specifically for this—[one-sentence value prop]. [Similar Company] saw [specific result] within [timeframe].

Worth a quick chat to see if relevant?

[Your name]
```

### Email 2 - Day 4: The Bump
```
Subject: Re: Question about [Company]'s [function]

[First name],

Quick bump on this—wanted to make sure it didn't get buried.

Companies like [Similar Company 1] and [Similar Company 2] are seeing [result] with our approach.

Open to 15 minutes this week?

[Your name]
```

### Email 3 - Day 9: Different Angle (Competitor/Industry)
```
Subject: What [industry] companies are doing

[First name],

Different angle on this.

Noticed [industry trend or competitor move relevant to them].

Most [company types] are responding by [approach your product enables].

That's what [Product] does—and it's working. [X] companies in [industry] have joined in the last [timeframe].

Curious if [Company] should be looking at this too?

[Your name]
```

### Email 4 - Day 15: Value Add
```
Subject: [Resource] for [Company]

[First name],

Regardless of whether we connect, thought you'd find this useful:

[Brief description of resource—guide, template, benchmark data]

[Link]

Most [titles] I share it with say it's [specific feedback].

Let me know if you have questions.

[Your name]
```

### Email 5 - Day 21: Social Proof/Case Study
```
Subject: How [Similar Company] hit [result]

[First name],

Quick case study that might be relevant:

[Similar Company] was in your situation—[brief context]. In [timeframe], they achieved [specific result].

Their [title] said: "[Brief quote]"

The playbook is repeatable. Would it help to see how it could work for [Company]?

[Your name]
```

### Email 6 - Day 28: Breakup
```
Subject: Should I close your file?

[First name],

Haven't heard back—totally understand if this isn't a priority.

I'll assume it's not the right time and won't keep emailing.

If things change, I'm here. Just reply whenever and we can pick up.

Best of luck with everything.

[Your name]
```

## Sequence 2: Funding Trigger Sequence

**Use Case**: Outreach to companies that recently raised funding
**Length**: 5 emails over 21 days
**Goal**: Leverage funding momentum for meeting

### Email 1 - Day 1: Funding Congrats + Value
```
Subject: Congrats on the [round]

[First name],

Congrats on the [round]—exciting milestone for [Company].

Post-funding, most [company types] scale aggressively on [relevant area]. The challenge is usually [problem your product solves].

We help with exactly that. [Similar funded company] used us to [specific result] after their [round].

Worth a quick chat to see if we could help [Company] during this growth phase?

[Your name]
```

### Email 2 - Day 4: Follow-Up
```
Subject: Re: Congrats on the [round]

[First name],

Following up on my note—know things are probably hectic post-funding.

Quick question: is scaling [function] on the roadmap for the next 6 months?

If so, we might be able to help. [Brief value prop].

[Your name]
```

### Email 3 - Day 9: Investor Connection
```
Subject: Something [Investor] portfolio companies do

[First name],

Noticed [Investor] led your round. Several of their portfolio companies use [Product]:

- [Company 1]
- [Company 2]
- [Company 3]

There's a reason—we help [outcome] during the scale-up phase.

Worth seeing if [Company] should do the same?

[Your name]
```

### Email 4 - Day 14: Resource
```
Subject: Post-Series [X] playbook

[First name],

Put together a guide on how [company types] scale [function] after raising a [round].

Based on what [X]+ companies did successfully—including [Notable Example].

[Link]

Thought it might be useful as [Company] plans the next phase.

[Your name]
```

### Email 5 - Day 21: Last Chance
```
Subject: Timing question

[First name],

Wanted to check one last time before backing off.

Is [function] optimization something you're looking at this quarter, or is it more of a later priority?

Just want to make sure I'm not bugging you if the timing's off.

[Your name]
```

## Sequence 3: Product-Led Growth Sequence

**Use Case**: Reaching companies where someone has used free tier/trial
**Length**: 5 emails over 18 days
**Goal**: Convert free user to paid/meeting

### Email 1 - Day 1: Acknowledge Usage
```
Subject: Noticed you're using [Product]

[First name],

Saw someone at [Company] signed up for [Product] recently—hope it's been useful!

Most [company types] start with [free tier use case], then expand to [paid use case] once they see the value.

Curious if [Company] is seeing that pattern. Worth a quick chat about what's possible with [paid tier/feature]?

[Your name]
```

### Email 2 - Day 4: Check-In
```
Subject: How's [Product] working for you?

[First name],

Quick check-in—how's [Product] working for [Company] so far?

Most teams at your stage find [specific feature/outcome] most valuable.

Anything I can help with or questions I can answer?

[Your name]
```

### Email 3 - Day 9: Expansion Value
```
Subject: What [Similar Company] does with [Product]

[First name],

[Similar Company] started using [Product] the same way you are.

Now they've expanded to [advanced use case] and are seeing [result].

Their setup might give you ideas for [Company]. Want me to walk through it?

[Your name]
```

### Email 4 - Day 13: Offer Help
```
Subject: Quick help for [Company]

[First name],

Noticed your team's been active in [Product] this week.

Happy to jump on a quick call to make sure you're getting the most out of it—no sales pitch, just help.

Useful?

[Your name]
```

### Email 5 - Day 18: Direct Ask
```
Subject: Ready for more?

[First name],

Last note on this.

If [Company] is ready to expand beyond [free tier], I can show you what [paid tier/feature] unlocks—usually takes 15 minutes.

If not, no worries—enjoy [Product] and reach out when you're ready.

[Your name]
```

## Sequence 4: Competitive Replacement Sequence

**Use Case**: Targeting companies using a specific competitor
**Length**: 5 emails over 21 days
**Goal**: Schedule competitive comparison meeting

### Email 1 - Day 1: Acknowledge Competitor
```
Subject: Thoughts on [Competitor]?

[First name],

Noticed [Company] uses [Competitor] for [function].

Curious—how's it working for you?

We work with several companies that switched from [Competitor] to [Product]. Common reasons: [Pain Point 1], [Pain Point 2].

Not saying you should switch—just curious if those resonate.

[Your name]
```

### Email 2 - Day 5: Differentiation
```
Subject: [Product] vs [Competitor]—the difference

[First name],

Following up with the key difference between [Product] and [Competitor]:

[Competitor]: [How they approach it]
[Product]: [How you approach it—better]

For teams like yours, that difference shows up in [specific outcome].

Worth 15 minutes to see the comparison?

[Your name]
```

### Email 3 - Day 10: Switcher Case Study
```
Subject: Why [Similar Company] switched from [Competitor]

[First name],

[Similar Company] used [Competitor] for [X years]. Here's why they switched:

[Brief story: Challenge with competitor + Result after switching]

Their [title] said: "[Quote about the difference]"

Might be worth a comparison. What do you think?

[Your name]
```

### Email 4 - Day 15: Risk Reduction
```
Subject: No-risk comparison

[First name],

Know switching [tools/vendors] is a big decision.

What if I put together a side-by-side comparison specific to [Company]'s setup?

No commitment—just data to help you evaluate. Takes 15 minutes.

Worth it?

[Your name]
```

### Email 5 - Day 21: Final Check
```
Subject: Still happy with [Competitor]?

[First name],

Last check on this.

If [Competitor] is working great, no need to change anything.

But if [common pain points] are issues, we might be worth a look.

Either way, let me know and I'll update my notes.

[Your name]
```

## Sequence 5: Enterprise Sequence

**Use Case**: High-value enterprise prospects requiring longer nurture
**Length**: 8 emails over 42 days
**Goal**: Multi-threaded meeting with enterprise account

### Email 1 - Day 1: Executive Introduction
```
Subject: [Company] + [Your Company]

[First name],

[Personalized opening referencing company initiative, leadership change, or strategy]

We work with [enterprise companies in their industry] on [outcome]. Companies like [Enterprise Customer 1], [Enterprise Customer 2], and [Enterprise Customer 3].

Given [Company]'s focus on [their priority], thought there might be alignment.

Worth a brief conversation to explore?

[Your name]
```

### Email 2 - Day 5: Value Expansion
```
Subject: Re: [Company] + [Your Company]

[First name],

Following up with more context on how we help companies like [Company]:

[Bullet point 1: Outcome]
[Bullet point 2: Outcome]
[Bullet point 3: Outcome]

[Enterprise Customer] credits us with [specific result] in their [function].

Would it be helpful to discuss how this could apply to [Company]?

[Your name]
```

### Email 3 - Day 10: Multi-Thread Attempt
```
Subject: Involving your team?

[First name],

In addition to yourself, who else at [Company] is involved in [function/decision]?

Often these conversations are more productive with [relevant roles] in the room.

Happy to coordinate a session that works for the right stakeholders.

[Your name]
```

### Email 4 - Day 17: Enterprise Case Study
```
Subject: How [Enterprise Customer] approached this

[First name],

Thought you'd find this relevant:

[Enterprise Customer] had similar [challenges/goals] to [Company]. Here's how they approached it:

[Brief case study: Challenge → Approach → Results]

Their [Executive Title] said: "[Quote]"

The scale matches [Company]. Worth discussing?

[Your name]
```

### Email 5 - Day 24: Industry Insight
```
Subject: [Industry] insight for [Company]

[First name],

Came across something interesting while working with other [industry] enterprises:

[Insight or trend relevant to them]

This is affecting how [company types] think about [relevant area].

Thought you'd want to be aware. Happy to discuss implications for [Company].

[Your name]
```

### Email 6 - Day 31: Resource Share
```
Subject: [Enterprise Resource] for [Company]

[First name],

Given [Company]'s focus on [area], thought this might be valuable:

[Resource: Whitepaper, benchmark study, enterprise guide]

[Link]

It's based on data from [X] enterprise [industry] companies.

Let me know if it sparks any questions.

[Your name]
```

### Email 7 - Day 38: Executive Offer
```
Subject: Executive briefing for [Company]

[First name],

Would it be valuable if our [Executive Title] briefed your team on [relevant topic]?

[Brief credentials of your executive]

These sessions typically cover [what they'd learn]. No pitch—just insights from working with [X] enterprise [industry] companies.

Worth setting up?

[Your name]
```

### Email 8 - Day 42: Graceful Close
```
Subject: Closing the loop on [Company]

[First name],

I've reached out several times without connecting—understand enterprise priorities shift.

I'll pause outreach for now. If [function/topic] becomes a focus, I'm here.

Meanwhile, feel free to reach out if anything from my previous notes is relevant.

Best to you and the [Company] team.

[Your name]
```

## Customization Notes

### For All Sequences
- Replace bracketed elements with specific details
- Adjust timing based on your sales cycle
- Personalize opening lines based on real research
- Modify CTAs based on your meeting type

### Tracking and Iteration
- Monitor which emails get replies
- A/B test subject lines and CTAs
- Update case studies and proof points regularly
- Retire underperforming emails

---

*These sequences are starting frameworks. The best results come from continuous testing and personalization.*
