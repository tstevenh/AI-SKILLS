# Opening Line Templates (50+)

Ready-to-use opening line templates organized by approach. Replace bracketed elements with specific details from your research.

---

## Observation-Based Openers (1-15)

### Growth & Hiring
1. `Noticed [Company] has been on a hiring spree—[X] new [roles] in the last [timeframe]. That kind of growth is exciting but comes with challenges.`

2. `Saw your team doubled this quarter—congrats. Scaling that fast usually means [relevant challenge] becomes a bottleneck.`

3. `Noticed you're building out the [department] team—[X] open roles right now. That's ambitious.`

### Content & Activity
4. `Your LinkedIn post about [specific topic] resonated—especially your point about [specific insight].`

5. `Saw your recent [blog post/podcast/talk] on [topic]. Your take on [specific point] was different from what I usually hear.`

6. `Your comment on [Person]'s post about [topic] caught my eye—you clearly understand the nuances.`

### Company News
7. `Noticed [Company] just launched [product/feature]—congrats on shipping. The [specific aspect] is really well done.`

8. `Saw [Company] is expanding to [market/region]—that's a significant move. Most companies at that stage face [relevant challenge].`

9. `Noticed the rebrand—the new positioning is much clearer. [Specific observation about it].`

### Technology & Approach
10. `Saw you're using [technology/tool]. Curious how that's working for your team.`

11. `Your approach to [specific function] is different from what most companies do—in a good way.`

12. `Noticed [Company]'s [specific process/page] is really well optimized—clearly a lot of thought went into it.`

### Recognition & Achievement
13. `Saw [Company] on [list/publication]—well deserved. That kind of recognition usually comes with [relevant challenge].`

14. `Your [achievement/metric] caught my attention. Not many companies in [industry] hit that milestone.`

15. `Noticed [Company] crossed [milestone]—impressive growth. At that stage, most teams start struggling with [relevant challenge].`

## Compliment-Based Openers (16-25)

### Content Compliments
16. `Your talk at [event] was excellent—especially your breakdown of [specific topic]. That's rare insight.`

17. `Been following your newsletter. The piece on [topic] was the clearest explanation I've seen on the subject.`

18. `Your Twitter thread on [topic] was one of the best breakdowns I've read. Clearly you've thought deeply about this.`

### Product/Company Compliments
19. `The [Company] product is really well designed—the [specific feature] is particularly clever.`

20. `[Company]'s customer reviews are stellar. You're clearly doing something right on the [specific aspect].`

21. `The new [Company] website is beautiful—your design team is talented. The [specific element] especially stands out.`

### Work Compliments
22. `Your team's work on [project/initiative] is impressive. The [specific result] speaks for itself.`

23. `The way you've positioned [Company] against [competitors] is smart. Most companies in [industry] don't differentiate that clearly.`

24. `Your engineering blog is excellent—the post on [topic] saved me hours.`

25. `The culture at [Company] comes through in everything you do. That's rare and valuable.`

## Trigger Event Openers (26-35)

### Funding
26. `Congrats on the [round]—exciting milestone. As you scale from here, most companies at your stage run into [relevant challenge].`

27. `Saw the funding news—[amount] is going to fuel some serious growth. That usually means [relevant opportunity/challenge].`

28. `After a [round] like yours, the next 12 months are critical. Most teams I talk to are focused on [relevant priority].`

### Role Changes
29. `Congrats on the new role at [Company]. The first 90 days as [title] are crucial—most new [titles] are immediately focused on [relevant priority].`

30. `Saw you just joined [Company]—welcome aboard. Starting as [title] at a company in [stage/situation] usually means [relevant challenge] is top of mind.`

### Company Events
31. `Saw the news about [Company]'s [announcement]—that's a big move. It probably means [relevant implication].`

32. `Your recent [product launch/update] caught my attention. Launching something new usually intensifies [relevant challenge].`

33. `Noticed [Company] just hit [milestone]—congrats. Companies at that stage often start wrestling with [relevant challenge].`

### Hiring
34. `Saw you're hiring [X] [roles]—sounds like [function] is a big focus. Scaling that team fast usually means [relevant challenge].`

35. `Your job posting for [specific role] caught my eye—particularly the part about [specific requirement]. That tells me [inference about their priorities].`

## Mutual Connection Openers (36-42)

36. `[Name] at [Company] suggested I reach out—she mentioned you're exploring [topic] and thought we should connect.`

37. `[Name] speaks highly of you—when I mentioned I was reaching out to [industry] leaders about [topic], your name came up immediately.`

38. `Fellow [community/group] member here. Loved your post on [topic]—wanted to follow up on something you mentioned.`

39. `We met briefly at [event]. You mentioned struggling with [challenge]—I've been thinking about that and have an idea.`

40. `Noticed we're both connected to [Name]. She's mentioned your work on [topic] before—impressive stuff.`

41. `Fellow [alma mater/previous company/background] here. Your approach to [topic] as a fellow [background] caught my attention.`

42. `We were both at [event] last month. Your [session/comment/question] about [topic] stuck with me.`

## Problem/Challenge Openers (43-48)

43. `Most [titles] I talk to are drowning in [specific problem]. Curious if that's true for you too.`

44. `Scaling from [stage A] to [stage B] is notoriously difficult—especially when it comes to [specific aspect]. Curious how you're approaching it.`

45. `Companies growing as fast as [Company] usually hit a wall with [specific challenge] around now.`

46. `Every [title] at a [company type] I've talked to this quarter mentions [specific problem]. Seems to be universal.`

47. `The shift from [current state] to [desired state] is where most [industry] companies struggle. The common pattern I see is [specific issue].`

48. `At your stage, [specific challenge] usually becomes the bottleneck—it's what holds back companies from [next milestone].`

## Direct/Honest Openers (49-55)

49. `I'll be direct: I think [Company] could [specific outcome] with a few changes to [specific area].`

50. `Quick and honest: I help [company types] [achieve outcome]. Reaching out because [specific reason].`

51. `Not going to pretend this isn't a cold email—but I genuinely think this could be worth your time. Here's why:`

52. `Straight to it: I noticed [observation] and think we could help with [challenge].`

53. `I could send the typical intro email, but let me just ask directly: is [specific challenge] something you're actively trying to solve?`

54. `Reaching out because I've helped [X] companies like yours solve [specific problem]. Think I could do the same for you.`

55. `Two questions for you: Is [challenge] a priority? And would you be open to a new approach?`

## Usage Guidelines

### Customization Requirements
Each opening line should be customized with:
- Specific company name
- Specific observations or details
- Relevant challenges or opportunities
- Names of mutual connections (if applicable)

### Quality Checklist
Before using an opening line:
- [ ] Is the observation/compliment specific and genuine?
- [ ] Does it connect logically to your pitch?
- [ ] Would this work in a real conversation?
- [ ] Is it based on actual research?

### Common Mistakes to Avoid
- Generic compliments ("Love what you're doing!")
- Outdated observations (check dates on news/posts)
- Over-personalization (feels stalker-ish)
- Disconnect from email body

*The best opening lines are custom-written based on genuine research. Use these templates as starting points, not copy-paste solutions.*
