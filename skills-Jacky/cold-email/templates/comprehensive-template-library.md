## Part 1: First Email Templates by Situation

### Situation: You've Never Contacted Them

**The Researched Introduction**
```
Subject: [Personalized observation] at [Company]

[First name],

[Specific observation from research—their content, company news, or activity].

[One sentence connecting observation to a challenge your product solves].

We help [company types] with exactly this. [Similar Company] [achieved specific result] in [timeframe].

Worth a quick conversation to see if relevant?

[Your name]
```

**The Direct Value Proposition**
```
Subject: [Outcome] for [Company]

[First name],

I help [titles] at [company types] [achieve outcome].

Most struggle with [specific challenge]. We solve that—typically seeing [quantified result] within [timeframe].

[Similar Company] and [Similar Company 2] use us for this.

15 minutes to see if it applies to [Company]?

[Your name]
```

**The Problem-Focused**
```
Subject: The [specific problem] challenge

[First name],

[Companies like Company] at your stage often face [specific problem]. It leads to [consequence].

The typical solutions ([common approaches]) have [limitation].

We built [Product] specifically because of this. [Similar Company] went from [state A] to [state B] in [timeframe].

Worth exploring?

[Your name]
```

## Part 2: Follow-Up Email Templates

### Follow-Up #1: The Bump (3-4 days later)

**Short Bump**
```
Subject: Re: [Original subject]

[First name],

Wanted to bump this up—know how things get buried.

Still think [brief value prop] could be relevant for [Company].

Worth a quick chat?

[Your name]
```

**Bump with Social Proof**
```
Subject: Re: [Original subject]

Following up—wanted to mention [Similar Company] just [achieved result] using our approach.

Given [Company]'s focus on [relevant area], thought that might be relevant.

Open to discussing?

[Your name]
```

### Follow-Up #4: Case Study (18-21 days)

**Case Study Focus**
```

[First name],

Quick case study that might be relevant:

[Similar Company] was dealing with [challenge you assume they have].

In [timeframe], they achieved:
- [Result 1]
- [Result 2]
- [Result 3]

Their [title] said: "[Brief quote]"

[Your name]
```

### Follow-Up #5: Breakup (25-30 days)

**The Graceful Exit**
```
Subject: Closing the loop

[First name],

I've reached out a few times without connecting—no worries, I get it.

I'll assume [topic] isn't a priority right now and won't keep emailing.

If things change, I'm here. Just reply whenever.

Best of luck with everything.

[Your name]
```

**The Direct Question**
```
Subject: Should I stop emailing?

[First name],

Been trying to connect about [topic] without luck.

Want to make sure I'm not being annoying. Should I:
A) Stop reaching out—this isn't relevant
B) Try again in [timeframe] when timing might be better
C) Connect me with the right person if it's not you

Just let me know either way.

[Your name]
```

## Part 3: Reply Response Templates

### Positive Reply Responses

**To: "Yes, let's talk"**
```
Great to hear from you!

Here are some times this week:
- [Day 1] at [Time]
- [Day 2] at [Time]
- [Day 3] at [Time]

Or grab what works: [Calendar link]

Looking forward to connecting!
```

**To: "Tell me more"**
```
Happy to share more. Quick overview:

[2-3 bullet points of key value]

That said, the best way to see if this fits [Company] is a quick call. I can tailor it to your situation.

Does [specific time] work?
```

**To: "What does it cost?"**
```
Good question. Pricing depends on [factors], typically ranging [range].

For an accurate quote, I'd need to understand [2-3 factors].

Worth a quick call to scope it out? Takes 15 minutes and I can give you real numbers.

[Times] or [Calendar link]
```

**To: "Send more info"**
```
Absolutely. What specifically would be most useful?

1. How it works (overview)
2. Pricing details
3. Case studies from similar companies
4. Something else?

Or, if easier, I can walk through everything in a quick call and send follow-up materials after.

What works best for you?
```

### Objection Responses

**To: "Too expensive"**
```
I hear you—budget matters.

Quick question: what are you comparing against?

Understanding that helps me see if we have options that work, or if there's an ROI case worth looking at.

[Similar Company] had the same concern initially. After calculating [specific ROI], the math made sense.

Worth discussing the numbers for [Company]?
```

**To: "We already have a solution"**
```
Got it—makes sense you've addressed this.

Mind if I ask what you're using and how it's working?

Not trying to push you to switch—just curious how approaches compare. Sometimes companies use us alongside [common solutions] for [specific use case].

What's your current setup?
```

**To: "Not the right time"**
```
Totally understand—timing is everything.

Quick question: is it about [specific thing], or more general bandwidth?

Just want to understand when would make sense to reconnect. Happy to follow up at the right time rather than bug you now.

When should I circle back?
```

**To: "I'm not the decision maker"**
```
Appreciate you letting me know.

Who would be the right person to talk to about [topic]?

Happy to reach out directly—or if you could make a quick intro, that would be even better.

What works for you?
```

## Part 6: Quick Reference Template Formulas

### The Universal Template Structure
```
[Personalized opening - 1 sentence]

[Problem/context - 1-2 sentences]

[Your solution + proof - 1-2 sentences]

[CTA - 1 sentence]

[Sign-off]
```

### Word Count Guidelines

| Email Type | Word Count |
|------------|------------|
| First email | 50-125 |
| Follow-up | 25-75 |
| Value-add | 50-100 |
| Breakup | 25-50 |
| Reply | 25-75 |

### CTA Options

**Low Commitment**:
- "Worth a quick conversation?"
- "Does this resonate?"
- "Relevant to [Company]?"

**Medium Commitment**:
- "Would 15 minutes work?"
- "Open to a quick call?"
- "Can I show you how it works?"

**High Commitment**:
- "Does [specific time] work?"
- "[Calendar link]"
- "When should we schedule?"

## Template Customization Guidelines

### Before Using Any Template

1. **Research the recipient** - Find something specific
2. **Customize the opening** - Make it personal
3. **Adapt the value prop** - Match their situation
4. **Select relevant proof** - Use similar customers
5. **Adjust the CTA** - Match commitment level

### Red Flags That Kill Templates

- {{merge_field}} errors showing
- Generic compliments
- Irrelevant case studies
- Mismatched tone
- Obvious copy-paste feeling

### Testing Templates

- A/B test variations
- Track by template version
- Iterate based on data
- Retire underperformers

---

*Templates are starting points, not final products. The best emails combine proven structures with genuine personalization.*
