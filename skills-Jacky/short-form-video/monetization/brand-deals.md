# Brand Deals: The Complete Sponsorship Guide

## Overview

Brand deals are typically the highest-paying monetization method for short-form video creators. A single sponsored video can earn more than months of platform program revenue. This guide covers finding, negotiating, and executing successful brand partnerships.

## Why Brand Deals Pay More

### Value Proposition for Brands

Brands pay creators because:
- **Authentic reach:** Your audience trusts you more than ads
- **Targeted demographics:** Access to specific audience segments
- **Content creation:** They get professional content without in-house production
- **Social proof:** Association with trusted creators
- **Engagement:** Creator content typically outperforms brand content

### Market Rates

Brand deal rates have increased significantly with short-form video growth.

**Typical Rates by Following (2026):**

| Follower Count | Rate Per Video | Notes |
|----------------|----------------|-------|
| 5K-10K | $100-300 | Often product only |
| 10K-25K | $200-500 | Mix of cash and product |
| 25K-50K | $400-1,000 | More cash offers |
| 50K-100K | $800-2,000 | Reliable cash deals |
| 100K-250K | $1,500-4,000 | Professional partnerships |
| 250K-500K | $3,000-8,000 | Major brand interest |
| 500K-1M | $5,000-15,000 | Premium rates |
| 1M+ | $10,000-100,000+ | Celebrity-level |

**Factors Affecting Rates:**
- Engagement rate (higher = higher rates)
- Niche (finance, tech pay more than lifestyle)
- Platform (TikTok often highest for organic reach)
- Deliverables (more content = higher pay)
- Usage rights (brands paying for ad use)
- Exclusivity (competitive exclusion)

## Finding Brand Deals

### Method 1: Inbound (Brands Come to You)

**How to Attract Inbound:**
- Clear niche positioning
- Professional bio with contact info
- Consistent, high-quality content
- Engagement with brand content
- Being discoverable (business email in bio)

**Setting Up for Inbound:**
1. Switch to Business/Creator account
2. Add professional email to bio
3. Create a link in bio landing page
4. Mention you work with brands (optional)
5. Keep DMs open or check message requests

**Common Inbound Channels:**
- Instagram DMs
- Email (if in bio)
- TikTok messages
- Platform creator marketplaces

### Method 2: Outbound (You Reach Out to Brands)

**Identifying Target Brands:**
1. Products you actually use and love
2. Brands that work with similar creators
3. Brands advertising in your niche
4. Local businesses (starting out)
5. Brands running influencer programs

**Research Process:**
1. Find brands in your space
2. Check if they work with creators (look at tagged content)
3. Find the right contact (marketing/influencer team)
4. Identify their current needs
5. Prepare personalized pitch

**Where to Find Contacts:**
- LinkedIn (Influencer Marketing Manager, Social Media Manager)
- Company website (About/Team page)
- "Partnerships" or "Collaborations" page
- Cold email with educated guess (firstname@company.com)

**Outreach Template:**

```
Subject: [Brand Name] x [Your Name] Partnership Opportunity

Hi [Name],

I'm [Your Name], a [niche] creator on [platform] with [X followers] and [engagement rate]% engagement.

I've been using [Brand's Product] for [timeframe] and genuinely love it because [specific reason].

I noticed you're [current marketing effort you observed] and I think my audience of [audience description] would be highly interested in [product/campaign].

A few relevant videos I've created:
- [Link] - [views/engagement]
- [Link] - [views/engagement]

I'd love to explore a partnership. Would you be open to a quick call to discuss?

[Media kit attached]

Best,
[Your Name]
[Social links]
```

### Method 4: Agencies and Management

**When to Consider:**
- 100K+ followers
- Regular inbound interest
- Want to focus on content, not business
- Seeking larger brand deals

**What Agencies Provide:**
- Deal sourcing
- Negotiation
- Contract review
- Payment collection
- Strategic guidance

**Agency Fees:**
Typically 15-25% of deal value

**Finding Representation:**
- Research agencies in your niche
- Check who represents creators you admire
- Apply through agency websites
- Network at creator events

## Building Your Media Kit

A media kit is your professional resume for brand partnerships.

### Essential Elements

**Page 1: Overview**
- Professional photo/brand image
- Name and handle
- Niche/positioning statement
- Quick stats highlight

**Page 2: Audience Demographics**
- Follower count per platform
- Engagement rate
- Age breakdown
- Gender split
- Location (top countries/cities)
- Interests/behaviors

**Page 3: Performance Metrics**
- Average views per video
- Average engagement per video
- Notable viral content
- Growth trajectory

**Page 4: Past Collaborations**
- Previous brand partnerships
- Results achieved
- Testimonials (if available)

**Page 5: Services and Rates**
- Content types offered
- Rate card (optional—can omit to negotiate)
- Package options
- Additional services

**Page 6: Contact**
- Email
- Phone (optional)
- Social links
- Website/portfolio

### Media Kit Tools

**Premium:**
- MediaKits.com
- Press.kit
- Adobe InDesign templates

### Keeping It Updated

Update media kit:
- Monthly for stats
- After viral videos
- After major brand deals
- When rates change

## Negotiation

### Know Your Worth

**Calculate Your Value:**
- CPM (cost per thousand views): Your rate ÷ expected views × 1,000
- Engagement value: Engagement rate × industry benchmark rates
- Production value: Time, equipment, and skill required
- Usage rights value: What additional use is worth

**Industry Benchmarks:**
- Micro-influencer CPM: $10-50
- Mid-tier creator CPM: $50-150
- Large creator CPM: $100-300+

### Negotiation Tactics

**Never Accept First Offer:**
Initial offers are typically negotiable. Polite counter-offers are expected.

**Ask Questions First:**
"What's the budget range for this campaign?"
"What are all the deliverables you're looking for?"
"How will you be using this content?"

**Quantify Your Value:**
"My last 10 videos averaged X views with Y engagement. Based on that performance, this should reach approximately Z people."

**Bundle or Unbundle:**
- Add deliverables to increase total (Stories, additional videos)
- Remove deliverables to fit budget

**Understand Their Constraints:**
- Fixed campaign budgets
- Timeline pressures
- Usage right requirements
- Approval processes

### Red Flags in Offers

**Exposure Pay:**
"We can't pay but it's great exposure." Unless it's a massive brand with genuine value, decline.

**Excessive Deliverables:**
Many videos, Stories, multiple platforms, revisions, usage rights—for a small fee.

**Perpetual Usage:**
Asking for unlimited rights forever should cost significantly more.

**Unrealistic Expectations:**
Guaranteed views/engagement, overnight turnaround, specific viral results.

**No Contract:**
Always get agreements in writing.

## Contracts and Deliverables

### Key Contract Elements

**Scope of Work:**
- Number of videos
- Platforms
- Content type
- Key messaging requirements
- Hashtags/tags required

**Timeline:**
- Content due date
- Review period
- Publication date
- Campaign duration

**Compensation:**
- Total fee
- Payment schedule
- Payment method
- Net terms (Net 30, Net 60)

**Usage Rights:**
- Organic use only
- Paid media rights
- Duration of rights
- Territories covered

**Exclusivity:**
- Competitive exclusion period
- Categories covered
- Duration

**Approval Process:**
- Number of revisions included
- Review timeline
- Approval authority

### Contract Red Flags

- No payment terms/timeline
- Unlimited revisions
- Perpetual usage rights (without premium)
- Excessive exclusivity
- Liability for results
- Non-negotiable terms

**When in Doubt:**
Have a lawyer review. For deals over $5,000, this is almost always worth it.

## Executing Brand Deals

### Pre-Production

1. **Understand the brief thoroughly**
   - Key messages
   - Mandatory elements
   - Restrictions
   - Reference examples

2. **Plan your concept**
   - How to integrate authentically
   - Hook strategy
   - Content structure
   - Your unique angle

3. **Get pre-approval (if required)**
   - Share concept before filming
   - Confirm messaging approach
   - Clarify any questions

### Production

1. **Stay authentic**
   - Don't change your style completely
   - Integrate product naturally
   - Maintain your voice

2. **Hit the requirements**
   - All mandatory elements included
   - Proper disclosures (#ad, Paid Partnership)
   - Brand name pronounced correctly
   - Product shown clearly

3. **Exceed expectations**
   - Production quality
   - Creativity
   - Enthusiasm
   - Professionalism

### Post-Production and Review

1. **Self-review against brief**
   - All requirements met?
   - Disclosures included?
   - Quality standard maintained?

2. **Submit for review**
   - On time or early
   - All requested formats
   - Clear communication

3. **Handle feedback professionally**
   - Reasonable revisions expected
   - Pushback on unreasonable requests
   - Document changes

### Reporting and Follow-Up

**Provide Results:**
- Screenshot performance metrics
- Engagement breakdown
- Audience feedback highlights
- Comparison to typical performance

**Build Relationship:**
- Thank them professionally
- Express interest in future work
- Stay in touch periodically

**Get Testimonial:**
After successful campaigns, ask for quotes you can use in future pitches.

## Disclosure Requirements

### FTC Guidelines (US)

**Required When:**
- Paid to create content
- Received free product
- Any "material connection" to brand

**How to Disclose:**
- Clear and conspicuous
- Beginning of content (not buried)
- Platform-specific tools when available

**Acceptable Disclosures:**
- #ad
- #sponsored
- #partner
- "Paid partnership with [Brand]"
- Verbal disclosure in video

**Not Sufficient:**
- #sp
- Buried in hashtags
- Only in description (not video)
- Unclear language

### Platform-Specific

**TikTok:**
Use "Paid Partnership" label in posting flow

**Instagram:**
Use "Paid Partnership" tag (appears above content)

**YouTube:**
Check "includes paid promotion" box + verbal disclosure

## Building Long-Term Brand Relationships

### Why Long-Term Deals Are Better

- Predictable income
- Deeper brand knowledge
- Better rates over time
- Less constant pitching
- More creative freedom

### How to Get Long-Term Deals

1. **Overdeliver on first project**
2. **Propose ongoing relationship**
3. **Offer package discounts**
4. **Stay top of mind**
5. **Grow with the brand**

### Ambassador Programs

Many brands have formal ambassador programs:
- Monthly/quarterly content requirements
- Consistent monthly pay
- Product allowance
- Exclusive opportunities
- Longer-term commitment

## Summary: Brand Deal Success

1. **Build genuine audience first** - Brands pay for your influence
2. **Stay authentic** - Your credibility is your value
3. **Know your worth** - Research rates, don't undersell
4. **Professionalism matters** - Communication, deadlines, quality
5. **Relationships over transactions** - Long-term > one-off
6. **Always disclose** - FTC compliance protects everyone
7. **Deliver results** - Performance leads to repeat business
