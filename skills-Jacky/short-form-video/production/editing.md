# Video Editing for Short-Form: Complete Guide

## Overview

Editing is where good footage becomes great content. Short-form video editing has its own language—fast pacing, dynamic transitions, punchy cuts, and strategic use of text and effects. This guide covers tools, techniques, and workflows.

## Editing Philosophy for Short-Form

### The Short-Form Editing Mindset

**Every Second Counts:**
In short-form, there's no room for filler. Every second must earn its place.

**Mobile-First:**
Your content will be watched on phones. Edit for small screens.

**Sound On AND Sound Off:**
30-40% of viewers watch without sound. Captions and visual storytelling matter.

**Hook First:**
The first 3 seconds get edited first and get the most attention.

## Core Editing Techniques

### Pacing and Cuts

**The Rule of 2-3 Seconds:**
In short-form, change the visual every 2-3 seconds to maintain attention:
- New angle
- B-roll insert
- Text appearance
- Transition

**Jump Cuts:**
Cut dead air, ums, pauses, and unnecessary words. Short-form audiences expect fast pacing.

**How to Jump Cut:**
1. Review footage
2. Mark unnecessary segments
3. Cut and delete
4. Smooth transitions (or embrace the jump)

**Beat Matching:**
Align cuts with audio beats for satisfying rhythm:
1. Add music first
2. Identify beat points
3. Cut visuals to match beats
4. Preview and refine

### Transitions

**Types of Transitions:**

**Cut (Most Common):**
Instant switch between clips. Clean and professional.

**Fade/Dissolve:**
Gradual transition. Use sparingly—can feel dated.

**Zoom Transitions:**
Zoom in/out between clips. Dynamic and modern.

**Swipe/Slide:**
Clips slide in direction. Good for lists and sequences.

**Morph/Match Cut:**
Shapes or movements match across cut. Most sophisticated.

**When to Use Transitions:**
- Between distinct sections
- To indicate time passing
- For visual interest
- To hide awkward cuts

**When NOT to Use:**
- Every single cut
- When simple cut works
- When it distracts from content

### Text and Captions

**Caption Styles:**

**Full Transcription:**
Every word captioned. Best for accessibility and sound-off viewers.

**Key Words Only:**
Just important words emphasized. Less cluttered.

**Subtitle Style:**
Standard bottom captions. Professional but less engaging.

**Dynamic Captions:**
Animated, color-coded, moving. Most engaging but time-consuming.

**Caption Best Practices:**
- Large enough to read on mobile (minimum 40px)
- High contrast with background
- Centered or slightly above center
- 3-5 words per line maximum
- 1-2 lines visible at once
- Sync perfectly with audio

**Text Overlays (Non-Caption):**
- Hook text in first 3 seconds
- Key points emphasized
- CTAs (save this, follow for more)
- Labels and identifiers

### Audio Editing

**Audio Levels:**
- Voice: -6 to -12 dB
- Music: -18 to -24 dB (under voice)
- Music without voice: -12 to -15 dB

**Audio Ducking:**
Automatically lower music when voice speaks. Most apps have this feature.

**Sound Effects:**
Strategic use of:
- Whoosh for transitions
- Pop for text appearance
- Click for emphasis
- Ambient sounds for scenes

**Music Selection:**
- Trending sounds for discovery (TikTok/Reels)
- Copyright-free for YouTube
- Match energy to content
- Consider sound-off viewers

### Color Correction and Grading

**Basic Corrections:**
- Exposure (brightness)
- White balance (warmth)
- Contrast
- Saturation

**Color Grading:**
Stylistic color treatment for mood:
- Warm/cool tones
- High contrast (dramatic)
- Low contrast (soft)
- Specific color looks (cinematic, vibrant, etc.)

**Consistency:**
Apply same grade across all clips for cohesive look.

**LUTs (Look-Up Tables):**
Pre-made color grades you can apply:
- Save time
- Consistent look
- Professional results

## Effects and Enhancements

### Zoom Effects

**Zoom In:**
Creates emphasis, urgency, draws attention.

**Zoom Out:**
Reveals context, creates breathing room.

**Implementation:**
- Keyframe scale from 100% to 110-130%
- Duration: 0.3-0.5 seconds
- Ease in/out for smooth motion

### Speed Ramping

**Speed Up:**
- Boring segments
- Repetitive actions
- Time passage
- Dramatic effect

**Slow Down:**
- Important moments
- Dramatic reveals
- Detail work
- Emotional impact

**Implementation:**
- Cut clip at speed change point
- Adjust speed of segment
- Blend transitions between speeds

### Green Screen/Chroma Key

**Uses:**
- Custom backgrounds
- Inserting yourself into footage
- Creating effects

**Best Practices:**
- Even lighting on green screen
- Minimal shadows
- Don't wear green
- Key carefully around edges

### Split Screen

**Uses:**
- Before/after comparisons
- Multiple perspectives
- Duet-style content
- Reaction format

**Implementation:**
- Position and scale clips
- Add divider line if needed
- Balance audio between sources

## Workflow Optimization

### The Efficient Editing Workflow

**1. Import and Organize (5 min)**
- Import all footage
- Rename clips descriptively
- Create project structure

**2. Assembly Cut (15 min)**
- Rough arrangement of clips
- No polish, just structure
- Identify missing pieces

**3. Hook Edit (10 min)**
- Perfect the first 3 seconds
- This is most important

**4. Fine Cut (20 min)**
- Tighten timing
- Add transitions
- Remove dead air

**5. Text and Captions (15 min)**
- Add all text overlays
- Sync captions
- Check readability

**6. Audio (10 min)**
- Music selection
- Level balancing
- Sound effects

**7. Color (10 min)**
- Basic correction
- Style grading
- Consistency check

**8. Review (10 min)**
- Watch full through
- Check on mobile
- Fix issues

**9. Export (5 min)**
- Proper settings
- Platform-specific formats

### Batch Editing

Edit multiple videos efficiently:

**Template Method:**
1. Create one video with all elements
2. Save as template
3. Swap footage in template
4. Minor adjustments only

**Assembly Line Method:**
1. Import all footage for batch
2. Do all assembly cuts
3. Do all fine cuts
4. Add all text
5. Add all audio
6. Color all
7. Export all

### Keyboard Shortcuts (Learn These)

**Universal (vary by software):**
- Cut/slice: C or Razor tool
- Delete: Delete/Backspace
- Undo: Cmd/Ctrl + Z
- Zoom timeline: +/- or scroll
- Play/Pause: Spacebar
- Mark in/out: I and O
- Add to timeline: . or ,

Learning shortcuts cuts editing time by 50%+.

## Export Settings

### TikTok/Reels/Shorts Universal

**Resolution:** 1080x1920 (1080p vertical)
**Aspect Ratio:** 9:16
**Frame Rate:** 30fps (60fps for high motion)
**Codec:** H.264
**Bitrate:** 8-15 Mbps
**Audio:** AAC, 256kbps

### Platform-Specific Notes

**TikTok:**
- Max 10 min
- Max 287.6 MB (Android), 500 MB (iOS)
- Supports most formats

**YouTube Shorts:**
- Max 60 seconds
- Same specs as YouTube
- Higher bitrate accepted

**Instagram Reels:**
- Max 90 seconds
- Max 4GB file size
- MP4 or MOV

## Common Editing Mistakes

### Mistake 1: Over-Editing
Adding effects, transitions, and text on every frame. 

**Fix:** Let content breathe. Use effects purposefully.

### Mistake 2: Slow Pacing
Not cutting enough dead air.

**Fix:** Watch and cut ruthlessly. Every second must earn its place.

### Mistake 3: Unreadable Text
Text too small, wrong color, or in wrong position.

**Fix:** Check on phone before posting. Test with friend.

### Mistake 4: Unbalanced Audio
Music too loud or voice too quiet.

**Fix:** Use audio ducking. Test with headphones.

### Mistake 5: Ignoring Hook
Spending same time on all parts when hook needs most attention.

**Fix:** Edit hook first. Perfect it before anything else.

### Mistake 6: No Captions
Ignoring sound-off viewers.

**Fix:** Always add captions. Use auto-caption tools.
