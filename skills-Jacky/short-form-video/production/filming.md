# Filming Techniques for Short-Form Video

## Overview

Great filming is the foundation of great content. While short-form video is forgiving of imperfection, understanding filming fundamentals elevates your content above the competition. This guide covers camera techniques, composition, movement, and practical filming workflows.

## Camera Fundamentals

### Understanding Your Camera

Whether using a phone or dedicated camera, master these basics:

**Focus**
- **Auto-focus:** Let camera track focus automatically (default for most short-form)
- **Manual focus:** Control focus for creative effect
- **Face tracking:** Enable for talking head to maintain face focus
- **Tap to focus:** Tap subject on phone screen to set focus point

**Exposure**
- **Auto exposure:** Camera adjusts brightness automatically
- **Exposure lock:** Hold to lock exposure on specific area
- **Manual exposure:** Control brightness for consistency
- **HDR:** High dynamic range for balanced shadows/highlights

**White Balance**
- **Auto white balance:** Camera adjusts for light color
- **Manual white balance:** Set for consistent color across clips
- **Presets:** Daylight, cloudy, tungsten, fluorescent

**Frame Rate**
- **24fps:** Cinematic look
- **30fps:** Standard for most content
- **60fps:** Smooth motion, good for slow-mo

**Resolution**
- **1080p:** Standard quality, smaller file sizes
- **4K:** Higher quality, allows cropping in post

### Phone Camera Settings

**iPhone:**
- Settings > Camera > Record Video: Set to 4K/30fps
- Settings > Camera > Formats: High Efficiency
- Enable grid lines for composition

**Android:**
- Settings > Camera > Video Size: Highest available
- Enable guidelines/grid
- Consider pro video apps for more control

## Composition

### The Rule of Thirds

Divide frame into 3x3 grid. Place key elements along lines or at intersections.

**For Talking Head:**
- Eyes at top third line
- Slight space above head
- Off-center for visual interest

**For Demonstrations:**
- Action in center or offset
- Key elements at intersections
- Subject has "room" to move into

### Headroom and Framing

**Headroom:**
Space above subject's head.
- Too much: Subject feels small, wasted space
- Too little: Cramped feeling, cut-off look
- Just right: Slight space (about fist-width)

**Framing Options:**
- **Extreme close-up:** Face only (intimate, intense)
- **Close-up:** Head and shoulders (standard talking head)
- **Medium shot:** Waist up (shows more body language)
- **Full shot:** Full body (shows environment/action)

### Safe Zones

Keep important content out of areas covered by platform UI:

**Top:** ~10% (notification bar, username)
**Bottom:** ~15% (captions, buttons, description)
**Center:** Safe for all important content

### Background Considerations

**Clean Background:**
- Minimal distractions
- Solid colors work well
- No clutter visible

**Contextual Background:**
- Relevant to content
- Adds credibility
- Not distracting

**Depth:**
- Some background depth adds visual interest
- Avoid flat walls when possible
- Create separation between subject and background

### Vertical vs. Horizontal Thinking

Short-form is vertical (9:16). Think vertically:

**Vertical Advantages:**
- More focus on subject
- Natural phone viewing
- Immersive full-screen

**Vertical Considerations:**
- Less horizontal space for action
- Important elements stack vertically
- Movement vertical or toward camera works best

## Lighting Techniques

### Three-Point Lighting

**Key Light:**
Main light source. Position 45° to side, slightly above eye level.

**Fill Light:**
Softer light opposite key to fill shadows. Less intense than key.

**Back Light:**
Light from behind to separate subject from background.

**For Simple Setup:**
- Window as key light
- Reflector as fill
- Small light as back light

### Natural Light

**Window Light:**
- Face window (never back to window)
- Overcast days provide soft, even light
- Morning/evening for warm, flattering light
- Midday for neutral, brighter light

**Outdoor Light:**
- Open shade for even light
- Golden hour (sunrise/sunset) for warmth
- Overcast for diffused light
- Direct sun creates harsh shadows

### DIY Lighting Hacks

**Shower Curtain Diffusion:**
Hang white shower curtain in front of harsh light to soften.

**Poster Board Reflector:**
White poster board bounces light into shadows.

**Aluminum Foil Reflector:**
Crumpled foil on cardboard for harder reflection.

**Phone Flashlight Fill:**
Second phone as fill light in pinch.

## Camera Movement

### Steady Shots

**Tripod:**
Best for talking head and tutorials. Zero movement.

**DIY Stability:**
- Lean against wall
- Elbows tucked to body
- Hold breath while filming short clips
- Stack books for phone mount

### Intentional Movement

**Pan:**
Horizontal camera movement. Reveal environment or follow action.

**Tilt:**
Vertical camera movement. Reveal height or follow vertical action.

**Zoom:**
Movement toward/away from subject. Creates emphasis or reveals.

**Tracking:**
Following subject as they move. Maintains subject in frame.

### Gimbal Movement

**When to Use:**
- Walking/moving shots
- Smooth following shots
- Dynamic vlog content

**Gimbal Tips:**
- Practice before filming
- Walk smoothly, bend knees
- Keep movements intentional
- Don't overuse (can be disorienting)

### In-Camera Transitions

**Zoom Transition:**
End clip zooming into object, start next clip zooming out from similar object.

**Swipe Transition:**
Swipe camera quickly at end of clip, start next with matching swipe.

**Object Wipe:**
Cover lens with hand/object, start next from covered lens.

**Rotation Transition:**
Spin camera at end, match rotation at start of next.

## Filming Workflow

### Pre-Filming Checklist

**Technical Prep:**
- [ ] Battery charged (phone at 100%)
- [ ] Storage space available
- [ ] Camera settings verified
- [ ] Audio tested
- [ ] Lighting set up
- [ ] Tripod/mount positioned
- [ ] Frame checked for composition

**Content Prep:**
- [ ] Outline/script ready
- [ ] Props gathered
- [ ] Wardrobe/appearance ready
- [ ] Environment tidied
- [ ] Quiet environment ensured

### Filming Best Practices

**Multiple Takes:**
Film 3-5 takes of each segment. You'll have options in edit.

**Start and End Buffer:**
Start recording 2-3 seconds before action. End 2-3 seconds after. Gives editing room.

**Check Focus:**
Review first take to ensure focus is correct before filming more.

**Monitor Audio:**
Wear headphones to monitor audio while filming if possible.

**Review Footage:**
Check footage periodically to catch issues early.

### Batch Filming

**Benefits:**
- More efficient use of setup time
- Consistent look across videos
- Content backlog creation
- Less frequent setup/breakdown

**Batch Filming Process:**
1. Set up environment (lighting, background, camera)
2. Film all talking head segments for multiple videos
3. Adjust setup minimally between videos
4. Change outfits for variety if filming many
5. Film all B-roll separately
6. Break down setup once

**Batch Filming Tips:**
- Plan 5-10 videos per session
- Have all scripts/outlines ready before starting
- Take short breaks (energy maintenance)
- Stay hydrated (voice quality)
- Track which takes go with which video

### Energy Management

**Before Filming:**
- Light exercise to energize
- Vocal warm-up (humming, scales)
- Review content/scripts
- Get in positive headspace

**During Filming:**
- Take breaks every 30-45 minutes
- Stay hydrated (water on set)
- Reset energy between videos
- Don't film when tired/frustrated

**Energy Hacks:**
- Jump up and down before takes
- Power pose for confidence
- Smile before speaking (changes vocal tone)
- Review good takes for momentum

## Specific Filming Scenarios

### Talking Head

**Setup:**
- Camera at eye level
- Distance: Chest to head in frame
- Good lighting on face
- Clean background
- Audio source close

**Tips:**
- Look at lens, not screen
- Slight body angle (not square to camera)
- Hand gestures in frame
- Energy 30% higher than normal

### Tutorials/Demonstrations

**Setup:**
- Camera angle shows action clearly
- Overhead for desk work
- Side angle for body movement
- Close enough to see detail

**Tips:**
- Film steps separately for easier editing
- Show and narrate simultaneously
- Get close-ups of important details
- Film full process, cut in edit

### Outdoor/Mobile Filming

**Challenges:**
- Inconsistent lighting
- Background noise
- Stability issues
- People/distractions

**Solutions:**
- Use shade for even lighting
- External microphone essential
- Gimbal or stabilization
- Multiple takes (some will be interrupted)
- Scout location first

### Screen Recording

**Setup:**
- Clean desktop (close unnecessary apps)
- Hide personal/irrelevant info
- Cursor visible and moving deliberately
- Audio recording clear

**Tips:**
- Zoom in on relevant areas
- Slow down mouse movement
- Narrate as you demonstrate
- Plan the flow before recording

### Product Filming

**Setup:**
- Good lighting on product
- Clean background
- Multiple angles planned
- Props if needed

**Tips:**
- Show scale (hand, common object)
- Rotate to show all sides
- Close-ups of details
- Action/usage shots

## Troubleshooting Common Issues

### Issue: Bad Lighting

**Symptoms:**
Dark, uneven, harsh shadows

**Fixes:**
- Move to face window
- Add light source
- Use reflector for shadows
- Film at different time of day

### Issue: Out of Focus

**Symptoms:**
Blurry subject

**Fixes:**
- Tap to focus before recording
- Ensure adequate light (cameras focus better)
- Check lens cleanliness
- Don't move too quickly

### Issue: Audio Problems

**Symptoms:**
Echo, low volume, background noise

**Fixes:**
- Film in quieter location
- Use external microphone
- Get closer to audio source
- Add soft surfaces to room (rugs, curtains)

### Issue: Shaky Footage

**Symptoms:**
Unstable, motion sickness-inducing video

**Fixes:**
- Use tripod
- Brace against stable surface
- Hold breath during short takes
- Enable stabilization (phone settings)

### Issue: Wrong Framing

**Symptoms:**
Cut off head, too much empty space, poor composition

**Fixes:**
- Enable grid lines
- Check frame before filming
- Leave room for cropping in post
- Review first take immediately

## Advanced Techniques

### Creating Depth

**Foreground Elements:**
Place objects between camera and subject for depth.

**Background Separation:**
Distance between subject and background creates visual depth.

**Aperture Control:**
Wider aperture (lower f-number) creates background blur.

### Dynamic Angles

**Low Angle:**
Camera below eye level. Makes subject appear powerful.

**High Angle:**
Camera above eye level. Makes subject appear smaller/humble.

**Dutch Angle:**
Camera tilted. Creates tension or unease.

### Creative Techniques

**Time-Lapse:**
Speed up extended processes (cleaning, cooking, building)

**Slow Motion:**
Capture detail in fast motion (cooking sizzle, makeup application)

**Stop Motion:**
Frame-by-frame animation effect

## Summary: Filming Excellence

### The Essentials

1. **Good lighting** — Most important technical element
2. **Stable shot** — Tripod or stabilization
3. **Clear audio** — External mic if possible
4. **Proper framing** — Rule of thirds, safe zones
5. **Multiple takes** — Options in edit

### The Workflow

1. Prepare environment
2. Check technical settings
3. Test with short clip
4. Film with energy
5. Review and adjust
6. Batch for efficiency

### The Mindset

Technical quality supports content quality, but doesn't replace it. Master the basics, then focus on what you're saying rather than how you're filming it. Authenticity beats perfection.
