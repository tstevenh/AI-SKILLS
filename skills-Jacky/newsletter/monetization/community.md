# Building Paid Communities: Complete Guide

A paid community transforms a newsletter from one-way content into a two-way relationship. This guide covers community strategy, platforms, pricing, and operations.

---

## Why Communities Work

### The Community Advantage

**Newsletter alone:**
- You create all value
- One-to-many relationship
- Value stops when content stops
- Easy to churn

**Newsletter + Community:**
- Members create value
- Many-to-many relationships
- Value continues between sends
- Network effects reduce churn

### Community Economics

**Revenue Math:**
```
500 members × $49/month = $24,500 MRR
1,000 members × $49/month = $49,000 MRR
```

**Churn Comparison:**
| Product | Monthly Churn |
|---------|---------------|
| Newsletter subscription | 5-8% |
| Community membership | 3-5% |
| Active community | 2-4% |

Communities retain better because of social bonds.

## Community Models

### Model 1: Content + Community

**Structure:**
- Premium newsletter access
- Private community space
- Content discussions
- Member Q&A

**Best for:** Writers, analysts, educators

**Example:**
- $29-49/month
- Weekly premium content
- Slack/Discord community
- Monthly AMA calls

### Model 2: Networking Community

**Structure:**
- Access to members
- Introductions and connections
- Events and meetups
- Member directory

**Best for:** Professional niches, industries

**Example:**
- $99-299/month
- Curated member base
- Networking events
- Collaboration opportunities

### Model 3: Accountability Community

**Structure:**
- Goals and tracking
- Accountability partners
- Progress sharing
- Support and motivation

**Best for:** Skills, habits, goals

**Example:**
- $49-99/month
- Weekly check-ins
- Partner matching
- Celebration of wins

### Model 4: Expert Access Community

**Structure:**
- Direct access to expert (you)
- Office hours and Q&A
- Personalized advice
- Group coaching

**Best for:** Coaches, consultants, specialists

**Example:**
- $149-499/month
- Weekly office hours
- Async Q&A
- Hot seat coaching

## Platform Selection

### Discord

**Best for:** Tech-savvy audiences, younger demographics, gaming-adjacent

**Pros:**
- Free to use
- Highly customizable
- Rich features (bots, roles)
- Good mobile app

**Cons:**
- Can feel overwhelming
- Learning curve
- Gamer association
- Limited payment integration

**Cost:** Free

### Slack

**Best for:** Professionals, B2B, remote workers

**Pros:**
- Familiar to professionals
- Clean interface
- Good integrations
- Searchable history

**Cons:**
- Expensive at scale
- Limited free tier
- No built-in payments
- Can feel like work

**Cost:** Free tier limited; $8.75/user/mo for Pro

### Circle

**Best for:** Course creators, premium communities

**Pros:**
- Built for communities
- Payment integration
- Course features
- Clean design

**Cons:**
- Monthly cost
- Newer platform
- Less customization

**Cost:** $39-199/month

### Skool

**Best for:** Course + community combos, gamification

**Pros:**
- Simple interface
- Built-in gamification
- Course integration
- Payment processing

**Cons:**
- $99/month
- Less customization
- Newer platform

**Cost:** $99/month flat

### Mighty Networks

**Best for:** All-in-one creators

**Pros:**
- App creation
- Events and courses
- Payment processing
- Branded experience

**Cons:**
- Complex
- Learning curve
- Higher cost

**Cost:** $33-119/month

### Platform Comparison

| Platform | Best For | Monthly Cost | Payment Built-In |
|----------|----------|--------------|------------------|
| Discord | Tech/gaming | Free | No |
| Slack | Professional | Free-$$/user | No |
| Circle | Premium | $39-199 | Yes |
| Skool | Course+Community | $99 | Yes |
| Mighty | All-in-one | $33-119 | Yes |

## Pricing Strategy

### Pricing Factors

**Consider:**
- Value delivered
- Time commitment
- Competitor pricing
- Audience willingness to pay
- Your positioning

### Pricing Tiers

**Budget Tier ($19-39/month):**
- Basic community access
- Content archive
- Limited direct access

**Standard Tier ($49-99/month):**
- Full community
- Live events
- Some direct access
- Most choose this

**Premium Tier ($149-299/month):**
- Everything plus
- More direct access
- Priority support
- Exclusive perks

### Founding Member Pricing

**Launch Strategy:**
- 30-50% off "forever" price
- Limited time or spots
- Creates urgency
- Builds initial base

**Example:**
- Regular: $99/month
- Founding: $49/month (locked forever)
- First 100 members only

### Annual vs. Monthly

| Billing | Pros | Cons |
|---------|------|------|
| Monthly | Lower barrier, easy trial | Higher churn |
| Annual | Better retention, cash upfront | Harder sell |
| Both | Flexibility | Complexity |

**Recommendation:** Offer both, incentivize annual (20-30% discount)

## Community Operations

### Launch Checklist

**Before Opening:**
- [ ] Platform set up
- [ ] Channels/spaces organized
- [ ] Welcome message written
- [ ] Rules/guidelines documented
- [ ] Onboarding process designed
- [ ] First week of content planned

**At Launch:**
- [ ] Invite founding members
- [ ] Post welcome thread
- [ ] Make introductions
- [ ] Ask starter questions
- [ ] Be highly active

**First Month:**
- [ ] Daily presence
- [ ] Respond to everything
- [ ] Create momentum
- [ ] Gather feedback
- [ ] Iterate

### Channel Structure

**Recommended Channels:**
```
📢 ANNOUNCEMENTS
- Community news
- New content
- Event reminders

👋 INTRODUCTIONS
- New member intros
- Welcome thread

💬 GENERAL
- Open discussion
- Random chat

📚 RESOURCES
- Curated links
- Member contributions

❓ Q&A
- Questions for you
- Member help

🏆 WINS
- Success sharing
- Celebrations

[TOPIC-SPECIFIC CHANNELS]
- Based on your niche
```

### Engagement Tactics

**Daily:**
- Post something (question, insight)
- Respond to discussions
- Welcome new members
- Highlight good posts

**Weekly:**
- Host live event (AMA, office hours)
- Share exclusive content
- Run engagement prompt
- Celebrate member wins

**Monthly:**
- Member spotlight
- Community stats
- New feature/benefit
- Feedback collection

### Moderation

**Community Rules:**
1. Be respectful
2. Stay on topic
3. No self-promotion (unless allowed)
4. Add value
5. Protect privacy

**When to Moderate:**
- Rule violations
- Toxic behavior
- Spam
- Off-topic derailing

**How to Moderate:**
1. Private message first
2. Public only if needed
3. Clear explanation
4. Consistent enforcement
5. Ban as last resort

## Member Retention

### Why Members Churn

1. Not enough value
2. Forgot about it
3. No connections made
4. Life circumstances
5. Found alternative

### Retention Tactics

**1. Strong Onboarding**
```
Day 1: Welcome email + first steps
Day 2: Check-in + intro prompt
Day 3: First live event invite
Day 7: "How's it going?" check-in
Day 30: Value reminder
```

**2. Build Connections**
- Introduction threads
- Member matching
- Collaboration opportunities
- Small group activities

**3. Regular Touchpoints**
- Weekly events
- Daily engagement
- Email reminders
- New content

**4. Track Engagement**
- Who's active?
- Who's lurking?
- Who's gone silent?
- Re-engage at-risk members

**5. Gather Feedback**
- Monthly surveys
- Exit interviews
- Suggestion channels
- Act on feedback

### Engagement Scoring

```
MEMBER ENGAGEMENT TRACKING

Score each member monthly:

Posts/comments: +1 per activity
Event attendance: +5 per event
Helping others: +3 per instance
Contributions: +10 per resource shared
Silent 7+ days: -5
Silent 30+ days: -20

HIGH (50+): Active member, may be ambassador material
MEDIUM (20-50): Engaged, typical member
LOW (5-20): At risk, needs re-engagement
CRITICAL (<5): Likely to churn, urgent outreach
```

## Scaling Community

### When You Need Help

**Signs:**
- Can't keep up with activity
- Quality declining
- Missing messages
- Burnout symptoms

**Typically at:**
- 200+ active members
- 10+ hours/week community time
- Growing beyond capacity

### Community Roles

**Community Manager:**
- Day-to-day operations
- Member support
- Moderation
- Event coordination

**Cost:** $2-5k/month (part-time) to $5-10k (full-time)

**Member Moderators:**
- Volunteer or paid
- Enforce rules
- Answer questions
- Report issues

**Compensation:** Free membership, small stipend, or per-hour

### Systems for Scale

**Automate:**
- Welcome messages
- Recurring prompts
- Event reminders
- Member milestones

**Template:**
- Common responses
- Event formats
- Onboarding messages
- Feedback surveys

**Document:**
- Community guidelines
- Moderation decisions
- FAQs
- Processes

## Community + Newsletter Integration

### How They Work Together

**Newsletter drives community:**
- Promote community in newsletter
- Preview community discussions
- Highlight member wins
- Create FOMO

**Community supports newsletter:**
- Content ideas from discussions
- Feedback on content
- Testimonials
- Deeper engagement

### Integration Tactics

**In Newsletter:**
- "This week in the community..."
- Member spotlight
- Discussion preview
- Join CTA

**In Community:**
- Newsletter discussion thread
- Early access to content
- Behind-the-scenes
- Input on future content

### Conversion Funnel

```
Free Newsletter
    ↓
Engaged reader (opens, clicks)
    ↓
Community awareness (mentions, CTAs)
    ↓
Free trial or entry offer
    ↓
Community membership
    ↓
Active member
    ↓
Ambassador (brings others)
```

## Common Mistakes

### Mistake 1: Launching Empty

**Problem:** Community with no members feels dead
**Solution:** Seed with 20-50 founding members before opening

### Mistake 2: Over-Automation

**Problem:** Feels robotic and impersonal
**Solution:** Personal touch on key moments

### Mistake 3: No Clear Purpose

**Problem:** Members don't know why they're there
**Solution:** Clear positioning and regular value reminders

### Mistake 4: Founder Disappears

**Problem:** If you're not there, why should they be?
**Solution:** Consistent presence, especially early

### Mistake 5: Too Many Channels

**Problem:** Fragmented, confusing experience
**Solution:** Start minimal, add based on need

### Mistake 6: Not Charging Enough

**Problem:** Low price = low commitment
**Solution:** Price for value, not accessibility
