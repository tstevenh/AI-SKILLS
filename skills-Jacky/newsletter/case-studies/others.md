# Additional Newsletter Case Studies: Growth Lessons

Five more newsletter case studies with detailed breakdowns and actionable lessons.

## Case Study 1: Stratechery (Ben Thompson)

### The Numbers

| Metric | Value |
|--------|-------|
| Founded | 2014 |
| Model | Paid subscription |
| Price | $12/month or $120/year |
| Est. Subscribers | 40,000+ paid |
| Est. Revenue | $5-6 million ARR |
| Format | Long-form analysis |

### The Story

**Background:**
Ben Thompson was a tech industry analyst who wanted to write about tech strategy. He launched Stratechery as a free blog, then added a paid Daily Update subscription.

**The Model:**
- Free weekly article (growth driver)
- Paid daily analysis (monetization)
- Premium podcast (additional value)
- No sponsors (pure subscription)

**Growth Strategy:**

*Phase 1: Authority Building (2014-2015)*
- Published consistently
- Developed unique frameworks
- Built reputation in tech circles
- Twitter as distribution

*Phase 2: Monetization (2015-2016)*
- Launched paid Daily Update
- Maintained free weekly content
- Word of mouth from industry
- No hard selling

*Phase 3: Scale (2017-present)*
- Added podcast
- International audience
- Premium positioning
- Consistent quality

### Key Lessons

**1. Expertise Beats Volume**
One deep analysis weekly > seven shallow posts.

**2. Free Content Funds Paid**
The free weekly article drives all growth.

**3. Premium Pricing Works**
$12/month for genuine expertise = sustainable business.

**4. Consistency Over Years**
10+ years of weekly publishing built massive trust.

**5. Network Effects Through Quality**
Industry insiders share because it makes them look smart.

## Case Study 2: The Skimm

### The Numbers

| Metric | Value |
|--------|-------|
| Founded | 2012 |
| Peak Subscribers | 7+ million |
| Funding | $25+ million raised |
| Model | Free + sponsors |
| Target | Millennial women |

### The Story

**Background:**
Carly Zakin and Danielle Weisberg, former NBC producers, noticed their friends weren't keeping up with news. They created a daily briefing specifically for busy millennial women.

**The Approach:**
- Daily email before 6 AM
- News explained simply
- Conversational voice
- Lifestyle + hard news blend

**Growth Strategy:**

*Phase 1: Niche Down (2012-2013)*
- Targeted millennial women specifically
- Voice matched audience
- Morning routine placement
- Personal network launch

*Phase 2: Referral Engine (2013-2015)*
- "Skimm'bassadors" program
- Campus ambassadors
- Incentivized sharing
- Community building

*Phase 3: Product Expansion (2015-present)*
- Skimm Ahead (calendar app)
- Skimm Studios (video)
- Skimm Well (wellness)
- Podcasts

### Key Lessons

**1. Know Your Audience Deeply**
Built specifically for one persona, not everyone.

**2. Voice as Differentiator**
The tone was as important as the content.

**3. Community Over Audience**
Skimm'bassadors created belonging.

**4. Expand Thoughtfully**
Only expanded to adjacent products.

**5. Venture Capital Trade-offs**
Funding enabled scale but created pressure.

## Case Study 3: 1440 (Tim Huelskamp)

### The Numbers

| Metric | Value |
|--------|-------|
| Founded | 2017 |
| Subscribers | 3+ million |
| Model | Free + sponsors |
| Format | Daily news brief |
| Differentiator | Unbiased news |

### The Story

**Background:**
Tim Huelskamp, former politician, noticed news was increasingly polarized. He created a deliberately balanced daily newsletter.

**The Positioning:**
- No opinion or bias
- Facts only
- Multiple perspectives
- Short format

**Growth Strategy:**

*Phase 1: Position Clearly (2017-2019)*
- "Unbiased" as core promise
- Fact-check everything
- Balanced source selection
- Trust building

*Phase 2: Paid Acquisition (2019-2021)*
- Heavy Facebook advertising
- Newsletter network ads
- Podcast sponsorships
- Strong unit economics

*Phase 3: Scale (2021-present)*
- Multi-million subscriber base
- Major sponsor revenue
- Editorial team growth
- Maintained positioning

### Key Lessons

**1. Counter-Position Wins**
In a polarized market, neutral stood out.

**2. Trust as Product**
The promise of no bias IS the value proposition.

**3. Paid Growth at Scale**
With unit economics working, scale aggressively.

**4. Simple Format Scales**
Basic brief format = consistent production.

**5. Editorial Discipline**
Maintaining neutrality requires constant vigilance.

## Case Study 4: Not Boring (Packy McCormick)

### The Numbers

| Metric | Value |
|--------|-------|
| Founded | 2020 |
| Subscribers | 200,000+ |
| Model | Free + sponsors + venture |
| Format | Long-form analysis |
| Niche | Business strategy + tech |

### The Story

**Background:**
Packy McCormick started writing about business during COVID lockdowns. His deep dives on companies and trends gained rapid following.

**The Approach:**
- 5,000-10,000 word deep dives
- Heavy visual elements
- Optimistic tone
- Business + tech focus

**Growth Strategy:**

*Phase 1: Twitter-First (2020)*
- Built Twitter audience
- Engaged in tech circles
- Newsletter as Twitter extension
- Thread previews

*Phase 2: Quality Over Quantity (2020-2021)*
- One epic piece per week
- Shareable insights
- Twitter as distribution
- Substack network

*Phase 3: Business Building (2021-present)*
- Not Boring Capital (venture fund)
- Sponsor revenue
- Premium depth
- Industry authority

### Key Lessons

**1. Twitter and Newsletter Synergy**
Built both simultaneously, each feeding the other.

**2. Go Long When Others Go Short**
10,000 words when everyone said keep it short.

**3. Optimism Differentiates**
Positive framing in often cynical media landscape.

**4. Newsletter as Platform**
Used newsletter to launch venture fund.

**5. Visual Investment**
Heavy use of graphics and memes.

## Case Study 5: The Hustle's Trends

### The Numbers

| Metric | Value |
|--------|-------|
| Launched | 2019 |
| Price | $299/year |
| Model | Paid subscription |
| Parent | The Hustle |
| Focus | Business trends |

### The Story

**Background:**
The Hustle launched Trends as a premium companion to the free newsletter—deeper research, exclusive content, and community.

**The Product:**
- Trend reports and analysis
- Business ideas database
- Community forum
- Premium support

**Growth Strategy:**

*Phase 1: Free-to-Paid Funnel (2019)*
- Free Hustle builds audience
- Trends for superfans
- Premium positioning
- Exclusive content

*Phase 2: Community Value (2020-2021)*
- Forum engagement
- Member contributions
- Networking value
- Discussions

*Phase 3: Content Library (2021-present)*
- Deep trend reports
- Research archive
- Searchable database
- Ongoing updates

### Key Lessons

**1. Premium Tier Works**
5-10% of engaged audience will pay more.

**2. Community as Moat**
Forum discussions keep subscribers engaged.

**3. Content + Community Combination**
Neither alone; both together.

**4. Clear Free/Paid Split**
Free = news; Paid = analysis + community.

**5. Ongoing Value Required**
Subscription needs continuous new content.

## Cross-Case Learnings

### Growth Patterns

**All successful newsletters share:**
1. Clear, specific positioning
2. Consistent publishing schedule
3. Distinctive voice or approach
4. Multiple growth channels
5. Years of persistent effort

### Business Model Insights

| Model | Best For | Challenge |
|-------|----------|-----------|
| Free + Sponsors | Scale, broad appeal | Need volume |
| Paid Subscription | Deep expertise | Need authority |
| Hybrid | Balanced approach | Complexity |
| Products | Creator economy | Development time |

### Common Success Factors

**Content:**
- Quality over quantity
- Consistent publishing
- Unique perspective
- Audience-first approach

**Growth:**
- Multiple channels working together
- Referral programs
- Paid when economics work
- Social presence

**Monetization:**
- Match model to content
- Don't rush monetization
- Multiple revenue streams
- Protect trust

### What Separated Winners

**The top newsletters did differently:**

1. **Patience:** Years of consistent publishing
2. **Specificity:** Clear niche and positioning
3. **Voice:** Distinctive and memorable
4. **Systems:** Sustainable operations
5. **Audience obsession:** Everything for the reader

## Applying These Lessons

### For New Newsletters

**Month 1-6:**
- Find your positioning (like all case studies)
- Establish voice (Hustle, Skimm)
- Build consistently (Stratechery)
- Start small, grow organically

**Month 6-12:**
- Add referral program (Morning Brew, Skimm)
- Build social presence (Not Boring)
- Test monetization (small)
- Find what works

**Year 1-2:**
- Scale what works
- Add revenue streams
- Build team (if needed)
- Deepen positioning

### For Growing Newsletters

**Evaluate:**
- Which case study matches your model?
- What tactics haven't you tried?
- Where's your growth ceiling?
- What's your unfair advantage?

**Apply:**
- Test new channels
- Consider adjacencies
- Build sustainable systems
- Protect what works
