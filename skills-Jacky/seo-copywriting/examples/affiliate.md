## What to Look for in Podcast Hosting

### Storage & Bandwidth

Most hosts charge by storage (hours uploaded) or bandwidth 
(downloads). Calculate your needs:

- 30-min episode = ~30MB
- 10,000 downloads = ~300GB bandwidth

### Distribution

Your host should submit to:
- Apple Podcasts
- Spotify
- Google Podcasts
- Amazon Music
- Other directories

### Analytics

At minimum, you need:
- Download counts
- Listening platforms
- Geographic data
- Episode performance

### Monetization

Some hosts offer built-in monetization:
- Dynamic ad insertion
- Patron/subscription options
- Sponsorship marketplaces

## Frequently Asked Questions

### What's the best free podcast hosting?

Spotify for Podcasters (formerly Anchor) is the best free 
option. It's truly free with unlimited storage. The trade-off 
is limited analytics and less control.

### How much does podcast hosting cost?

Quality podcast hosting typically costs $10-25/month for 
hobbyist podcasters. High-download shows may pay $50-100+.

### Can I switch podcast hosts later?

Yes, you can redirect your RSS feed to a new host. Most hosts 
have import tools. It's not difficult, but do it before your 
old host cancels your account.

### Do I need a website for my podcast?

Most hosts provide a basic website. For a professional presence, 
you might want your own site—but it's not required to start.

## Affiliate Content Best Practices

### Disclosure Requirements

Always disclose affiliate relationships:

```markdown
*Disclosure: This post contains affiliate links. I may earn 
a commission if you make a purchase through these links, at 
no extra cost to you. I only recommend products I've personally 
used and trust.*
```

**Placement:** Top of post and near CTAs

### Building Trust

1. **Be honest about cons** — Credibility over commissions
2. **Show personal experience** — Screenshots, stories, results
3. **Compare fairly** — Don't trash competitors unfairly
4. **Update regularly** — Outdated info loses trust
5. **Disclose clearly** — FTC requires it anyway

### Conversion Optimization

1. **Multiple CTAs** — Intro, mid-content, conclusion
2. **Comparison tables** — Easy decision-making
3. **Clear recommendations** — "Best for X" statements
4. **Urgency when real** — Limited-time offers only if true
5. **Answer objections** — FAQ section addressing concerns

### Content That Converts

| Content Type | Conversion Rate | Best For |
|--------------|-----------------|----------|
| Product reviews | High | Single product |
| "Best X" listicles | Very high | Multiple products |
| Comparison posts | High | Decision stage |
| How-to guides | Medium | Building trust |
| Alternatives posts | High | Competitor traffic |
