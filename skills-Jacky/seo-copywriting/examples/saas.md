## Example 4: How-To Guide

**Target keyword**: "how to track local SEO rankings"

### Title Tag
```
How to Track Local SEO Rankings (Step-by-Step Guide)
```

### Content Structure

```markdown
# How to Track Local SEO Rankings (Step-by-Step Guide)

Want to track your local SEO rankings accurately? Here's 
exactly how to do it.

Most businesses track rankings wrong—using tools that only 
show city-level data. Real local rankings vary block by block.

In this guide, you'll learn:
- Why traditional rank tracking is misleading
- How to set up accurate local tracking
- The metrics that actually matter

Let's dive in.

---

## What You'll Need

- A local SEO rank tracking tool (I recommend LocalRank.so)
- Your target keywords
- Your business location(s)
- 15 minutes for initial setup

---

## Step 1: Choose the Right Tracking Method

Traditional rank checkers show one data point per city. For 
local businesses, this is misleading.

**Use grid-based tracking instead.** It measures rankings from 
multiple points across your service area.

[Screenshot comparing single-point vs grid tracking]

**Why this matters:** A restaurant might rank #1 three blocks 
away but #8 at their location. Grid tracking shows both.

---

## Step 2: Set Up Your Locations

Add each business location you want to track:

1. Enter the business address
2. Define the service area radius
3. Set tracking point density

[Screenshot of location setup]

**Pro tip:** For most service businesses, a 5-mile radius with 
medium grid density works well.

---

## Step 3: Add Your Target Keywords

Focus on keywords that matter for local intent:

- "[Service] near me"
- "[Service] + [City]"
- "[Service] + [Neighborhood]"
- Category keywords (e.g., "plumber")

[Screenshot of keyword setup]

**How many keywords?** Start with 10-20 core keywords. You 
can always add more later.

---

## Step 4: Analyze Your Results

Once tracking is set up, here's what to look for:

### Ranking Distribution
- Where do you rank #1-3? (Your strong zones)
- Where do you rank #10+? (Opportunity zones)

### Trends Over Time
- Are rankings improving or declining?
- Which actions correlate with improvements?

### Competitive Gaps
- Where do competitors outrank you?
- What are they doing differently there?

---

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

### Mistake 1: Using City-Level Data Only

Single-point tracking misses the geographic nuance of local 
search. Always use grid-based tracking for local businesses.

### Mistake 2: Tracking Too Few Keywords

Local intent keywords are specific. "Plumber" and "emergency 
plumber" and "plumber near me" are all different keywords.

### Mistake 3: Ignoring Ranking Fluctuations

Local rankings fluctuate daily. Look at trends over weeks, 
not day-to-day changes.

---

## FAQ

### How often should I check local rankings?

For most businesses, weekly reviews are sufficient. Set up 
automated reports rather than checking daily.

### Do Google Business Profile and organic rankings differ?

Yes. Track both. A business can rank #1 in the Local Pack 
but not appear on page 1 organically (and vice versa).

### How long does it take to improve local rankings?

Typically 3-6 months for meaningful improvements, depending 
on competition and starting point.

1. Grid-based tracking (not single-point)
2. The right keywords for local intent
3. Consistent monitoring over time
```
