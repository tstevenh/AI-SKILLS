# Body Copy: The Main Content That Ranks

> Headlines get clicks. Intros hook readers. But body copy is where you deliver value—and where Google decides if you deserve to rank.

## The Core Principle

**Write for humans, optimize for machines.**

Body copy needs to:
1. Deliver on the headline's promise
2. Cover the topic comprehensively  
3. Keep readers engaged throughout
4. Include keywords naturally
5. Be structured for scanning

---

## Writing Style for SEO Content

### Conversational Tone

Write like you're explaining to a smart friend:

```markdown
❌ Formal: "It is imperative that one comprehends the fundamental 
principles of search engine optimization prior to commencing..."

✅ Conversational: "Before you start optimizing, you need to 
understand how SEO actually works. Here's the deal..."
```

### Active Voice

Active voice is clearer and more engaging:

```markdown
❌ Passive: "The content was written by the team"
✅ Active: "The team wrote the content"

❌ Passive: "Rankings can be improved by building backlinks"
✅ Active: "Build backlinks to improve your rankings"
```

### Second Person ("You")

Address the reader directly:

```markdown
❌ "One should consider keyword research"
✅ "You should start with keyword research"

❌ "Website owners often struggle with SEO"
✅ "If you're struggling with SEO, you're not alone"
```

### Short Sentences and Paragraphs

- **Sentences**: 15-20 words average
- **Paragraphs**: 2-3 sentences max
- **One idea per paragraph**

```markdown
❌ This is a really long sentence that contains multiple ideas 
and concepts all crammed together without any breaks which makes 
it difficult to read and understand, especially when you're 
scanning quickly and trying to find the specific information 
you need.

✅ Keep your sentences short. Each one should convey a single 
idea. This makes your content easier to scan and understand.
```

## The Readability Formula

### Target Grade Level

Aim for **6th-8th grade reading level** for most content.

**Why?**
- Faster to process
- Works for all education levels
- Mobile-friendly
- No one complains content is too easy to read

**How to check**:
- Hemingway Editor (free)
- Grammarly (readability score)
- Yoast SEO (WordPress)

### Hemingway Guidelines

| Metric | Target |
|--------|--------|
| Grade level | 6-8 |
| Sentences with 20+ words | < 25% |
| Adverbs | Minimal |
| Passive voice | < 10% |
| Complex words | Use simpler alternatives |

## Keeping Readers Engaged

### Bucket Brigades

Transitional phrases that pull readers through:

```markdown
Here's the thing:
Now:
But wait—
Here's why that matters:
The bottom line?
In other words:
Think about it:
Want to know the best part?
Here's where it gets interesting:
```

**Use after**:
- Complex explanations
- Before important points
- When sections transition
- Every 200-300 words

### The "Cliffhanger" Technique

End paragraphs with something that makes readers continue:

```markdown
That strategy worked well for months. But then something 
changed.

[Next paragraph explains what changed]
```

### Pattern Interrupts

Break up text with:
- **Bold text** for key points
- *Italics* for emphasis
- Bullet points
- Numbered lists
- Callout boxes
- Images and screenshots

**Rule**: Visual break every 300 words maximum.

### Questions

Sprinkle rhetorical questions to engage:

```markdown
So what does this mean for your content strategy?

Why does this matter? Because...

Ready for the advanced tactics?
```

## Comprehensive Coverage

### The Content Gap Approach

Cover what competitors cover, plus more:

1. **Analyze top 10 results** for your keyword
2. **List all H2s/H3s** they cover
3. **Identify common themes** (must cover these)
4. **Find gaps** (add these to stand out)
5. **Add unique value** (original insights, data, examples)

### The "People Also Ask" Method

Add sections that answer related questions:

```markdown
## How Long Does SEO Take?
[Answer the PAA question]

## Is SEO Still Worth It in 2026?
[Answer another PAA question]

## How Much Does SEO Cost?
[Another common question]
```

### Subtopics That Signal Expertise

Don't just answer the main query—cover adjacent topics:

```markdown
Main topic: "How to build backlinks"

Related subtopics to include:
- Types of backlinks
- What makes a quality backlink
- Backlink tools
- How to check competitor backlinks
- Common link building mistakes
- How many backlinks do you need
```

## Formatting for Scannability

### The F-Pattern

Users scan in an F shape:
- Read the first line
- Scan down the left side
- Stop at interesting points

**Implications**:
- Front-load important info
- Start paragraphs with key words
- Use left-aligned headers
- Put key terms at paragraph starts

### Subheadings Every 200-300 Words

```markdown
## Main Section (H2)

[100-150 words of content]

### Subsection (H3)

[100-150 words of content]

### Another Subsection (H3)

[100-150 words of content]

## Next Main Section (H2)
```

### Bullet Points and Lists

Use bullets for:
- Lists of items
- Feature lists
- Quick tips
- Key takeaways

Use numbers for:
- Steps in a process
- Rankings
- Sequential information

```markdown
**Tips for better body copy:**
- Keep paragraphs short
- Use active voice
- Include bucket brigades
- Add visuals every 300 words
- Front-load important information
```

### Tables for Comparisons


### Callout Boxes

```markdown
> **Pro Tip**: Always check search intent before writing. What 
> ranks tells you what to create.

> **Warning**: Don't keyword stuff. It hurts more than it helps.

> **Example**: Here's how Backlinko structures their content...
```

## Transitions Between Sections

### Connecting Sections

Don't just jump between topics—bridge them:

```markdown
## Keyword Research

[Content about keyword research]

Now that you know how to find keywords, let's talk about how 
to use them in your content.

## Keyword Placement

[Content about keyword placement]
```

### Transition Phrases

| Type | Examples |
|------|----------|
| Addition | Furthermore, Additionally, Also, Plus |
| Contrast | However, But, On the other hand, That said |
| Cause/Effect | Therefore, As a result, Because of this |
| Example | For example, For instance, Such as, Like |
| Sequence | First, Next, Then, Finally |
| Summary | In summary, In short, The bottom line |

## Adding Value Beyond Competitors

### Original Examples

Don't just explain—show:

```markdown
❌ "Use short paragraphs for better readability."

✅ "Use short paragraphs for better readability.

Here's an example:

[Shows before/after of a paragraph]

See how the broken-up version is easier to scan?"
```

### Screenshots and Visuals

Show your work:
- Tool screenshots
- Process diagrams
- Before/after comparisons
- Data visualizations
- Original images

### Data and Research

Back up claims:

```markdown
❌ "Long-form content performs better."

✅ "Long-form content performs better. According to Ahrefs' 
study of 1 million pages, the average top-10 result is 1,447 
words long."
```

### Personal Experience (E-E-A-T)

Share what you've actually done:

```markdown
"When we tested this on our site, traffic increased 34% in 
three months. Here's exactly what we did..."
```

### Downloadable Resources

Add value with extras:
- Templates
- Checklists
- Worksheets
- Swipe files
- Tools

## Clarity Techniques

### Define Terms

When using jargon, explain it:

```markdown
"E-E-A-T (Experience, Expertise, Authoritativeness, Trustworthiness) 
is Google's framework for..."
```

### Use Analogies

Make complex concepts relatable:

```markdown
"Think of backlinks like votes. Each link is a vote of confidence 
from one site to another."

"Your website's architecture is like a house. The homepage is 
the front door, category pages are rooms, and blog posts are 
the furniture."
```

### The "One Thing" Per Section

Each H2 should have ONE main takeaway:

```markdown
## How to Find Keywords

[This entire section is about ONE thing: finding keywords]
[Don't sneak in content about using keywords—that's next section]
```

## Writing for Different Content Types

### How-To Content

```markdown
## Step 1: [Action]

[Explanation of what to do]

[Screenshot or example showing the action]

[Common mistakes to avoid in this step]

## Step 2: [Action]

[Continue pattern...]
```

### Listicles

```markdown
## 1. [Item Name] — Best for [Use Case]

[Brief intro paragraph]

**Pros:**
- Pro 1
- Pro 2

**Cons:**
- Con 1
- Con 2

**Pricing:** $X/month

**Best for:** [Specific use case]

[2-3 paragraph detailed review]
```

### Comparison Posts

```markdown
## [Feature] Comparison

When it comes to [feature], here's how they stack up:

| Feature | Product A | Product B |
|---------|-----------|-----------|
| Detail 1 | ✅ Yes | ❌ No |
| Detail 2 | ✅ Yes | ✅ Yes |

**Winner:** [Product] wins for [reason].
```

## The ASMR Editing Formula

Before publishing, edit using ASMR:

### A = Annotations

Add elements that break up text:
- Sidenotes
- Blockquotes
- Social embeds
- Callout boxes
- Editor's notes

### S = Short Sentences

Break long sentences:
- Use periods instead of commas
- One idea per sentence
- Vary length for rhythm

### M = Multimedia

Include non-text elements:
- Images
- Screenshots
- Videos
- GIFs
- Diagrams
- Charts

### R = Read Aloud

Read your content out loud:
- Does it flow?
- Does it sound natural?
- Where do you stumble?

## Body Copy Checklist

Before publishing each section:

- [ ] Delivers on section headline promise
- [ ] One main idea per section
- [ ] Short paragraphs (2-3 sentences)
- [ ] Visual break every 300 words
- [ ] Bucket brigades where needed
- [ ] Active voice predominant
- [ ] Jargon explained
- [ ] Examples and screenshots included
- [ ] Smooth transition to next section
- [ ] 6th-8th grade reading level

## Common Body Copy Mistakes

### 1. Wall of Text

Break it up with formatting, subheads, and visuals.

### 2. Repetition

Don't say the same thing three different ways. Say it once, well.

### 3. Fluff Words

Cut: "basically", "actually", "really", "just", "very", "that" (often unnecessary)

### 4. Passive Voice Overuse

Convert to active voice where possible.

### 5. Missing Examples

Theory without examples is forgettable. Show, don't just tell.

### 6. Inconsistent Tone

Match tone throughout. Don't be casual in the intro and formal in the body.

### 7. Burying the Lead

Important information should come first in each section.
