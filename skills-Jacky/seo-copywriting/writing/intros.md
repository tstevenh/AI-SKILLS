# Intros That Keep People Reading

> You have 10-15 seconds to convince someone to keep reading. Your intro is make-or-break.

## Why Intros Matter

### The Reality of Online Reading

- **55% bounce rate** is average for blog content
- Most users decide to stay or leave in **10-15 seconds**
- Google measures **dwell time** as a quality signal
- The intro sets expectations for everything that follows

### What a Good Intro Does

1. **Hooks attention** — Stops the scroll
2. **Validates the click** — Confirms they're in the right place
3. **Promises value** — Tells them what they'll get
4. **Creates momentum** — Makes them want to keep reading

## The Inverted Pyramid for Intros

Give the most important information first:

```
┌──────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│          ANSWER THE QUERY (immediately)          │
├──────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│         Context and supporting info              │
├──────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│            Promise of what's coming              │
└──────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

**Example for "What is SEO copywriting?"**:

```markdown
SEO copywriting is the practice of creating keyword-optimized 
content designed to rank in search engines while engaging human 
readers. [← Answer immediately]

It combines traditional SEO techniques with persuasive writing 
to drive both traffic and conversions. [← Supporting context]

In this guide, you'll learn exactly how to write content that 
Google loves and that converts readers into customers. [← Promise]
```

## The APP Method

Brian Dean's framework for SEO intros:

### A = Agree

Start by acknowledging a problem or belief your reader has:

```markdown
Writing content that ranks on Google is hard.
```

This signals: "I understand your situation."

### P = Promise

Promise a solution or outcome:

```markdown
But it doesn't have to be. With the right approach, you can 
create content that ranks AND converts.
```

### P = Preview

Tell them exactly what they'll learn:

```markdown
In this guide, I'll show you the exact SEO copywriting process 
I use to rank content on the first page—including templates you 
can swipe.
```

**Complete APP intro**:

```markdown
# SEO Copywriting: The Complete Guide

Writing content that ranks on Google is hard. [Agree]

But it doesn't have to be. With the right approach, you can 
create content that ranks on the first page AND converts 
readers into customers. [Promise]

In this guide, I'll show you the exact process I use, including:
- How to research topics that drive traffic
- The content structure Google loves
- Templates you can use today
[Preview]

Let's dive in.
```

## The PAS Method

**P**roblem → **A**gitate → **S**olution

### P = Problem

Identify the pain point:

```markdown
You've published 50 blog posts. Traffic? Still flat.
```

### A = Agitate

Twist the knife (make the problem feel urgent):

```markdown
Meanwhile, competitors who started after you are ranking on 
page one. Their content isn't even better than yours. So what 
gives?
```

### S = Solution

Present your content as the answer:

```markdown
The difference isn't the content itself—it's how it's written 
and structured. This guide shows you exactly how to fix that.
```

**Complete PAS intro**:

```markdown
# Why Your Blog Posts Aren't Ranking (And How to Fix It)

You've published 50 blog posts. You've done keyword research. 
You've even built some backlinks. [Problem]

But traffic? Still flat. Meanwhile, sites that started after 
you are dominating page one with seemingly identical content. 
It's frustrating. [Agitate]

Here's the thing: the difference usually isn't the content 
itself—it's how it's structured for SEO. This guide shows you 
exactly how to audit and fix your underperforming content. 
[Solution]
```

## Hook Techniques

### The Statistic Hook

Start with a surprising or compelling data point:

```markdown
43.8% of ChatGPT citations come from "Best X" listicles.

If you're not creating this type of content, you're invisible 
to the fastest-growing search channel.
```

### The Question Hook

Ask a question that makes readers think:

```markdown
What if you could rank #1 on Google AND in ChatGPT with a 
single piece of content?

Most marketers treat these as separate channels. That's a 
mistake.
```

### The Story Hook

Open with a brief, relevant narrative:

```markdown
Last year, we published a blog post that got 14 visits in 
its first month. Six months later, it drives 15,000 monthly 
visitors.

What changed? Not the topic. Not the links. Just the way we 
structured the content.
```

### The Contrarian Hook

Challenge a common belief:

```markdown
Everything you know about keyword density is wrong.

Not slightly off—completely, totally, counterproductively wrong.
```

### The "Imagine" Hook

Paint a picture of success:

```markdown
Imagine checking your analytics and seeing organic traffic 
up 300%—without publishing more content or building more links.

That's the power of properly optimized content structure.
```

### The Direct Answer Hook

For informational queries, answer immediately:

```markdown
SEO takes 3-6 months to show significant results for most websites.

But that's the short answer. The real timeline depends on 
your domain authority, competition, and content strategy. 
Let me break it down.
```

## Bucket Brigades

**Bucket brigades** are transitional phrases that keep readers moving through your content. Use them in intros (and throughout):

**Classic bucket brigades**:
- Here's the deal:
- Now:
- But here's the thing:
- Here's why:
- The bottom line?
- Think about it:
- Get this:
- And the best part?
- But wait—there's more:
- Sound familiar?
- Want to know the secret?
- Here's what I discovered:

**Example in an intro**:

```markdown
SEO copywriting isn't just about keywords anymore.

Here's the thing:

Google's algorithms have evolved. They understand context, 
intent, and user satisfaction. Keyword stuffing? Dead.

But here's what still works:

Content structure. User experience signals. And strategic 
keyword placement that feels natural.

In this guide, I'll show you exactly how to nail all three.
```

## The First 100 Words

Google pays special attention to your opening content. Include:

### 1. Your Primary Keyword

Work it in naturally within the first 100 words:

```markdown
SEO copywriting is the foundation of organic growth. [✓ keyword in first sentence]
```

### 2. A Clear Topic Signal

Make it obvious what the page is about:

```markdown
This guide covers everything you need to know about writing 
content that ranks—from keyword research to on-page optimization.
```

### 3. A Reason to Keep Reading

Promise specific value:

```markdown
By the end, you'll have a repeatable process for creating 
content that drives traffic and converts.
```

## Intro Formulas by Content Type

### For How-To Guides

```markdown
# How to [Do Thing]

[One-sentence description of what they'll learn.]

I've [relevant credential/experience] and in this guide, 
I'll show you [specific outcome].

You'll learn:
- [Benefit 1]
- [Benefit 2]
- [Benefit 3]

Let's get started.
```

### For Listicles

```markdown
# [Number] Best [Things] in [Year]

Looking for the best [thing]?

I've tested [number] [things] over [time period] to find the 
ones that actually [deliver result].

Here are my top picks, starting with the best overall.
```

### For Comparison Posts

```markdown
# [Thing A] vs [Thing B]: Which is Better?

[Thing A] and [Thing B] are two of the most popular [category]. 
But which one is right for you?

Short answer: [Thing A] is better for [use case], while 
[Thing B] excels at [different use case].

Here's the full breakdown.
```

### For Definition/Explainer Posts

```markdown
# What is [Term]?

[Term] is [concise definition in one sentence].

Understanding [term] is essential for [who] because [reason].

In this guide, you'll learn:
- What [term] actually means
- Why it matters for [outcome]
- How to [action] with [term]
```

### For Product Reviews

```markdown
# [Product] Review: Is It Worth It?

TL;DR: [Product] is [verdict]—best for [use case], but not 
ideal for [other case].

I've used [Product] for [time period] and [specific experience]. 
Here's my complete review, including what I love and what 
drives me crazy.
```

## What to Avoid in Intros

### 1. The "Dictionary Definition" Opening

```markdown
❌ According to Wikipedia, SEO stands for Search Engine 
Optimization, which is the process of...
```

This is lazy and adds no value. Everyone knows they can look up definitions.

### 2. The Endless Preamble

```markdown
❌ In today's digital landscape, content marketing has become 
increasingly important. With millions of websites competing for 
attention, it's more crucial than ever to stand out. That's why 
understanding how to create quality content matters. Before we 
dive into the specifics, let's take a moment to understand the 
broader context of why this matters...
```

Get to the point. Readers are impatient.

### 3. The Personal Tangent

```markdown
❌ I was sitting in my home office last Tuesday, drinking my 
third cup of coffee, when I realized something important about 
SEO...
```

Save personal stories for after you've established value. The reader doesn't care about your coffee yet.

### 4. The Obvious Statement

```markdown
❌ SEO is important for businesses.
```

This tells the reader nothing they don't already know.

### 5. The Bait-and-Switch

```markdown
❌ Hook: "I made $1 million from SEO last year"
Reality: Content doesn't deliver on this promise
```

If your intro promises something, your content must deliver.

## Intro Length Guidelines

### Short Intros (50-100 words)

Best for:
- How-to content
- Simple topics
- Readers who want to get started immediately

### Medium Intros (100-200 words)

Best for:
- Most blog content
- Guides and tutorials
- Commercial content

### Long Intros (200-300 words)

Best for:
- Complex topics needing context
- Stories that hook readers
- Building strong emotional connection

**Rule of thumb**: Long enough to hook and promise value, short enough that readers reach the first H2 without scrolling on mobile.

## Testing Your Intro

### The "So What?" Test

After each sentence, ask "So what?"

```markdown
SEO is important for businesses. [So what?]
Good SEO drives organic traffic. [So what?]
Organic traffic = free customers. [Better—there's value]
```

### The Friend Test

Read your intro aloud. Would you actually say this to a friend?

```markdown
❌ "In today's digital landscape, content marketing has become..."
✅ "Here's the thing about SEO—most people do it wrong."
```

### The Scroll Test

On mobile, does the reader see:
- Your headline
- At least the first paragraph of your intro
- Something that makes them want to scroll

If your intro doesn't fit above the fold, consider shortening it.

---

## Intro Checklist

Before publishing, verify:

- [ ] Primary keyword in first 100 words
- [ ] Clear topic signal (reader knows what page is about)
- [ ] Value promise (what will they learn/get?)
- [ ] No wasted words or obvious statements
- [ ] Hook that stops the scroll
- [ ] Preview of what's coming
- [ ] Matches the headline's promise
- [ ] Works on mobile (visible above fold)
