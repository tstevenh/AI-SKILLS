## When You're Ready for Sponsors

### Minimum Requirements

**Technical requirements**:
- 1,000+ subscribers (practical minimum)
- Consistent posting schedule
- Professional video quality
- Clear niche/audience

**Realistic sweet spot**:
- 10,000+ subscribers for small brands
- 50,000+ subscribers for established brands
- 100,000+ subscribers for premium rates

### Signs You're Ready

**Audience indicators**:
- [ ] Engaged comment section
- [ ] Viewers ask about products you use
- [ ] Consistent view counts
- [ ] Growing subscriber base

**Content indicators**:
- [ ] Professional quality
- [ ] Consistent style
- [ ] Clear niche
- [ ] Regular schedule

**Personal indicators**:
- [ ] You can handle additional work
- [ ] You understand your audience well
- [ ] You can maintain authenticity
- [ ] You have products you'd genuinely recommend

## Media Kit Creation

### What Is a Media Kit?

A media kit is your channel's resume for brands. It summarizes your audience, performance, and sponsorship offerings in a professional format.

### Media Kit Elements

**Page 1: Introduction**
- Channel name and logo
- Your photo
- Brief bio (2-3 sentences)
- Niche/content type
- Channel URL

**Page 2: Audience Overview**
- Subscriber count
- Monthly views
- Geographic breakdown
- Age/gender demographics
- Audience interests

**Page 3: Performance Metrics**
- Average views per video
- Average watch time
- Engagement rate
- Recent growth trends
- Best performing content

**Page 4: Sponsorship Offerings**
- Integration types offered
- Pricing tiers (or "rates upon request")
- Previous sponsors (if applicable)
- Testimonials (if available)

**Page 5: Contact Information**
- Email for inquiries
- Response time expectation
- Social media handles

### Media Kit Best Practices

**Design**:
- Clean, professional layout
- Consistent with channel branding
- Easy to scan quickly
- PDF format (2-5 pages)

**Content**:
- Update monthly with new stats
- Highlight strengths
- Be honest about numbers
- Include recent screenshots

**Presentation**:
- Name file professionally: "[Channel]_MediaKit_[Month][Year].pdf"
- Host on Google Drive or Dropbox for easy sharing
- Keep file size reasonable (<5MB)

## Pricing Your Channel

### Pricing Fundamentals

**Base formula**:
```
Rate = (Average Views) × (CPM Rate)

Where CPM = Cost Per Mille (per 1000 views)
```

**Typical CPM ranges for sponsorships**:
| Niche | Sponsorship CPM |
|-------|-----------------|
| Finance/Business | $40-80 |
| Tech | $30-50 |
| Health/Fitness | $20-40 |
| Lifestyle | $15-30 |
| Gaming | $10-25 |
| Entertainment | $10-20 |

### Pricing Examples

**10,000 average views + Tech niche**:
- 10,000 × $35 CPM = $350 base rate
- Range: $250-$500 per integration

**50,000 average views + Finance niche**:
- 50,000 × $50 CPM = $2,500 base rate
- Range: $2,000-$4,000 per integration

**100,000 average views + Lifestyle niche**:
- 100,000 × $25 CPM = $2,500 base rate
- Range: $2,000-$4,000 per integration

### Pricing Adjustments

**Charge more when**:
- Dedicated video (not just integration)
- Multiple deliverables included
- Exclusivity required
- Rush timeline
- Highly engaged audience
- Premium production quality

**Consider charging less when**:
- Long-term partnership
- Product you love
- Great fit for audience
- Portfolio building opportunity
- Recurring deal

### Pricing Tiers

**Tier 1: Basic Integration**
- 30-60 second mention
- One video
- Price: Base rate

**Tier 2: Standard Integration**
- 60-90 second integration
- Social media post
- Price: Base rate × 1.5

**Tier 3: Premium Package**
- Dedicated video
- Multiple social posts
- Price: Base rate × 3-4

### Pricing Psychology

**Never price first**: Ask about their budget first
**Anchor high**: Start higher than you'd accept
**Package deals**: Offer bundles at slight discount
**Value framing**: Emphasize ROI for the brand

### Method 1: Identify Natural Fits

**Look at what you already use and recommend**:
- Tools you mention
- Products you show
- Services you use
- Brands you admire

### Method 2: Competitor Research

**Who sponsors similar channels?**:
- Watch sponsored segments
- Note brand names
- Research their influencer marketing

### Method 3: Industry Directories

**Platforms connecting creators and brands**:
- Grapevine Logic
- FameBit/BrandConnect
- Channel Pages
- Intellifluence
- AspireIQ
- Creator.co

**Find brands actively sponsoring**:
- LinkedIn search for "Influencer Marketing Manager"

## Outreach Strategy

### Outreach Principles

**Be professional**: You're a business partner, not a fan
**Be specific**: Show you know their brand
**Be valuable**: Focus on what you offer them
**Be brief**: Respect their time
**Be persistent**: Follow up appropriately

### The Outreach Email Template

**Subject lines that work**:
- "Partnership opportunity: [Channel Name] × [Brand]"
- "[Channel Name] sponsorship inquiry"
- "Reaching [audience description] for [Brand]"

**Email structure**:

```
Subject: YouTube Partnership: [Your Channel] × [Brand Name]

Hi [Name],

I'm [Your Name], creator of [Channel Name], a YouTube channel
that reaches [audience description] interested in [topic].

I've been [specific thing about using/admiring their brand],
and I think my audience of [X] subscribers would love to learn
more about [specific product/service].

Here's why I think we'd be a great fit:
• My audience: [specific relevant demographic]
• Recent video example: [link to relevant video]
• My engagement: [specific metric, e.g., "5% engagement rate"]

I'd love to discuss how we could work together. I've attached
my media kit with more details about my channel.

Are you open to a brief call this week to explore possibilities?

Best,
[Your Name]
[Channel URL]
[Social handles]

Attached: [Channel]_MediaKit.pdf
```

### Finding the Right Contact

**Where to look**:
- LinkedIn (search "[Brand] influencer marketing")
- Brand website (About page, Careers, Partnership section)
- Social media (Instagram sometimes lists PR contacts)
- Influencer platforms (if brand is listed)

**Who to contact**:
- Influencer Marketing Manager
- Partnership Manager
- Digital Marketing Manager
- PR/Communications Manager
- Marketing Director (smaller brands)

### Follow-Up Strategy

**Timeline**:
- Day 1: Send initial email
- Day 5: First follow-up if no response
- Day 12: Second follow-up
- Day 20+: Final follow-up or move on

**Follow-up email**:
```
Subject: Re: YouTube Partnership: [Your Channel] × [Brand]

Hi [Name],

Following up on my email from last week about a potential
partnership between [Brand] and my channel.

I recently posted a video about [related topic] that got
[X views/engagement], showing my audience's interest in
this space.

Would you have 15 minutes this week to chat?

Best,
[Name]
```

## Negotiation Tactics

### Before Negotiating

**Know your numbers**:
- Your minimum acceptable rate
- Your ideal rate
- Your walk-away point
- What you're willing to negotiate on

**Know their position**:
- Standard rates in your niche
- What they've paid others (if knowable)
- Their budget constraints
- What they value most

### Negotiation Principles

**Never go first**: Let them share their budget
**Silence is powerful**: Don't rush to fill silence
**Trade, don't give**: "I can do X if you can do Y"
**Keep options open**: "Let me think about it"

### Common Negotiation Scenarios

**They offer below your rate**:
"Thanks for that offer. Based on my audience size and engagement
rates, my typical rate for this type of integration is [X].
I could potentially do [slightly lower] if we can [adjust scope/
add long-term commitment]."

**They want more for same price**:
"I'd be happy to include [extra thing]. To make that work
within the project, I'd need to adjust the rate to [X], or
we could reduce [other thing] to keep it at current price."

**They have strict budget**:
"I understand budget constraints. At that rate, I could offer
[reduced scope]. Alternatively, if we do multiple videos, I
could offer a package rate that might work better."

### What's Negotiable

| Item | How to Negotiate |
|------|------------------|
| Price | Start higher, have walking away point |
| Timeline | Rush costs more, flexibility costs less |
| Usage rights | Limited time/platform costs less |
| Revisions | Set number in contract |
| Exclusivity | Non-compete costs premium |
| Payment terms | 50% upfront is standard |

### Red Flags to Watch

- No written agreement
- Payment only "if video performs"
- Excessive revision demands
- Full ownership of content
- Unreasonably tight deadlines
- Sketchy payment methods
- No clear point of contact

## Deliverables and Contracts

### Standard Deliverables

**For video integration**:
- X-second mention (typically 60-90s)
- Script/talking points (reviewed by brand)
- One video publication
- Link in description
- Pinned comment (optional)

**For dedicated video**:
- Full video about product/service
- Title/thumbnail incorporating brand
- Description with links
- Call-to-action for brand

**For social package**:
- Video deliverable(s)
- X Instagram posts/stories
- X Twitter posts
- Cross-platform promotion

### Contract Must-Haves

**Protect yourself with**:
- Clear scope of work
- Payment terms and schedule
- Revision limits
- Timeline with milestones
- Content approval process
- Usage rights limitations
- Exclusivity terms (if any)
- Termination clauses
- FTC disclosure requirements

### Contract Template Elements

```
SPONSORSHIP AGREEMENT

PARTIES
• Creator: [Your legal name/business]
• Brand: [Brand legal name]

DELIVERABLES
1. One (1) YouTube video integration of approximately
   60-90 seconds featuring [Product/Service]
2. Link to [Brand URL] in video description
3. Pinned comment mentioning [Brand]

TIMELINE
• Draft script due: [Date]
• Brand approval: Within 5 business days
• Video filming: [Date]
• Draft video for review: [Date]
• Publication date: [Date]

COMPENSATION
• Total fee: $[Amount]
• Payment schedule: 50% upon signing, 50% upon publication
• Payment method: [PayPal/Wire/etc.]
• Payment due: Net 30 from invoice

USAGE RIGHTS
• Brand may repost video clips on their social channels
• Brand may use screenshots for marketing purposes
• Creator retains ownership of original content
• Limited to [X months/years] unless renewed

REQUIREMENTS
• FTC disclosure included in video and description
• Content must align with brand guidelines (attached)
• Maximum [X] revisions included

EXCLUSIVITY
• Creator agrees not to promote competing products in
  the category of [specific category] for [X] days
  before and after publication

TERMINATION
• Either party may terminate with [X] days written notice
• If terminated after work begins, Creator will be
  compensated for work completed

SIGNATURES
___________________ Date: ________
[Creator Name]

___________________ Date: ________
[Brand Representative]
```

### Payment Best Practices

**Standard terms**:
- 50% upfront, 50% on publication
- Net 15 or Net 30 for final payment
- Invoice with clear details

**Protect yourself**:
- Never start work without deposit
- Use contracts always
- Document everything in writing
- Have bank details secure

## Maintaining Sponsor Relationships

### Delivering Excellence

**Exceed expectations**:
- Deliver on time or early
- Match or exceed promised metrics
- Provide usage statistics post-campaign
- Be easy to work with

### Post-Campaign Communication

**After video publishes**:
- Send performance report (48 hours, 1 week)
- Thank them for partnership
- Ask for feedback
- Plant seed for future collaboration

**Performance report includes**:
- Views
- Watch time
- Engagement metrics
- Notable comments
- Click-through (if trackable)

### Building Long-Term Partnerships

**Repeat sponsors are valuable**:
- Less negotiation time
- Easier workflow
- Often better rates
- Consistent income

**How to encourage repeat business**:
- Exceptional delivery
- Proactive communication
- Suggest future opportunities
- Stay in touch between campaigns

### Managing Multiple Sponsors

**Organization system**:
- Spreadsheet tracking all partnerships
- Calendar for deadlines
- Folder system for assets
- Invoice tracking

**Avoid conflicts**:
- Check exclusivity clauses
- Don't oversaturate with sponsors
- Space out competitor products
- Maintain audience trust

## Sponsor Outreach Quick Reference

### The Process

1. **Prepare**: Media kit, pricing, prospect list
2. **Research**: Find right contacts, personalize approach
3. **Reach out**: Professional email, follow up
4. **Negotiate**: Know your value, trade don't give
5. **Contract**: Clear terms, protect yourself
6. **Deliver**: Exceed expectations
7. **Follow up**: Share results, build relationship
8. **Repeat**: Long-term partnerships are gold

### Key Numbers to Know

| Your Channel | Typical Integration Rate |
|--------------|------------------------|
| 10K views/video | $250-500 |
| 25K views/video | $500-1,000 |
| 50K views/video | $1,000-2,500 |
| 100K views/video | $2,000-5,000 |
| 250K+ views/video | $5,000-15,000+ |

*Rates vary significantly by niche, audience quality, and negotiation.*
