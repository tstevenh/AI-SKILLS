## What Makes Shorts Perform

### The Shorts Success Formula

```
Shorts Success = (Hook Power × Watch-Through) + (Loop Rate × Engagement)
```

**Hook Power**: Do they stop scrolling in the first second?
**Watch-Through**: Do they watch to the end?
**Loop Rate**: Do they watch again?
**Engagement**: Do they like, comment, share?

### Key Performance Factors

| Factor | Importance | What It Means |
|--------|------------|---------------|
| First frame | Critical | Visual that stops the scroll |
| First second | Critical | Audio/visual hook |
| Watch completion | High | Reaching the end |
| Loop potential | High | Rewatchability |
| Engagement velocity | Medium | How fast likes accumulate |
| Comments | Medium | Conversation generation |

### Shorts vs Long-Form Mindset

| Long-Form | Shorts |
|-----------|--------|
| 30-second hook | 1-second hook |
| Depth | Punch |
| Build-up | Immediate |
| Retention curve | Binary (watch or swipe) |
| CTR matters | First frame matters |
| Title important | Almost irrelevant |

## Hook Techniques for Shorts

### The First Frame Hook

Your thumbnail equivalent is the first frame. Optimize it:
- **Movement**: Something happening immediately
- **Contrast**: Bright, eye-catching colors
- **Face**: Expressive emotion
- **Text**: Bold statement visible

### The First Second Hook

**Visual hooks**:
- Unexpected image
- Satisfying action beginning
- Before/transformation starting
- Problem visualization

**Audio hooks**:
- Trending sound
- Startling sound effect
- Confident statement
- Question

**Text hooks** (on screen):
- "You're doing this wrong..."
- "This changed everything"
- "Wait for it..."
- "POV: [scenario]"

### Hook Examples That Work

**The direct statement**:
[Looking at camera] "Stop scrolling. You need to hear this."

**The visual intrigue**:
[Already mid-action with something unusual]

**The question**:
"Why does [thing] happen? Let me explain."

**The promise**:
"In 30 seconds, you'll know how to [valuable skill]."

**The pattern interrupt**:
[Unexpected visual or sound that breaks the feed pattern]

## Optimal Length and Pacing

### Length Recommendations

| Content Type | Optimal Length |
|--------------|----------------|
| Quick tip | 15-30 seconds |
| Story/journey | 30-45 seconds |
| Tutorial | 45-60 seconds |
| Entertainment | 15-30 seconds |
| Complex explanation | 60+ seconds |

### Pacing Guidelines

**Shorts pace is FAST**:
- No slow introductions
- No "hey guys" openings
- Cut every pause
- Jump cuts acceptable
- Value density maximal

**Pacing rules**:
- New information every 3-5 seconds
- No segment longer than 10 seconds without change
- Cut dead space ruthlessly
- Better too fast than too slow

### The "No Filler" Standard

Every second must earn its place:
- ❌ "So I was thinking about..."
- ✅ "Here's the truth about..."
- ❌ "Um, basically what I mean is..."
- ✅ "Three things: [thing]. [thing]. [thing]."

## Text Overlays and Captions

### Why Text Matters for Shorts

**Most Shorts are watched with sound off** (scrolling in public, before turning sound on).

Text ensures:
- Message gets across without audio
- Hooks viewers before they turn sound on
- Accessibility for hearing impaired
- Additional engagement layer

### Text Overlay Best Practices

**Placement**:
- Center-ish (avoid edges)
- Avoid bottom 15% (interface overlap)
- Consistent position throughout

**Style**:
- Bold, readable fonts
- High contrast (white with black outline)
- Large enough for mobile
- Animation to match energy

**Content**:
- Key words/phrases only
- Not full sentences (captions handle that)
- Emphasize important moments
- Hook text in first frame

### Captions

**Auto-captions**: YouTube generates, but review for accuracy
**Custom captions**: More control, better styling
**Tools**: CapCut, Premiere, Descript

**Caption style**:
- 1-3 words at a time
- Sync with speech rhythm
- Animated/emphasized for punch
- Consistent styling

## Sound and Music

### Sound Strategy

**Trending sounds**:
- Ride existing trends
- Algorithm often boosts
- Viewers recognize and engage
- Limited time relevance

**Original audio**:
- Your voice/content
- Can become trend if popular
- More unique/branded
- No trend boost

**Licensed music**:
- Professional feel
- No copyright issues
- Less trend potential
- Good for consistency

### Music Selection

**For energy**:
- Upbeat tracks
- Match cuts to beat
- Build to climax

**For information**:
- Subtle background
- Not distracting
- Consistent energy

**For emotion**:
- Match mood
- Support not dominate
- Consider no music for impact

### Audio Editing Tips

- Music volume: Background, not overpowering
- Voice clarity: Always prioritize
- Beat sync: Cut on beats for professional feel
- Trending sounds: Use full sound when possible

## Trends vs Evergreen

### Trend Strategy

**Finding trends**:
- Browse Shorts feed daily
- Note sounds/formats appearing multiple times
- Check TikTok for cross-platform trends
- Follow trend accounts

**Executing trends**:
- Add your niche twist
- Act fast (trends die quickly)
- Don't force irrelevant trends
- Your version should add value

**Trend timeline**:
- Days 1-3: Early adopters benefit
- Days 4-7: Peak engagement
- Days 8-14: Declining returns
- Days 15+: Trend is dead

### Evergreen Strategy

**Evergreen Shorts topics**:
- Tips that always apply
- Facts that don't change
- Universal struggles/experiences
- Timeless humor

**Evergreen benefits**:
- Compound views over time
- Always relevant for new viewers
- Can resurface in algorithm
- Less production pressure

### Recommended Mix

| Creator Type | Trend % | Evergreen % |
|--------------|---------|-------------|
| Entertainment | 60-70% | 30-40% |
| Educational | 20-30% | 70-80% |
| Business | 30-40% | 60-70% |
| Lifestyle | 50-50% | 50-50% |

## Shorts Production Workflow

### Batch Creation

**Shorts batch differently than long-form**:
- Film 10-20 concepts in one session
- Many don't need extensive planning
- Higher volume, faster iteration
- Accept that some won't perform

### Filming Shorts

**Setup**:
- Vertical camera setup (9:16)
- Good lighting
- Clean background
- Tripod or stable mount

**Filming tips**:
- Multiple takes are fast
- Energy higher than long-form
- Look at camera (phone selfie style)
- Keep moving/dynamic

### Editing Shorts

**Editing workflow**:
1. Select best take
2. Cut all dead space
3. Add captions/text
4. Add music (if using)
5. Check loop point
6. Export (1080x1920 minimum)

**Export settings**:
- Resolution: 1080x1920
- Frame rate: 30fps
- File: MP4

### Publishing Shorts

**Title**: 
- Less important than long-form
- Include relevant keywords
- Can be simple/descriptive

**Description**:
- Brief or empty is fine
- Include relevant hashtags
- Link to related long-form

**Timing**:
- Less sensitive than long-form
- Test different times
- Consistency helps

### Analyzing Shorts Performance

**Key metrics to track**:
- Views (obvious)
- Average percentage viewed
- Viewer retention graph
- Traffic sources (Shorts feed %)
- Subscriber gain

**What constitutes success**:
| Metric | Good | Great | Viral |
|--------|------|-------|-------|
| Views (48hr) | 1K+ | 10K+ | 100K+ |
| Watch % | 60%+ | 80%+ | 90%+ |
| Like rate | 3%+ | 5%+ | 8%+ |

### Learning from Performance

**When a Short performs**:
1. Note what made it work
2. Create variations
3. Make more in that style
4. Don't just remake the same thing

**When a Short flops**:
1. Check the swipe-off point
2. Was hook strong enough?
3. Was content compelling?
4. Try different approach next time

## Shorts Best Practices Summary

### Always Do

- Hook in first frame/second
- Cut all filler
- Add captions
- End with loop potential
- Test and iterate quickly

### Never Do

- Slow introductions
- "Hey guys" openings
- Landscape format
- Assume one will go viral
- Repost exact same content

### The Shorts Mindset

Shorts are a numbers game:
- Most won't perform
- Some will do okay
- Few will pop
- Very few will go viral

**Strategy**: Create volume, learn fast, double down on what works.
