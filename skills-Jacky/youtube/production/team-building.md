## When to Hire

### Signs You're Ready

**Time indicators**:
- You're spending 30+ hours/week on YouTube
- Editing takes more time than content creation
- You're missing upload deadlines
- Quality is suffering due to time constraints

**Revenue indicators**:
- Consistent $2,000+/month revenue
- Revenue exceeds potential hiring costs
- Clear path to revenue growth with more content

**Mental indicators**:
- Burnout is setting in
- You dread certain tasks
- Creative energy is depleted on logistics
- You can't scale without help

### The ROI Calculation

**Should you hire?**

Calculate the value of your time:
```
Your hourly value = Monthly Revenue / Hours Spent

Example:
$4,000 revenue / 160 hours = $25/hour

If editing takes 20 hours/video:
Cost of your editing = 20 × $25 = $500/video

If editor costs $200/video:
Net savings = $300/video (plus 20 hours freed up)
```

**The real question**: What would you do with those freed hours?
- Create more content = more revenue
- Improve strategy = better performance
- Rest = sustainability

### When NOT to Hire

**Don't hire if**:
- Revenue doesn't support it
- You can't effectively delegate
- Your systems aren't documented
- You're not committed long-term
- You're hiring to avoid learning basics

## The Hiring Roadmap

### Typical Hiring Order

**Hire #1: Video Editor**
- Biggest time saver
- High impact on output
- Most common first hire
- Typical cost: $15-50/hour or $100-300/video

**Hire #2: Thumbnail Designer**
- Directly impacts CTR
- Relatively affordable
- Quick turnaround work
- Typical cost: $20-75/thumbnail

**Hire #3: Virtual Assistant**
- Handles repetitive tasks
- Uploads, scheduling, comments
- Often part-time
- Typical cost: $10-25/hour

**Hire #4: Scriptwriter/Researcher**
- Accelerates content creation
- Maintains quality at volume
- Requires close collaboration
- Typical cost: $50-200/script

**Later hires**:
- Social media manager
- Community manager
- Production assistant
- Additional editors
- Operations manager

### Hiring Timeline by Channel Size

| Channel Size | Typical Team |
|--------------|--------------|
| 0-10K subs | Solo |
| 10K-50K subs | Editor (part-time) |
| 50K-100K subs | Editor + Thumbnail designer |
| 100K-250K subs | Full editor + Designer + VA |
| 250K-500K subs | Multiple editors + Full support team |
| 500K+ subs | Full production team |

## Editor Hiring and Training

### Finding Editors

**Where to look**:
- YouTube-specific job boards (YTJobs, Creators Hub)
- Twitter/X #YouTubeEditor hashtag
- Reddit (r/editors, r/youtubers)
- Fiverr/Upwork (for testing)
- Video editing schools
- Referrals from other creators

**What to look for**:
- Experience with your content type
- Understanding of YouTube pacing
- Technical proficiency
- Communication skills
- Availability and reliability

### Editor Job Posting Template

```
YOUTUBE VIDEO EDITOR WANTED

About the channel:
[Brief description of your channel, niche, and style]

What you'll do:
- Edit [X] videos per [week/month]
- [Specific tasks: color correction, captions, etc.]
- Work with raw footage to create engaging content
- Meet consistent deadlines

Requirements:
- Experience editing YouTube content
- Proficiency in [software]
- Understanding of YouTube retention and pacing
- Strong communication skills
- Ability to meet deadlines

Nice to have:
- Experience in [your niche]
- Motion graphics capabilities
- Sound design skills

Compensation: [Rate or "Competitive"]

To apply:
- Send portfolio with YouTube-specific work
- Include [specific requirement]
- Answer: [screening question]
```

### The Editor Hiring Process

**Step 1: Collect applications**
- Review portfolios
- Look for YouTube-specific work
- Check pacing and style fit

**Step 2: Initial screening**
- Quick video call
- Assess communication
- Discuss availability and rates

**Step 3: Paid test project**
- Give one video to edit
- Pay fair rate for test
- Evaluate quality and process

**Step 4: Trial period**
- 4-8 week trial
- Regular feedback
- Clear expectations

**Step 5: Ongoing relationship**
- Formal agreement
- Consistent work
- Growth together

### Training Your Editor

**Create an editing guide including**:
- Style reference videos
- Pacing expectations
- Graphics and text styles
- Music preferences
- Common mistakes to avoid
- Revision process

**Onboarding process**:
1. Watch 10+ videos together, explaining choices
2. Edit first video with heavy feedback
3. Gradually reduce oversight
4. Establish feedback rhythm

**Feedback framework**:
```
VIDEO: [Title]
DATE: [Date]

WHAT WORKED WELL:
- [Specific positive]
- [Specific positive]

WHAT TO IMPROVE:
- [Specific feedback with timestamp]
- [Specific feedback with timestamp]

OVERALL: [Rating or general note]
```

### Editor Compensation Models

**Per-video rate**:
- Simple calculation
- Clear expectations
- Variable income for editor

| Video Length | Typical Rate |
|--------------|--------------|
| 5-10 min | $75-150 |
| 10-20 min | $150-300 |
| 20-30 min | $250-400 |
| 30+ min | $350-500+ |

**Hourly rate**:
- Flexible for varying workloads
- Track time carefully
- Typical: $15-50/hour

**Monthly retainer**:
- Consistent income for editor
- Predictable cost for you
- Ensures availability
- Typical: $1,000-4,000/month for part-time

## Thumbnail Designer

### Finding Thumbnail Designers

**Where to look**:
- Fiverr (good for starting)
- Dribbble
- Behance
- Twitter design community
- Referrals from other creators

**Portfolio evaluation**:
- YouTube-specific work?
- Understanding of CTR principles?
- Style match for your brand?
- Turnaround time?

### Thumbnail Designer Requirements

**Must have**:
- Understanding of YouTube thumbnail best practices
- Ability to work from brief
- Quick turnaround capability
- Consistent quality
- Communication responsiveness

**Nice to have**:
- Experience in your niche
- Photo editing/manipulation skills
- Brand design capability
- A/B testing experience

### Working with Thumbnail Designers

**Clear brief template**:
```
THUMBNAIL BRIEF

Video title: [Title]
Video topic: [Brief description]

Thumbnail concept:
- Main visual element: [What should be shown]
- Text (if any): [Exact text]
- Emotion/feeling: [What viewer should feel]
- Reference thumbnails: [Links]

Photo assets: [Links to photos]
Brand colors: [Hex codes]
Deadline: [Date/time]

Notes: [Any special requirements]
```

**Feedback process**:
- First round: Full concept review
- Second round: Refinements
- Final: Polish and export

## Scriptwriter

### When to Hire a Scriptwriter

**Hire when**:
- You have clear content direction
- You can effectively brief
- Script quality is bottleneck
- You need to scale content
- Your ideas exceed your writing time

### Finding Scriptwriters

**Where to look**:
- Writing communities
- Copywriting job boards
- Other YouTube channels
- Content agencies
- Journalism/media backgrounds

**What to look for**:
- Writing samples (ideally video scripts)
- Understanding of YouTube hooks
- Research capability
- Interview/collaboration skills
- Niche knowledge (or ability to learn)

### The Scriptwriting Process

**Collaboration model**:
1. You provide: Topic, angle, key points, references
2. They create: Research, outline, first draft
3. You review: Feedback, your voice additions
4. They revise: Incorporate feedback
5. You finalize: Add personal touches, approve

**Brief template**:
```
SCRIPT BRIEF

Video title: [Working title]
Target length: [X minutes]
Video type: [Tutorial/Listicle/Story/etc.]

Core message:
[One sentence describing the main point]

Key points to cover:
1. [Point 1]
2. [Point 2]
3. [Point 3]

Reference videos:
- [Link 1]
- [Link 2]

Audience context:
[What they know, what they need, where they are]

Tone: [Conversational/Professional/Energetic/etc.]

Must include:
- [Required element 1]
- [Required element 2]

Must avoid:
- [Thing to avoid]

Deadline: [Date]
```

### Scriptwriter Pricing

| Level | Price Per Script |
|-------|------------------|
| Entry-level | $50-100 |
| Experienced | $100-200 |
| Expert/Specialized | $200-500 |
| Agency | $300-1,000 |

**Per-word rates**: $0.10-0.50/word depending on expertise

## Managing Your Team

### Communication Systems

**Tools to use**:
- Slack/Discord: Daily communication
- Asana/Monday: Project management
- Google Drive: File sharing
- Loom: Video feedback
- Frame.io: Video review

### Meeting Rhythm

| Meeting | Frequency | Duration | Purpose |
|---------|-----------|----------|---------|
| Check-in | Daily | 5-15 min | Status updates |
| Review | Weekly | 30-60 min | Work review, planning |
| Strategy | Monthly | 60-90 min | Big picture |

### Feedback Culture

**Give feedback that's**:
- Specific (not vague)
- Timely (close to the work)
- Actionable (clear next steps)
- Balanced (positive and constructive)

**Request feedback on**:
- Your briefs (clear enough?)
- Your communication (helpful?)
- The process (efficient?)

### Delegation Framework

**What to delegate**:
- Tasks that don't require your unique input
- Repetitive tasks
- Tasks others can do well (or better)
- Time-consuming but straightforward work

**What to keep**:
- Creative direction
- Final approval
- Audience relationship
- Strategy decisions
- Your on-camera presence

## Budget and Compensation

### Building a Team Budget

**Budget framework**:
```
Total Team Budget = Revenue × Team Investment Percentage

Conservative: 20-30% of revenue
Growth-focused: 30-50% of revenue
Scaling: 50%+ of revenue (reinvesting)
```

**Example budget allocation**:
```
Monthly revenue: $5,000
Team budget (35%): $1,750

Allocation:
- Editor: $800 (4 videos × $200)
- Thumbnail designer: $200 (4 × $50)
- VA: $300 (20 hours × $15)
- Buffer: $450

Total: $1,750
```

### Compensation Best Practices

**Pay fairly**:
- Research market rates
- Pay on time (always)
- Clear payment terms
- Bonuses for exceptional work

**Build loyalty**:
- Consistent work
- Growth opportunities
- Credit and recognition
- Professional development

**Written agreements**:
- Scope of work
- Payment terms
- Deadlines
- Revision policies
- Termination terms

### Scaling Your Team

**Phase 1: Essential (1-2 people)**
- Editor
- Thumbnail designer

**Phase 2: Core (3-4 people)**
- Full editor
- Designer
- VA
- Part-time scriptwriter

**Phase 3: Full (5-8 people)**
- Multiple editors
- Design team
- VA/Operations
- Writers
- Social media

**Phase 4: Organization (8+ people)**
- Creative director
- Production manager
- Full departments
- You become CEO, not creator
