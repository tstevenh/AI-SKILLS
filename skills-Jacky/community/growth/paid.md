# Paid Community Growth

## Overview

Paid acquisition accelerates community growth when organic strategies aren't fast enough. While more expensive, paid channels offer predictable, scalable growth when executed properly. This guide covers paid growth strategies specifically optimized for community businesses.

---

## Part 1: Paid Growth Fundamentals

### When to Use Paid

**Good Time for Paid**:
- Validated organic traction
- Clear unit economics (LTV > 3x CAC)
- Proven conversion funnel
- Budget available
- Ready to scale

**Bad Time for Paid**:
- Unvalidated community concept
- Unknown LTV/churn
- Broken onboarding
- No organic foundation
- Tight budget

### The Paid Math

```
Paid Acquisition is Profitable When:
LTV > 3× CAC

Example:
- Average member stays 8 months at $49 = $392 LTV
- Need CAC below $130 for 3:1 ratio
- Can afford to spend up to $130 to acquire a member
```

### Paid Channels Overview

| Channel | Best For | Typical CPL | Learning Curve |
|---------|----------|-------------|----------------|
| Facebook/Meta | B2C, broad audiences | $5-30 | Medium |
| Instagram | Visual, lifestyle | $5-30 | Medium |
| YouTube | Education, how-to | $10-50 | High |
| Google Search | High intent | $20-100 | Medium |
| LinkedIn | B2B, professional | $30-150 | Low |
| TikTok | Gen Z, viral | $3-20 | Medium |

## Part 2: Facebook/Meta Ads

### Why Facebook Works for Communities

- Massive audience (3B+ users)
- Detailed targeting
- Lookalike audiences
- Visual formats
- Proven for subscriptions

### Campaign Structure

**Testing Phase (ABO)**:
```
Campaign: Community - Testing
├── Ad Set 1: Interest - [Topic 1]
│   ├── Ad 1: Video testimonial
│   ├── Ad 2: Founder story
│   └── Ad 3: Results-focused
├── Ad Set 2: Interest - [Topic 2]
│   └── [Same ad variations]
└── Ad Set 3: Lookalike - Email list
    └── [Same ad variations]
```

**Scaling Phase (CBO)**:
```
Campaign: Community - Scale (CBO)
├── Winning Ad Set 1
├── Winning Ad Set 2
└── Winning Lookalike
```

### Ad Creative for Communities

**Video Ad Structure** (60-90 seconds):
```
Hook (0-3 sec): Pattern interrupt or bold claim
Problem (3-15 sec): Relatable pain point
Solution (15-30 sec): Introduce community
Proof (30-50 sec): Results and testimonials
CTA (50-60 sec): Clear next step
```

**Ad Copy Framework**:
```
[Hook - Question or Statement]

Are you tired of [pain point]?

I created [Community Name] for [audience] who want [outcome].

Inside you'll get:
✓ [Benefit 1]
✓ [Benefit 2]
✓ [Benefit 3]

Join [X]+ members who have [result].

[CTA] → [Link]
```

### Audience Targeting

**Cold Audiences**:
- Interest-based (related topics)
- Behavior-based (purchase behaviors)
- Demographic targeting

**Warm Audiences**:
- Website visitors
- Email list
- Video viewers
- Engagement audiences

**Lookalike Audiences**:
- LAL of buyers/members (1-3%)
- LAL of email list (1-5%)
- LAL of engaged users (1-5%)

### Facebook Ads Funnel

```
COLD TRAFFIC
├── Video ad (brand awareness)
├── Image ad (offer introduction)
└── Carousel (feature showcase)
        ↓
WARM TRAFFIC (Retargeting)
├── Testimonial ads
├── Objection-handling ads
└── Urgency/scarcity ads
        ↓
HOT TRAFFIC
├── Final push ads
└── Limited-time offers
```

## Part 3: YouTube Ads

### Why YouTube Works

- High-intent audience (searching)
- Long-form education
- Trust building
- Evergreen content
- Lower competition

### YouTube Ad Types

**Skippable In-Stream**:
- Plays before/during videos
- Skip after 5 seconds
- Pay for views 30+ seconds
- Best for brand awareness

**Non-Skippable In-Stream**:
- 15-20 seconds max
- Higher completion
- Higher CPM
- Best for simple messages

**Discovery Ads**:
- Appears in search results
- Pay for clicks
- High intent
- Best for educational content

### YouTube Ad Strategy for Communities

**The "Long-Form Sell"**:
```
5-15 minute ad video:
- Intro and hook (30 sec)
- Your story (2 min)
- The problem you solve (2 min)
- The solution (community) (3 min)
- Proof and testimonials (2 min)
- Detailed offer (2 min)
- CTA (30 sec)
```

**Targeting**:
- Keywords (what they search)
- Placements (specific channels/videos)
- Custom audiences (your data)
- In-market audiences

### YouTube Budget Guidelines

```
Testing: $50-100/day for 2-4 weeks
Scaling: $200-500+/day when profitable
Minimum: $30/day to gather data
```

Flat fee + affiliate bonus

## Part 7: Affiliate Programs

### Building an Affiliate Program

**Why Affiliates Work for Communities**:
- Pay for performance only
- Scale without budget risk
- Leverage others' audiences
- Ongoing relationship

### Affiliate Program Structure

**Commission Options**:
```
Option 1: First Month Commission
- 30-50% of first payment
- One-time payout
- Lower risk for you

Option 2: Recurring Commission
- 20-30% ongoing
- Higher lifetime payouts
- Motivates affiliate retention

Option 3: Tiered Commission
- Base rate: 20%
- 10+ referrals: 30%
- 25+ referrals: 40%
- Rewards top performers
```

**Who to Recruit**:
- Current members (best advocates)

## Part 8: Retargeting Strategies

### Why Retargeting Matters

**Stats**:
- Only 2-3% convert on first visit
- Retargeted visitors 70% more likely to convert
- Keeps you top of mind
- Lower CPM than cold traffic

### Retargeting Audiences

**Website Visitors**:
```
- All visitors (general)
- Pricing page visitors (high intent)
- Blog readers (content interest)
- Cart abandoners (very high intent)
- Previous members (win-back)
```

**Video Viewers**:
```
- 25% watched (aware)
- 50% watched (interested)
- 75% watched (very interested)
- 95% watched (hot)
```

**Email Engagement**:
```
- Opened but didn't click
- Clicked but didn't convert
- Inactive subscribers
```

### Retargeting Ad Strategy

**Sequenced Retargeting**:
```
Day 1-3: Education/Value
"Here's what you'll learn inside [Community]"
[Free resource or preview]

Day 4-7: Social Proof
"See what members are saying..."
[Testimonials and results]

Day 8-14: Objection Handling
"Wondering if this is right for you?"
[FAQ and common concerns]

Day 15-21: Urgency
"Limited time: [Offer]"
[Deadline or scarcity]

Day 22-30: Final Push
"Last chance: [Strong offer]"
[Final CTA]
```

## Part 9: Budget and Scaling

### Budget Allocation

**Starting Budget**:
```
Minimum: $500-1,000/month to test
Recommended: $2,000-5,000/month for real data
Scaling: 10-20% of revenue reinvested
```

**Budget Split**:
```
Testing Phase (Month 1-2):
- 70% Prospecting (cold)
- 30% Retargeting (warm)

Scaling Phase (Month 3+):
- 50% Prospecting
- 30% Retargeting
- 20% Lookalikes
```

### Scaling Rules

**When to Scale**:
- Consistent positive ROAS
- Conversion funnel working
- Onboarding handles volume
- Community can absorb growth

**How to Scale**:
```
1. Increase budget 20-30% every 3-4 days
2. Add new audiences
3. Expand placements
4. Test new creatives
5. Open new channels
```

### CAC and LTV Tracking

**Calculate Monthly**:
```
CAC = Total Ad Spend / New Members from Paid

LTV = Average Monthly Revenue × Average Months

Target: LTV > 3× CAC

Example:
- Spent $3,000 on ads
- Acquired 30 members
- CAC = $100
- Average stay: 6 months
- Monthly price: $49
- LTV = $294
- Ratio: 2.94:1 (close, but improve retention)
```

## Summary

### Paid Growth Principles

1. **Unit Economics First**: Know your LTV before spending
2. **Test Small, Scale Winners**: Don't bet big untested
3. **Creative Is King**: Great ads beat great targeting
4. **Full Funnel**: Cold → Warm → Hot
5. **Track Everything**: Data-driven decisions

### Paid Growth Quick Start

```
Week 1: Set up tracking and pixels
Week 2: Create 3-5 ad variations
Week 3: Launch test campaigns ($500)
Week 4: Analyze and iterate
Month 2: Scale what works
```

### Paid Growth Success Formula

```
Winning Creative + Right Audience + Optimized Funnel + Proper Budget = Scalable Growth
```
