# Community & Paid Groups Skill

## Overview

This skill provides comprehensive knowledge for building, launching, growing, and monetizing online communities and paid membership groups. Whether you're creating a free community for lead generation, a paid subscription group, or a full membership site, this skill covers every aspect of community building.

## When to Use This Skill

Use this skill when:
- Planning or launching a new community
- Choosing a community platform (Skool, Discord, Circle, etc.)
- Developing community strategy and content plans
- Creating pricing and monetization strategies
- Improving engagement and reducing churn
- Scaling an existing community
- Analyzing community business models

## Quick Reference

### Platform Selection Guide

| Platform | Best For | Price Range | Transaction Fee |
|----------|----------|-------------|-----------------|
| Skool | Course creators, coaches | $9-99/mo | 2.9-10% |
| Discord | Gaming, tech, younger demos | Free | 10% (subscriptions) |
| Circle | Professional communities | $89-199+/mo | 0.5-7% |
| Mighty Networks | All-in-one membership | $79-354+/mo | 0.5-2% |
| Slack | B2B, professional | $0-15/user/mo | N/A |
| Facebook Groups | Mass reach, older demos | Free | N/A |

### Community Business Models

1. **Free Community (Lead Gen)**: Build audience, nurture leads, upsell products
2. **Paid Community (Subscription)**: Monthly/annual membership fee
3. **Tiered Community**: Free + paid tiers with increasing value
4. **Course + Community Bundle**: One-time course fee includes community access
5. **SaaS + Community**: Product customers get community access
6. **Membership Site**: Gated content + community features

### Key Metrics to Track

- **MRR/ARR**: Monthly/Annual Recurring Revenue
- **Churn Rate**: % members leaving per month (aim for <5%)
- **LTV**: Lifetime Value of a member
- **CAC**: Cost to Acquire a Customer
- **Engagement Rate**: % active members per week/month
- **NPS**: Net Promoter Score

## Community Success Formula

```
Success = (Clear Purpose × Right Platform × Consistent Value) ÷ Friction
```

The fundamentals:
1. **Clear Purpose**: Why does this community exist? What transformation do members get?
2. **Right Platform**: Match platform to audience behavior and expectations
3. **Consistent Value**: Regular content, events, and connection opportunities
4. **Low Friction**: Easy to join, easy to engage, easy to get value

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

1. **Building before validating**: Launch to founding members first
2. **Over-engineering**: Start simple, add features based on member needs
3. **Neglecting onboarding**: First 7 days determine retention
4. **No engagement rituals**: Habits drive long-term engagement
5. **Pricing too low**: Undervalues offering, attracts wrong members
6. **Trying to please everyone**: Niche down, serve deeply
