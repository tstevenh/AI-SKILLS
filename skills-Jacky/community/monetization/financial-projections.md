# Community Financial Projections and Planning

## Overview

Understanding community economics enables better decision-making, from pricing to hiring to growth investment. This guide provides frameworks for projecting revenue, managing costs, and building a sustainable community business.

---

## Part 1: Community Economics Fundamentals

### Key Financial Metrics

**Revenue Metrics**:
```
MRR (Monthly Recurring Revenue):
- Total recurring revenue per month
- Foundation of community business

ARR (Annual Recurring Revenue):
- MRR × 12
- How investors value subscription businesses

ARPU (Average Revenue Per User):
- Total revenue / Total members
- Measures pricing efficiency

Expansion Revenue:
- Revenue from upsells, upgrades
- Key to growth without new acquisition
```

**Cost Metrics**:
```
CAC (Customer Acquisition Cost):
- Marketing spend / New customers
- How much to acquire one member

LTV (Lifetime Value):
- ARPU × Average lifetime (months)
- Total value of a member

COGS (Cost of Goods Sold):
- Direct costs of delivery
- Platform, content, events

Gross Margin:
- (Revenue - COGS) / Revenue
- Available for operations and profit
```

**Efficiency Metrics**:
```
LTV:CAC Ratio:
- LTV / CAC
- Target: 3:1 or higher

Payback Period:
- CAC / Monthly ARPU
- Months to recover acquisition cost

Net Revenue Retention (NRR):
- (Starting MRR + Expansion - Contraction - Churn) / Starting MRR
- Target: >100% (growth from existing customers)

Churn Rate:
- Lost members / Total members
- Monthly: <5% healthy, <10% acceptable
```

## Part 2: Revenue Forecasting

### Simple Revenue Model

**Formula**:
```
MRR = Active Members × Average Price

Example:
500 members × $49/month = $24,500 MRR
$24,500 × 12 = $294,000 ARR
```

### Growth-Adjusted Revenue Model

**Formula**:
```
End of Period Members = 
Starting Members + New Members - Churned Members

Churned Members = Starting Members × Churn Rate

Monthly Revenue = End of Period Members × Average Price
```

**12-Month Projection Example**:
```
Assumptions:
- Starting members: 100
- Monthly new members: 30
- Monthly churn: 5%
- Price: $49/month

Month 1:  100 + 30 - 5 = 125 members × $49 = $6,125
Month 2:  125 + 30 - 6 = 149 members × $49 = $7,301
Month 3:  149 + 30 - 7 = 172 members × $49 = $8,428
Month 4:  172 + 30 - 9 = 193 members × $49 = $9,457
Month 5:  193 + 30 - 10 = 213 members × $49 = $10,437
Month 6:  213 + 30 - 11 = 232 members × $49 = $11,368
Month 7:  232 + 30 - 12 = 250 members × $49 = $12,250
Month 8:  250 + 30 - 13 = 267 members × $49 = $13,083
Month 9:  267 + 30 - 13 = 284 members × $49 = $13,916
Month 10: 284 + 30 - 14 = 300 members × $49 = $14,700
Month 11: 300 + 30 - 15 = 315 members × $49 = $15,435
Month 12: 315 + 30 - 16 = 329 members × $49 = $16,121

Year 1 Total: ~$138,621
Year 1 Ending ARR: $193,452
```

### Multi-Tier Revenue Model

**Formula**:
```
Total MRR = (Tier 1 Members × Tier 1 Price) + 
            (Tier 2 Members × Tier 2 Price) + 
            (Tier 3 Members × Tier 3 Price)
```

**Example with Tiers**:
```
Tier Distribution:
- Basic ($29): 60% of members
- Pro ($79): 30% of members
- VIP ($199): 10% of members

500 Total Members:
- Basic: 300 × $29 = $8,700
- Pro: 150 × $79 = $11,850
- VIP: 50 × $199 = $9,950

Total MRR: $30,500
Blended ARPU: $61/member

vs Single Tier:
500 × $49 = $24,500 MRR
ARPU: $49/member

Tiered model = 24% higher ARPU
```

### Annual Plan Impact

**Formula**:
```
Monthly Recognized Revenue = (Monthly subscribers × Monthly price) +
                            (Annual subscribers × Annual price / 12)

Cash Flow = New monthly payments + New annual payments
```

**Example**:
```
Mix: 60% monthly, 40% annual
Members: 500
Monthly: $49
Annual: $399 (32% discount)

Monthly subscribers: 300 × $49 = $14,700 MRR
Annual subscribers: 200 × $399/12 = $6,650 MRR
Total MRR: $21,350

Annual Cash Impact:
- Year 1: Annuals pay $79,800 upfront
- Better cash flow for investment
```

## Part 4: Profitability Analysis

### Gross Margin Calculation

**Formula**:
```
Gross Profit = Revenue - Direct Costs (COGS)
Gross Margin = Gross Profit / Revenue × 100%

For Community:
COGS typically includes:
- Platform costs
- Transaction fees
- Direct content costs
```

**Example**:
```
Revenue: $50,000/month

COGS:
- Platform: $200
- Transaction fees (5%): $2,500
- Content production: $1,500
Total COGS: $4,200

Gross Profit: $45,800
Gross Margin: 91.6%
```

### Operating Margin

**Formula**:
```
Operating Profit = Gross Profit - Operating Expenses
Operating Margin = Operating Profit / Revenue × 100%

Operating Expenses include:
- Team salaries
- Marketing
- Tools and software
- Administrative costs
```

**Example**:
```
Gross Profit: $45,800

Operating Expenses:
- Team: $12,000
- Marketing: $5,000
- Tools: $500
- Admin/Legal: $500
Total OpEx: $18,000

Operating Profit: $27,800
Operating Margin: 55.6%
```

### Net Margin

**Formula**:
```
Net Profit = Operating Profit - Taxes - Other Expenses
Net Margin = Net Profit / Revenue × 100%
```

**Typical Community Margins by Stage**:
```
Early Stage: 70-90% (low costs)
Growth Stage: 50-70% (adding team)
Scale Stage: 30-50% (full team)
Mature Stage: 25-40% (optimized)
```

## Part 5: Unit Economics

### LTV Calculation

**Simple LTV**:
```
LTV = ARPU × Average Lifetime Months

Example:
ARPU: $49
Average lifetime: 10 months
LTV = $49 × 10 = $490
```

**Detailed LTV**:
```
LTV = (ARPU × Gross Margin) × (1 / Churn Rate)

Example:
ARPU: $49
Gross Margin: 90%
Monthly Churn: 5%

LTV = ($49 × 0.90) × (1 / 0.05)
LTV = $44.10 × 20
LTV = $882
```

**LTV with Expansion**:
```
LTV = Base LTV + (Expansion Rate × Expansion Value)

Example:
Base LTV: $490
20% upgrade to $199 tier (additional $150/mo × 10 months)
Expansion: 0.20 × $1,500 = $300

Enhanced LTV: $490 + $300 = $790
```

### CAC Calculation

**Formula**:
```
CAC = Total Acquisition Costs / New Customers

Acquisition Costs include:
- Paid advertising
- Marketing tools
- Content creation (portion)
- Affiliate/referral costs
- Time value of marketing effort
```

**Example**:
```
Monthly Acquisition Spending:
- Ads: $3,000
- Content: $500
- Affiliates: $500
- Tools: $200
Total: $4,200

New members this month: 50
CAC = $4,200 / 50 = $84
```

### LTV:CAC Analysis

**Interpretation**:
```
Ratio < 1: Losing money (unsustainable)
Ratio 1-2: Breaking even to marginal
Ratio 2-3: Acceptable, improve if possible
Ratio 3-5: Healthy, scale acquisition
Ratio > 5: Excellent, consider spending more
```

**Example Analysis**:
```
LTV: $882
CAC: $84
Ratio: 10.5:1

Interpretation: 
Excellent unit economics. 
Can afford to spend significantly more on acquisition.
Consider increasing marketing investment.
```

### Payback Period

**Formula**:
```
Payback Period = CAC / Monthly ARPU

Example:
CAC: $84
ARPU: $49
Payback = $84 / $49 = 1.7 months

Interpretation:
Recover acquisition cost in under 2 months.
Excellent for cash flow.
Target: <12 months
```

## Part 7: Investment Decisions

### Hiring ROI

**When to Hire Framework**:
```
Hire when:
1. Clear ROI on role
2. Can afford 6+ months salary
3. Will save significant founder time
4. Required for growth bottleneck
```

**ROI Calculation**:
```
Founder time freed: 20 hrs/week
Founder hourly value: $150
Monthly value: 80 hrs × $150 = $12,000

Hire cost: $5,000/month

ROI: ($12,000 - $5,000) / $5,000 = 140%

Plus: Growth enabled, quality improved
```

### Marketing Investment

**Rule of Thumb**:
```
Invest in marketing when:
LTV:CAC > 3:1
Payback < 12 months
Acquisition channels proven
Capacity exists to onboard
```

**Scaling Framework**:
```
At $10K MRR: Invest $1-2K/month in marketing (10-20%)
At $50K MRR: Invest $5-10K/month in marketing (10-20%)
At $100K MRR: Invest $10-20K/month in marketing (10-20%)
```

### Platform/Tool Decisions

**Upgrade Triggers**:
```
Upgrade platform when:
- Hitting limits
- Missing critical features
- Losing members due to UX
- ROI clear

Calculate ROI:
- Upgrade cost: $X/month additional
- Value gained: Time saved, features, members retained
- If value > cost × 3: Upgrade
```

**Assumptions**:
- Lower growth (15 members/month)
- Higher churn (8%)

**Assumptions**:

## Summary

### Financial Health Indicators

**Green (Healthy)**:
- LTV:CAC > 3:1
- Monthly churn < 5%
- Gross margin > 80%
- Operating margin > 30%
- MRR growth > 5%/month

**Yellow (Concerning)**:
- LTV:CAC 2-3:1
- Monthly churn 5-10%
- Gross margin 60-80%
- Operating margin 15-30%
- MRR growth 2-5%/month

**Red (Urgent)**:
- LTV:CAC < 2:1
- Monthly churn > 10%
- Gross margin < 60%
- Operating margin < 15%
- MRR declining

### Financial Decision Framework

```
For every major decision, ask:
1. What's the expected ROI?
2. What's the payback period?
3. What's the risk if it doesn't work?
4. Can we afford to be wrong?
5. What does success look like in 3-6 months?
```
